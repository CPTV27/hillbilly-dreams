# OUTSIDER ECONOMICS — Cover Art Briefs
## Style: Solarpunk meets Deep South. Woodcut/linocut. Bold, limited palette.

---

## SERIES DESIGN RULES

- Same woodcut/linocut style across all three volumes
- Same bold sans-serif typography (matches V1)
- Title: OUTSIDER ECONOMICS (large, top)
- Subtitle: varies per volume (smaller, bottom)
- Author: CHASE TUTHILL PIERSON (bottom)
- Each cover tells you where you are in the story through color and image
- NOT generic solarpunk (no glass domes, no bullet trains)
- THIS IS Deep South solarpunk: red clay, shotgun houses, porches, river, live oaks, cotton fields, juke joints, church steeples

---

## V1 (PUBLISHED — reference)

**Title:** Outsider Economics: A Field Manual for Independent Economic Systems
**Image:** Ruined buildings behind a single flower growing through cracked earth

**Mood:** The problem. Extraction. Decay. But life pushing through.

## V2 COVER BRIEF

**Title:** Outsider Economics: The Implementation Playbook
**Subtitle:** Building Community Economic Sovereignty in 90 Days

**Image concept:** A community barn raising — but solarpunk Deep South. Five or six people building a structure together. The structure could be a shotgun house with solar panels on the tin roof, or a front porch being framed with visible wiring/tech integration growing like vines through the wood. Hands holding hammers, pulling rope, lifting beams. The task board (whiteboard with three columns) is visible in the foreground or leaning against the structure.

**Key elements:**
- People working together (not looking at screens — physical labor, coordination)
- The structure is half-built — you can see the skeleton, the promise of what it'll be
- Southern architectural DNA: tin roof, porch columns, wood frame, maybe a pecan tree nearby
- A garden growing in the foreground (callback to V1's flower — it grew)
- Tools visible: hammer, saw, level, but also a laptop or tablet sitting on a sawhorse (tech is present but not dominant)

**Palette:** Green and gold on cream. Black linework.
- Primary: Deep leaf green (#2D5016) and warm gold (#C49A2A)
- Background: Cream/warm paper (same as V1)
- Linework: Black

**Mood:** Construction. Community. Hands building something real. The flower from V1 has become a garden, and now people are building around it. Forward motion. Not theory — action.

**Typography:**
```

ECONOMICS

[IMAGE]

THE IMPLEMENTATION PLAYBOOK
```

## V3 COVER BRIEF

**Title:** Outsider Economics: What Happens Next
**Subtitle:** The Deep South in 2028

**Image concept:** A two-lane highway (Highway 61 or the Natchez Trace) stretching toward a small town on the horizon at dawn. The town is alive — porch lights glowing, a radio tower with a signal emanating, solar panels on rooftops catching the first light, a marquee lit up (the venue), smoke rising from a kitchen (the restaurant). The road has mile markers or signs with town names: Natchez, Vicksburg, Clarksdale, Greenville.

On the near side of the image (foreground), the road is darker — the past, the extraction economy. On the far side, the town glows. The transition from dark to light happens across the image.

**Key elements:**
- The corridor — a road connecting towns, not a single location
- The town is small but alive (not a metropolis — a Main Street with 20 buildings)
- A Prevost bus on the road (small, in the distance — the Rise Up tour)
- The river visible in the background (the Mississippi — always present)
- Dawn light — the future is arriving, not arrived
- Power lines or a radio tower with signal waves (connectivity, not isolation)
- A porch with a figure sitting on it (small, silhouette — watching the dawn)

**Palette:** Deep blue and warm gold on cream. Black linework.
- Primary: Delta blue (#1B3A5C) and sunrise gold (#D4A843)
- Background: Cream/warm paper (same as V1 and V2)
- Linework: Black
- Accent: A single warm orange glow from the town (callback to V1's orange — but now it's light, not fire)

**Mood:** Prophecy. Calm inevitability. The future you can see from the porch. Not utopian — grounded. This is a real road, real towns, real people. But the direction has changed.

**Typography:**
```
OUTSIDER
ECONOMICS

[IMAGE]

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
CHASE TUTHILL PIERSON
```
