# Andrew Freedman Home — The Bronx doesn't need your pity. It needs the pipe.

A Gilded Age hotel for the rich became a homeless shelter. Then it became something rarer — a place where the building itself is the media plant, the classroom, and the proof that local infrastructure can look like a network, not a charity line item.

## The town / the place

South Bronx. A landmark with weight — the kind of architecture cities pretend to care about until the grant runs out. The Freedman wasn't a metaphor. It was a roof, a kitchen line, and a population that gets talked *about* more than *with*.

## The problem

Money for "digital inclusion" usually buys laptops nobody maintains and hotspots that expire. The extraction isn't always obvious — it's consultants, vendors, and program administrators who clock out and drive home somewhere else.

The leak: **capacity lives in PDFs and slide decks**, not in systems residents can run, touch, or inherit.

## What we built

Community media infrastructure scoped like a small broadcaster — **local WiFi**, **studio production**, **distribution paths** for what gets made in the building, and **economic development tooling** that ties content to real work (training, gigs, neighborhood commerce).

Source packet: AFH Special Projects Division materials, studio notes, Winsonic + AFH partnership docs — the full stack spec, not a one-pager.

## The math

The receipts live in those project files — **studio equipment line items**, **telco / partnership valuation work**, **operating budgets** built for a building that has to justify every dollar to funders and neighbors. I'm not pasting proprietary tables here; the point is the *category* of math:

- CapEx for gear you actually use weekly beats CapEx for gear that sits in a closet.
- If the network is yours, you stop renting "access" as a monthly extraction.

## What it means

**The same architecture that runs a major media company can run a community center** — if you stop treating residents as an audience and start treating them as crew.

## Start This Week

**24 hours:** Inventory who in your building can teach one skill on camera — audio, lighting, interview, edit.

**7 days:** Map one physical path from "we recorded it" to "someone outside the building can see it" without handing keys to a platform you don't control.

**90 days:** Price the difference between *owning* your WiFi edge versus *subsidizing* someone else's subscriber growth.
