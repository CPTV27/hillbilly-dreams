# Regional Technology Provider — Market Research
## Source: Perplexity Deep Research, March 25, 2026

### Key Findings

#### ARPA Funding Window
- Mississippi: ~$826M unspent ARPA SLFRF
- Hard deadline: December 31, 2026
- Eligible uses: cybersecurity, IT modernization, broadband, digital inclusion, government service delivery
- Louisiana and Arkansas hold similar unspent balances

#### Competitive Landscape
- Tyler Technologies: 11-year MS state relationship, ms.gov via NIC subsidiary
  - Min contract for city <25K: $25-75K/year before implementation
  - Often 3-5% of a small town's entire general fund
- OpenGov: Landed Starkville, MS in 2025 (permitting/licensing)
- CivicPlus: ~$6-8K/year for municipal websites
- CentralSquare: Owns public safety in Alabama
- **VERIFIED GAP: No regional provider serves all four segments (civic, K-12, DMO/tourism, SMB) anywhere in the Deep South**

#### E-Rate Opportunity
- Mississippi: $33.3M in FY2026 E-Rate requests filed
- FY2025 committed: $26.3M
- New Category 2 five-year cycle opens FY2026 (fresh budget for every district)
- ~1 in 4 MS districts still below 1 Mbps/student bandwidth goal

#### Title IV-A
- $17.4M/year allocated to Mississippi for K-12 technology
- Includes infrastructure and digital tools

#### Additional Federal Programs
- USDA Rural Development / ReConnect: broadband and digital infrastructure grants
- EDA Tech Hubs: regional innovation funding
- HUD CDBG: community development block grants with technology-eligible line items

#### Technology Spend Per Capita
- National average for cities <50K: ~$150-250/capita/year on IT
- Mississippi cities: significantly below national average
- Natchez city government currently runs legacy financial systems (GovTech article confirmed)
