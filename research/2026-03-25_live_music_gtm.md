# Live Music / Venue GTM Analysis
## Source: Perplexity Deep Research, March 25, 2026

### Market Size
- U.S. live music market: $19.7B (2026)
- 82% of independent artists can't afford to tour (Ditto Music, 2025)
- Venue management software market growing rapidly

### The Four Incumbents

| Player | What They Do | What They Don't | Cost | Structural Problem |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|------|-------------------|
| Ticketmaster/Live Nation | Ticketing, promotion | Venue ops, artist development | High fees (25-30% total) | Fee extraction model, anti-competitive |
| FareHarbor | Booking/reservations | Music-specific features | Per-booking fees | Tourism-focused, not music |
| Prism.fm | Booking management for agents | Ticketing, venue ops, media | $200-500/mo | Agent-side only, no venue tools |
| Bandsintown/Songkick | Concert discovery, fan alerts | Booking, ticketing, revenue | Free/ads | Discovery only, no transaction layer |

### Big Muddy Differentiation
- **Ecosystem already built:** Hotel + venue + magazine + radio + touring + records on one platform
- **Revenue model:** Flat SaaS + owned media (not fee extraction on transactions)
- **Geography is defensible:** Mississippi corridor is a physical moat
- **Stripe Connect rails:** Direct artist payouts, transparent splits
- **Sovereign GCP stack:** Not dependent on third-party ticketing infrastructure

### Last Six Steps (from GTM analysis)
1. Touring dashboard configuration (HDX deployment)
2. Venue inventory build (capacity, tech specs, green rooms)
3. Ticketing go-live (Stripe Connect + HDX)
4. Media flywheel activation (magazine + radio promoting shows)
5. Insurance and compliance (venue liability, artist contracts)
6. Pricing architecture (flat monthly + per-event, no percentage fees)

### Key Stat for Pitch
- Ticketmaster charges 25-30% total fees
- Big Muddy: flat monthly SaaS, artist keeps 100% of ticket revenue minus processing
