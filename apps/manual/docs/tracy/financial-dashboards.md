# Financial Dashboards

Chase built a financial dashboard at `/tracy` on the website. Here's how to read it.

## How to Access

Go to: `bigmuddytouring.com/tracy`

You may need to sign in with your Google account.

## What You're Looking At

The dashboard shows a summary of the business finances — revenue, expenses, entities, and key metrics. It's designed to look like a Senate briefing document, not a spreadsheet.

### Key Sections

**Revenue:** What's coming in. This includes:
- Bar sales (show nights)
- Lodging income
- MBT/DSD subscriptions (Chase's daytime sales)
- Any one-off income

**Expenses:** What's going out. This includes:
- Rent/mortgage
- Utilities
- Inventory (liquor, supplies)
- Software and tools
- Insurance
- IRS payments

**Entities:** All the business entities we operate under, with their EINs and filing status.

## What to Check Weekly

Every Monday, spend 10 minutes looking at:

1. **Did last week's show night revenue get recorded?** If we had a show and the number isn't there, flag it.
2. **Are any expenses higher than usual?** Spikes mean something changed.
3. **Is the cash position OK?** Can we cover the next 2 weeks of obligations?

## What to Check Monthly

First week of each month:

1. **Revenue vs. last month** — Going up, down, or flat?
2. **Expense trends** — Anything creeping up?
3. **IRS payment confirmed** — Check that the monthly $100 went through
4. **Any outstanding invoices** — Are we owed money that hasn't come in?

## What "Looks Normal" vs. "Flag This"

| Normal | Flag It |
|---|---|
| Bar revenue $300-500 per show night | Bar revenue under $200 (something's wrong) |
| Monthly expenses within 10% of last month | Any single expense 2x higher than usual |
| IRS payment confirmed by 28th | Payment failed or missed |
| Positive cash position | Less than 2 weeks of expenses in the account |

## If Something Looks Wrong

1. Take a screenshot
2. Add a comment in Asana on the relevant task
3. Text Chase: "Something looks off on the dashboard — check Asana"
