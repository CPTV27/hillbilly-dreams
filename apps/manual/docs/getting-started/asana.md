# Using Asana

Asana is where your tasks live. Think of it as a to-do list that the whole team can see.

## Getting Set Up

1. **Check your email** for an invite from Chase (Asana)
2. **Click the link** and create your account
3. **Download the app** on your phone (search "Asana" in the App Store)
4. **Set up your profile** — add your name and a photo so we know it's you

## Your Project Board

Each person has their own project board:
- **Amy — Inn & Bar Ops**
- **Tracy — Business & Finance**
- **JP — Shows & Programming**

When you open Asana, click your project name to see your tasks.

## What a Task Looks Like

Each task has:
- **Title** — What needs to be done
- **Due date** — When it's due
- **Description** — Details and context
- **Comments** — Where you can ask questions or add updates

## What You Need to Do

1. **Check your board** once a day (morning is good)
2. **Mark tasks done** when you finish them (click the checkmark)
3. **Add a comment** if you have a question or need help
4. **Don't stress about it.** If something's confusing, just text Chase.

## How Tasks Get Added

Chase adds most tasks. If you think of something that needs doing, you can:
- Add it yourself (click "+ Add Task" at the top of your project)
- Or just text Chase and he'll add it

## Notifications

Asana will send you email notifications when:
- A new task is assigned to you
- Someone comments on your task
- A task is due soon

You can adjust these in Asana's settings if they get noisy.
