# Bar Inventory & Pricing

## Weekly Inventory Count

Do this every Monday morning. Takes about 20 minutes.

### What to Count

| Category | Items |
|---|---|
| **Liquor** | Whiskey, vodka, rum, gin, tequila — count by bottle (full, half, quarter) |
| **Beer** | Cases and individual bottles/cans in the cooler |
| **Wine** | Bottles on hand |
| **Mixers** | Sodas, juices, tonic, simple syrup, bitters |
| **Supplies** | Cups, napkins, ice, garnishes |

### How to Record It

For now, just keep a simple list. A notes app on your phone works. We'll get a proper system later.

Format:
```
Monday March 31
- Whiskey: 3 full, 1 half
- Vodka: 2 full
- Beer: 2 cases Lazy Magnolia, 1 case PBR
- Wine: 4 bottles red, 2 white
- Low on: tonic water, limes
```

### When to Order

If anything is below 2 units, order it. Don't wait for show night to realize we're out of something.

## Pricing

### The Basic Rule

**Pour cost should be 20-25%.** That means if a drink costs you $2 in ingredients, charge $8-10.

### Standard Pours

| Drink | Our Cost | Sell For | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Well drink | ~$1.50 | $7 | 79% |
| Premium drink | ~$3.00 | $10-12 | 70-75% |
| Domestic beer | ~$1.50 | $5 | 70% |
| Craft beer | ~$2.50 | $7 | 64% |
| Wine (glass) | ~$3.00 | $9 | 67% |

### Show Night Pricing

Keep it simple. Don't change prices for shows — it confuses people and slows down service. Specials are fine ("$5 PBR tall boys tonight") but don't discount the good stuff.

### Cash vs. Card

Accept both. Square takes 2.6% + $0.10 per swipe. That's the cost of doing business. Don't push cash-only — it loses sales.
