# Outsider Economics — Illustration Spec

Style: Warm, hand-drawn feel. Woodcut/linocut aesthetic matching the burnt orange (#C4441A) and cream (#F0E6D3) palette. Think editorial illustration — teaching concepts, not decorating pages. Each should explain an idea at a glance.

Format: SVG or PNG with transparent background. 800x500px minimum. Works on cream/light backgrounds.

---

## Philosophy Section

### 1. What Is Outsider Economics?
**Concept:** A small town seen from above — buildings, streets, dotted lines showing connections between businesses. Arrows flowing INWARD (not outward). The opposite of extraction.
**Teaching:** Value circulates locally instead of flowing out.

### 2. People Are the Currency
**Concept:** Network diagram where the nodes are people (simple figures), not dollar signs. Lines connecting them represent skills, trades, favors. A few nodes are highlighted as "connectors."
**Teaching:** Wealth = reliable people who coordinate, not dollars in a bank.

### 3. The $450,000 Secret
**Concept:** A simple flow diagram: 50 people → small coordination acts → arrows accumulating into a large sum. Like tributaries feeding a river.
**Teaching:** Many small exchanges compound into real economic activity.

### 4. The Extraction Trap
**Concept:** Split illustration. Left side: money flowing OUT of a town (arrows leaving buildings toward distant corporate logos). Right side: same town with money circulating IN loops between local businesses.
**Teaching:** Before and after stopping the leak.

### 5. Coordination, Not Scale
**Concept:** Two towns side by side. Left: one giant factory (scale). Right: many small connected shops with shared infrastructure lines between them (coordination). The coordinated town is thriving.
**Teaching:** Connected beats big.

### 6. The Federation Effect
**Concept:** Farmers' market diagram — individual stalls (independent) sharing a roof, parking lot, and foot traffic (shared infrastructure). Each stall has its own personality but benefits from the collective.
**Teaching:** Independence + shared infrastructure = federation.

## Toolkit Section

### 7. The Task Board
**Concept:** A kanban board (3 columns: Needs Doing, In Progress, Done) with task cards. Some cards have small skill icons (camera, wrench, pen). Arrows showing tasks flowing from left to right.
**Teaching:** Work finds the right person through visibility.

### 8. Building Without Banks
**Concept:** Five stacked blocks, each a different alternative finance method: Revenue Share, Micro-Patronage, Sweat Equity, Grant Stacking, Pre-Sales. No bank building anywhere. A plant growing through the stack.
**Teaching:** Multiple small funding sources replace one big loan.

### 9. The Directory
**Concept:** A phone screen showing a local business listing — photo, story, reviews, map pin. Next to it, a tourist walking down a Main Street pointing at the phone. Dotted line from phone to actual storefront.
**Teaching:** Digital discovery drives physical foot traffic.

### 10. The $20 Revolution
**Concept:** Price comparison ladder. Left side: stack of SaaS logos totaling $800/mo. Right side: single simple tool at $20/mo. A magnifying glass showing they do the same core thing.
**Teaching:** One affordable tool replaces a stack of expensive ones.

### 11. Technology Sovereignty
**Concept:** Two scenarios. Left: strings (puppet strings) going from a small business up to distant cloud logos — dependency. Right: same business with a solid foundation underneath it — ownership. The strings are cut.
**Teaching:** Own your stack or someone else controls your business.

### 12. Shared Services
**Concept:** Circle of 10 small business icons, all connected to a central hub. The hub has icons for: camera, calculator, megaphone, computer. Each business has a thin line to the hub. Cost label: "$100/each" vs. solo cost "$1,100 each."
**Teaching:** Pooling resources makes professional services affordable.

## Resources Section

### 13. Grants & Funding Map
**Concept:** Map of Mississippi/Louisiana/Tennessee with pin markers for key organizations (USDA, SBA, Hope Enterprise, CDFIs). Color-coded by type: federal (blue), state (green), CDFI (gold).
**Teaching:** Where the money is and who to call.

### 14. The Grant Stacking Strategy
**Concept:** Three small grant blocks ($10K each) stacking into a tower vs. one large grant block ($30K) with a "REJECTED" stamp. The stacked approach wins.
**Teaching:** Many small grants beat one big application.

### 15. Legal Structure Decision Tree
**Concept:** Flowchart. "Are you one person?" → LLC ($50). "Are you a group?" → "Sharing profits?" → Federation LLC. "Serving a cause?" → 501(c)(3). Simple, friendly decision tree.
**Teaching:** Pick the right structure without a lawyer consultation.

## Team Manual Illustrations

### 16. Photo Workflow
**Concept:** 4-step horizontal flow: Phone camera → Folder icon (BigMuddy-Dropbox) → Cloud (GCS) → Browser showing Media Vault grid. Arrows between each step. "2 min" label on the cloud step.
**Teaching:** How photos get from your phone to the website.

### 17. Show Night Timeline
**Concept:** Horizontal timeline from 4PM to midnight. Key moments marked: 4PM Stock Bar, 5PM Sound Check, 7PM Doors, 8PM Show, 11PM Last Call, 12AM Lock Up. Icons at each milestone.
**Teaching:** The rhythm of a show night at a glance.

### 18. The Flywheel
**Concept:** Circular diagram with 4 segments: Shows → Audience → Bar Revenue → More Shows. Arrows going clockwise. A small "2:1 multiplier" label showing downstream effects (rooms, directory, magazine) branching off.
**Teaching:** How each part of the business feeds the others.

### 19. Operator Split
**Concept:** Day/night split illustration. Left (daytime, warm): Chase on Main Street with a phone, walking between businesses. Right (nighttime, cool blue): Amy behind bar, JP on stage, Tracy at desk. A dotted line divides day and night.
**Teaching:** Two engines running simultaneously, different operators.

### 20. Content Studio Pipeline
**Concept:** Funnel diagram. One input at top (business name or event). Four outputs at bottom: Social Post, Magazine Article, Radio Spot, Review Response. Each with a small channel icon.
**Teaching:** One input generates content for four channels.

## Generation Notes

- All illustrations should feel cohesive — same line weight, same color palette, same warmth
- Avoid photorealism — this is a teaching tool, not a gallery
- Text labels are OK and encouraged (keep them short)
- Each illustration should make sense without reading the article — it teaches the concept standalone
- Accessible: sufficient contrast, no color-only information
