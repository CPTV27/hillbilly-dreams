# Chapter 3: Rise Up — The Talent Was Always Here

## Overview

Rise Up is a gospel and blues talent development program structured as a franchisable touring brand. This chapter examines the model: how a house band with a rotating cast sources talent from the corridor, how each performance generates seven revenue streams, and how the format scales to multiple units without centralizing creative control.

## The Talent Drain

The Mississippi corridor — Memphis to New Orleans — produced more foundational American music than any other region. Blues, gospel, jazz, rock and roll, soul, and country all trace roots to this 500-mile stretch.

The talent is still here. The infrastructure is not.

Documented pattern in corridor towns:

1. A vocalist sings in a church choir for fifteen years
2. They are recognized locally as exceptional
3. No recording studio, no label, no booking agent operates within 100 miles
4. They either stay in the choir or move to Nashville, Atlanta, or Los Angeles
5. The town loses the talent. The culture that produced it receives nothing.

Rise Up interrupts this pattern at step 3.

## The Model

Rise Up is structured as a performing group with three components:

### The House Band
A fixed core of professional musicians who rehearse, record, and tour together. The house band provides the musical foundation for every performance. Current status: in assembly, pre-production.

### The Rotating Cast
Local vocalists and instrumentalists who join the group for specific shows or runs. Sourced from:
- Church choirs in corridor towns
- Open talent searches at tour stops
- Referrals from local music directors and venue operators
- Directory network (musicians listed as businesses on the Deep South Directory)

The rotating cast is the talent pipeline. A vocalist discovered in Clarksdale joins for the Clarksdale show, impresses, and gets invited for the next three-city run. The best performers become semi-permanent. The pipeline never stops flowing because every show is a talent search.

### The Format
Each Rise Up performance follows a repeatable format:
1. House band opens with three songs
2. Rotating cast members perform featured sets
3. Collaborative finale with full ensemble
4. Post-show audience engagement (meet the artists, merch, recordings)

The format is designed for replication. Like Blue Man Group or Yachtley Crew, the brand is the format — not any individual performer. Multiple units can operate simultaneously in different regions with different rotating casts, all using the same format and platform.

## The Seven Revenue Streams

A single Rise Up performance at a venue generates activity across seven channels:

| Stream | Revenue type | Captured by |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| 1. Ticket sales | Direct | Entertainment division |
| 2. Live recording | Catalog asset | Records |
| 3. Radio content | Programming | Radio |
| 4. Editorial coverage | Marketing asset | Magazine |
| 5. Social/video content | Audience growth | All divisions |
| 6. Touring route data | Network value | Touring |
| 7. Merch sales | Direct | Entertainment division |

No single stream justifies the cost of touring. All seven together make every show profitable.

## The Artist Deal

Big Muddy Records offers artist services on these terms:

| Term | Value |
|------|-------|
| Master ownership | 100% retained by artist |
| Revenue split | Rev-share on label services (distribution, sync, marketing) |
| Recording cost | Covered by the platform (recordings from live shows) |
| Distribution | All major streaming platforms + sync licensing marketplace |
| Rights management | PRO registration, royalty tracking via platform |

The standard industry comparison:

| Term | Major label | Indie label | Big Muddy Records |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| Master ownership | Label owns | Varies | Artist owns 100% |
| Revenue split | 15–20% to artist | 50/50 | Rev-share on services only |
| Recoupment | Yes (artist pays back advances) | Sometimes | No advances, no recoupment |
| Creative control | Label decides | Negotiated | Artist decides |

The deal structure is designed to be the most artist-friendly in the industry. This is the recruiting advantage: talent joins Rise Up because they keep their masters and the infrastructure handles everything else.

## The Talent Search as Community Event

Every Rise Up show includes an open talent search component. This transforms a concert from entertainment into community investment:

- Local artists audition before or during the event
- The audience participates as judges (engagement driver)
- Selected performers join the rotation for future shows
- The talent search generates additional content (audition footage, backstage interviews)
- Local press covers the talent search (free marketing)

The talent search is the mechanism that keeps the roster fresh and keeps each corridor town invested in the program. The vocalist from the church choir in Greenville now performs on the Snowbird Circuit. That story belongs to Greenville. Every town wants one.

## Scaling to Multiple Units

The Rise Up format is designed for replication:

| Component | Unit 1 (Natchez) | Unit 2 (Memphis) | Unit 3 (New Orleans) |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| House band | Dedicated | Dedicated | Dedicated |
| Rotating cast | Corridor south | Corridor north | Gulf coast |
| Home venue | Big Muddy Inn | TBD | TBD |
| Tour range | MS, LA, AR | TN, MS, AL | LA, MS, FL |
| Platform | Shared | Shared | Shared |
| Brand | Shared | Shared | Shared |

Each unit operates semi-independently with its own house band and regional rotating cast. All units share the brand, the platform, and the recording/distribution infrastructure. Creative decisions are made at the unit level, not centrally.

Target timeline:
- Unit 1 (Natchez): Currently in development
- Unit 2: After Unit 1 completes one full touring season
- Unit 3: After two units demonstrate the model
- European expansion: After three domestic units are operational

## The Platform's Role

The Measurably Better platform handles the operational layer that makes Rise Up possible with a small team:

- **Booking and scheduling** — tour dates, venue coordination, rider management
- **Ticket sales** — integrated with directory and kiosk mode
- **Recording pipeline** — live show recordings processed and distributed
- **Royalty tracking** — per-artist splits calculated automatically
- **Content distribution** — performance media distributed across Radio, Magazine, social
- **Talent database** — roster management, audition history, availability
- **Financial reporting** — per-show P&L, per-artist earnings, division-level dashboard

Without the platform, this operation requires a tour manager, a booking agent, a recording engineer, a distributor, a social media manager, and an accountant. With the platform, it requires a division head with creative authority.

## Implementation

To launch a Rise Up unit:

1. Assemble the house band (4–6 musicians)
2. Identify the home venue
3. Book the first 5 shows within a 200-mile radius
4. Conduct talent search at each venue to build the rotating cast
5. Record every performance
6. Distribute recordings through Records
7. Publish editorial coverage through Magazine
8. Air live session content through Radio
9. Add all venues to the Touring route on the directory
10. Track per-show P&L through the platform
