# Chapter 6: Vicksburg — The Battlefield and the Balance Sheet

## Overview

Vicksburg, Mississippi receives 850,000 visitors per year to its National Military Park. Downtown Vicksburg captures a fraction of that traffic. This chapter examines how the directory bridges the gap between the battlefield and the balance sheet — connecting the tourist who drives in for the monuments to the restaurant, hotel, and shop owner who needs that visitor to walk through the door.

## The 850,000 Problem

Vicksburg National Military Park is one of the most visited Civil War sites in the United States. The National Park Service reports approximately 850,000 recreational visits annually. The park is free to enter. Visitors drive in, tour the battlefield, visit the USS Cairo Museum, and drive out.

The disconnect between park visitation and downtown economic capture is Vicksburg's central challenge:

| Metric | Estimated value |
|--------|----------------|
| Annual park visitors | 850,000 |
| Visitors who enter downtown | 180,000-250,000 (estimated 20-30%) |
| Average downtown spend per visitor | $25-45 |
| Total downtown tourism revenue | $4.5-11.25 million |
| Revenue if capture rate doubled | $9.0-22.5 million |

*Note: Downtown spend estimates are based on comparable heritage tourism towns. Vicksburg does not publish visitor spending data at the downtown level. These are modeled figures.*

The park sits on the eastern edge of town. A visitor can enter from I-20, drive through the park, and return to the interstate without ever passing through downtown. The physical infrastructure does not route visitors to Main Street. The digital infrastructure does not either — a Google search for "where to eat near Vicksburg battlefield" returns chain restaurants on the I-20 corridor before downtown options.

### The CVB Pitch

The CVB adoption conversation in Vicksburg centers on one proposition: the directory can produce more content, more frequently, at lower cost, with money staying local.

The proof is the content volume. A marketing agency produces 20-50 content assets per year for $100,000+. Thirty businesses on the directory, each submitting two photos per week, produce 240 assets per month — more in one month than the agency produces in five years. The AI assembles these into social posts, city guide articles, and video content. The CVB reviews and publishes.

This is not a theoretical comparison. The Natchez deployment (Chapter 2) demonstrates the content volume achievable from business contributions. The Vicksburg CVB can see the Natchez results before committing.

## The Battlefield-to-Downtown Bridge

The directory creates a specific digital pathway from the National Military Park to downtown businesses:

### The Itinerary Feature

A visitor planning a trip to Vicksburg finds the directory's Vicksburg page through search. The page includes:

- All listed businesses with photos, reviews, and endorsements
- A "Plan Your Visit" tool that generates a one-day or two-day itinerary combining the battlefield with downtown experiences
- Walking distance calculations from the park to downtown businesses
- Real-time information ("The Biscuit Company is open until 2 PM today")

The itinerary is not static. It updates daily based on business submissions, events, and seasonal patterns. A visitor searching in March sees different recommendations than one searching in October.

### The In-Park Touchpoint

The NPS does not allow commercial promotion within the park. However, the park's official website and visitor center can link to community resources. The directory positions itself as a community resource, not a commercial platform:

- "Explore Downtown Vicksburg" — a directory-powered page linked from the Visit Vicksburg website
- QR codes at the visitor center (if permitted by NPS guidelines) linking to the directory's Vicksburg page
- Integration with Google Maps "nearby" results through structured data on directory listings

### The Post-Visit Prompt

Visitors who access the directory during their Vicksburg visit can opt in to a follow-up: a single email or SMS 24 hours after their visit asking for reviews of the businesses they visited. This creates a feedback loop:

1. Visitor finds business through directory
2. Visitor has experience
3. Directory prompts for review
4. Review appears on the business's directory listing (and optionally on Google)
5. More reviews increase the business's visibility on the directory
6. More visibility attracts more visitors

The post-visit prompt is opt-in only and does not share visitor data with businesses. The directory facilitates the review; it does not sell the visitor's information.

## The Casino Question

Vicksburg's four casino properties represent significant economic activity but operate largely outside the local business ecosystem. The directory's approach to casinos:

**Do not target casino properties as directory members.** Casinos have their own marketing departments, digital infrastructure, and customer databases. They do not need the directory.

**Do target the businesses that serve non-casino visitors.** The directory's value proposition in Vicksburg is specifically for the downtown and waterfront businesses that compete with casinos for visitor attention and spending. The directory helps these businesses become visible to the 850,000 park visitors who are not casino customers.

**Do offer casino-adjacent data.** If the CVB adopts the directory, the economic pulse data can distinguish between casino-driven and non-casino-driven economic activity — a data point that currently does not exist and that the CVB can use to advocate for downtown investment.
