# People Are the Currency

![Network of people connected by skills and trades](https://storage.googleapis.com/bmt-media-bigmuddy/illustrations/outsider-economics/02-people-network-skills.webp)

In a small town, the most valuable resource isn't money. It's the person who shows up.

## The Insight

Traditional economics measures wealth in dollars. Outsider Economics measures it in **reliable people who will do the work**. A town of 10,000 with 50 people who consistently show up and coordinate is wealthier than a town of 100,000 where nobody talks to each other.

This isn't metaphor. It's math.

When you have a network of people who trust each other, you can:
- Share equipment instead of buying it
- Trade skills instead of hiring contractors
- Fill gaps instead of posting job listings
- Build reputation instead of buying advertising

## The $450,000 Secret

A small network of coordinated operators can generate $450,000 or more in economic activity per year — not through one big play, but through hundreds of small coordination acts that compound.

The money was always there. It was just leaking out to platforms, franchises, and services that could be handled locally.

## How to Apply This

1. **Map your 50 people.** Who shows up? Who has skills? Who has space, equipment, or time?
2. **Make them visible to each other.** A directory, a group chat, a weekly meetup — the format matters less than the consistency.
3. **Start trading.** Not barter (that's a tax nightmare). Real coordination: "I'll shoot your menu photos if you cater my event."
4. **Track the value.** When you start measuring coordination instead of transactions, you'll see the real economy.
