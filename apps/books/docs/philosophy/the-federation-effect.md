# The Federation Effect

![Independent stalls sharing infrastructure in a federation](https://storage.googleapis.com/bmt-media-bigmuddy/illustrations/outsider-economics/06-farmers-market-federation.webp)

A federation is a group of independent operators who share infrastructure without surrendering independence. It's the organizational model Outsider Economics is built on.

## Not a Co-op, Not a Corporation

Co-ops require consensus. Corporations require hierarchy. Federations require neither.

In a federation:
- Each member runs their own operation
- Shared services are opt-in, not mandatory
- No one votes on how you run your business
- You contribute what you want and use what you need
- The federation makes everyone stronger without making anyone dependent

Think of it like a farmers' market: shared space, shared foot traffic, individual businesses. But extended to marketing, technology, booking, and finance.

## How Federations Work

**Shared layer:** The things everyone needs — a directory listing, a booking system, a social media presence, a monthly report card, access to bulk purchasing.

**Independent layer:** The things that make each business unique — menu, pricing, hours, culture, personality, relationships.

The federation handles the shared layer so operators can focus on the independent layer. This is the opposite of a franchise, which standardizes everything and strips out personality.

## The Math

A single business paying for its own:
- Website: $100/month
- Review management: $300/month
- Social media tool: $100/month
- Booking system: $200/month
- Bookkeeper: $400/month
- **Total: $1,100/month**

A federation of 20 businesses sharing those services:
- Per-business cost: **$50-100/month**
- Same tools, same quality, local provider, local data

The savings are real. But the bigger win is that the money stays in town instead of flowing to 6 different SaaS companies in 6 different cities.

## Building a Federation

1. Start with 3-5 operators who already trust each other
2. Pick one shared service to test (directory listing is easiest)
3. Set a price everyone can afford ($20/month is the magic number)
4. Deliver value in the first week, not the first quarter
5. Add services as trust grows
