# What Is Outsider Economics?

Outsider Economics is a way of building local economies that work for the people who actually live there.

It starts with a simple observation: small towns already have most of what they need. They have skilled people, empty buildings, local knowledge, and customers who want to buy local. What they don't have is coordination.

## The Core Problem

Most economic development follows the same script: attract outside investment, offer tax incentives, hope a factory moves in. When that doesn't work, the talent leaves, the buildings decay, and the town waits for someone else to save it.

This is the **extraction model**. Value flows out. Decisions are made elsewhere. The town becomes a cost center for someone else's balance sheet.

## The Alternative

Outsider Economics flips this. Instead of waiting for outside capital, you coordinate what's already here:

- **People** become the currency, not dollars
- **Tasks** replace jobs as the unit of work
- **Federations** replace corporations as the unit of organization
- **Directories** replace marketing departments as the unit of discovery
- **Shared services** replace overhead as the unit of cost

The result isn't a commune or a co-op (though it can include both). It's a coordination system that lets independent operators share infrastructure without surrendering independence.

## Who This Is For

- Small-town operators who are tired of waiting
- Main Street business owners who can't afford the tools that big chains use
- Artists, musicians, and creators who want to stay where they are
- Economic development professionals who want an alternative to the incentive game
- Anyone who's looked at a dying downtown and thought, "There has to be another way"
