# The Extraction Trap

![Money leaving town versus circulating locally](https://storage.googleapis.com/bmt-media-bigmuddy/illustrations/outsider-economics/04-extraction-vs-circulation.webp)

Every dollar that leaves your town for a service that could be provided locally is extraction. Most small towns don't realize how much they're losing.

## What Extraction Looks Like

- A Main Street restaurant paying $300/month for a review management tool built in San Francisco
- A hotel paying 15-25% commission to an OTA (online travel agency) for bookings they could get directly
- A musician paying a Nashville booking agent for gigs 200 miles from Nashville
- A town paying a consultant from Jackson to write a grant that a local teacher could write
- A business paying for Google Ads to reach customers who drive past their door every day

None of these are evil. But they add up. A town of 500 businesses each leaking $500/month in extractive services is losing $3 million a year.

## Why It Happens

1. **Convenience.** The outside tool is easier to find and sign up for.
2. **Trust.** The national brand feels safer than the neighbor with a laptop.
3. **Visibility.** Local alternatives aren't discoverable — nobody knows the neighbor has a laptop.
4. **Scale pricing.** Big platforms subsidize prices with venture capital until local alternatives can't compete.

## How to Break Out

The answer isn't protectionism. It's **making the local alternative visible, reliable, and easy**.

- **Build a directory** so people can find local providers ([The Directory](/toolkit/the-directory))
- **Set prices that Main Street can afford** ([The $20 Revolution](/toolkit/the-20-revolution))
- **Pool shared services** so small operators get big-operator tools ([Shared Services](/toolkit/shared-services))
- **Own your technology** so your data and customers stay local ([Technology Sovereignty](/toolkit/technology-sovereignty))

## The Test

For any service your town pays for, ask: "Could someone within 50 miles do this?" If yes, the only thing missing is coordination.
