# Grants & Funding

Money available to small-town operators in the Deep South. Organized by source, with deadlines and typical award sizes where known.

## Federal Programs

### USDA Rural Development
The single most relevant federal agency for small-town operators.

- **Rural Business Development Grants (RBDG):** Up to $500,000 for business development, technical assistance, and training in rural areas. Rolling applications.
- **Community Facilities Direct Loan & Grant Program:** Funds essential community facilities (buildings, equipment) in rural areas under 20,000 population.
- **Rural Microentrepreneur Assistance Program:** Microloans up to $50,000 + technical assistance for businesses with 10 or fewer employees.
- **Value-Added Producer Grants (VAPG):** Up to $75,000 for planning, $250,000 for working capital. For agricultural producers adding value to their products.

**Apply:** [rd.usda.gov](https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services)

### Small Business Administration (SBA)
- **Community Advantage Loans:** Up to $350,000 through mission-oriented lenders. Lower barriers than traditional SBA loans.
- **Microloans:** Up to $50,000 through intermediary lenders. Average loan is ~$13,000.
- **HUBZone Program:** Government contracting preferences for businesses in historically underutilized zones (much of rural Mississippi qualifies).

**Apply:** [sba.gov](https://www.sba.gov)

### National Endowment for the Arts (NEA)
- **Our Town:** $25,000-$150,000 for creative placemaking projects. Perfect for music venues, arts spaces, and cultural tourism.
- **Challenge America:** $10,000 for arts projects in underserved communities.

**Apply:** [arts.gov/grants](https://www.arts.gov/grants)

### Economic Development Administration (EDA)
- **Public Works Program:** Infrastructure investments for economically distressed communities.
- **Economic Adjustment Assistance:** Flexible grants for communities experiencing economic disruption.

## Mississippi State Programs

### Mississippi Development Authority (MDA)
- **Ace Fund:** Gap financing for small businesses, $25,000-$250,000
- **Small Business Loan Guarantee Program:** State-backed guarantees to help businesses access bank loans
- **Tourism Development Program:** Grants for tourism-related infrastructure

**Contact:** [mississippi.org](https://www.mississippi.org)

### Mississippi Main Street Association
- Technical assistance and networking for downtown revitalization. Active programs in Natchez, Vicksburg, Clarksdale, and other corridor towns.

### Mississippi Arts Commission
- Grants for arts organizations, individual artists, and arts-in-education programs.

## Louisiana State Programs

### Louisiana Economic Development (LED)
- **Small Business Loan and Guaranty Program:** Up to $1.5M in loan guarantees
- **Micro Enterprise Program:** Loans for businesses with 5 or fewer employees

### Louisiana Main Street Program
- Active in 30+ communities including New Orleans and smaller corridor towns.

## Tennessee State Programs

### Tennessee Small Business Development Center
- Free consulting and training for small businesses statewide.

### Tennessee Arts Commission
- Grants for arts organizations and individual artists.

## Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs)

CDFIs are mission-driven lenders that serve communities traditional banks ignore. These operate in the Deep South:

- **Hope Enterprise Corporation (Jackson, MS):** The largest CDFI in the Deep South. Loans, policy advocacy, financial services for underserved communities.
- **Southern Bancorp (Arkadelphia, AR):** Community development bank serving the Delta region.
- **First South Financial Credit Union (Memphis, TN):** Lending in underserved Memphis communities.
- **LiftFund (San Antonio, TX + MS presence):** Microloans and small business lending across the South.

## Private and Foundation Funding

- **Kellogg Foundation:** Strong presence in Mississippi. Funds community-based economic development.
- **Robert Wood Johnson Foundation:** Health-focused but funds community infrastructure.
- **Mary Reynolds Babcock Foundation:** Specifically funds economic opportunity in the South.
- **Foundation for the Mid South:** Regional foundation focused on AR, LA, MS.

## Grant Writing Tips for Small Towns

1. **Start with the smallest grants.** $5,000-$10,000 grants have less competition and build your track record.
2. **Stack grants.** Three $10,000 grants from different sources is easier than one $30,000 grant.
3. **Use USDA first.** They're the most small-town friendly federal agency.
4. **Get a DUNS number and SAM registration now.** Most federal grants require these. They take 2-4 weeks. Don't wait until you find a grant.
5. **Partner with a 501(c)(3).** If you don't have nonprofit status, partner with a local one for fiscal sponsorship. Churches, chambers of commerce, and community foundations often do this.
