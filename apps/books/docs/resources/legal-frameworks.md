# Legal Frameworks

How to structure your operation in Mississippi and the Deep South. Not legal advice — practical frameworks from the field.

:::caution
This is informational, not legal advice. Consult an attorney for your specific situation. Mississippi Secretary of State: [sos.ms.gov](https://www.sos.ms.gov)
:::

## Business Structures

### LLC (Most Common)
- **Cost:** $50 filing fee in Mississippi (cheapest in the country)
- **Time:** Same-day online filing at [sos.ms.gov](https://www.sos.ms.gov)
- **Good for:** Single operators, partnerships, small businesses
- **Annual report:** Due by April 15 each year, $0 filing fee
- **Tax:** Pass-through by default (profits flow to your personal return)

### Series LLC
- Mississippi allows Series LLCs — one parent LLC with multiple "series" (sub-entities)
- Each series has separate assets and liabilities
- **Good for:** Operating multiple businesses (hotel + bar + music venue) under one umbrella with liability separation
- **Cost:** Same $50 filing fee for the parent. No separate filing for each series.

### Cooperative
- Mississippi has a cooperative statute, but it's underused
- **Good for:** Shared services, group purchasing, community-owned infrastructure
- **Considerations:** More complex governance than LLC. One member, one vote. Democratic control.
- Fiscal sponsorship through an existing co-op or 501(c)(3) is often easier than forming a new co-op

### 501(c)(3) Nonprofit
- **Good for:** Arts organizations, community development, education programs
- **Cost:** $275-$600 IRS filing fee depending on size
- **Time:** 3-6 months for IRS determination
- **Shortcut:** Fiscal sponsorship through an existing 501(c)(3) gives you nonprofit benefits while your application processes

## Federation Structure

A federation of independent operators doesn't need a single legal entity. The recommended structure:

1. **Each operator has their own LLC** (independence preserved)
2. **A shared LLC or co-op** holds the federation's shared assets (directory, website, shared services fund)
3. **Service agreements** (not bylaws) define what each member contributes and receives
4. **No equity exchange** between members. Revenue share, not ownership share.

This gives you the benefits of coordination without the legal complexity of a single entity.

## Tax Considerations

### Mississippi State Taxes
- **No franchise tax** for LLCs (eliminated 2023)
- **Income tax:** 0% on first $10,000, 5% above $10,000 (being phased out)
- **Sales tax:** 7% general rate. Food: 7% (being reduced). Some exemptions for manufacturing.

### Federal Tax for Small Operators
- **Quarterly estimated taxes** if you expect to owe $1,000+ (most self-employed operators)
- **Self-employment tax:** 15.3% on net self-employment income
- **Section 199A deduction:** 20% deduction on qualified business income for pass-through entities
- **Home office deduction:** If you work from home (many small-town operators do)

### Record-Keeping
- Keep every receipt. Use an app (Wave, QuickBooks Self-Employed) or a shoebox — but keep them.
- Separate bank account for business. Non-negotiable.
- Track mileage if you drive for business (IRS rate: check current year)

## Licensing

### Liquor License (Mississippi)
- **On-premise:** Application through MS Department of Revenue, Alcohol Beverage Control Division
- **Cost:** Varies by county and municipality
- **Time:** 30-90 days
- **Note:** Some Mississippi counties are still "dry" — check your county status
- **Key requirement:** Local governing authority approval required before state application

### Business License
- **City/county business license:** Required in most municipalities. Check with your city clerk.
- **Sales tax permit:** Required if selling goods. Free from MS Department of Revenue.

### Music Venue Specific
- **ASCAP/BMI/SESAC licenses:** Required for live music venues. Costs vary by venue capacity.
- **Fire marshal inspection:** Required for public assembly spaces. Capacity limits set here.
- **Sound ordinance compliance:** Check local noise ordinances for outdoor events.
