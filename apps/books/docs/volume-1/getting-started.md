# Chapter 7: Getting Started — Your First 90 Days

## Overview

This chapter provides a day-by-day, week-by-week implementation roadmap for launching a task board community from zero. It synthesizes the theory from Chapters 1-6 into a sequential action plan. Every step has a specific timeline, a measurable output, and a defined success threshold.

The 90-day window is divided into three phases:
- **Phase 1 (Days 1-30):** Foundation — assemble the group, run the audit, launch the board
- **Phase 2 (Days 31-60):** Stabilization — establish rhythms, resolve first conflicts, measure results
- **Phase 3 (Days 61-90):** Expansion — add members, introduce multipliers, plan first infrastructure project

Volume 2 provides 90-day roadmaps customized for specific community types. Volume 3 describes how to launch multiple communities simultaneously as part of a corridor deployment.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

### Week 1: Assemble the Core Group

**Day 1-2: Identify Candidates**
- List 15-25 people in the immediate network with diverse skills
- Prioritize people who are already exchanging favors informally
- Target: identify 8-12 people willing to attend an introductory meeting

**Day 3-4: Conduct Initial Outreach**
- Contact each candidate individually (not group message)
- Explain the concept in 2-3 sentences: coordinating existing skills to reduce external spending
- Ask two questions: What do you pay someone else to do that someone in our group could do? What skill do you have that others in the group need?
- Schedule a 90-minute meeting within the next 10 days

**Day 5-7: Prepare Meeting Materials**
- Create a one-page summary of the coordination economics model (Chapter 1 math)
- Draft a simple skills inventory form (name, 3-5 skills, hours available per month)
- Set up a shared spreadsheet or notebook for hour tracking
- Prepare a physical task board (whiteboard, corkboard, or poster board with three columns)

### Week 2: Launch Meeting and First Audit

**Day 8-10: Hold Launch Meeting (90 minutes)**

Meeting agenda:
1. Why coordination beats individual hustle (15 min) — present the $450,000 math from Chapter 1
2. Skills inventory (20 min) — each person shares skills and needs
3. Task board walkthrough (15 min) — explain the three-column system
4. First task posting (25 min) — each person posts 2-3 needs and claims 1-2 tasks
5. Set commitments (15 min) — each person commits to 5-10 hours for the first month

**Day 10-14: Begin Personal Money Audit**
- Each member tracks every dollar spent for 48 hours (Chapter 3 methodology)
- Calculate personal extraction rate
- Identify top 3 externally purchased services that could be coordinated internally

### Week 3: First Task Exchanges

**Day 15-21: Execute First Tasks**
- Members begin completing claimed tasks
- Track hours using the shared system (spreadsheet or notebook)
- Daily check-in via group text or chat (one-sentence status updates)
- Coordinator (rotating role) monitors board and resolves any assignment questions

**Success metrics for Week 3:**
- At least 50% of posted tasks have been claimed
- At least 3 task exchanges completed
- Every member has either given or received value

### Week 4: First Monthly Review

**Day 22-28: Collect Data**
- Compile total hours contributed by each member
- Calculate total value created (hours x base rate)
- Identify tasks that went unclaimed (these indicate skill gaps or mismatched priorities)
- Note any friction points or process issues

**Day 28-30: Monthly Review Meeting (60 minutes)**

Agenda:
1. Completed tasks since launch (15 min)
2. Hours contributed per member and total value created (10 min)
3. What worked, what did not (15 min)
4. Adjustments to task categories or processes (10 min)
5. New task postings for Month 2 (10 min)

**Phase 1 success thresholds:**
- 6+ active members completing tasks
- 80% of initial tasks completed
- Every member both contributed and received value
- Zero unresolved conflicts
- Total hours tracked accurately

## Phase 2: Stabilization (Days 31-60)

### Week 5-6: Establish Rhythms

**Daily:** One-sentence check-ins via group chat
**Weekly:** 15-minute stand-up (in person or video) to review board status
**Monthly:** 60-minute review meeting with data presentation

**Key actions:**
- Introduce the conflict resolution protocol (Chapter 4, four-tier system)
- Distribute the meeting agenda template to all members
- Assign a rotating coordinator role (1-month terms)
- Begin tracking task completion rates by category

### Week 7-8: Resolve First Conflicts and Gaps

By week 7, typical issues emerge:
- Hour estimate mismatches (task took longer than posted)
- Uneven contribution (some members contributing more than others)
- Task quality disagreements
- Scheduling conflicts

**Resolution steps:**
1. Apply Tier 1 (direct conversation) for each issue
2. Document resolution for community learning
3. Adjust task descriptions and hour estimates based on actual experience
4. If uneven contribution persists, have direct conversation about capacity — adjust commitments rather than force participation

**Data collection (ongoing):**
- Track completion rate by category (target: 80%+)
- Track hours per member (identify over-contributors and under-contributors)
- Calculate cumulative value created and external spending displaced

**Phase 2 success thresholds:**
- Weekly stand-ups happening consistently
- Conflict resolution protocol used at least once
- Completion rate above 75%
- At least 2 new members added (growing from 6-8 to 8-12)
- No member dropped out due to unresolved conflict

## Phase 3: Expansion (Days 61-90)

### Week 9-10: Introduce Priority Multipliers

Based on two months of data, identify:
- Which task categories are oversubscribed (reduce multiplier)
- Which task categories are undersubscribed (increase multiplier)
- Which community needs are going unmet

**Introduce the multiplier system (Chapter 4):**
- Set base hour value ($25/hour or local equivalent)
- Apply 1.0x-1.5x multipliers based on community priority
- Explain the system at the monthly review meeting
- Run multipliers for one month before adjusting

### Week 11-12: Plan First Infrastructure Project

Using the methodology from Chapter 5:

1. **Survey members:** What physical asset would most improve coordination?
2. **Calculate surplus labor:** Total hours available beyond current task board needs
3. **Select project:** Apply Needs x Use x Cost matrix
4. **Map the build:** Break project into tasks with hour estimates and cash requirements
5. **Schedule build sprint:** Plan the 4-week execution for Month 4

### Week 12: 90-Day Review

**Full community meeting (90 minutes):**

1. Present 90-day data (20 min)
   - Total hours coordinated
   - Total value created
   - External spending displaced
   - Member growth

2. Assess system health (20 min)
   - Completion rates by category
   - Conflict resolution effectiveness
   - Member satisfaction (simple 1-5 rating)

3. Plan next quarter (30 min)
   - Infrastructure project timeline
   - New member onboarding plan
   - Federation exploration (identify potential sister communities per Chapter 6)

4. Celebrate (20 min)
   - Acknowledge contributions
   - Share individual impact stories
   - Set vision for Month 4-6

## Minimum Viable Launch Requirements

Do not delay launch for perfection. The minimum requirements:

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Members | 5 (minimum), 8 (recommended) |
| Skills diversity | 3+ distinct skill categories |
| Task board | Any surface with three columns |
| Hour tracking | Shared notebook or spreadsheet |
| Meeting cadence | Monthly minimum, weekly recommended |
| Governance | One conflict resolution protocol |
| Time commitment per member | 5-10 hours/month |

Everything else — multipliers, infrastructure projects, federation — comes later.

---

## Implementation

### Step 1: Set a Launch Date
Choose a date within 14 days. Do not wait for the perfect group or perfect conditions.

### Step 2: Invite 10-15 People
Contact individually. Use the two-question script from Week 1, Day 3-4.

### Step 3: Hold the Meeting
Follow the 90-minute agenda. Walk out with a live task board and committed hours.

### Step 4: Execute for 30 Days
Track hours. Complete tasks. Hold the monthly review.

### Step 5: Iterate for 60 More Days
Add structure (multipliers, conflict resolution, new members) based on observed needs, not theoretical planning.
