# The Task Board

A task board is how you coordinate work without hierarchy. Instead of job titles and org charts, you have tasks that need doing and people who can do them.

## The Problem It Solves

In a small town, the work doesn't fit neatly into jobs. The person who can fix the sound system is also the person who can build a website is also the person who plays guitar on Saturdays. Traditional employment can't capture this. A task board can.

## How It Works

1. **List the work.** Not job descriptions — actual tasks. "Shoot 10 menu photos for Fat Mama's." "Fix the PA speaker at the venue." "Write a social media post about Saturday's show."

2. **Match to people.** Who has the skill? Who has the time? Who's nearby? The task finds the person, not the other way around.

3. **Set the value.** Each task has a clear deliverable and a clear value — cash, trade, credit, or reputation. No ambiguity.

4. **Track completion.** Done is done. The board updates. The network sees who delivers.

## What Makes It Different from Gig Work

Gig platforms extract 20-30% and own the customer relationship. A task board is owned by the community. The operator keeps 100% of the value. The board is the coordination layer, not the middleman.

| | Gig Platform | Task Board |
|---|---|---|
| Fee | 20-30% | $0 (or federation dues) |
| Customer relationship | Platform owns it | Operator owns it |
| Data | Platform owns it | Community owns it |
| Pricing | Platform sets it | Operator sets it |
| Reputation | Locked to platform | Portable |

## Getting Started

**Week 1:** Create a shared document or board (Asana, Notion, even a whiteboard) with three columns: Needs Doing, In Progress, Done.

**Week 2:** Add 10 tasks from 3-5 different operators. Real work, real value.

**Week 3:** Match and complete. Track who did what and what it was worth.

**Week 4:** Review. What worked? What tasks went unclaimed? That's your skills gap — and your next hire or training opportunity.
