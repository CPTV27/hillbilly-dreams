# Make Money

![Small exchanges flowing into a river of value](https://storage.googleapis.com/bmt-media-bigmuddy/illustrations/outsider-economics/03-tributaries-river-value.webp)

This isn't theory. Here are concrete ways to generate revenue using the Outsider Economics toolkit — whether you're a single operator or running a federation.

## For Individual Operators

### 1. Resell Directory Services ($2,000-5,000/month)

You don't need to build a directory from scratch. Use the [Deep South Directory](/toolkit/the-directory) infrastructure and sell it to businesses in your town.

**The pitch:** "For $20/month, I'll manage your Google presence, write your listing, and send you a monthly report."

**The math:**
- 50 businesses at $20/month = $1,000/month (you keep it all)
- 20 businesses upgrade to $49/month = $980/month
- 10 businesses at $99/month = $990/month
- **Total: $2,970/month** from a territory of 80 businesses

**How to start:**
1. Pick your town (population 5,000-25,000 is the sweet spot)
2. Walk into 5 businesses this week with the [$20 pitch](/toolkit/the-20-revolution)
3. Use the Deep South Directory tools to deliver the service
4. Upgrade them as features roll out

### 2. Sell Content Packages ($500-2,000/month)

Most small businesses can't create their own social media content. You can.

**What you sell:**
- 4 social media posts/week ($300/month)
- Monthly email newsletter ($200/month)
- Quarterly photo shoot ($500/session)
- Video content for social ($400/month)

**Tools you need:**
- A phone with a good camera
- Content Studio (generates posts from a business name)
- Canva for graphics (free tier works)

**Start with 3 clients at $500/month = $1,500/month.** One day of shooting and writing per week covers all three.

### 3. Coordinate Events ($1,000-5,000/event)

Use the [Task Board](/toolkit/the-task-board) model to coordinate events that nobody else is organizing.

**Event types:**
- Music night at a venue ($1,000-3,000 gross, you keep the door or a production fee)
- Pop-up market (charge vendors $50-100/booth, you coordinate logistics)
- Workshop series ($25-50/ticket, 20 people, monthly)
- Festival (bigger lift but $5,000-20,000 revenue potential)

**Your role:** You're the coordinator, not the talent. You match the venue, the performers, the vendors, and the audience. The [federation model](/philosophy/the-federation-effect) means everyone brings their piece.

### 4. Offer Shared Services ($100-300/month per client)

Pool services that small businesses need but can't afford individually.

**Services to resell:**
- Bookkeeping ($100/month per client, outsource to one bookkeeper serving 10)
- Photography rotation ($150/month, photographer visits each client monthly)
- Social media management ($200/month through Content Studio)
- Website maintenance ($100/month, you handle updates)

**The margin:** Charge each client $100-300. Pay the service provider $1,000-2,000/month. With 10-15 clients, you profit $500-2,500/month after paying the provider.

## For Federations

### 5. Launch a Local Directory ($4,000-10,000/month)

A federation of 20 operators can launch a directory for their entire town.

**Revenue model:**
- 100 free listings (SEO value, attracts visitors to the directory)
- 50 paid listings at $20/month = $1,000/month
- 20 enhanced listings at $49/month = $980/month
- 10 premium listings at $99/month = $990/month
- **Total: $2,970/month** split among the federation or funding a coordinator

### 6. Run a Content Studio ($3,000-8,000/month)

One photographer + one writer + the Content Studio platform = a content agency.

**What the studio sells:**
- Social media packages (4 posts/week = $300/month)
- Magazine features ($500/feature)
- Event coverage ($750/event)
- Brand photography ($1,000/half-day shoot)

**Federation advantage:** The content studio serves federation members at cost and external clients at market rate. External revenue funds the federation infrastructure.

### 7. Tourism Packages ($5,000-15,000/month)

Federated hospitality: hotel + restaurant + music venue + tour guide sold as one package.

**What you sell:**
- "Blues Weekend" — 2 nights lodging + dinner + show + walking tour ($350/person)
- "Deep South Culinary" — 3 restaurants + cooking class + market tour ($200/person)
- "Artist Residency" — week-long stay + studio access + mentorship ($1,500/person)

**The margin:** Each business in the package handles their piece. The federation takes 10-15% coordination fee for booking, marketing, and customer management.

### 8. Grant-Funded Programs ($25,000-250,000/year)

Use the [Resources](/resources/grants-and-funding) section to find grants that fund your coordination activities.

**What's fundable:**
- Workforce development (task board = job training)
- Downtown revitalization (directory + events)
- Arts programming (shows, residencies, workshops)
- Technology infrastructure (directory platform, content tools)

**The trick:** Frame your federation activities in the language the funder uses. A task board is "workforce coordination infrastructure." A directory is "tourism marketing technology." A content studio is "cultural heritage documentation."

## Revenue Stacking

The real power is combining these. A single operator in a town of 15,000 can realistically build:

| Stream | Monthly Revenue |
|---|---|
| Directory reselling (50 businesses) | $1,500 |
| Content packages (5 clients) | $2,500 |
| Monthly event coordination | $1,500 |
| Shared services (10 clients) | $1,000 |
| **Total** | **$6,500/month** |

That's $78,000/year from a town most people write off as too small to matter.

## The Rule

Every tool in this toolkit has a revenue model attached to it. If you're using a tool and not making money from it, you're volunteering. Volunteers burn out. Operators sustain.

Build the revenue model first. The mission follows the money.
