# The Directory

A directory makes local businesses discoverable to visitors and to each other. It's the simplest, highest-impact tool in the Outsider Economics toolkit.

## The Problem

Tourists Google "best restaurants in Natchez" and get Yelp, TripAdvisor, and Google Maps. All three are extractive platforms that charge for visibility, own the customer data, and optimize for their own ad revenue — not for the town.

Meanwhile, the best restaurant in Natchez doesn't have a website, hasn't updated their Google listing since 2019, and gets most of their business from a hand-painted sign on the highway.

## What a Directory Does

A local directory is a curated, maintained list of businesses that:
- Lives on a domain the town controls
- Shows up in search results because it's optimized for local SEO
- Tells the story of each business (not just name/address/phone)
- Cross-promotes related businesses ("If you liked this, try...")
- Collects reviews and reputation locally
- Costs $20/month, not $300/month

## How to Build One

1. **Pick 20 businesses.** The ones tourists actually visit. Restaurants, shops, attractions, lodging.
2. **Write their stories.** Not a listing — a story. Why they exist, who runs them, what makes them worth visiting.
3. **Optimize for search.** Each listing is a page. Each page targets "[business type] in [town name]." This is basic SEO but most small towns don't do it.
4. **Launch at $20/month.** This is the anchor price. Same as ChatGPT. Below the pain threshold for any business.
5. **Add services.** Review monitoring, social media posts, monthly reports. These upgrade paths fund the directory's operation.

## The Deep South Directory

This tool is deployed today at [deepsouthdirectory.com](https://deepsouthdirectory.com). It serves the Mississippi corridor with AI-enhanced listings, review alerts, and social media generation — starting at $20/month.

## Why $20?

See [The $20 Revolution](/toolkit/the-20-revolution) for the pricing philosophy. Short version: $20 is the price of a ChatGPT subscription. If your AI-powered business tool costs the same as ChatGPT, nobody argues about the price.
