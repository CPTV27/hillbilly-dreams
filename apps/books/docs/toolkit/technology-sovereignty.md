# Technology Sovereignty

![Business cutting puppet strings to cloud platforms](https://storage.googleapis.com/bmt-media-bigmuddy/illustrations/outsider-economics/11-cut-the-strings.webp)

Own your stack. If a company in San Francisco can turn off your tools tomorrow, you don't have technology — you have a dependency.

## The Problem

A typical Main Street business relies on:
- Google for search visibility (free until it isn't)
- Facebook/Instagram for social reach (declining every year)
- Yelp for reviews (pay-to-play)
- Square or Toast for payments (2.6-3.5% of every dollar)
- Mailchimp for email (free until 500 subscribers)
- Canva for design (works great, actually)

Each one is a single point of failure controlled by someone who doesn't know your town exists.

## What Sovereignty Means

Technology sovereignty doesn't mean building everything from scratch. It means:

1. **Own your data.** Your customer list, your reviews, your photos, your content — stored on infrastructure you control.
2. **Own your domain.** Your website lives at your URL, not on Facebook or a free Wix site.
3. **Portable tools.** If one service dies, your data migrates. No lock-in.
4. **Local administration.** Someone in your town can fix it, update it, or customize it without calling a support line in another timezone.

## The Sovereign Stack

For a federation of businesses, the minimum sovereign stack is:

| Layer | Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | Local directory | Replaces Yelp/TripAdvisor dependency |
| Presence | Own domain + site | Replaces Facebook page as primary presence |
| Communication | Text/email list you own | Replaces social media reach |
| Payments | Direct processing | Minimizes platform fees |
| Content | Local media production | Replaces stock photos and generic marketing |
| Data | Local database | Customer info stays in town |

## How to Get There

You don't need to build this yourself. The [federation model](/philosophy/the-federation-effect) pools the cost across members:

1. **Month 1:** Get everyone on the directory with a real listing
2. **Month 2:** Set up shared social media posting
3. **Month 3:** Launch a local content production pipeline
4. **Month 6:** Migrate key business data to shared, locally-controlled infrastructure

## The Test

For every tool your business uses, ask: "What happens if this company goes away tomorrow?" If the answer is "we're screwed," that's your next sovereignty project.
