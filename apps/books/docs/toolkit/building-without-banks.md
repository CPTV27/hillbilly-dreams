# Building Without Banks

Traditional capital doesn't flow to small towns. The good news: you don't need it.

## The Problem

Banks want collateral. VCs want 10x returns. CDFIs are great but oversubscribed. Grant cycles take 18 months. Meanwhile, the roof leaks and the website is broken.

Small-town operators can't wait for the capital stack to align. They need to build with what they have.

## Alternative Finance Tools

### 1. Revenue-Based Coordination

Instead of raising money, share revenue streams:
- "I'll build your website for free if I get 5% of online bookings for 12 months"
- "I'll shoot your photos if you feed my crew during festival week"
- Revenue share aligns incentives without debt or equity

### 2. Micro-Patronage

$20/month from 100 locals = $2,000/month = a part-time salary for a community coordinator. This is the [Deep South Directory model](/toolkit/the-directory) — small contributions that compound into infrastructure.

### 3. Sweat Equity Pools

Track contributed hours. When revenue arrives, distribute proportionally. No cash changes hands until the project makes money.

### 4. Grant Stacking

Don't apply for one $50,000 grant. Apply for five $10,000 grants from different sources. Smaller grants have less competition, faster timelines, and more flexible reporting.

### 5. Pre-Sales and Deposits

Sell the thing before you build it. A show ticket sold 3 weeks early is working capital. A hotel deposit is a zero-interest loan. A directory listing paid annually is 12 months of runway.

## What You Don't Need

- You don't need a bank loan to start
- You don't need an investor to grow
- You don't need a line of credit to survive a slow month (if you have a federation sharing the load)

## The Rule

If your plan requires capital you don't have, the plan is wrong. Redesign the plan to work with what's available.
