# Chapter 18: The Natchez Porch Network

## Overview

Natchez, Mississippi (population 14,000) receives 600,000 tourists annually — a 43:1 tourist-to-resident ratio. The town contains over 1,000 historic homes, an estimated 300+ of which have guest-ready rooms, carriage houses, or garden apartments sitting vacant. Solo Airbnb hosts in Natchez average 22-33% occupancy. This chapter documents a 10-property coordination protocol that achieves 55% occupancy, generates $250,000/year in gross revenue, produces $20,000/year per property owner, and replaces platform dependency with a locally operated coordination layer. Total startup cost per homeowner: under $300.

### Barriers to Individual Listing

Based on homeowner surveys, three obstacles prevent the remaining 300+ rooms from entering the market:

| Barrier | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Technical complexity | Many homeowners are retirees who cannot manage booking platforms, pricing algorithms, and digital guest communication. |
| Operational burden | Managing a single Airbnb listing (inquiries, pricing, cleaning, supplies, lockouts, reviews) is a part-time job. Multiple homeowners tried and quit within months. |
| Trust deficit | Platform-matched anonymous guests with no local vetting system. Support is a 1-800 number in San Francisco. |

## The Coordination Protocol

### Structure

10 homeowners with guest rooms, carriage houses, or garden apartments consolidate listings onto a single booking platform (CloudBeds, ~$100/month total) under a shared brand.

### Three Coordinator Roles

| Role | Function | Profile |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Bookings Coordinator** | Manages CloudBeds account, adjusts pricing by season/event, responds to inquiries, handles guest communication from booking through checkout | Semi-retired hotel management professional (20 years experience) |
| **Operations Coordinator** | Manages cleaning rotation (3 cleaners covering 3-4 properties each), quality checks, standardized turnover process (fresh linens, bathroom restock, welcome card, local restaurant menus, printed guide) | Runs a small cleaning business serving existing B&Bs |
| **Guest Experience Coordinator** | Coordinates check-ins, handles guest questions, manages small crises (lockouts, AC failures, restaurant recommendations), provides personalized local knowledge | Lifelong Natchez resident with deep local knowledge and relationships |

### Revenue Distribution

| Allocation | Percentage |
|------------|-----------|
| Property owner | 80% |
| Coordination fund | 20% |

## Prerequisites

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Trust | Homeowners must trust coordinators with bookings and property access. This trust requires pre-existing relationships (neighbors, not strangers). If trust does not exist, start with shared meals. Coordination follows trust, not the reverse. |
| Hospitality expertise | At least one coordinator must understand pricing, occupancy optimization, listing copywriting, and tourism calendar rhythms. This is learnable but requires committed study. |
| Operational consistency | Cleaning must be perfect every time. Communication must be prompt. Check-in must be smooth. One bad experience damages all 10 properties because the brand is shared. |

## Implementation

### Step 1: Survey Available Rooms
Walk the neighborhood. Count empty rooms: guest rooms, carriage houses, guest cottages, mother-in-law suites. In historic towns, the supply of unused space is typically larger than expected.

### Step 2: Identify Three Coordinators
- Bookings: organized, tech-competent, understands pricing
- Operations: detail-oriented, reliable, quality-focused
- Guest experience: personable, knows the town deeply, has local relationships

### Step 3: Start with Three Properties
Prove the model small. Three properties, one coordinator managing all three, 30 days of bookings. When the first check arrives and the owner did not manage anything, additional homeowners will request to join.

### Step 4: Name the Network
Select a name that communicates location, experience type, and collective scale. The name transforms individual rooms into a destination. Guests do not book a spare room. They book a named network.
