The Thesis

Broadcast infrastructure, miniaturized for Main Street

I designed a complete media production-to-distribution pipeline in 2022. It was global infrastructure — broadcast, production, analytics, distribution — built on open source tools. I realized the same architecture that runs a network can run a small-town media economy. Big Muddy is that architecture, applied to Main Street, powered by AI, anchored in the Mississippi corridor.

The gap isn't technology — it's organization. Every small town has talent, venues, stories, and businesses worth knowing about. What they don't have is the infrastructure to capture, produce, distribute, and monetize that content. That's what we sell.

The Multiplier

One show feeds everything. The math is uncheateable.

A $1,000 show generates $500+ in downstream revenue — Inn bookings, bar revenue, radio content, Magazine features, directory impressions, record sales, social media engagement. We've measured this at a 2:1 ecosystem multiplier. No other venue, label, or touring company can claim that, because none of them own the full stack.

We book the band. We drive them in the Sprinter van. We run the sound. We record the show. We release the recording on our label. We write about it in our magazine. We play it on our radio station. We list the venue in our directory. We promote it through our social channels. One booking, six revenue events.

The Broadcast Play

This is where my 25 years come in

I built the CyberCar — a 15-camera mobile satellite studio with Jon Alpert at DCTV. I launched Democracy Now on television. I launched Hollywood Today Live on FOX. I ran Microsoft's esports broadcast studio in Manhattan.

Now I'm taking the same broadcast infrastructure and packaging it as a product. A restaurant in Natchez gets the same quality linear TV channel that a network affiliate gets — scheduled programming, live event cutaways, branded graphics, 24/7 playout. Delivered via Plex to any smart TV. $49-$499/month.

The playout system is built on OpenBroadcaster (open source, broadcast-grade). The streaming infrastructure is Icecast. The content pipeline is AI-powered — Google Cloud Vision tags every photo, Gemini generates social content, the editorial pipeline turns one show into six outputs automatically. The broadcast architecture is the same. The cost structure is Mississippi, not Manhattan.
