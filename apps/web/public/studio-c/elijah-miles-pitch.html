What we are selling

Measurably Better Things is a chatbot-first hybrid SaaS plus service. Customer signs up, we ingest their data, they get a chat that knows their business, and behind that chat is us. Software answers what software can answer. When it cannot, the request escalates to a human on our team. Same chat, two meters: software tokens for the AI work, human tokens for our work, billed at a much higher rate because that is where the real value is.

Six tiers. Free for the funnel, $20 Standard for the people who would otherwise buy ChatGPT, $100 Service as the target sell, $200 Operator and $500 Business above that, Enterprise as custom. The $100 tier is the one we push. It is the only consumer-priced AI product on the market that includes thirty minutes of human help every month. Nobody else offers that. The whole pricing model is built around the gap.

Why now

The trend is called Inverted Intelligence. As AI gets cheaper and better at the cognitive work, the value of human work inverts: showing up, holding accountability, making the judgment call, picking up the phone — those become the premium layer. Most software companies are still pricing humans at $50 to $200 an hour and software at $20 a month. They have it backward. We priced for the inverted regime first. Software cheap, humans expensive, same chat surface for both. The marketing window is now: be the people who name the trend, not the people who explain it three years later.

The pricing math

Why this scales by marketing volume rather than enterprise sales motion.

Tier Price Net per user (rough) Per cohort of 100 free signups
Free $0 Lead-gen 100 signups
Standard $20/mo ~$15 30 convert → $450/mo
ServiceTarget $100/mo ~$60–80 15 convert → $1,050/mo (mid)
Operator $200/mo Higher 3 convert → $600/mo
Business $500/mo Much higher 1 convert → $500/mo

That is roughly $2,500 per month of recurring revenue per cohort of one hundred free signups, at maturity. Marketing volume drives the funnel. Six cohorts and we are at $15k a month recurring without selling a single enterprise seat. Twenty cohorts and the math gets interesting. The whole point of the model is that we win by getting the funnel humming, not by chasing twenty $5,000-a-month logos.
