Hillbilly Dreams LLC: Operations Briefing HD-2026-001
Summary

Hillbilly Dreams LLC is a proposed Mississippi limited liability company that will serve as the holding entity for the Big Muddy ecosystem—a network of eight interconnected brands spanning hospitality, media, music, publishing, touring, and technology licensing. The entity would be equally owned in thirds by Tracy Allen, Amy Allen, and a family trust.

The company projects annual revenue of $760,000 across five revenue categories, representing a 726% increase over the prior year’s $92,000 in revenue (which resulted in a net operating loss of $22,000). The revenue model is diversified across hospitality operations ($293,000), services and licensing ($224,000), touring and experiences ($126,000), media and content ($94,000), and miscellaneous income ($23,000).

Key organizational changes include: (1) the hiring of a professional hospitality coordinator to assume all daily Inn operations; (2) a 30-day transition plan under which Tracy Allen will exit all property management responsibilities by April 20, 2026; (3) the establishment of Tracy Allen as Editor-in-Chief of Big Muddy Magazine and General Manager of Big Muddy Radio; (4) the designation of Amy Allen as the primary on-air talent and performing artist; and (5) the designation of Chase Pierson as Creative Director and Executive Producer with responsibility for technology, content production, and strategic direction.

The immediate action item is the filing of the LLC with the Mississippi Secretary of State ($50 filing fee, online, approximately 15 minutes).

Editor-in-Chief & Station Manager Role

Big Muddy Magazine

Big Muddy Magazine is a digital editorial publication covering the Mississippi music corridor: city guides, feature stories, photo essays, artist profiles, food writing, and travel pieces across 18 cities in five states. Tracy Allen serves as Editor-in-Chief with final editorial authority over all published content.

The editorial workflow operates as follows:

  • Chase and the automated content engine produce drafts, photographs, and layouts
  • Tracy reviews, edits, and either approves for publication or returns with notes
  • She applies the same editorial process to Outsider Economics book chapters
  • She sets the publication’s tone, standards, and voice—nothing publishes without her sign-off
  • All review work is conducted via printed drafts, red pen, and written memos back to Chase

Big Muddy Radio & LPFM Initiative

Tracy Allen has expressed a lifelong interest in radio station management, informed by family experience in the broadcasting industry. In her capacity as Station Manager, she oversees all aspects of Big Muddy Radio operations:

  • Programming schedule: which shows run, when, and featuring whom
  • Management of Amy Allen’s on-air schedule and guest bookings
  • Playlist approval and curation oversight
  • Sponsorship placement and advertising decisions

Big Muddy Radio currently operates as an internet streaming station with on-demand playlists, live sessions, and podcast content. Amy Allen (performing as Arri Aslin) serves as the primary on-air talent.

Future Initiative: Low Power FM (LPFM) Broadcasting

The organization intends to pursue an LPFM broadcast license from the Federal Communications Commission when the next filing window opens.6 An LPFM license would allow Big Muddy Radio to broadcast on the FM dial in Natchez and potentially other communities along the corridor, converting the station from an internet-only platform to a terrestrial broadcast operation.

In the interim, the strategy is to build programming, audience, and content archive so that when the license is secured, the station launches with an established identity and library. Tracy Allen will oversee this initiative.

6 The FCC opens LPFM filing windows on an irregular basis. The most recent window was in 2013. Eligible applicants include nonprofits, educational institutions, and community organizations. Hillbilly Dreams LLC may need to establish a qualifying entity or partner with an existing nonprofit for eligibility. See 47 CFR §§ 73.801–73.881.

Communications Protocol

Internal communications will follow an old-school memo and print-based system designed for simplicity and minimal technology dependence.

Daily Printout

An automated daily summary sheet will print each morning containing: current guest status, scheduled events, pending tasks, key metrics, editorial items requiring review, radio schedule, and any memos from team members. No action is required to generate the printout—it is produced automatically by the platform.

Memo System

All business communications between team members (Tracy, Amy, Chase, Rich, the hospitality coordinator) will use written memos in the format of this report or the simpler typewriter-style memo format. Memos may be drafted by dictation to Delta Dawn (the AI assistant), printed, and filed physically. Memos appear on the daily printout or as standalone documents.

Device Simplification

All personal computers and phones used for business purposes will be simplified to a minimal interface:

  • A small number of hotkeys for common actions (send a memo, ask Delta Dawn, view daily sheet)
  • Delta Dawn voice or text interface for all other inquiries
  • No complex dashboards, no multi-tab workflows, no passwords to manage

Delta Dawn (AI Assistant)

Delta Dawn is an AI assistant integrated into the Big Muddy Command platform with comprehensive knowledge of all eight brands, policies, pricing, schedules, and operational history. It is available via voice or text at all times and can draft memos, answer operational questions, generate reports, and print summaries on request.7

Technology Infrastructure

The Big Muddy Command platform is a custom-built application that serves all eight brands from a single codebase. It manages websites, the booking engine, content pipeline, newsletter distribution, social media scheduling, analytics, media library, and administrative functions.

A separate technology platform, S2PX, powers an industrial laser scanning business (Scan2Plan LLC) in the commercial construction sector. The S2PX licensing arrangement generates $134,000 per year in revenue that flows through Hillbilly Dreams LLC.

Both platforms are owned exclusively by Tuthill Design LLC (a subsidiary of the Trust) and licensed to Hillbilly Dreams LLC. This structure ensures that the technology intellectual property is protected from any claims arising from the LLC’s operations, dissolution, or membership changes.8

Tracy and Amy are not expected to interact with the technology platform directly. That is Chase’s responsibility. This section is provided for informational completeness.
