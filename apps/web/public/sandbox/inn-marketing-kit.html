Voice Rules — Read Before Posting

  • The joke IS the insight — funny first, then the truth lands
  • Small-town specific — use names, places, real details
  • Never punch down at the guest — punch at generic hotels
  • Tracy and Amy are equity partners who built this — not employees, not managers
  • The technology is invisible — guests don't know about AI. Don't mention it.
  • Photography first — every post needs a real photo, not stock
  • The Inn is a music venue AND a hotel — that's the point, not a quirk
  • Don't say: corridor, leverage, utilize, robust, scalable, synergy, journey
  • Sound like the smartest person in the room who happens to be from Mississippi

Section 7

Review Response Templates

Three templates for Google reviews. Warm, specific, never corporate.

Response — Positive Review (5 stars) Copy

Example review: "Amazing place! The bar was incredible and the live music was the highlight of our trip. Will definitely be back!"

This made our morning. Glad the music caught you — the band that night was something special, and Amy was particularly proud of the bourbon lineup she put together for the set. We'll save you a seat at the bar next time. Natchez always has something new playing.

Rules: Reference something specific from their review. Mention a team member by name. End with a reason to come back. Never say "we appreciate your feedback" or "thank you for your kind words."
Response — Mixed Review (3 stars) Copy

Example review: "Nice place, cool vibe. Room was a bit small and could hear music late at night. Good drinks though."

Fair point on the sound — it's a Victorian house with live music downstairs, and we know that's not for everyone on a Tuesday when you just want to sleep. We've been working on the sound insulation, and we're always happy to put you in the room furthest from the stage if quiet is what you need. The drinks part, though? That's all Amy. Glad that landed. Hope we get a second chance to get the whole night right.

Rules: Acknowledge the issue honestly. Explain without excusing. Offer a concrete solution. Close with warmth. Never be defensive. Never say "we're sorry you feel that way."
Response — "We'll Be Back" Review (4-5 stars) Copy

Example review: "What a gem! Loved everything about this place. Already planning our next trip down."

That's the best thing we hear. The second trip is always better than the first — you already know where to sit at the bar, Amy already knows your drink, and you can skip straight to the part where the music starts and the night gets interesting. Give us a call when you're heading back and we'll make sure you're here on a good night. They're almost all good nights, but some are better.

Rules: Make the return feel like coming home, not a transaction. Reference the repeat experience as an upgrade. End with a specific, human invitation.
