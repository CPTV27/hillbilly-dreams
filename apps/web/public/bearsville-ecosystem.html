1. The Interface

The Heritage Journal

The first thing people see is the Magazine. We've designed this to feel like a high-end, $25 coffee-table book rather than a standard website.

—
The 100vh Hero: A full-bleed, cinematic image (like those Bearsville theater shots) that stays fixed as you scroll.
—
The Slide-Under: Content blocks with high-contrast typography slide over the imagery, creating a tactile, editorial feel.
—
The Standard: We use real photography — never stock — to ensure the interface feels as authentic as the music.

This is where the work you create for your clients at Tuthill and Studio C gets published. The magazine becomes your portfolio and your content marketing at the same time.

2. The Playout System

One event. Six outputs. Mostly automatic.

In Natchez, we found that one single event can — and should — power every channel we own. A show happens at the Blues Room or Bearsville Theater. Through a mix of human craft and AI assistance, that one show automatically feeds:

1
Radio — Audio enters the scheduled rotation.
2
Social Media — Video clips move through CapCut to Postiz for distribution.
3
The Magazine — Photos and stories become a featured editorial article.
4
The Directory — Venue and artist listings are automatically updated.
5
Records — Recordings are prepped for potential release.
6
The Archive — High-res assets are tagged by AI and stored for future use.

In Natchez, this is how we keep the lights on and the music playing while we sleep. Imagine what happens when the Hudson Valley's creative output hits the same engine.

3. The Production Pipeline

Capture to cloud — how a photo or recording moves through the system

Capture
Camera, iPhone, field recorder You shoot and edit in Lightroom as usual. Nothing changes about your creative process.
Tag
Adobe Bridge + Google Cloud Vision AI Bridge for your keywords. Vision API auto-tags everything — "night scene," "performer," "landscape." Tags travel with the file.
Store
Google Cloud Storage Three sizes (hero, grid, thumb), named descriptively, backed up with versioning. Instantly accessible everywhere.
Publish
The platform pulls it in automatically Photos show up on the website, in articles, in the directory, on social. You upload once, it appears everywhere.
Measure
Engagement tracking Photos that perform on social get promoted to hero images on the website. The audience tells us what works.

We're currently processing 18,000+ photos through this pipeline. Your Bearsville and Studio C photos would flow through the same system.
