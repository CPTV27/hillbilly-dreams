The Snowbird Circuit

How the corridor works

1

Artist signs with Big Muddy Touring

Non-exclusive. They keep their masters. We book, transport, promote, record, release.

2

Southern leg: Natchez → Clarksdale → Memphis

The Sprinter van carries them through the Mississippi corridor. Shows at the Blues Room, corridor venues, festivals.

3

Northern leg: Hudson Valley → Woodstock → Beacon

Same artist, same Sprinter, different corridor. Bearsville venues, Studio C recording sessions, Hudson Valley audience.

4

Content flows everywhere

The show gets recorded → released on Big Muddy Records. Written about in the Magazine (both editions). Played on Radio (both stations). Listed in both directories.

5

European connection

European blues and roots artists come to Natchez for "Destination Sessions" — fly in, play with the house band, record, release. Then they play Bearsville on the way back. The corridor is international.

Revenue Model

How the Bearsville node makes money

Studio Sessions

Recording, mixing, video production at Studio C. Charge per session or per day. External clients pay full rate. Corridor artists get ecosystem pricing.

Design Clients

Tuthill Design serves branding, web, and creative clients. Existing revenue stream — now amplified by the platform tools and the Bearsville brand.

Directory Subscriptions

Hudson Valley businesses pay $20-$99/mo to be listed in the Bearsville Directory. Same DSD model. You sell it, platform delivers it.

Show Revenue

Door splits, bar revenue, merch from shows at Hudson Valley venues. Every show feeds the 2:1 ecosystem multiplier — a $1K show generates $500+ downstream.

Destination Sessions

Artists pay $25K all-inclusive for a week: studio time, recording, release on Big Muddy Records, Magazine feature, Radio airplay, video crew. Premium product.

Content & Media

Bearsville Magazine advertising, sponsored content, event promotions. As the audience grows, the ad revenue follows.

Platform Revenue Share

When Bearsville signs directory clients or records artists, revenue flows through the ecosystem. HDI provides the platform, Bearsville provides the relationships.

The Corridor Multiplier

Artists who play Bearsville also play Natchez. Tourists who find Bearsville businesses also visit the South. The two nodes feed each other — your growth is Big Muddy's growth and vice versa.

Your First 30 Days

What to do right now

1

Log into the admin tools

Go to bearsvillemediagroup.com/admin. Click around. Create a test event. Upload a photo. Ask Delta Dawn a question.

2

List 10 Hudson Valley businesses in the directory

Restaurants, studios, shops, venues you actually know and respect. Use /directory/onboard. These become your first directory clients when you start selling.

3

Book one session at Studio C through the platform

Create the event in /admin/events. Record it. Upload the photos. Write a short Magazine piece. This is the dogfood — prove the pipeline works end to end.

4

Identify 3 local bands for the corridor

Who in the Hudson Valley would play Natchez? Who would travel the Snowbird Circuit? These are your first touring roster candidates.

5

Start selling the directory

Walk into businesses you already know. Show them the listing on your phone. $20/mo to start. Same pitch that works in Natchez works in Woodstock.
