What a token actually is, and why we use the word.

A token is a unit of work. Roughly one word of text the AI writes, or one word it reads. The big AI companies bill in fractions of a cent per token. That is the whole concept.

If you already work with AI, you know this. We are explaining it for the 95% of people who do not.

We borrowed the same idea for human work. One minute of our team's time is one unit. We bill that unit at a much higher rate than an AI token, because a person costs more than a model. Both meters run at the same time on the same job. You see both on the invoice.

Here is the part that matters to you: the meters are ours, not yours. You pay a fixed price for the answer. We carry the cost of the path. If the AI takes three tries instead of one, that is on us. If a person spends twenty minutes instead of ten, that is on us.

What this is not
  • No surprise bills at the end of the month.
  • No contract lock-in. Stop any time.
  • No upsell pressure once you sign.
  • No prepaid token credits you have to buy in a bundle and use before they expire.
  • No metered pricing where heavy use costs you more.
What is confusing about this, honestly

The word "token" gets used in two different ways in tech right now. One is the AI billing unit we are talking about. The other is crypto. They have nothing to do with each other. We mean the AI one.

The other thing that trips people up: human time and AI time are billed in the same units but at very different rates. That is on purpose. It lets us put both on one line so you can see what the work actually cost to produce. Most shops hide that. We do not.
