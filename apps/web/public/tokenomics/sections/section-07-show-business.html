Cover Charge and Green Room

Every working music room runs on the same split: a low door that gets people through it, and a higher tier for the people who came for something specific. Tokenomics is the same room.

Run a Tuesday at the Blues Room. The cover is ten bucks. House act on stage, drinks at the bar, room stays warm. Friday the headliner pulls in — cover jumps to forty, the meet-and-greet runs ninety, and the rail seats with the bottle service clear two hundred. Same venue. Same PA. Different price for different attention.

That is the entire model. Software tokens are the Tuesday cover. Human tokens are Friday's meet-and-greet.

Why The Bar Stays Open

Nobody runs a venue on headliners alone — you would go broke between Fridays. Nobody runs one on Tuesdays alone either — the bar tab does not cover the lights. The room works because the cheap nights pay rent and the marquee nights pay the band.

A chat product runs the same math. Most of the traffic is Tuesday traffic — quick answers, lookups, the kind of work the cheap model handles without breaking a sweat. The premium queries are the headliner nights. You charge for what they are, not what the cheap night costs.

What The Customer Is Actually Buying

  • Cover charge — access. Get in the door, hear something, leave when you want.
  • Drink minimum — the platform fee. Keeps the lights on whether you order the well or the top shelf.
  • Headliner ticket — the named act. You came specifically for this.
  • Meet-and-greet — a human in the room. Not a recording. Not a bot.

Sell the door at one price and the green room at another. Let the customer pick what they came for.
