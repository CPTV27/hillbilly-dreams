## Big Muddy Touring Voice

### Voice Attributes

1.  **Direct:** Gets straight to the point. No fluff, no wasted words. Information is delivered clearly and concisely.
2.  **Practical:** Focused on actionable details, logistics, and what needs to happen. Prioritizes efficiency and problem-solving.
3.  **Experienced:** Speaks with the authority of someone who's been on the road. Anticipates common issues and understands the realities of touring.
4.  **Professional:** Treats artists, agents, and venues as partners. Respects the craft and the business. Demands the same in return.
5.  **Unsentimental:** Focuses on outcomes and facts. Avoids emotional language or hyperbole. A good show is a good show because the numbers and the experience on the ground prove it, not because of flowery descriptions.

### Tone

The tone is no-nonsense, reliable, and efficient. It's the voice of someone you trust to get the job done, who understands the grind, and who won't waste your time. It’s focused on execution and mutual benefit.

### How this voice differs from the Inn/Magazine voice

The Big Muddy Touring voice is for the business of music. It’s about logistics, contracts, and getting artists from point A to point B successfully. It's practical and focused on the mechanics of a tour.

The Inn/Magazine voice, by contrast, is guest-facing. It focuses on the charm of the region, the history, the comfort of the accommodations, and the richness of the travel experience. It uses evocative language to inspire and delight travelers. Where Touring might say, "The venue has a 200-cap room," the Magazine might say, "Step into a historic juke joint where legends once played, offering an intimate setting for unforgettable nights." The Touring voice tells you *what* is happening and *how*. The Inn/Magazine voice tells you *why* it matters to a visitor and *how it will feel*.

### Email/Text Conventions

*   **Subject Lines:** Short, clear, and informative. (e.g., "Offer: [Artist Name] - [Date] - [Venue]", "Confirm: [Artist Name] 10/17", "Backline request: [Artist Name]").
*   **Content:** Bullet points or numbered lists for key information (dates, times, financials, specific needs).
*   **Direct Questions:** Ask what you need to know directly.
*   **Confirmations:** Always confirm receipt of information or agreement on terms.
*   **Tone:** Professional, concise. No emojis. Keep pleasantries brief.
*   **Example Email Body:**
    "Hi [Contact Name],

    Following up on [Artist Name] for 10/17.
    Offer attached.
    Load-in: 4 PM
    Soundcheck: 5:30 PM
    Doors: 7 PM
    Hard out: 11 PM
    Please confirm receipt and let me know if the terms work.

    Thanks,
    [Your Name]"
