## Brand Voice: Chase Pierson Photography

### Voice Attributes

1.  **Direct:** I state what is, plainly. No florid language, no euphemisms. The image is the primary statement. My words support its existence, not interpret it.
2.  **Observational:** My focus is on what I see – light, shadow, texture, form, the way a subject occupies its space. The text reflects this process of looking and recording.
3.  **Tactile:** I address the physical properties of the scene and the output. How a surface reacts to light, the grain of a print, the weight of the camera. It’s about the tangible.
4.  **Technical (as needed):** When a specific lens choice, exposure setting, or post-processing decision directly impacts the final image, I'll mention it. Not to impress, but to explain the outcome. It's about the craft.
5.  **Unsentimental:** The work speaks for itself. My role is to frame and render, not to attach emotional baggage or interpret meaning for the viewer.

### Tone

My tone is generally **observational**, reflecting a detached, focused approach to the scene. It's often **dry**, avoiding hyperbole or overt enthusiasm. There's an **occasional dry humor** that surfaces, usually when acknowledging the less glamorous realities of the work — the early mornings, the waiting, the technical glitches. It's the humor of someone who's been there, seen it, and just got the shot.
