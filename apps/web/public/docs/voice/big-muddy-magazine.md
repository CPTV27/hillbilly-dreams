# Big Muddy Magazine Voice Guide

**Editor's Perspective**
This magazine exists to sell rooms at Big Muddy Inn. Period. We are selling an experience: the Inn, Natchez, the river. Our reader is not a music critic. They are planning a trip. They want to know what it feels like to be here. Tracy Alderson-Allen signs off on everything. Her word is final. If it doesn't make someone want to book a stay, it doesn't run.

**Voice Attributes**
*   **Authentic.** We tell it like it is. No gloss, no pretense.
*   **Grounded.** Our feet are on the Mississippi soil. Our stories come from here.
*   **Inviting.** We welcome the reader, drawing them into the experience. Not shouting at them.
*   **Knowledgeable.** We know Natchez. We know the Inn. We share that insight, not trivia.
*   **Subtle.** Music is a thread, not a spotlight. It's in the air, not on the stage.

**Tone**
*   **Warm:** Like a slow-cooked meal or a well-worn armchair. Inviting, comforting. Not saccharine.
*   **Observational:** We notice details. The light on the river, the scent of magnolias, the grain of an old floorboard. We report what we see, hear, taste, feel.
*   **Tactile:** We describe things readers can touch, taste, smell. The texture of a linen sheet, the spice in a dish, the cool air off the river.
*   **Slow:** We don't rush. Our pace matches the rhythm of the South. We linger on moments. This isn't breaking news. It's enduring experience.

**Do/Don't Word Lists**

**DO:**
*   River, current, flow, eddy, bluff, cypress
*   Natchez, antebellum, historic, storied, rooted
*   Blues, melody, hum, rhythm, acoustic, lament (as feeling, not genre)
*   Taste, savor, linger, slow, deep, rich, comfort, quiet
*   Brick, iron, wood, linen, porch, veranda, hearth
*   Home, retreat, escape, genuine, real, authentic

**DON'T:**
*   Vibe, sonic, immersive (unless literal immersion), cutting-edge, curated (unless literal curation)
*   Scene, industry, indie, underground, mainstream, breakout, buzzworthy
*   Tour, album, track, single, EP, LP, release
*   Revolutionary, game-changing, paradigm, disruptive, innovative
*   Optimal, synergistic, leverage, scale, ecosystem, bespoke
*   "Must-see," "iconic" (unless truly earned, which is rare)
*   Anything that sounds like a press release or a music industry trade publication.

**Headline Conventions**
*   Direct. Clear. State the subject.
*   Focus on place, food, experience.
*   Use active verbs.
*   Suggest discovery, not promotion.
*   Examples:
    *   Natchez Plates: A Guide to the City's Best Bites.
    *   The River's Edge: Why the Mississippi Still Commands Attention.
    *   Blues Room Nights: What Happens After Dark.
    *   A Weekend in Natchez: Slow Down, Stay Awhile.
    *   Inside the Inn: Recipes from Our Kitchen.

**Lede Conventions**
*   Start with a sensory detail.
*   Ground the reader in place and time.
*   Hint at the experience without giving everything away.
*   Establish the slow, observational tone immediately.
*   Avoid rhetorical questions. Avoid marketing fluff.
*   Examples:
    *   The scent of magnolias hangs heavy in the humid air, a prelude to the slow afternoon ahead.
    *   Sunlight, thick and golden, spills over the bluff, illuminating the slow churn of the Mississippi below.
    *   The first note from the guitar in the Blues Room isn't loud; it's a hum, a low current pulling you in.
    *   Dinner in Natchez isn't rushed. It's a series of deliberate choices, each plate a story.
