## Big Muddy Records: Brand Voice Document

### Our Voice in a Nutshell

Big Muddy Records speaks like a seasoned independent musician or manager – someone who's been in the trenches, knows the score, and values good music and honest work above all else. We're direct, no-nonsense, and grounded in the practical realities of the music business. We believe in artists as collaborators, not commodities, and our words reflect that respect. We cut through the industry fluff, offering transparent, supportive, and experienced guidance, whether we're talking to potential partners, fans, or sync agencies. We don't oversell, we don't pretend, and we're here to help get real music heard.

---

### Voice Attributes

1.  **Authentic:** We speak plainly and genuinely, without pretense or industry jargon. We're real people talking about real music and real work.
2.  **Experienced:** We've been around the block. Our voice carries the weight of practical knowledge and a clear understanding of the music landscape, both its opportunities and its challenges.
3.  **Collaborative:** We treat artists as partners. Our language emphasizes mutual respect, shared goals, and working *together* to get things done.
4.  **Transparent:** We're upfront about what we do, how we do it, and what we don't do. No hidden agendas, no vague promises.
5.  **Respectful:** We deeply value the art and effort that goes into making music. Our voice always honors the artist's vision and autonomy.

---

### Tone

Our tone is best described as **conversational but direct**. It's like talking to a trusted peer or a mentor who's seen it all – supportive, but never sycophantic. There's a slight world-weariness that comes from knowing the grind, yet it's underpinned by an undeniable passion for the music itself. We're unfussy, confident without being arrogant, and always focused on the practicalities of making things happen.

---

### Vocabulary: Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
*   Use terms like "we work with you," "our artists," "your music," "the folks we partner with."
*   Refer to "the grind," "the work," "getting it out there," "making it happen."
*   Employ practical industry terms: "promotion," "distribution," "licensing," "media structure," "catalogue."
*   Express genuine appreciation: "great tunes," "real art," "solid tracks," "honest songs."
*   Formulate direct statements and questions: "Here's what we offer," "Let's talk," "We believe in..."

**DON'T:**
*   **Major-label boilerplate:** "synergy," "disruptive innovation," "paradigm shift," "monetization strategies," "leverage assets."
*   **Over-the-top hype:** "breakthrough artist," "game-changer," "next big thing," "curated talent" (implies we're just selecting, not partnering), "global dominance."
*   **Corporate speak:** "optimize outreach," "maximize ROI," "strategic imperatives."
*   **Proprietary language:** Anything that implies ownership over an artist's creative output or identity, e.g., "our talent," "our roster" (unless specifically referring to our owned catalogs).
*   **Empty promises:** "guaranteed success," "viral potential," "unparalleled artistry" (unless genuinely specific and earned).
*   **Fluffy, insincere praise:** Avoid vague superlatives that don't mean anything.
