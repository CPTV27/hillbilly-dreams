## The economics

This is the actual revenue line. Records and Radio are 12–24 month plays. Photography pays now.

**Current revenue baseline (estimate from last 6 months):**
- Commercial shoots: $500–$2,500 each, ~2–4/month = $1,000–$10,000/month range
- Average month: ~$3,000

**Year 1 target with storefront live:**
- Commercial: hold at $3,000–$5,000/month
- Print storefront: $500–$2,000/month after launch
- Total: $40,000–$80,000/year, depending on storefront traction

**Cost structure:**
- Equipment: already owned (Sony bodies, Sigma + GMaster glass, lighting)
- Editing: in-house, Capture One + Photoshop
- Print fulfillment: 35–50% of retail to vendor (WHCC pricing model)
- Storefront tech: stripe + existing Next.js site = ~$0 marginal
- Travel: passed through to client on commercial work

**DBA relationship to MBT:**
- All revenue flows to MBT bank account
- Chase draws as MBT founder distribution, not 1099
- Storefront margins fund Records + Radio shortfalls
- Image library is MBT-owned IP, not a personal asset

Photography is the cash engine that subsidizes the long-horizon plays.

## The customer

Three customers, three different pricing models:

1. **Hospitality + commercial clients.** Inns, restaurants, venues, bands needing PR photography. Project rate, $500–$5,000.
2. **Editorial + brand work.** Magazine spreads, brand campaigns, partner agencies (Tuthill, Studio C). Day rate or project, $1,500–$2,500/day.
3. **Print storefront customers.** Direct-to-consumer art buyers. Tourists at the Inn, Magazine readers, Hudson Valley walk-ups. $40–$300 per print.

Storefront is the volume play. Commercial is the margin play. Editorial is the brand play.

## The risk

1. **Tracy's review takes too long.** The storefront is bottlenecked on her print curation. Mitigate by giving her a deadline and a smaller batch (start with 10, not 30).
2. **Day rate vs. project rate confusion.** If we don't decide, every quote is custom and slow. Decide once, stick to it.
3. **Print fulfillment quality.** Bad prints kill brand trust faster than no prints. Order samples from 2 vendors before committing.
4. **Chase's time is the bottleneck.** Every shoot is Chase-only. Doesn't scale past ~6 shoots/month unless we hire a second shooter (not in 2026 plan).
5. **Photo policy enforcement.** Every image used on a Big Muddy property must be in `/approved/`. AI-generated images cannot ship. This is Chase's gate — a CMS slip-up that publishes an unapproved image hurts the whole ecosystem.

## Why it matters to the ecosystem

Photography is the visual identity of the entire Big Muddy ecosystem. It's the asset every other module depends on.

- **Inn uses it.** Every room, every menu, every site image.
- **Touring uses it.** Every band shot, tour poster, PR image.
- **Magazine uses it.** Every editorial spread, cover, social post.
- **Records uses it.** Album covers, artist press kits, music videos (eventually).
- **Radio uses it.** Show art, host headshots, brand visuals.
- **MBT uses it.** Sales decks, B2B pitches, partner case studies.
- **Bearsville uses it.** Hudson Valley shoots become Bearsville Creative's regional library.

Without Chase's photo library, every Big Muddy property looks generic. With it, the visual world is consistent across 14 domains and 5 brands. That's a competitive moat — competitors have to either license stock or hire a photographer; we have one in-house who knows the corridor and the people in it.

The DBA relationship to MBT also means image IP stays with the platform, not the individual. If Chase steps back from a brand, the photos don't leave with him. That's structurally important for long-term ecosystem value.
