# "AI Tells You. We Do It." — Campaign Brief

*Drafted 2026-04-30. The Gen Z / Boomer-meets-AI ad campaign for Measurably Better Things.*

## The thesis (Chase, verbatim 2026-04-30)

> "AI can tell you how to do anything, but you start reading the instructions and you're like, 'Oh, I can't do any of this stuff.' We do that stuff."

That's the whole campaign. Every ad is a variation on the same script.

## The tagline (LOCKED 2026-04-30 evening)

**We do the rest.**

The full campaign closer (verbatim, every ad):

> *"You don't have a clue where to begin. We do. We do the rest."*

## The master script (every ad uses this)

```
AI VOICE (computer / screen / phone):
"I just told you exactly how to [VERB + OBJECT]. Ten easy steps."

[Person stares at screen. Beat.]

VOICEOVER (or text card on screen):
"You don't have a clue where to begin."

[Beat.]

"We do. We do the rest."

[Studio C mark, hold 2 sec.]
```

That's the entire campaign. The only variable per ad is the **[VERB + OBJECT]** the AI claims to have explained. Everything else stays verbatim. Same beat, infinite topics, instant brand recognition.

Why this works:
- "Ten easy steps" is the universal lie. Everyone has been told something is ten easy steps and it never is.
- The silence after the AI's confidence is the joke.
- "You don't have a clue where to begin" lands because it's true. Of you. Of me. Of everyone.
- "We do the rest" is the relief. The product. The pitch.

## The format

Short-form video — TikTok / Reels / YouTube Shorts — 15-30 seconds each. Static meme variants for IG / X / Threads. The setup is always the same: someone asks AI for help, AI gives the cosmically helpful answer, the person stares at it, then a card appears: **"or just text Studio C."**

The humor is the gap between what AI says you should do and what an actual human can actually execute. Gen Z laughs at the boomer not knowing what "clear your cache" means. Boomer laughs at Gen Z not knowing what an "escrow" is. Both end up in the same place: paying us to do it.

## The "Ten Easy Steps" running gag

Every ad uses the phrase "Ten easy steps." Always ten. Never seven or fifteen. The repetition becomes part of the brand.

Optional sub-gag: occasionally show the actual ten steps on screen as the AI reads them — small text, scrolling fast, technically accurate, completely unactionable. The viewer can't read them but recognizes the shape of an instruction list. The joke lands harder.

## Voice rules for the campaign

## Voice rules for the campaign

- Boomers are warm, not stupid. They're *bewildered*, not incompetent. Punching down is not the joke.
- Gen Z is exasperated but loving. "Bestie" energy, not contempt.
- The AI is the antagonist. Always over-explains. Always assumes context the user doesn't have. Never does the thing.
- Studio C is the relief. One line. One text. Done.
- Real-world tasks only. No hypothetical "imagine if you needed to..." — show actual, mundane shit people deal with.

## Production notes

- **Talent:** Cast a real boomer (one of Chase's parents' generation) and a real Gen Z (someone Tracy or Amy knows in their early 20s). Authenticity beats actors here. Two people, real reactions, no script polish.
- **Length:** 15 seconds for TikTok / Reels. 30 seconds for YouTube. 6 seconds for the static image meme version.
- **Music:** Diegetic only (the sound of the boomer's laptop fan, the Alexa voice, etc.). No score. The silence after AI's answer is the joke.
- **Card:** Black screen, white serif type (Playfair Display), small "Studio C" mark in the corner. Minimal. The product mark does not yell.
- **Frequency:** 2-3 ads per week for 8 weeks. Each one stands alone. Together they build a recognizable rhythm.

## What this proves about MBT

The campaign IS the product positioning:

1. AI is everywhere. It's free. It's verbose.
2. Most of what AI tells you to do, you can't do. Because:
   - You don't have time
   - You don't have the password
   - You don't know the term it just used
   - You're not going to learn it for one task
3. We do it. Same chat interface AI uses. We're on the other end. We charge for our hands.

The ads don't have to explain MBT's product. They have to land the gap between "AI told me" and "I got it done." MBT lives in that gap.

## Risk

The concept gets old fast if every ad is identical. The 10 above use different setups (Java update / cache / deployment / tax / screenshot / thermostat / domain / spreadsheet / wedding / universal montage) — keep the mix wide. If we lean too hard on the boomer-can't-tech trope, it stops being affectionate and starts being mean. Vary the protagonists. Make the Gen Z one as bewildered as the boomer for the next 3.
