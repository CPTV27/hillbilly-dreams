The Modules

Measurably Better Things is a chat-first AI platform with a service operation behind it. The chat is the front door. Modules are what the chat can do. Customers turn modules on as they need them. They pay only for what they use. They never wait for a sales call.

A walkthrough of every module class, the interface options, the verticals it serves, and what it costs.

Most software companies bundle features into tiers and force customers to pay for things they will never use. Measurably Better Things does the opposite. The platform is a base subscription — the chat itself, the router that picks the right model, the agent registry, the human escalation path, the billing meter — and on top of that, customers add modules à la carte. Each module is a focused capability with its own monthly fee, often bundled with included human-tokens for the work the module will actually do.

"The chat is always the front door. Modules just expand what the chat can do behind the scenes. From the customer's perspective: 'I need this.' → 'We have a module for that. Activate it?'"

The four module classes

Modules are organized by how much human-token work they typically consume per month. Light modules are mostly automation. Standard modules add an hour of skilled human time. Specialized modules dedicate a person on a queue. High-touch modules wrap a multi-skill team.

Light modules
+$20/month
Chat capability + occasional automation. Mostly software-token work, with the chat itself doing the lift.
Examples: Email triage. Reminders sync. Calendar coordination. Link-in-bio management. Custom prompts tuned to the customer's voice. Routine scheduling. Inbox-zero loops.
Standard modules
+$50–$100/month · ~1 hr of human-tokens included
Chat capability plus about an hour of human-token work each month for the module's recurring needs. The sweet spot — most operating customers live here.
Examples: Bookkeeping ingest. Customer support triage. Lead-gen automation. Social posting cadence. Weekly reporting. Inventory alerts. Auto-generated analytics dashboards.
Specialized modules
+$200–$500/month · 3-8 hr of human-tokens + dedicated specialist on queue
Modules that require humans with rare skills. The customer pays the real cost of the capability plus margin. The specialist is on the queue, not generic ops.
Examples: Video production (editor + colorist + sound designer). Podcast editing + show notes + clip generation. Brand identity refresh. Sales pipeline operations. Recruiting screen + scheduling. Content engine — weekly blog + newsletter + repurposed clips.
High-touch modules
+$500–$2,000/month · 10+ hr of human-tokens + multi-skill team rotation
Full agency-grade capability. A team rotation around a single customer relationship. Comparable to hiring a small agency, billed inside the same chat.
Examples: Full social media management. Ongoing PR and press outreach. Agency-of-record on retainer. Custom ML model maintenance. Dedicated customer success manager.

How customers actually shop

No procurement. No sales call. No 30-day trial that turns into a 12-month contract. The customer asks the chat for help with something. The chat tells them which module covers it, what it costs, and whether to turn it on.

Customer
Can you help me with my Instagram?
Chat
You don't have the Social Media module activated. It's $100/mo and includes scheduled posts plus engagement responses across three platforms. Want me to turn it on?
Customer
Yes.
Chat
Activated. Tell me your handles and what kind of content you want to run. I'll come back in fifteen minutes with a draft posting calendar.

Pay for what's used, the moment it's needed. Pause when it's not. The chat keeps the meter visible at the bottom of every conversation, so the customer always knows what software-token work cost and what human-token work cost.

MBT bills custom builds the same way it bills everything else — token-metered, transparent, paused when the customer says pause. Engineering is just more tokens.

Why this works

The four reasons modular pricing wins
  • Customers don't pay for what they don't use. A solo operator might add Bookkeeping plus Social Media for $175/mo and skip everything else. A small business might add Content Engine plus Customer Support plus Lead Gen. The bill matches the actual need.
  • The chat is easy to ship. Major model APIs handle the model layer. The data layer is a stable open-source stack. Custom dashboards are built on demand, not pre-spec'd. The platform is leveraged, not bespoke.
  • The service is where defensibility lives. Anyone can stand up a chatbot. Few can stand up a chatbot that escalates cleanly to humans who actually resolve the operational task. That's MBT's edge — the chat plus the team behind it.
  • The same engine serves any vertical. Hospitality, music, scanning, real estate, civic. Each vertical is a system prompt and a data pipeline away. Big Muddy Inn is the proof case running on the same stack.

Where Studio C fits

Measurably Better Things is the platform. Studio C is the production arm — the operating vendor that handles the human-token side of every module that requires real production work. Video editing, podcast cutting, brand work, content building, the deliverables a customer can hold and look at. When the chat escalates, somebody has to actually do the thing. That's Studio C.

Studio C's responsibility is production work plus the admin of the production pipeline — intake, scheduling, vendor coordination, quality control, delivery. Studio C is not responsible for the platform itself. The chat working, the agents running, the billing system staying up — that's Chase, on the MBT side. Studio C delivers what gets escalated. Same screen, two meters, two operating teams behind the same surface.
