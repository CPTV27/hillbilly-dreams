### No One Owns the Corridor

**Existing operators (small, analog):**
- Delta Bohemian Tours (Clarksdale) — Jeep tours, word-of-mouth, boutique
- Real Delta Tours (Clarksdale) — custom packages for cuisine/music/civil rights

**National tour companies skip Mississippi entirely:**

| Company | Route | Days | Price | Includes MS? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Collette | Nashville-Memphis-NOLA | 8 | ~$3,000 | No |
| Tauck | Nashville-NOLA | 8 | ~$4,500 | No |
| Trafalgar | Multi-city South | 10 | ~$3,500 | Barely |
| Globus | Heritage | 8-10 | ~$3,000 | No |

**The corridor between Memphis and New Orleans is a white space in commercial touring.**

### Blues Trail Infrastructure
- 200+ interpretive markers statewide (est. 2006)
- No commercial booking layer on top
- No annual visitor count published (distributed marker system)

### The Broken Step
Visitor journey: Discover → Research → Plan → **Book** → Visit → Share. The Plan-to-Book step is broken. No platform packages lodging + shows + editorial into a bookable experience.

## BUYER PERSONAS

### Cultural Seeker (30-45, $150K+)
- Discovers via Instagram, TikTok, travel publications, podcasts
- Wants authentic, non-tourist experiences
- Needs the Plan-to-Book step fixed
- Would spend $200-500/night for the right lodging + experience package

### Accomplished Escapist (40-55, $250K+)
- Buys Tauck tours ($4,500), Viking cruises ($4-8K)
- Wants curated itineraries — does not want to plan
- Wants luxury that feels earned, not conspicuous
- The corridor's Blackberry Farm equivalent doesn't exist yet

### Main Street Business Owner (MBT buyer)
- Age: 50+, Gen X or Boomer
- Tech spend: $0-50/month
- Biggest frustration: "I know I need to be online but I don't know how"
- Decision-making: relationship-driven, buys from someone they trust at Chamber meeting
- Price sensitivity: $20/mo is reasonable, $50 is a gamble, $300+ is impossible

### Municipality / Economic Development Buyer
- Title: Economic Development Director, City Administrator
- Budget source: CDBG ($600K), ARPA, general fund, tourism tax
- Wants: business retention metrics, job creation numbers, downtown occupancy improvement
- **One CDBG grant funds MBT for every business in Adams County for years**

### Indie Musician in Mississippi
- Age: 25-45 (two clusters)
- Income: $20-50K from all music sources; many have day jobs
- Pain points: no studio within 90 min, no label investing locally, no sync pipeline
- Would pay: $500-2K for recording, $25-50/yr for distribution, 15-20% of sync revenue

## STRATEGIC HEADLINES

1. **7.36M Natchez Trace visitors + 45K cruise passengers = massive untapped market.** Nobody is capturing them with a branded touring product.

2. **$20/mo MBT undercuts enterprise competitors by 90%.** The municipality bulk buy via CDBG is the real play — one grant funds the whole county.

3. **Zero digital-native media in Natchez.** No internet radio, no digital magazine, no podcast network. Big Muddy enters with zero competition.

4. **Fat Possum left the local blues lane open.** They went national indie rock. Big Muddy Records can own Mississippi corridor music.

5. **The extraction math is devastating and quotable.** 10+ chain stores pulling $7-10M/year from a town of 14,000 with $35K median income.

6. **Mississippi Songwriters Festival permanently in Natchez (Jan 2026).** Plan around this.

7. **Cultural travelers spend 2x more** ($994 vs $457/trip). Heritage tourism market is $624B globally.

8. **Offline bookings still dominate at 68%.** The opportunity is curated, personalized service — not just a website.
