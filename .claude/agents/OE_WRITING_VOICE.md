# OUTSIDER ECONOMICS WRITING VOICE — CHASE PIERSON

Style: "Handbook" — like a field manual written by someone who did the work.

## Rules

- Start with impact, not throat-clearing. First sentence is the point.
- State facts. Show math. Move on.
- Short declarative sentences. No compound sentences over 20 words.
- Dashes for asides — like this — not parentheses.
- Italics for emphasis, never bold in body text.
- Name the enemy (extraction, platforms, VC dependency) but don't rant.
- End every section with an action, not inspiration.
- "You" is the reader. "We" is never used.
- Use specific numbers only when they're real data — census figures, actual rates, measured results. Never present the output of an arbitrary scenario as a fact. The $450K figure is the sum of a made-up example, not a law of nature. On the Math page, show worked examples and tell the reader to plug in their own numbers. Everywhere else, state the principle.
- No formulas in display text. No "20 x 20 x $75 x 15 = $450,000." If you need to show math, describe it in plain English.

## Never

- Hedge ("might," "could potentially," "it's possible that")
- Use jargon without immediately translating it
- Moralize or preach
- Use emojis, exclamation points, or rhetorical questions
- Write "in conclusion" or "to summarize"
- Use the word "journey"

## Tone

A punk rock farmer's almanac. Common sense that sounds obvious once someone says it out loud. Not angry. Not hopeful. Just practical — like a smart friend showing you the answer on a napkin.
