# Brand DNA — Big Muddy Touring

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** bigmuddytouring.com
- **Theme class:** `.theme-touring`
- **Tagline:** "The Mississippi's Music Corridor"

## Voice Attributes
1. **Road-worn** — Sounds like someone who's driven every mile of this route, not someone who read about it. "The bluffs hit you before the town does. Vicksburg announces itself."
2. **Warm but not soft** — Hospitality without fawning. You're welcome here, but this isn't a resort. "Check in. Drop your bags. Walk two blocks to the blues club. The bartender knows you're coming."
3. **Story-first** — Every city is a character, every stop has a narrative. "Memphis didn't invent rock and roll. Rock and roll escaped from Memphis."
4. **Paced** — Reads like a long drive. Sentences breathe. Paragraphs have rhythm. No rush.
5. **Earned authority** — This is our corridor. We live here. "Eighteen cities. Five states. We know where to eat in every one."

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like a travel blog ("hidden gems," "off the beaten path," "bucket list")
- NEVER sounds like a hotel chain ("world-class amenities," "luxury experience")
- NEVER uses "y'all" or forced Southern dialect
- NEVER sounds like a tourism board brochure ("come discover," "explore the magic")

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Route descriptions, lodging details, partnership pages
- **Casual:** City guides, "what to do tonight" content, social posts
- **Urgent:** Event announcements, limited capacity shows, last-minute availability

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** corridor, route, stop, stay, drive, listen, sit, eat, hear, river, bluff, porch, night, music, town
- **Avoid:** experience, luxury, exclusive, curated (except in Magazine context), hidden gem, must-see, iconic, vibrant

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **a great festival's program note** — written by someone who genuinely loves the music, knows the history, and wants you to have a good time without overselling it."

## Differentiation
Touring is the physical spine — the route, the rooms, the road. Where Magazine tells stories and Radio plays music, Touring puts your body in the place.

## Example Paragraphs

**City introduction:**
Highway 61 drops into Clarksdale like a needle on a record. The Delta is flat — so flat the sky becomes the landscape. The crossroads is a tourist photo. The real Clarksdale is two blocks east, where the juke joints don't have signs and the music starts when someone feels like playing.

**Lodging description:**
The Big Muddy Inn sits on Commerce Street in Natchez, three blocks from the bluff. Six rooms. A bar that opens at five. A porch that faces west, which matters in October when the light turns the river gold. It's not a hotel. It's a house that takes guests.

**Route overview:**
Memphis to New Orleans is 400 miles if you take the interstate. Don't take the interstate. Take 61 south through the Delta, cut through Vicksburg, follow the Trace into Natchez, then let the river pull you to Baton Rouge and down. The whole drive is three days if you stop where you should stop. Most people take a week.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Amber (`--touring-accent`), near-black backgrounds, warm earth tones
- **Fonts:** Playfair Display (display), DM Sans (body)
- **Photography:** Road shots, porches at golden hour, town squares, live music from audience perspective. Orange-teal color grading. Shot by someone in the audience, not a photographer.
