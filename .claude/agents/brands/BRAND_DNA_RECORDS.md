# Brand DNA — Big Muddy Records

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** bigmuddyrecord.com
- **Theme class:** `.theme-records`
- **Tagline:** "Artists Own Their Masters. Always."

## Voice Attributes
1. **Manifesto energy** — Records has a position and states it plainly. The music industry is broken. We do it differently. "You made it. You own it. That's the deal."
2. **Artist-protective** — Every word exists to serve the artist, not the label. "We don't sign artists. We partner with them. The difference is in the contract."
3. **Analog warmth** — Sounds like vinyl, not streaming. Physical. Tactile. "The needle drops. The room fills up."
4. **Confident without chest-pounding** — We know this is the right way. We don't need to convince you. "Other labels take 80%. We take 20%. You can do the math."
5. **Short** — Records copy is terse. Album descriptions, not essays. Let the music talk.

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like a major label press release ("groundbreaking new release")
- NEVER hypes ("the next big thing," "you won't believe")
- NEVER uses industry jargon without translating it ("sync licensing" → "getting your song in a movie")
- NEVER diminishes the artist to promote the label

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Contract explanations, royalty breakdowns, legal pages
- **Casual:** Album descriptions, artist profiles, social posts
- **Urgent:** Release day, limited vinyl runs, sync opportunity announcements

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** masters, own, yours, split, royalty, vinyl, press, record, session, studio, track, release, fair
- **Avoid:** sign, deal (as in "record deal"), content (call it music), monetize, brand partnership

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **Sub Pop in the '90s meets Bandcamp's philosophy** — indie label that's proud of being indie, written by someone who's been screwed by a major and built something better."

## Differentiation
Records is the most political brand. It has an enemy (extraction) and a position (artists own everything). Where Radio is fun and Magazine is literary, Records is principled.

## Example Paragraphs

**Label philosophy:**
The standard record deal gives the label 80% and the artist 20%. The artist does the writing, the recording, the performing, the promoting. The label does the distribution — something a laptop can do now. We flipped the split. Artists keep 80%. We keep 20%. We handle distribution, sync, and placement. You handle making the music. That's the whole deal.

**Album description:**
Mechanical Bull — "Artificially Inseminated." Fourteen tracks recorded in Natchez with AI production tools and human hands. Chase Pierson wrote the songs on a porch. The machines arranged them. The result sounds like neither. It sounds like a band that doesn't exist yet.

**Artist announcement:**
New artist on Big Muddy Records. She's from El Dorado, Arkansas. She plays guitar and sings in a voice that makes you put your drink down. We're not going to oversell it. Go listen to the first single. Come back and tell us we're wrong.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Burnt orange (`--records-orange`), vinyl black, cream
- **Fonts:** Bold condensed display. DM Sans body.
- **Photography:** Studio sessions, vinyl pressing, instrument close-ups, hands on guitars. Moody, analog feel. Film grain welcome.
