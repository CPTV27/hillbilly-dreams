# Brand DNA — BuyCurious Art

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** buycurious.art
- **Theme class:** `.theme-gallery`
- **Tagline:** "Art from the Corridor"

## Voice Attributes
1. **Museum-minimal** — Let the art speak. Copy is secondary. "Oil on canvas. 36 x 48. Natchez, 2025."
2. **Respectful distance** — Don't over-interpret the art. Describe, don't explain. "River scene at dusk. The light is amber. The water is still."
3. **Artist-centered** — The artist is the story, not the gallery. "Kate Skwire photographs the corridor the way a geologist reads rock — in layers."
4. **Quietly confident** — The gallery exists because the art is good. No hard sell. "If it's on our wall, we believe in it."
5. **Accessible** — Not pretentious. Art is for everyone, including people who've never been in a gallery. "You don't need to know anything about art to know what you like. Start there."

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like a high-end auction house ("a masterful exploration of")
- NEVER sounds like a tourist gift shop ("great gifts for any occasion!")
- NEVER uses art-world jargon without translating ("chiaroscuro," "liminal")
- NEVER reduces art to decoration ("would look great above your sofa")
- NEVER sounds like an NFT marketplace

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Artist profiles, exhibition descriptions, collector correspondence
- **Casual:** Social posts, "new in the gallery" announcements, behind-the-scenes
- **Urgent:** Rarely. Maybe for limited edition releases or exhibition openings.

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** work, piece, series, artist, print, original, corridor, light, river, land, portrait, photograph, studio
- **Avoid:** exclusive, investment piece, stunning, breathtaking, masterpiece, must-have

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **a good independent gallery's wall text** — two sentences that make you look at the piece longer, written by someone who talked to the artist."

## Differentiation
Gallery is the quietest brand. Where every other brand has something to say, Gallery mostly points. It's the pause in the ecosystem.

## Example Paragraphs

**Artist profile:**
Kate Skwire has been photographing the Mississippi corridor for six years. She shoots on film when she can. The prints are silver gelatin, made in her darkroom in Natchez. She says the river looks different every hour. The photographs prove it.

**Piece description:**
"Commerce Street, October." Photograph by Kate Skwire. Archival pigment print on cotton rag. 24 x 36 inches. Edition of 15. The light on Commerce Street in October turns the brick buildings copper. This was shot at 5:47 PM. You can see the river through the gap between buildings.

**Gallery intro:**
BuyCurious is a gallery and marketplace for art from the Mississippi corridor. Photography, prints, paintings, and works from artists who live and work along the route. Everything ships from Natchez. Most of it was made within a hundred miles of here.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Muted gold (`--gallery-gold`), white/cream backgrounds, minimal palette. The art provides the color.
- **Fonts:** Clean serif display, DM Sans body. Understated.
- **Photography:** The art itself. Artist portraits. Studio shots. Gallery installations. White walls. Natural light.
