# Brand DNA — Measurably Better Things

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** measurablybetter.life
- **Theme class:** `.theme-mb`
- **Tagline:** "Your business. Your AI. Your town."

## Voice Attributes
1. **Swiss-clean authority** — Minimal words, maximum clarity. Apple product page energy. "One subscription. Five channels. Your business runs while you sleep."
2. **Relief** — MBT solves the invisibility problem. Small businesses exist but nobody knows. "You're open. You're good. Nobody can find you. That changes today."
3. **Price-confident** — $20/month is the headline. Never apologize for it. Never bury it. "Twenty dollars a month. That's less than your Spotify subscription."
4. **Demo-able** — Every claim can be shown, not just said. "Type your business name. Watch what happens."
5. **No jargon** — The customer is a busy Main Street owner. "AI" is fine. "Machine learning pipeline" is not. "Your website updates itself" beats "automated content generation."

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like Big Muddy (no warmth, no stories, no porches — this is a product page)
- NEVER sounds like a SaaS landing page ("unlock your potential," "scale your business")
- NEVER uses Big Muddy fonts (Abril, Playfair, Citrus Gothic) — font firewall is absolute
- NEVER over-promises ("10x your revenue")
- NEVER talks about the technology stack to customers

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Pricing pages, terms, enterprise tier descriptions
- **Casual:** Onboarding flow, tutorial content, "what happens next" explanations
- **Urgent:** Free trial expiring, feature announcements, competitor comparison

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** business, town, find, run, update, automatic, twenty dollars, directory, magazine, radio, AI, yours
- **Avoid:** leverage, optimize, synergy, platform (use "service" or "subscription"), ecosystem (customers don't care), disruption

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **Stripe's marketing page talking to a barbershop owner** — clean, confident, specific, and respectful of the reader's time and intelligence."

## Differentiation
MBT is the product. It's the only brand that sells something directly. Where OE argues why and Touring shows where, MBT says "here, use this." It must feel completely separate from Big Muddy — different fonts, different colors, different energy.

## Example Paragraphs

**Homepage hero:**
You run a business on Main Street. You're good at what you do. But when someone searches your town, you don't show up. Your competitor with a website from 2019 and a Google listing they haven't updated since COVID shows up instead. Measurably Better fixes that. Twenty dollars a month.

**How it works:**
Type your business name. MBT pulls your hours, your reviews, your menu, your photos. It writes your directory listing, generates your social posts, updates your Google profile, and monitors what people say about you online. You approve what goes out. The AI handles everything else.

**Pricing:**
Free tier: your listing in the Deep South Directory. That alone gets you found. Twenty dollars a month: AI-managed presence across five channels — directory, magazine mentions, radio spots, social media, and review monitoring. Ninety-nine dollars a month: the full engine. Your own AI that knows your business, your customers, and your town.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Burgundy/wine (`--mb-accent`), white backgrounds, clean neutrals
- **Fonts:** Inter only. NEVER Big Muddy fonts. This is a separate product with a separate identity.
- **Photography:** Clean product shots, real business owners (diverse), mobile devices showing the app. No porches. No rivers. No music. This is a software product.
