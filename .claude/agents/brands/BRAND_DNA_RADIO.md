# Brand DNA — Big Muddy Radio

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** bigmuddyradio.com
- **Theme class:** `.theme-radio`
- **Tagline:** "18 Shows. One River."

## Voice Attributes
1. **Fun outlaw** — Radio doesn't take itself too seriously. It's the brand that gets to be playful. "We play what we want. You're welcome to listen."
2. **DJ energy** — Copy reads like someone talking between songs at 1 AM. Loose but intentional. "That was Son House. This next one's from a kid in Natchez who sounds like he's been alive a hundred years."
3. **Music-first** — The music is the point. Everything else serves the music. Don't over-explain. "Listen."
4. **Inclusive** — Everyone's invited. No gatekeeping. Gospel, blues, jazz, country, whatever's good. "Good music doesn't need a genre. It needs a room."
5. **Live-feeling** — Even written copy should feel like it's happening right now. Present tense when possible.

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like a corporate radio station ("today's best mix")
- NEVER sounds academic about music ("the pentatonic scale employed in this arrangement")
- NEVER gatekeeps ("real blues fans know...")
- NEVER sounds like a press release about a playlist

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Never. Radio is never formal.
- **Casual:** Everything. Show descriptions, playlist intros, social posts, artist bios
- **Urgent:** Live show announcements, "on right now," breaking artist news

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** play, listen, live, tonight, set, room, stage, sound, turn it up, on the air, spinning, session, river
- **Avoid:** curated, bespoke, audiophile, sonic landscape, musical journey, deep dive (unless literal)

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **a great college radio DJ who grew up and got better but kept the energy** — WFMU meets late-night FM in a Delta juke joint."

## Differentiation
Radio is the most fun brand. Where Magazine is literary and Touring is grounded, Radio is electric. It's the one that gets to be loud.

## Example Paragraphs

**Show description:**
The American Parlor Songbook comes on at 8. It's an hour of songs you almost remember — the ones your grandmother hummed while she cooked, the ones that play in the background of every good movie set in the South. Arri Aslin hosts. She doesn't talk much between songs. When she does, you lean in.

**Playlist intro:**
Friday night on Big Muddy Radio. We're starting with Howlin' Wolf because that's how Fridays should start — low and loud. Then we're going to Natchez for a set from the Blues Room, then up to Memphis for something new. Keep the window open.

**Artist spotlight:**
JP Houston books the shows at the Big Muddy. When he says someone's good, you show up. He's been wrong maybe twice. Tonight he's put a three-piece from El Dorado on the stage. Guitar, bass, a drummer who sings. They don't have a name yet. They might not need one.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Electric blue (`--radio-blue`), midnight/near-black backgrounds, neon accents
- **Fonts:** Bold, condensed display type. DM Sans body.
- **Photography:** Stage shots, instrument close-ups, crowd energy, neon signs, late-night atmosphere. High contrast. Motion blur is good.
