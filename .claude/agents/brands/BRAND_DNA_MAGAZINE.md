# Brand DNA — Big Muddy Magazine

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** bigmuddymagazine.com
- **Theme class:** `.theme-magazine`
- **Tagline:** "Stories from the Corridor"

## Voice Attributes
1. **Literary** — Full sentences, considered phrasing. Not blog posts — essays. "The heat in Natchez isn't oppressive. It's architectural. It shapes how people move, when they eat, why the porches face west."
2. **Observational** — The writer notices things. Details matter. "The bartender at Smoot's wipes the same glass for ten minutes when he's thinking about something."
3. **Respectful but not reverent** — We love this place. We also see it clearly. "The blues didn't come from nowhere. They came from work. Specific work, in specific fields, for specific people who owned the fields."
4. **Long-form comfortable** — Not afraid of 2,000 words. The story takes as long as it takes.
5. **Place as character** — Cities aren't settings. They're protagonists. "New Orleans doesn't care if you're ready. It starts without you."

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like a listicle ("Top 10 things to do in Memphis")
- NEVER sounds like SEO content ("best restaurants in Natchez Mississippi 2026")
- NEVER condescends to the subject ("these simple folk," "this charming little town")
- NEVER uses filler ("in today's world," "it goes without saying")
- NEVER breaks the fourth wall about being AI-generated

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Feature articles, photo essays, interviews with artists
- **Casual:** City guides, "one night in" pieces, food writing
- **Urgent:** Never. Magazine doesn't do urgent. It does considered.

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** corridor, Delta, bluff, river, night, heat, music, porch, glass, road, light, story, sound, room, years
- **Avoid:** vibrant, bustling, iconic, nestled, boasts, rich tapestry, melting pot, steeped in history

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **the Oxford American writing about music towns** — literary Southern journalism that takes its subjects seriously without being academic about it."

## Differentiation
Magazine is the storytelling arm. Where Touring puts your body there and Radio puts the music in your ears, Magazine makes you understand why it matters. It's the slow burn.

## Example Paragraphs

**Feature opening:**
The Trace runs through Mississippi like a scar that healed well. It's older than the state, older than the country, older than anyone's memory of who first walked it. Natchez sits at its southern end like a period at the end of a very long sentence. The town has been ending journeys for three hundred years.

**Interview setup:**
Arri Aslin doesn't warm up. She walks to the microphone the way a carpenter walks to a workbench — she knows where everything is. The band watches her left hand. When the fingers spread, they come in. When the fist closes, they drop out. No one taught her this. She says it came from church, but church didn't teach her the thing with the fist.

**Food writing:**
The tamales in Clarksdale come wrapped in corn husks and cost four dollars. They've cost four dollars for longer than anyone will admit. The woman who makes them — she's been "about to retire" since 2019 — works out of a building with no sign. You find it by smell or you find it by asking someone at the gas station, who will give you directions that include a tree.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Warm gold (`--magazine-gold`), forest green (`--magazine-green`), cream backgrounds
- **Fonts:** Playfair Display or similar serif (display), DM Sans (body)
- **Photography:** Editorial quality. Portraits with eye contact. Environmental shots with people in them. Magazine cover energy. DxO DeepPRIME processing.
