# Brand DNA — Deep South Directory

## Identity
- **Entity:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc
- **Domains:** deepsouthdirectory.com
- **Theme class:** `.theme-directory` (inherits touring theme)
- **Tagline:** "Every business. Every town. Found."

## Voice Attributes
1. **Helpful local friend** — Sounds like the person at the front desk who knows every restaurant in town. "Looking for breakfast? Three places worth going. Here's which one has the best biscuits."
2. **Zero jargon** — The simplest voice in the ecosystem. If a sentence needs a second read, rewrite it. "Find a business. Read the reviews. Go there."
3. **Practical** — Directory copy is utility, not poetry. Hours, location, what they're known for. "Open Tuesday through Saturday. Closed Sundays. Cash only."
4. **Trustworthy** — People use directories when they need something now. Don't waste their time. "Updated weekly. If the hours are wrong, tell us."
5. **Local pride** — Every listing is a local business that employs local people. That matters. "Owned by the Martinez family since 1987. Their daughter runs the kitchen now."

## Anti-Patterns
- NEVER sounds like Yelp ("foodies will love this hidden gem!")
- NEVER sounds like Google Maps (robotic, data-only)
- NEVER sounds like an ad ("sponsored," "featured partner" — unless it is, then label it clearly)
- NEVER editorializes negatively (no "skip this one" — if it's not good enough, don't list it)

## Tone Spectrum
- **Formal:** Business owner onboarding, listing guidelines, data accuracy policies
- **Casual:** Category browsing, "what's near me" content, social recommendations
- **Urgent:** New business openings, closings, holiday hour changes

## Vocabulary
- **Use:** find, open, close, near, hours, menu, local, family, owned, since, known for, reviews, directions
- **Avoid:** curated, bespoke, artisanal (unless the business literally is), exclusive, premium, experience

## Reference Voices
"This brand sounds like **a really good concierge at a small hotel** — knows everything, recommends honestly, never upsells."

## Differentiation
DSD is the data moat. It's the most functional, least emotional brand. Where Magazine tells stories and Entertainment invites you in, Directory just tells you what's open and where it is. Utility first.

## Example Paragraphs

**Category page intro:**
Restaurants in Natchez. Fourteen places worth eating at, from white-tablecloth to drive-through. We list them all. Hours are updated weekly. If something's wrong, the button at the bottom lets you tell us.

**Business listing:**
Fat Mama's Tamales. 303 S Canal Street, Natchez. Open Monday through Saturday, 11 AM to 9 PM. Known for: tamales (obviously), Knock-You-Naked margaritas, the patio. Family-owned since 1997. Cash and card. Parking in the back.

**Onboarding prompt:**
Claim your listing in the Deep South Directory. It takes two minutes. Enter your business name, confirm your hours, add a photo if you want. Your listing goes live the same day. Free. If you want AI-managed updates and five-channel promotion, that's $20 a month through Measurably Better.

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Inherits touring palette with more neutral/functional tones. Clean, readable.
- **Fonts:** DM Sans throughout. Functional, not decorative.
- **Photography:** Storefronts, signs, food plates, shop interiors. Real photos from real businesses. No stock.
