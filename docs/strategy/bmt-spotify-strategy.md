# Big Muddy Radio — Spotify Playlist Strategy
### A Working Roadmap for Chase & the Big Muddy Touring Team
*Prepared: March 2026 | First 5 Playlists Live in 90 Days*

---

## Overview

Big Muddy Radio sits at the cultural heart of the Memphis-to-New-Orleans Blues Trail corridor — the stretch of Highway 61 where American music was born, refined, and carried north. These five playlists are not just streaming products; they are the sonic identity of the Big Muddy Touring media-hospitality ecosystem. Each one should feel like a room in the Big Muddy Inn: warm, particular, deeply sourced, and impossible to replicate anywhere else.

The strategy below covers five fully developed playlists with track-by-track listings using real, Spotify-available songs, cover art direction keyed to the brand palette (dark backgrounds, warm amber/gold #c8943e accents, Playfair Display font overlays), growth tactics to move from 0 to 1,000+ followers in six months, and a roadmap for Month 4+ expansion.

### Playlist SEO: Naming, Descriptions & Keywords

Spotify's search algorithm indexes playlist titles, descriptions, and the names of artists included in the playlist. Based on [current SEO research](https://playlistpush.com/blog/top-seo-secrets-for-your-spotify-playlists/):

**Naming Best Practices**
- Front-load primary keywords in the title: "Delta Blues Essentials" ranks better than "Essentials of the Delta" because the genre comes first
- Include searchable terms real listeners use: "delta blues," "juke joint," "highway 61," "new orleans jazz," "natchez"
- Avoid generic titles ("Best Blues") that face overwhelming competition; own the niche
- The word "Essentials" outperforms "Best Of" because it signals curation authority

**Description Best Practices** *(per [Rexius Records' Spotify ranking guide](https://artists.rexiusrecords.com/noteworthy/how-to-rank-your-playlists-on-spotify/))*
- Include primary keywords in the first 2 sentences (Spotify truncates descriptions in search)
- Name key artists in descriptions — "Robert Johnson, Muddy Waters, Son House" generates search discovery
- Describe the mood and occasion: "late-night blues," "road trip music," "juke joint dance"
- Keep descriptions to 3–5 sentences; longer descriptions dilute keyword relevance
- Use keywords like "Natchez Mississippi," "Memphis to New Orleans," "Blues Trail" to capture regional searches

**Example optimized description (Playlist #1):**
> *Delta Blues Essentials — the definitive collection of Delta blues music, featuring Robert Johnson, Son House, Muddy Waters, Skip James, Charley Patton, and Christone "Kingfish" Ingram. From the crossroads of Highway 61 to the Natchez blues corridor, this playlist documents the full arc of Delta blues tradition from 1929 to today. Essential listening for blues fans, road trippers, and anyone following the Memphis-to-New-Orleans Blues Trail.*

### Submitting to Spotify Editorial Playlists

Spotify editorial playlists (Spotify-curated, not user-created) require you to submit **unreleased music** through Spotify for Artists — this does not apply to cover playlists. However, if Big Muddy Radio ever records original content (live sessions, Big Muddy Room Sessions), that content should be submitted to editorial playlists at least **28 days before release** per [current 2025 guidance from Hypebot](https://www.hypebot.com/guide-to-get-on-spotify-editorial-playlist-in-2025/).

For the existing cover playlists, the editorial goal is different: **get Big Muddy Radio's playlists discovered by Spotify's algorithmic playlists**, which happens when:
- Playlist follower counts reach 1,000+
- Listener save rates are high (target >15%)
- Track completion rates are high (target >70%)
- Playlists are regularly updated (signals active curation)

Target Spotify editorial playlists where your tracks might be noticed as part of algorithmic clustering:
- **Blues Classics** (Spotify-curated)
- **Delta Blues** (Spotify-curated)
- **Southern Soul** (Spotify-curated)
- **New Orleans Bounce** (Spotify-curated)
- **Roots Revival** (Spotify-curated)

### Playlist Update Cadence

Keeping playlists fresh signals to Spotify's algorithm that the curator is active. More importantly, it gives followers a reason to return.

| Playlist | Suggested Update Frequency | What to Change |
|----------|---------------------------|----------------|
| Delta Blues Essentials | Quarterly | Add 2–3 contemporary tracks from new releases. Remove rarely-played tracks. |
| Juke Joint Saturday Night | Monthly | Add 2–4 new tracks; remove the 2–4 lowest-performing. Feature new Kingfish, Burnside, Bobby Rush releases. |
| Highway 61 Road Trip | Bi-monthly | Add new Southern rock/roots releases (Chris Stapleton, Leon Bridges new albums). |
| Natchez After Dark | Quarterly | Add 2–3 new finds. This playlist should evolve slowly; its mood is the product. |
| New Orleans Brass & Soul | Monthly | New Orleans is prolific. Add Jazz Fest discoveries, Tank and the Bangas releases, new Trombone Shorty. |

**General Principle:** Never remove a classic track (Robert Johnson, Muddy Waters, Son House) — only add and prune from the contemporary/recent end. The backbone stays; the tip of the spear stays sharp.

### Analytics: Spotify for Artists & Performance Tracking

**Setting Up Spotify for Artists**
- Register at [artists.spotify.com](https://artists.spotify.com/) — Big Muddy Radio should register as the curator, not an individual
- Connect all playlists to the account dashboard
- Enable weekly analytics digests sent to chase@scan2plan.io

**Key Metrics to Track Weekly**

| Metric | What It Means | Target |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| **Followers** | How many Spotify users follow each playlist | +15% month-over-month |
| **Streams** | How many times tracks in your playlist were played | Trending upward |
| **Saves (Heart Rate)** | How often listeners save individual tracks | >15% of streams |
| **Completion Rate** | % of tracks listened to completion | >70% |
| **Skip Rate** | % of listeners who skip a track early | <30% |
| **Playlist Adds** | How often listeners add tracks to their own playlists | High = good taste, good algorithm signal |
| **Listener Sources** | Where listeners find the playlist (search, editorial, social) | Track which source is growing |

**Monthly Analytics Review**
Each month, pull the analytics and ask:
1. Which playlist is growing fastest? Double down on promotion for that one.
2. Which tracks have the highest skip rate? Consider removing them.
3. Which tracks are getting saved most? Feature those in newsletter and social content.
4. Where are new followers coming from? Reallocate social effort accordingly.

**Tools to Use**
- **Spotify for Artists** (free) — Primary analytics
- **Chartmetric** (paid, ~$10/month) — Deeper playlist tracking, competitor intelligence
- **Soundcharts** (paid) — Track when your playlists are cited by other curators

All five playlist covers should feel like they belong to the same family — all clearly "Big Muddy Radio" — while each having its own mood and subject matter. The unifying elements:

**Brand Consistency Across All Five Covers**

- All use **Playfair Display** (serif) for the playlist title text overlay

## FUTURE PLAYLISTS: Month 4+ Expansion

Once the first five playlists are live and growing, these concepts should expand the catalog. Each is a distinct room in the Big Muddy Radio universe.

### Playlist 6: Blues Room Sessions *(Priority: High)*
**Concept:** Live recordings from the Big Muddy venue — original content that no one else has. This is the highest-value content play. Even a simple iPhone capture of a talented local act performing in the room creates a Spotify-exclusive asset.
**Track Count:** Start at 10–15 tracks; build over time
**Notes:** This is how Big Muddy Radio becomes a label, not just a curator.

### Playlist 7: Chicago Blues Electric
**Concept:** The sequel to Delta Blues Essentials — when the blues went north. Muddy Waters plugged in, Howlin' Wolf prowled the stage at Chess Records, and the city rebuilt the Delta sound into something urban and electric.
**Key Artists:** Muddy Waters (electric era), Howlin' Wolf, Buddy Guy, Junior Wells, Little Walter, Otis Rush, Magic Sam, Koko Taylor, Sonny Boy Williamson II
**Sample Tracks:** Smokestack Lightning (Howlin' Wolf), Little Red Rooster (Howlin' Wolf), Stone Crazy (Buddy Guy), Hoodoo Man Blues (Junior Wells)

### Playlist 8: Sunday Morning Gospel & Blues
**Concept:** The sacred side of the same tradition — field hollers, spirituals, gospel-blues hybrids. For Sunday mornings at the inn, before the day starts.
**Key Artists:** Blind Willie Johnson, Sister Rosetta Tharpe, Mahalia Jackson, Mavis Staples & The Staple Singers, Son House (religious recordings), Lead Belly (spirituals), Mississippi Mass Choir
**Sample Tracks:** Dark Was the Night (Blind Willie Johnson), This Train Is Bound for Glory (Sister Rosetta Tharpe), Amazing Grace (Mahalia Jackson), I'll Take You There (Staple Singers)

### Playlist 9: Zydeco & Cajun Dance Party
**Concept:** Just southwest of Natchez, the music changes — accordion, frottoir, and French Creole lyrics. This playlist captures the Louisiana French country sound.
**Key Artists:** Clifton Chenier, Buckwheat Zydeco, Beausoleil, BeauSoleil avec Michael Doucet, Steve Riley & the Mamou Playboys, Dwayne Dopsie & the Zydeco Hellraisers
**Sample Tracks:** Hot Tamale Baby (Clifton Chenier), Hey Mom! (Buckwheat Zydeco), Allons Danser (Beausoleil), Ma Tit Fille (Clifton Chenier)

### Playlist 10: Women of the Blues
**Concept:** The female voices who defined the blues from its earliest commercial recordings. Classic blues queens, country blues women, and contemporary artists.
**Key Artists:** Bessie Smith, Ma Rainey, Memphis Minnie, Victoria Spivey, Sippie Wallace, Etta James, Koko Taylor, Bonnie Raitt, Shemekia Copeland, Amythyst Kiah, Samantha Fish
**Sample Tracks:** St. Louis Blues (Bessie Smith), Prove It on Me Blues (Ma Rainey), When the Levee Breaks (Memphis Minnie, 1929 original), I Got What It Takes (Koko Taylor)

### Playlist 11: Hill Country Blues
**Concept:** The hypnotic, drone-based blues of North Mississippi — different from the Delta blues but equally elemental. RL Burnside, Junior Kimbrough, and their descendants.
**Key Artists:** R.L. Burnside, Junior Kimbrough, T-Model Ford, Cedric Burnside, Robert Finley, Big Jack Johnson
**Sample Tracks:** Shake 'Em On Down (R.L. Burnside), Meet Me in the City (Junior Kimbrough), Rollin' and Tumblin' (R.L. Burnside), Burned My Clothes (T-Model Ford)

### Playlist 12: Piedmont Blues & Ragtime
**Concept:** The East Coast cousin — finger-picked, ragtime-influenced blues from the Carolinas and Virginia. A sound distinct from but related to the Delta.
**Key Artists:** Blind Blake, Blind Boy Fuller, Gary Davis, Mississippi John Hurt, John Jackson, Elizabeth Cotten
**Sample Tracks:** West Coast Blues (Blind Blake), Step It Up and Go (Blind Boy Fuller), Freight Train (Elizabeth Cotten), Cocaine Blues (Gary Davis)

### Playlist 13: Memphis Soul & R&B
**Concept:** The upper anchor of the Blues Trail — Memphis-specific soul, R&B, and soul-blues from Stax, Hi Records, and beyond.
**Key Artists:** Otis Redding, Al Green, Ann Peebles, William Bell, Sam & Dave, Booker T. & the MGs, Isaac Hayes, Don Bryant
**Sample Tracks:** (Sittin' On) The Dock of the Bay (Otis Redding), Take Me to the River (Al Green), I Can't Stand the Rain (Ann Peebles), Green Onions (Booker T. & the MGs)
