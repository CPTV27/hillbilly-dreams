## The Ritz Theatre -- 127 N Commerce Street

### Status: NOT CURRENTLY FOR SALE -- Complex Ownership Situation

- **Built:** 1935 (opened December 1935 as a motion picture theater)
- **Style:** Art Deco facade with iconic projecting trapezoidal marquee
- **Size:** Approximately 6,385 sq ft first story (entrance loggia, lobby, theater with stage, balcony, upper concession area, projection room)
- **Condition:** Roof collapsed c. 2000. Front facade and marquee restored by Historic Natchez Foundation (spent $130K+). Interior remains gutted.

### Ownership History:
1. Ceased operating as theater ~50 years ago, used for storage
2. Roof collapsed ~2000
3. December 2002: David Paradise and Burk Baker purchased it and donated to Historic Natchez Foundation
4. HNF transferred to Franklin Commerce LLC (managing partners: Steve Campo and Mamie Henry)
5. 2021: Plans announced to convert interior to a food market for startup businesses
6. 2024-2026: No recent public updates on progress

### Assessment:
The Ritz is one of the most iconic buildings in downtown Natchez and would be extraordinary as a performance venue + recording studio + gallery. The Art Deco theater layout, stage, and balcony are purpose-built for performance. However:
- It's not for sale -- owned by Franklin Commerce LLC
- The roof is gone / rebuilt status unclear
- Interior is gutted
- Restoration would be very expensive (likely $500K-$1M+)
- The 2021 food market plan may or may not be active

**Recommendation:** Worth a conversation with Steve Campo and Mamie Henry at Franklin Commerce LLC, or with the Historic Natchez Foundation, to understand current plans. If their food market concept stalled, they might welcome a performing arts / recording studio proposal. The HNF has historically been willing to transfer the building to parties with viable plans.

## Natchez Commercial Real Estate Market Overview

### Pricing
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average commercial sale price/sq ft | ~$57-$68 (industrial/retail) |
| Average retail lease rate | ~$8/sq ft/year |
| Average office lease rate | ~$17/sq ft/year |
| Warehouse sale price range | $165,000 - $765,000 |
| Warehouse average sale price | ~$321,687 |

### Market Character
- Small market with limited inventory (~21 commercial listings at any time)
- Properties move slowly but bargains exist
- Strong historic building stock -- most downtown buildings are 1880s-1930s
- Historic tax credits are extremely favorable:
  - **Federal:** 20% credit on qualified rehab of income-producing historic structures
  - **State of Mississippi:** 25% credit on rehab of historic structures
  - **Combined:** 45% tax credit on rehabilitation costs
  - **Plus:** If state credit exceeds tax liability, 75% of excess is refundable as cash
  - **Local:** City of Natchez offers 7-year tax abatement on renovations over $20K in historic districts
- Natchez currently has more historic renovations than any other city in Mississippi
