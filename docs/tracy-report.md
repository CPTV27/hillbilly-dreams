## The Bigger Opportunities

### CivicX — Municipal Technology
**What it is:** A city hall front door — website, 311, payments, records search — built for cities under 50,000 people.
**Price:** $800-1,500/month ($9,600-18,000/year)

Tyler Technologies charges $25,000-75,000/year for this. CivicPlus serves 10,000+ municipalities. We come in at a fraction of the price, deploy in 90 days, and the AI actually works.

**Funding:** ARPA money, CDBG grants, municipal general funds. Mississippi still has hundreds of millions in unspent ARPA allocations, though the obligation deadline has passed — municipalities that already committed funds for technology are the target.

**Top targets:** Natchez, Vicksburg, Clarksdale, Greenville, Laurel.

### MB Learn — K-12 Education
**What it is:** Adaptive AI literacy curriculum for school districts.
**Price:** $25,000+/year per district

Students learn by using AI, not reading about it. Teachers learn alongside students. Mississippi already has 1:1 devices statewide from the $200M Mississippi Connects program — the hardware is in place, the software isn't.

**Funding:** Title IV-A ($17.4M/year for MS K-12 technology), Title I, Title II-A. The July 2025 USDE letter explicitly supports AI tool procurement under these programs. Small districts (most of Mississippi's 144 districts) have maximum flexibility in how they spend Title IV-A money.

**Five pilot districts identified:** Cleveland, Vicksburg-Warren, Clarksdale, Sunflower County, Yazoo City — all with stable superintendents.

**Deadline:** Mississippi's ESSA Consolidated Application window is summer 2026. Districts need to include MB Learn in their FY27 budgets by then.

### Community Enrichment Program
**What it is:** Free MBT access for qualifying people, funded by municipalities and tourism boards as workforce development.

| Track | Who Gets It | What They Get Free |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Artists/Musicians | Performers in the corridor | Up to $99/mo (The Engine) |
| Small Businesses | Main Street businesses learning the tools | Up to $49/mo (The Works) |
| Community/Individuals | Anyone building skills | Up to $20/mo (The Listing) |

People sign up, complete interactive learning modules, earn credits, and unlock platform features. By the time they finish, they either become paying customers or they're creating value in the ecosystem (performing, creating content, running a business).

**The pitch to municipalities:** "We're already bringing artists and audiences to your town. Add a community enrichment layer — free AI business tools for qualifying residents, delivered through interactive learning. Your residents learn modern business tools and graduate into self-sustaining businesses."

Two funding streams for one program: tourism boards fund it because entertainment drives visitors, and municipal economic development funds it because the enrichment program builds local businesses.

## How It All Connects

This is the part that makes everything worth more together than apart.

**One artist booking triggers seven revenue events:**
1. They play the Inn's stage (ticket + bar revenue)
2. The show gets recorded for Radio (content)
3. Magazine writes the city guide for the tour stop (sponsored content)
4. The artist goes on Rise Up's roster (touring revenue)
5. Their catalog lives on Records (streaming + sync)
6. The touring route adds the venue (network growth)
7. The kiosk at the front desk sells the ticket (direct sales)

**The flywheel:** Shows generate audience in the room → Bar monetizes that audience → Chase's daytime sales monetize the audience digitally through MBT → MBT revenue funds more shows → more shows generate more audience.

**Content compounds.** Nothing is disposable. Every photo becomes a social post, a magazine feature, a stock image, and a website asset. Every show becomes a radio episode, a video clip, and a marketing proof point. Every business that joins the Directory adds data that makes the AI smarter for every other business.
