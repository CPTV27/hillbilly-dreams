## 1. The Sanity Studio (Your Main Tool)

Think of the Sanity Studio as Microsoft Word, but for all the Big Muddy websites. It’s a simple, organized place where you can write articles, change photos, update pricing, and add events. Everything you do here can be published directly to the live sites.

### How to Log In

1.  Go to `https://bigmuddytouring.com/studio` in your web browser.
2.  Click the "Login with Google" button.
3.  Select the email account that you were invited with. You'll be taken directly into the Studio.

### The Left Sidebar: Your Content Library

The sidebar on the left organizes all the different types of content you can manage. Here’s what they mean:

*   **Touring Page:** Content for the main `bigmuddytouring.com` pages (e.g., the homepage hero).
*   **Article:** A single story for `bigmuddymagazine.com`. This is what you'll use most often.
*   **Location:** An address and details for a physical place, like the Inn or a partner venue.
*   **Event:** A special, one-off event (not a regular Blues Room show).
*   **Content Template:** Reusable blocks of text or content. (Advanced, ask Chase if needed).
*   **Podcast Episode:** For future use with Big Muddy Radio.
*   **Magazine Issue:** A way to group articles into a monthly "issue."
*   **Show — Blues Room:** A single concert happening at the Blues Room.
*   **Staff/Team Member:** Bios and photos for the team page.
*   **Partner Artist:** Info about artists we work with.
*   **Press Release:** Official announcements.
*   **FAQ Entry:** A single question-and-answer pair for the FAQ page.
*   **Page Content:** The main content for static pages like `/pricing`, `/private-events`, etc.

### Save vs. Publish

This is a key concept.

*   **Saving (Cmd-S or Ctrl-S):** When you save a document, you are creating a **draft**. This draft is visible only to you and the team inside the Sanity Studio. It is **not live** on the public website. This is perfect for work-in-progress.
*   **Publishing (The Green Button):** When you click the green **Publish** button, you are taking your saved changes and making them **live** on the public website. The world can now see them.

**Your workflow should always be: Edit → Save → Review → Publish.**

## 2. Editing an Article — Step by Step

Whether you're fixing a typo or adding a brand new story, the process is the same.

1.  **Log in** to the Sanity Studio at `https://bigmuddytouring.com/studio`.
2.  **Find your article.** In the left sidebar, click on **Article**. You'll see a list of all existing articles. You can use the search bar at the top to find one by its title.
    *   To create a new one, click the pencil icon next to **Article** and select **Article**.
3.  **Edit the main fields.**
    *   `Title`: The headline of the article.
    *   `Slug`: This is the web address for the article (e.g., a slug of `my-first-story` becomes `bigmuddymagazine.com/articles/my-first-story`). This should be auto-generated from the title, but you can edit it. Use all lowercase and hyphens instead of spaces.
    *   `Author`: Select the writer from the dropdown list.
    *   `Category`, `City`: Tag the article so readers can find it.
    *   `Hero Image`: The big picture at the top of the article. Click **Select** or **Replace** to open the Photo Library (see Section 4).
    *   `Excerpt`: A short, one-to-two-sentence summary that appears on the magazine homepage.
    *   `Read Time`: Automatically calculated, but you can override it if you want.
4.  **Write in the Body Editor.** This area works like a simple word processor.
    *   **Paragraphs:** Just type.
    *   **Headings:** Highlight text and choose a heading style (H2, H3) from the dropdown.
    *   **Pull Quotes:** Use the "Blockquote" style for pull quotes.
    *   **Photo Galleries:** Click the `+` button that appears on a new line and choose **Image Gallery** to add multiple photos in a grid.
5.  **Insert a Photo from the Library.**
    *   On any image field, click **Select**.
    *   The **Big Muddy Photo Library** will pop up.
    *   Use the filters at the top to narrow down by `Region`, `City`, `Shoot`, or `Category`.
    *   Click on the photo you want. It will be automatically inserted into your article.
6.  **Save your draft.** Press `Cmd-S` (on Mac) or `Ctrl-S` (on Windows). Your work is now saved but not yet live.
7.  **Publish your changes.** When you are happy with your edits, click the green **Publish** button at the bottom right.
8.  **Verify your work.** Open a new browser tab and go to `https://bigmuddymagazine.com`. It can take up to 60 seconds for your change to appear live. This delay is called "revalidation" — it's the time the system takes to rebuild the page with your new content.

## 3. Editing Page Heroes and Text

Need to change the headline on the Private Events page or update the pricing text? This is where you do it.

1.  **Log in** to the Sanity Studio.
2.  In the left sidebar, click on **Page Content**. This is a special content type for the main pages of the site.
3.  **Find the page you want to edit.** The documents are named by their `slug` (the part of the URL that identifies the page). For example, to edit `bigmuddytouring.com/pricing`, you would look for the document named `pricing`.
4.  **Click to open it.** You will see fields like:
    *   `Hero Eyebrow`: The small text above the main headline.
    *   `Hero Headline`: The big, main headline.
    *   `Hero Sub`: The descriptive text below the headline.
    *   `Footer Note`: Any special text that needs to appear at the bottom of that page.
5.  **Make your edits** directly in these fields.
6.  Click the green **Publish** button.
7.  **Verify your change.** Go to the public URL (e.g., `https://bigmuddytouring.com/pricing`) to see your update live within 60 seconds.

## 6. The AI Wizard for Article Drafts (`/admin/create`)

When you have an idea for an article but need help getting started, the AI Wizard is your new best friend. It creates a solid first draft that you can then polish in the Sanity Studio.

1.  **Go to the AI Wizard:** `https://bigmuddytouring.com/admin/create`.
2.  **Pick the Content Type.** Tell the wizard what you're making. Usually, this will be **Magazine Article**. Other options include `Social Post` or `Listing Description`.
3.  **Pick the Brand.** This helps the AI adopt the right voice. Choose from `Magazine`, `Inn`, `Touring`, `Records`, etc.
4.  **Provide a Brief.** This is the most important step. Be clear and concise. Tell the AI:
    *   What the article is about.
    *   Who the intended audience is.
    *   Any key facts, names, dates, or quotes to include.
    *   *Example Brief:* "An article for the magazine about the new spring menu at the Inn. Focus on Amy's farm-to-table philosophy. Mention the new strawberry-rhubarb pie, the ramp biscuits, and the partnership with Miller Family Farms for produce. The tone should be warm and inviting."
5.  **Generate, Review, and Send.**
    *   Click **Generate**. The AI will write a draft in a few moments.
    *   Read the draft. It won't be perfect, but it should be a great start. You can make quick edits right there.
    *   When you're ready, click **Send to Sanity as a Draft**.
6.  **Finish in the Studio.** The article will now appear as a new, unpublished draft in the **Article** section of the Sanity Studio. Open it there to add photos, refine the text, and hit **Publish** when it's perfect.

## 7. Handling Private Event Inquiries

This workflow turns a website inquiry into a confirmed booking.

1.  **Inquiry Arrives:** A potential client fills out the form on the `/private-events` page.
2.  **Email Notification:** Tracy immediately receives an email with the details of the inquiry.
3.  **Appears in Sanity:** The inquiry automatically shows up in the Sanity Studio under a new section called **QuoteRequest**.
4.  **Review and Quote:** Tracy reviews the request in Sanity. She then generates a quote (including price, deposit amount, and terms) and updates the status of the `QuoteRequest` to `Quote Sent`.
5.  **Customer Pays Deposit:** The customer receives the quote and a link to pay the deposit securely via Stripe.
6.  **Confirmation:** Once the deposit is paid, the system automatically updates the `QuoteRequest` status to `Confirmed`.
7.  **Booking Created:** A new booking row is automatically created and will appear on the internal events calendar at `/admin/calendar`.

## 8. Adding a Blues Room Show

Here’s how to get a new show on the calendar and ready for ticket sales.

1.  **Log in** to the Sanity Studio.
2.  In the left sidebar, find **Show — Blues Room**. Click the pencil icon next to it and select **New**.
3.  **Fill in the Show Details:**
    *   `Artist`: The name of the performer.
    *   `Date/Time`: The exact date and start time of the show.
    *   `Ticket Price`: The price per ticket (enter numbers only, e.g., `25`).
    *   `Capacity`: The number of tickets available for sale.
    *   `Notes`: Any extra info, like "21+ Only" or "Opener at 8pm".
4.  **Publish the Show.** Click the green **Publish** button.
5.  **It's Live!** The show will immediately appear on the `/shows` page of the website. The "Buy Tickets" button will be active, and customers can begin purchasing tickets through our secure Stripe Checkout.
6.  **Day of Show:** Amy runs the bar, the set list is on the wall, and the door person uses the scanner app to check tickets.

## 10. Things You Should Not Do (For Now)

To keep the ecosystem running smoothly and securely, please avoid the following actions. These are tasks for the technical team.

*   **Don't push code changes.** The code is the engine of the websites. Making changes here requires a developer (Chase or Cos).
*   **Don't change DNS or environment variables.** These are highly sensitive settings that control domain names and how the servers run. Changes here can take the entire site offline. This is for Chase only.
*   **Don't delete content in Sanity unless you are 100% sure.** If you want to unpublish an article, it's safer to "archive" it. To do this, open the article, click the three dots in the top right, and choose **Unpublish**. This removes it from the live site but keeps it in Sanity in case you need it later.
*   **Don't share API keys or passwords.** If a new contractor or partner needs access to a system, please route the request through Chase. He will create secure, limited-access credentials for them.

## 15. Glossary of Terms

A quick reference for some of the terms you might see.

*   **API (Application Programming Interface):** A way for different computer programs to talk to each other. Our Admin panel uses an API to get data from Stripe.
*   **CMS (Content Management System):** A tool for creating and managing digital content. **Sanity Studio** is our CMS.
*   **Cron Job:** A scheduled, automated task that runs at a specific time. The nightly job that syncs photo tags is a cron job.
*   **GCS (Google Cloud Storage):** A service from Google where we store all of our files, like the photos in the Big Muddy Library.
*   **IDOR (Insecure Direct Object Reference):** A type of security vulnerability. We mention it only because our systems are built to prevent it, ensuring users can only see their own data.
*   **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):** The predictable revenue a business can expect to receive every month. This is a key metric for our subscription business.
*   **Pro Forma:** A financial projection or forecast.
*   **Revalidate:** The process of rebuilding a web page to show new content. This is why it can take up to 60 seconds to see your changes live.
*   **Sanity:** The name of the company that makes our CMS, the Sanity Studio.
*   **Stripe:** Our payment processor. They handle all credit card transactions, subscriptions, and billing securely.
*   **Vercel:** The hosting platform where our websites live. It's responsible for making sure the sites are fast and always online.
*   **Webhook:** An automated message sent from one app to another when something happens. For example, when a customer pays a deposit, Stripe sends a webhook to our system to confirm the booking.
