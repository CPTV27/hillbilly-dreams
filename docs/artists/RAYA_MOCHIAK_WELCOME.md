# Welcome to Big Muddy — Raya Mochiak

*Artist-in-Residence, The Big Muddy Inn, Natchez, Mississippi*

---

## What This Is

You're the first. The first artist-in-residence at the Big Muddy Inn. What that means is simple: you come here, you make music, we handle everything else.

The Inn is at 411 North Congress Street in Natchez, Mississippi — a town of 14,000 people sitting on a 200-foot bluff above the Mississippi River. The Blues Room is downstairs — 50 seats, no barrier between you and the audience, a Steinway in the corner, and whatever backline you need.

You're not a guest. You're the reason people come.

## The Brands (What You Need to Know)

### Big Muddy Touring
We book bands on a circuit from Memphis to New Orleans — 13 cities, 735 venues. When you're here, you're not just playing Natchez. You have access to the whole corridor. Clarksdale (Ground Zero Blues Club — Morgan Freeman's place), Jackson, Vicksburg, Oxford, Tupelo, Baton Rouge, New Orleans. One call and you're on a stage anywhere between Memphis and the Gulf.

### Big Muddy Records
We record everything. The Blues Room has a live recording setup. The studio upstairs has the real gear — and a talented gay Frenchman who knows how to use it. 80% of royalties go to you. 15% to the label. 5% platform. That's not a negotiation — that's the deal for everyone.

### Big Muddy Radio
We have a streaming radio station. Your music gets first play. Not "we'll add it to a playlist" — first play, on our station, to our audience. Station IDs, DJ intros, the whole thing. When you record something in the Blues Room on Tuesday, it's on the radio by Wednesday.

### Big Muddy Entertainment
Shows, events, showcases. We produce everything — multi-camera video, live streaming, social clips cut the same night. You play a show, we turn it into a concert film, a radio session, a magazine feature, and 15 social media clips. One night of music becomes a month of content.

### Big Muddy Magazine
We write about you. Not a press release — a real editorial feature with real photography. The magazine covers the entire corridor and your story becomes part of the Natchez story.

## The House Band Vision

Think the Swampers. Think Booker T. & the MG's at Stax. Think the Wrecking Crew.

We're building a house band — a rotating cast of musicians who live in or come through Natchez and can back up anyone. You walk in with songs, we provide the band. Or you bring your people and our players sit in. Or it's all you.

The house band plays the Blues Room on regular nights. When a touring artist comes through, they fold in. When we record, they're the session players. When we tour the corridor, they're the road band.

You're the first artist-in-residence. That means you help shape what this house band becomes. Who plays well together. What the sound is. How the room feels on a Friday night when 50 people are packed in and the bass is rattling the glasses.

## The Touring Network

The corridor is pre-built. Venues are booked. Hotels are mapped (we know which ones have parking for the van and don't mind musicians checking in at 2am). Restaurants are flagged (who's open late, who feeds bands, who has a back room).

13 cities. 735 venues in our database. Real addresses, real contacts, real ratings.

You want to play Ground Zero in Clarksdale on a Thursday, the Blues Room in Natchez on Friday, and Under the Hill Saloon on Saturday? We make the call. The van is ready. The rooms are booked. The shows are promoted through our magazine, radio, and social channels before you even load in.

## The Recording Setup

The Blues Room captures everything live — multi-track recording synced to multi-camera video. The studio upstairs has:

- Sony cameras on DJI gimbals (cinema-quality on wireless rigs)
- Sennheiser wireless mics (6 channels)
- Aputure lighting (looks like a real session, not a YouTube video)
- The mixer is controlled from an iPad — Amy runs sound during shows

The gear is one-of-a-kind because the combination is one-of-a-kind. It's not about having the most expensive anything. It's about having exactly the right pieces connected by someone who knows what they're doing.
