# Big Muddy Touring — Ecosystem Overview

**Drafted by Chase Pierson · May 2026**
**Status:** Generic overview of the ecosystem — shareable with anyone. Not a pitch deck. Not a partnership proposal.

## What this is

Big Muddy Touring is an entertainment, hospitality, and media ecosystem on the Mississippi corridor, anchored in Natchez. We run an inn, a touring operation, a record label, a 24/7 radio station, a magazine, a directory, and three creative production studios — under one umbrella brand, one shared platform, one website.

Three equity partners run it. Shared infrastructure and shared audiences mean each business costs less and earns more than it would alone.

---

## The brands

Everything below serves from `bigmuddytouring.com`. Sub-brand domains (`bigmuddyradio.com`, `bigmuddyrecordlabel.com`, etc.) all redirect to it.

**Big Muddy Inn** *(Natchez)*
Home base. Six guest rooms, the Blues Room as a listening stage, regular live music, a bar. Operated by Tracy Alderson-Allen and Amy Allen. Every circuit run starts and ends here.

**Big Muddy Touring**
Booking, transport, season programming. Memphis to New Orleans down Highway 61, west to Ferriday. Sprinter van today; a 40-foot Prevost sleeper bus on the roadmap.

**Big Muddy Records** *(Melody Vault platform · `bigmuddytouring.com/records`)*
The label. Non-exclusive promotional signings. Artists keep their masters. We handle audience identification, targeting, and release marketing. Current roster: Mechanical Bull (20-year catalog), Amy Allen's three projects, plus a writers' catalog.

**Big Muddy Radio**
24/7 stream + podcast + curated playlists. Amplifies the touring calendar and the artist roster.

**Big Muddy Magazine**
Editorial pipeline. Long-form features, city guides, photo essays, scene reporting.

**Big Muddy Directory** *(`bigmuddytouring.com/circuit/venues`)*
A working scout map of the corridor — 67 rooms across 15 towns, organized by capacity, town, and routing distance from Natchez.

## How the ecosystem feeds itself

Big Muddy Touring isn't a booking agency. It's a working ecosystem where every piece earns its keep by making the next piece easier.

The **Inn fills** because radio sends listeners, the magazine sends readers, and the touring calendar sends people who want to see the show and sleep upstairs.

The **radio has shows** because touring books bands into the Blues Room and tracks every set into the archive — and because Amy, Mechanical Bull, and the catalog of writers feed the station originals nobody else has.

**Touring books artists** who actually want to come here, because the label promotes them on Big Muddy Records, the magazine writes about them, the radio plays them, and the inn puts them up. The deal makes more sense than a one-night cold call.

The **label has artists** because the touring engine gets them on stages and the platform handles the audience work. Artists keep their masters; we handle the marketing nobody else wants to do. The catalog gets deeper every year.

The **magazine has stories** because every show, every artist, every meal at the inn, every drive between two venues is a story. The Mississippi corridor is the content; we just write it down.

The **directory is a moat** because the venues and artists and writers we've already worked with are search-indexed and easy to find. Anyone planning a tour finds us before they find their alternative.

The **production studios** — Chase Pierson Photography, Studio C, Tuthill — shoot every release, every show, every cover. Each gig fills three pipelines at once: the artist's, the studio's, and ours.

*The shared spine costs less than building any one of these pieces alone, and every win in any single business shows up on the others' ledgers within a quarter.*

## The platform underneath

Everything Big Muddy runs on is built on **Measurably Better Things** — a working operating system for a multi-brand media company. Nine modules, all live or near-live, shared across every brand:

| Module | What it does |
|---|---|
| Records | Audience identification, targeting, release marketing — for any artist signed under Big Muddy Records. |
| Radio | 24/7 stream, podcast, curated playlists. |
| Magazine | Editorial pipeline — long-form, city guides, photo essays. |
| Touring & Events | Booking, ticketing, the show-to-content pipeline. |
| Directory | The corridor venue and artist database. |
| Commerce | Storefront and multi-party payment splits, so an artist, a venue, and a promoter can share a P&L cleanly. |
| Broadcasting | The live production stack — multi-stream a show to radio, web, and social simultaneously. |
| AI Content Pipeline | Auto-generated social, spotlights, SEO support. Hours, not weeks. |
| Analytics | Monthly report cards — radio listens, social reach, ticket sell-through. |

A typical artist doesn't use all nine. Some use four. Some use one. The modules are there, paid for, running, and don't cost an artist or a partner anything extra to plug into.
