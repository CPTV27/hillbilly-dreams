# Listing Description Content-Type Template Specification

This document outlines the content-template specification for the "Listing Description" content type within the MBT Content Creation Wizard. This template is designed to generate compelling, factual, and compliant real estate listing descriptions for Tuthill Design's realtor clients, with Vicki Wolpert serving as the prototype client. The overarching goal is to produce descriptions that resonate with homebuyers, provide clear value, and uphold the highest standards of professionalism and compliance.

---

## 1. Audience Profile

The primary audience for these listing descriptions consists of homebuyers actively searching for properties on various digital platforms, including Multiple Listing Services (MLS), Zillow, Redfin, Trulia, and individual brokerage websites. These individuals are typically in various stages of the home-buying journey, from initial browsing to serious consideration. They are often comparing multiple properties simultaneously, requiring descriptions to be highly informative, accurate, and engaging without resorting to hyperbole. This audience seeks concrete details about the property's features, condition, layout, and surrounding environment. They prioritize practical information that helps them visualize living in the home and assess its suitability for their lifestyle and needs. Descriptions must be concise enough to capture attention quickly, yet detailed enough to provide a substantive overview, distinguishing the property effectively in a competitive market. They are discerning readers who value transparency and factual accuracy over subjective, vague claims.

---

## 2. Voice Rules

The content generated for listing descriptions must strictly adhere to the Tuthill voice, which is defined by three core pillars: **professional, value-clear, and realtor-respectful.**

*   **Professional:** Descriptions must maintain a polished, sophisticated tone. This means employing precise, grammatically correct language, avoiding slang, jargon (unless industry-standard and universally understood by homebuyers), and overly casual phrasing. The writing should be articulate and articulate, reflecting the high-stakes nature of real estate transactions and the professionalism of our realtor clients. It should project confidence and expertise without being arrogant or overly formal.
*   **Value-Clear:** Every statement should convey tangible value or highlight a specific benefit derived from a property feature. Instead of generic adjectives, the descriptions must employ **sensory specifics**. For example, rather than "a nice kitchen," the text should describe "a chef’s kitchen featuring Sub-Zero refrigeration, a six-burner Wolf range, and custom walnut cabinetry," or "a bright living room with original oak flooring and abundant natural light from south-facing windows." This approach helps homebuyers visualize and appreciate the quality and utility of the property's elements. The focus is on *what* the feature offers and *how* it enhances the living experience, rather than simply stating its existence.
*   **Realtor-Respectful:** The content is designed to support the realtor's sales process, not replace it. It should provide a strong foundation for their discussions with clients, offering compelling yet factual narratives. The language should implicitly acknowledge the realtor as the primary expert and facilitator, avoiding any phrasing that might be perceived as overstepping or undermining their role. The descriptions should be tools for the realtor, helping them attract qualified buyers and streamline their efforts.
*   **Sensory Specifics:** As noted above, this is paramount. Engage the senses (sight, touch, sound, smell, even taste if applicable to a kitchen/garden). Use descriptive nouns and strong verbs. Instead of "big yard," use "a sprawling half-acre lot ideal for gardening" or "a meticulously landscaped backyard with mature oak trees."
*   **Room-by-Room Flow:** The description should guide the reader through the property in a logical, intuitive manner, mirroring a physical walk-through. Typically, this starts with the main living areas (entryway, living room, dining room, kitchen), progresses to bedrooms and bathrooms, and concludes with exterior features, amenities, and property grounds. This structured approach helps the buyer mentally map the space.
*   **No Fluff, No Real-Estate Cliches:** Every word must earn its place. Avoid redundant phrases, empty adjectives, and hyperbole that doesn't add concrete information. Specifically, this means strictly adhering to the "Forbidden Phrases" list. These overused terms diminish credibility and fail to provide meaningful insight to the discerning homebuyer. The aim is fresh, authentic, and impactful language.

## 3. Required Sections

Each listing description generated by the wizard must include the following distinct sections, ensuring a comprehensive and structured presentation of the property. The depth and detail of each section will vary based on the chosen length target (short, standard, luxury).

1.  **Opening Line (Concrete Property Highlight):**
    *   **Purpose:** To immediately capture the reader's attention and establish a compelling, factual highlight of the property.
    *   **Content:** This should be a concise, impactful statement focusing on a unique, desirable, or defining feature of the home. It must be concrete and verifiable, drawing directly from the MLS data or key selling points. Avoid generic statements; instead, pinpoint a specific architectural detail, a significant recent renovation, a premium material, or an exceptional natural attribute.
    *   **Example:** "Discover this meticulously updated 1950s ranch, featuring original hardwood floors and a newly renovated chef's kitchen with quartz countertops and bespoke cabinetry."

2.  **Neighborhood Paragraph:**
    *   **Purpose:** To provide context about the property's immediate surroundings and broader community.
    *   **Content:** This paragraph should describe the *objective characteristics* of the neighborhood or community. Focus on elements like the streetscape (e.g., "tree-lined streets," "established residential area"), architectural style prevalence, general ambiance ("vibrant urban district," "tranquil suburban enclave"), and proximity to *publicly accessible* amenities (e.g., "walkable to local cafes and boutique shops," "adjacent to a community park with walking trails," "convenient access to major transportation routes").
    *   **Crucial Compliance Note:** This section is highly sensitive to Fair Housing Act regulations. **Absolutely no steering language is permitted.** Descriptions must avoid any mention of demographics, suitability for specific types of families or individuals, subjective judgments on safety, or any implication of protected characteristics. Focus purely on the physical and infrastructural attributes of the area.

3.  **Room Walk-Through:**
    *   **Purpose:** To guide the reader through the interior spaces of the home, providing sensory and factual details.
    *   **Content:** This is the core descriptive section. It should detail each significant room or area of the house in a logical flow (e.g., entry, living, dining, kitchen, bedrooms, bathrooms, lower level/attic, utility spaces). For each area, describe key features, finishes, materials, and any recent upgrades. Use sensory specifics: "sun-drenched living room with panoramic city views," "primary suite featuring a spa-like ensuite bath with a freestanding soaking tub and dual vanities," "kitchen equipped with professional-grade stainless steel appliances and a large center island with seating." Integrate factual data like square footage (when relevant for specific rooms), number of bedrooms/bathrooms, and year of renovation if applicable.

4.  **Lifestyle Paragraph (Who would love this house):**
    *   **Purpose:** To articulate the *type of lifestyle* the property naturally supports, connecting its features to potential resident experiences.
    *   **Content:** This paragraph should describe the *benefits* of the home's features in terms of how someone might live there, without explicitly targeting specific demographics. Frame it around the property's inherent qualities and what they offer.
    *   **Example (compliant):** Instead of "perfect for a growing family," use "Ideal for those seeking a tranquil retreat with ample outdoor space for recreation and entertaining," or "Well-suited for urban professionals desiring walkability to cultural venues and modern amenities within a secure building." Focus on the *characteristics of the home* that would appeal to certain *preferences* (e.g., "an entertainer's dream," "a gardener's paradise," "a sanctuary for remote work," "designed for seamless indoor-outdoor living"). This section should highlight the unique opportunities the home presents.

5.  **Closing Line (Move-in Window / Showings):**
    *   **Purpose:** To conclude the description with practical information or a clear call to action regarding property access.
    *   **Content:** This line should provide logistical details. This might include information about the property's availability ("Available for showings starting [Date] by appointment"), instructions for scheduling a tour ("Connect with your agent to schedule a private tour"), or a direct, professional invitation to learn more ("Explore the possibilities this exceptional residence offers"). It should be concise and direct, guiding the next steps for interested buyers.

## 4. Length Targets

The wizard will generate descriptions tailored to different listing tiers, primarily determined by property value, market segment, and realtor preference. The length targets are designed to optimize impact and information density for each tier.

*   **Short (Under 100 words):**
    *   **Application:** Ideal for starter homes, properties with limited features, or listings where brevity is paramount (e.g., initial syndication to platforms with strict character limits).
    *   **Focus:** This length will prioritize 2-3 absolute key highlights of the property, a very brief mention of the neighborhood character, and the closing line. The room walk-through will be highly condensed or might combine multiple areas.
    *   **Structure:** Opening line, 1-2 sentences on key features/updates, 1 sentence on neighborhood, closing line.

*   **Standard (200-300 words):**
    *   **Application:** The most common length, suitable for the majority of mid-market properties.
    *   **Focus:** Provides a comprehensive overview, covering all required sections with sufficient detail to inform and engage. It allows for a more detailed room-by-room walk-through and a richer description of the lifestyle potential.
    *   **Structure:** All required sections are present and elaborated upon.

*   **Long-Form Luxury (400-600 words):**
    *   **Application:** Reserved for high-end, luxury, custom-built, or historically significant properties where extensive detail, bespoke features, and craftsmanship warrant a more expansive narrative.
    *   **Focus:** This length allows for deep dives into architectural nuances, premium materials, high-end appliances, custom finishes, unique property amenities (e.g., wine cellars, home theaters, smart home systems), and detailed landscaping. The language will be richer, more evocative, and emphasize the bespoke nature and elevated experience of the home.
    *   **Structure:** All required sections are significantly expanded, with detailed descriptions of individual rooms, architectural elements, and the surrounding grounds. More elaborate vocabulary and sophisticated sentence structures may be employed while maintaining clarity and professionalism.

The wizard will determine the appropriate length based on the 'Listing Tier' input provided by the user, or infer it from the property's value range and feature set if explicit tiering is not provided.
