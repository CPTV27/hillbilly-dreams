# Pitch Deck Section Content-Type Template Specification

This specification defines the generation parameters for a single section of a B2B pitch deck, tailored for various MBT Group brands (Tuthill, MBT, Big Muddy Touring) and diverse audiences. The AI wizard will generate one section at a time, based on user input for brand, audience, and section type.

---

## 1. Section Types & Required Elements per Section Type

Each pitch deck section is designed to be self-contained, impactful, and adhere to strict structural and voice guidelines.

### 1.1. 'Problem statement'

*   **Purpose:** Clearly articulate the core challenge or unmet need faced by the target audience.
*   **Opening:** A single, direct declarative sentence stating the primary problem.
*   **Content:** Quantify the problem's impact using data or relatable consequences. Directly connect the problem to the audience's specific pain points, highlighting lost opportunities, inefficiencies, or costs.
*   **Ending:** A specific, measurable consequence of the problem, a lost opportunity, or a quantifiable cost.
*   **Example Anchor:** "...resulting in an average annual loss of $25,000 per agent."

### 1.2. 'Our approach'

*   **Purpose:** Briefly describe the methodology, strategy, or unique process used to address the identified problem.
*   **Opening:** A single, direct declarative sentence introducing the core solution strategy.
*   **Content:** Focus on *how* the problem will be solved, emphasizing key steps, a unique framework, or core differentiators. Avoid jargon and explain the mechanism of action concisely.
*   **Ending:** A specific, identifiable process step, a key technology employed, or a core methodological differentiator.
*   **Example Anchor:** "...implemented through our proprietary 3-stage client integration protocol."

### 1.3. 'Specific deliverable / SKU'

*   **Purpose:** Detail the exact product, service package, or specific components being offered.
*   **Opening:** A single, direct declarative sentence naming the primary deliverable or package.
*   **Content:** List the concrete components, features, or quantifiable aspects of the deliverable. Clearly state what the client will *receive* or *gain access to*.
*   **Ending:** A specific count of items, a precise feature list, a concrete outcome tied to the deliverable, or a clear scope boundary.
*   **Example Anchor:** "...providing 5 custom reports and 2 dedicated training sessions per quarter."

### 1.4. 'Pricing'

*   **Purpose:** Clearly state the cost structure, what is included, and any relevant payment terms.
*   **Opening:** A single, direct declarative sentence stating the core pricing model or initial cost.
*   **Content:** Break down costs if applicable, specify payment frequency, or detail what is covered within the stated price. Emphasize transparency and value.
*   **Ending:** A specific price point, a payment frequency, a clear subscription term, or a deadline for a special offer.
*   **Example Anchor:** "...at a transparent rate of $750 per month for a 12-month contract."

### 1.5. 'Case study / proof'

*   **Purpose:** Provide verifiable evidence of past success with similar clients or projects.
*   **Opening:** A single, direct declarative sentence summarizing a key achievement or outcome from a past engagement.
*   **Content:** Briefly describe the client (if appropriate), their initial challenge, the specific solution provided, and the quantifiable results achieved. Focus on metrics.
*   **Ending:** A specific, measurable outcome, a percentage improvement, a date of achievement, or a concrete number of beneficiaries.
*   **Example Anchor:** "...leading to a 30% increase in community engagement within 6 months."

### 1.6. 'Next steps / CTA'

*   **Purpose:** Guide the audience on the immediate, actionable steps they should take.
*   **Opening:** A single, direct declarative sentence initiating the call to action.
*   **Content:** Provide clear, unambiguous instructions. Specify who to contact, how to initiate the next phase, and any relevant deadlines or information needed.
*   **Ending:** A specific contact detail (email, phone), a deadline for action, a date for the next meeting, or a clear deliverable from the client.
*   **Example Anchor:** "Please confirm your preferred demo slot by October 25th via email to [email protected]"

## 2. Audience Profiles

The content's emphasis and language will be dynamically adapted based on the target audience.

*   **Realtor:** Focus on concrete ROI, time saved, efficiency gains, competitive advantage, lead generation, and client satisfaction.
*   **Civic Partner:** Focus on community impact, local engagement, project scope clarity, citizen benefits, long-term sustainability, and resource optimization.
*   **Corporate Sponsor:** Focus on audience numbers, brand visibility, demographic alignment, measurable exposure, partnership value, media impressions, and market reach.
*   **Broker-Tier Client:** Focus on recurring revenue streams, scalability, agent retention, operational efficiency, market expansion, and competitive differentiation for their agents.

---

## 3. Voice Rules (Pitch Deck Specific)

*   **Declarative Opening:** Every section *must* open with a single, clear, declarative sentence.
*   **Quantitative/Specific Anchor:** Every section *must* end with a quantitative metric, a specific date, a precise deliverable count, or an explicit action point.
*   **No Abstractions Without Specifics:** Avoid all vague or abstract statements unless immediately followed by tangible, quantifiable, or concrete examples.
*   **Direct & Confident:** Maintain a confident, results-oriented tone.
*   **Action-Oriented:** Emphasize outcomes and benefits, not just features.

---

## 4. Forbidden Phrases

The following phrases are strictly forbidden to ensure direct, impactful, and jargon-free communication:

*   'value proposition'
*   'turnkey solution'
*   'best-in-class'
*   'industry-leading'
*   'unparalleled'
*   'cutting-edge'
*   'innovative approach'
*   'comprehensive solution'
*   'end-to-end' (when vague or undefined)
*   'mission-critical'
*   'world-class'
*   'next-generation'
