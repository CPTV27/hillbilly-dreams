## 1. Audience Profile

The primary audience for Big Muddy Radio episode descriptions consists of:

*   **Prospective Listeners (Human):** Individuals browsing podcast feeds, searching for new music, artists, or discussions. They are music-curious, often looking for authentic voices, deep dives, and a connection to the artists and scenes we cover. They make quick decisions based on the initial hook and overall tone. They appreciate clear, concise summaries that convey the episode's vibe and content without spoilers.
*   **Podcast Platform Algorithms (AI/Machine Learning):** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube Music, and others. These algorithms parse descriptions for keywords, artist names, genre tags, and contextual information to categorize episodes, recommend them to relevant users, and improve searchability. They prioritize clear entity recognition (artist names, album titles), genre indicators, and relevance to trending topics or user preferences. The first 200 characters are particularly critical for algorithmic parsing and user preview.

## 2. Voice Rules

The Big Muddy Radio voice for episode descriptions is distinct from our Magazine content, tailored for an audio-first experience:

*   **Music-Curious Tone:** Enthusiastic, exploratory, and open-minded about different sounds and genres. It invites listeners to discover rather than dictates what they should like.
*   **Host-on-Mic Feel:** Conversational, direct, and personable. Imagine the host speaking directly to the listener, setting the stage for the audio journey ahead. Use active voice and an engaging, slightly informal register.
*   **Opinionated Without Preachy:** Express a genuine passion for the music and artists, offering insights and perspectives without being dogmatic or exclusive. The opinions should feel informed and authentic, reflecting the Big Muddy ethos.
*   **No Industry-Trade Jargon:** Avoid terms specific to the music industry (e.g., "A&R," "sync licensing," "metadata optimization") unless absolutely necessary for context and immediately explained. Focus on language accessible to a broad music-loving audience.
*   **Music-First, Scene-First, Artist-First:** Descriptions should immediately immerse the reader in the sound, the cultural context, or the creator's world. Start with what makes the music compelling.

## 3. Required Sections

Each episode description must include the following sections, in order:

1.  **Opening Hook (1-2 sentences):**
    *   **Purpose:** Grab immediate attention and clearly state the episode's core appeal.
    *   **Content:** Lead with the most compelling aspect: a key artist, a unique sound, a provocative question, or the central theme. This is the "radio voice" equivalent of throwing to a track or a segment.
    *   **SEO:** Crucially, include primary artist names and/or key genre/scene keywords here for maximum algorithmic impact within the first 200 characters.

2.  **Guest/Topic Context (1 paragraph):**
    *   **Purpose:** Provide necessary background for the featured artist, guest, or central theme.
    *   **Content:**
        *   **For Artist-focused episodes:** Introduce the artist, their significance, recent releases, or their unique contribution to a scene. Mention their sound and influence.
        *   **For Thematic episodes:** Elaborate on the central concept, its relevance, and why it's a compelling listen now.
        *   **For Interview episodes:** Introduce the guest, their role, and the unique perspective they bring. Briefly mention their connection to Big Muddy Radio's interests.
    *   **Style:** Maintain the host-on-mic, music-curious voice.

3.  **Episode Arc (what gets covered, in order):**
    *   **Purpose:** Outline the flow of the episode, managing listener expectations and highlighting key segments.
    *   **Content:** Describe the journey the listener will take. This could include:
        *   Specific tracks played and discussed (for playlist episodes).
        *   Key discussion points or questions explored (for interview/thematic episodes).
        *   Any special segments or surprises.
    *   **Style:** Use descriptive language that builds anticipation without giving away every detail. Present it as a narrative progression.

4.  **Recommended Listening Pairings (related episodes, related artists):**
    *   **Purpose:** Encourage further engagement with Big Muddy content and expand listeners' musical horizons.
    *   **Content:**
        *   **Related Episodes:** Suggest 1-2 previous Big Muddy Radio episodes that share a similar artist, genre, theme, or vibe. Provide episode titles or key artists from those episodes.
        *   **Related Artists:** Suggest 1-2 other artists (not necessarily from Big Muddy's catalog, but relevant) that listeners who enjoy this episode might also appreciate. This demonstrates our deep musical knowledge.
    *   **Style:** Frame these as genuine recommendations from a knowledgeable host.

5.  **Call to Action (CTA):**
    *   **Purpose:** Drive engagement with Big Muddy Radio and its other platforms.
    *   **Content:**
        *   Encourage listeners to subscribe/follow Big Muddy Radio on their preferred podcast platform.
        *   Promote the corresponding Big Muddy TikTok Live session related to the episode, including the specific date/time (if applicable) and a direct link.
    *   **Style:** Clear, concise, and action-oriented.

## 4. Length Targets

The wizard must adhere to the following length guidelines based on the `episode_type` input:

*   **Short (Under 200 words):** For trailer, interlude, or very brief special announcement episodes. Focus on the hook and a concise overview.
*   **Standard (300-500 words):** For regular weekly episodes, including new music spotlights, artist features, or thematic discussions. This allows for full development of all required sections.
*   **Long-Form (600-900 words):** For special interview episodes, deep-dive documentaries, or multi-part series. These require more detailed context, a richer episode arc description, and potentially more extensive recommended listening.

## 5. Forbidden Phrases

To maintain a fresh, authentic voice and avoid clichés, the following phrases are strictly forbidden:

*   'check out this episode'
*   'don't miss'
*   'sit back and enjoy'
*   'today's special guest'
*   'all the latest'
*   'today's hottest'
*   'curated playlist' (overused – use 'our playlist,' 'this week's selection,' 'tracks we're spinning,' etc.)

## 7. SEO/Discovery Considerations

To maximize visibility and listener engagement:

*   **First 200 Characters Priority:** The initial 1-2 sentences (Opening Hook) are paramount. They appear in podcast app previews and are heavily scanned by algorithms. This space *must* contain the most critical information: primary artist names, key genres, and the core appeal of the episode.
*   **Artist Names Early and Often:** Include the main artist's full name and/or common alias early in the description, and naturally weave it in throughout the text. This helps algorithms categorize and recommend the episode.
*   **Genre/Scene Keywords:** Integrate relevant genre tags (e.g., "indie folk," "synth-pop," "blues rock revival") and scene descriptors (e.g., "the burgeoning Chicago jazz scene," "Nashville's alt-country circuit") organically within the narrative. Avoid keyword stuffing; ensure they flow naturally.
*   **Date Anchors:** For time-sensitive content (new releases, monthly playlists), explicitly mention the month/year or release date. This signals freshness to both listeners and algorithms.
*   **Structured Information:** The required sections (Guest/Topic Context, Episode Arc) provide a clear, logical structure that algorithms can easily parse for key entities and themes.
