## What This Is
A rewrite of Outsider Economics tailored to Measurably Better and the Deep South. Not the abstract theory book. The applied manifesto for what's actually being built in Natchez.

## What Changed From V1
V1 was about theory — Agorism, distributed networks, anti-fragility, circular economy. Smart frameworks for people who already think about systems. Useful for Twitter threads and podcast interviews. Not useful for a mayor in Vicksburg.

V2 is the same principles, different language, one region, actual proof.

## The Four Principles (Translated)

### 1. Sovereignty (was: Agorism / Counter-Economics)
Own your data. Own your tools. Own your story. Every MB deployment runs on your vault. When you cancel, you get a full export. Zero hostage data.

### 2. Density (was: Distributed Network Theory)
One platform runs the hotel, the scanning company, the tourism bureau, and the school district. They share an engine, not data. Every new node makes the network smarter. Trust networks are the distribution channel.

### 3. Compounding (was: Anti-Fragility)
Every dollar creates a permanent asset. A tourism photo shoot becomes a magazine article becomes a city guide that drives visitors for years. Nothing is disposable.

### 4. Local Velocity (was: Circular Economy)
When technology dollars stay in-region, they recirculate. A dollar that recirculates three times in-county creates three times the economic impact of one that leaves on day one.

## The Thesis (One Paragraph)
The Deep South has the talent, the land, the story, and the culture. What it hasn't had is the technology infrastructure to capture the value those things generate. Measurably Better is a regional technology and media provider built in Mississippi, for the South, by people who live here. We're not fixing what's broken. We're building what was never built.

## Tone Rules
- The South is NOT a victim. It's underleveraged. Frame everything as forward motion.
- No academic jargon. No Konkin, no Taleb, no Balaji name-drops unless explaining the lineage.
- Concrete over abstract. "Six rooms in Natchez" beats "a unique hospitality paradigm."
- "What happens when the economy works for the people who actually live here."
