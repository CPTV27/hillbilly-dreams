BIG MUDDY MICROMEDIA

Micromedia Deep Dive

The technical architecture, business model, and implementation plan for running an internet radio station and digital magazine as a micromedia business in Natchez, Mississippi.

Executive Summary

Big Muddy has a structural advantage no competitor in Natchez can match -- radio + magazine + directory + venue + inn + record label under one roof, powered by AI, running on open source tools. The Marfa TX comparison is the killer proof point: a town of 1,700 people became a nationally recognized cultural destination through the same formula Big Muddy is building in a town of 14,000.

Year 2: $162K revenue / $143K net

Biggest Cost Wildcard

SoundExchange royalties: $3K-$37K/year depending on listener count. Mitigate by programming local/owned content from Big Muddy Record artists.

SoundExchange Math

Rate

~$0.0028/performance (2024-2025 CRB rates). A "performance" = one song transmitted to one listener.

100 avg listeners

100 listeners x 15 songs/hr x 24/7 = ~$36,800/year

50 avg listeners

~$18,400/year

25 avg listeners

~$9,200/year

10 avg listeners

~$3,700/year. Minimum fee: ~$1,000/year per channel.

Reducing SoundExchange Costs

Play local artists

Artists who grant direct written permission reduce the performance count.

Big Muddy Record

Own label content = zero SoundExchange cost. Build a house label and sign local artists not registered with PROs.

Creative Commons

Free Music Archive, ccMixter, Jamendo. No per-performance cost.

More talk content

Interviews, talk shows, features. No per-performance cost for speech.

DMCA Compliance (Rules of Four)

Within any 3-hour window:

  • Max 3 songs from same album, max 2 consecutively
  • Max 4 songs by same artist, max 3 consecutively
  • No advance playlists published
  • Don't encourage copying/recording

FCC

  • Internet radio does NOT need FCC license
  • No obscenity rules, no equal-time, no EAS requirements
  • Copyright law (DMCA) is the primary regulatory framework

Other Legal

  • Privacy Policy required (CCPA applies to California visitors)
  • Terms of Service for stream and website
  • FTC disclosure rules for sponsored content
  • COPPA if content could attract children under 13
  • Mississippi consumer protection laws for advertising

Case Studies -- 23 Documented

Real-world models proving the Big Muddy thesis. Top 3 are the most directly relevant.

1. The Lot Radio

Brooklyn, NY

Glass-booth radio station + community gathering space. 100+ volunteer DJs provide 24/7 programming. Revenue: listener donations, merch, events.

Key Lesson: Physical space + volunteer programmers = sustainability.

2. Bitter Southerner

Atlanta, GA

Small team (~5-8), nationally relevant digital magazine about the progressive South. Membership ($5-10/mo) + e-commerce (Southern-made goods) + sponsored content.

Key Lesson: Small team can build national magazine from the South.

3. Marfa, Texas (Pop. 1,700)

Marfa, TX -- SMALLER than Natchez

Art (Chinati Foundation) + boutique hotels + Marfa Public Radio (KRTS) + events = internationally recognized cultural destination.

Key Lesson: If a desert town of 1,700 with no river, no antebellum architecture, and no music history can do it, Natchez with ALL those advantages has every reason to succeed.

8 Patterns From All Case Studies

  1. 1Diversify revenue across 4-5+ streams. No single source > 40%.
  2. 2Physical space is the secret weapon. Lot Radio's glass booth, Ace's lobby, 21c's museum.
  3. 3Nonprofit unlocks real money. Grants, CPB, foundation support.
  4. 4Volunteers are the workforce. WFMU: 200. BFF.fm: 60-80. Paid team handles ops.
  5. 5"Cultural institution" beats "media company." Changes how everyone perceives you.
  6. 6Annual tentpole events create spikes. Oxford American music issue. WFMU pledge drive.
  7. 7Archives compound. Every show, article, listing gains SEO value permanently.
  8. 8Marfa proves the math. Pop. 1,700 became a destination. Pop. 14,000 has every advantage.
