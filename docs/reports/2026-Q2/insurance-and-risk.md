## 3. Strategic Growth — Insurance as an Enabler

Insurance is not just defense. Every growth lane has an insurance prerequisite:

- **Touring bus financing (Prevost for Snowbird Circuit).** No lender finances a Prevost without physical damage coverage on the vehicle. Commercial auto is the gate to the bus.
- **Municipal contracts and civic pilots.** Any city, CVB, or Main Street org that buys MBT will require a COI naming them as additional insured at $1M/$2M GL minimum. No COI, no contract.
- **Enterprise broker and bank partnerships.** Vicki Wolpert-type pilots and any bank referral partnership will require cyber liability coverage ($1M+) before they put customer data near DSD.
- **Venue bookings at third-party sites.** Every festival, hotel ballroom, and public park requires GL with the venue listed as additional insured. Currently we can't accept those bookings cleanly.
- **Bearsville activation (summer 2026).** New state (NY), new physical location, new workers-comp jurisdiction. NY is a WC-enforcement state — materially different rules from MS.
- **Publishing partnerships.** Any syndication deal or distribution agreement for Magazine / Records content will require media E&O.

A coverage program is **sales infrastructure**, not overhead.

## 4. Operations — How This Department Runs

**Broker relationship.** One broker of record, appointed in writing. Broker shops the market annually. Do not direct-quote with multiple carriers simultaneously — that burns the market and drives premiums up.

**Shortlist (entertainment specialists to approach first — verify credentials at quote time):**
- **Heffernan Entertainment Insurance Services** — historic entertainment specialty
- **Front Row Insurance Brokers** — touring, production, venues
- **Allied Insurance (Nationwide)** — commercial package, entertainment endorsements
- **Higginbotham / Hub International** — mid-market commercial, regional MS/LA presence
- **Local Natchez/Jackson independent agents** — for the hospitality/auto lines specifically; they understand the Mississippi dram-shop and WC environment better than national specialty brokers

Get three quotes minimum. Favor a broker who will write **one program across both entities** rather than a broker who only knows one line.

**COI management.** Every contract we sign or receive triggers a COI request. Maintain a single `/docs/insurance/certificates/` index: counterparty, policy it references, limit, expiration, additional-insured language. Zero tolerance for verbal-only "we have insurance" claims from vendors.

**Claims process.** Claims go through the broker first, always. Never contact a carrier directly and never discuss fault with anyone but counsel and the broker. 24-hour reporting SLA on anything that could become a claim — even if no claim is filed yet.

**Renewal calendar.** 90 days before renewal: update asset schedules, revenue projections, payroll/contractor counts, vehicle list. 60 days out: broker begins re-marketing. 30 days out: bind decisions made. Never auto-renew without re-shopping every 2–3 years.

**Certificate holder list (to build):** Each venue BMT plays, each client Studio C shoots for, each municipality MBT licenses, each bank/broker partner, landlords for Blues Room / Studio C / Inn / Bearsville space.

**Recommendation in principle (not a purchase):** Target the **Base** tier. Conservative leaves the five red-status items open. Aggressive is premature until revenue justifies E&O and the bus is actually acquired.

## 6. Entity Structure Gotchas

**Can one policy cover Big Muddy Touring + Studio C under one program?**
Yes — common structure is a single package policy with **FarleyPierson LLC as primary named insured** and each DBA/affiliate entity added as **additional named insured**. Studio C, if it's a separate legal entity, can be added either as additional named insured (full coverage) or additional insured (liability protection only, no first-party property). Broker will recommend based on ownership overlap and contract flow.

**What changes if/when HDI incorporates as a holding company?**
- Add HDI as the top-of-chain named insured. All operating entities roll up as additional named insureds.
- D&O liability becomes relevant the moment HDI has a board, outside investors, or advisory committee.
- Entity restructuring is a policy endorsement, not a re-shop, if done mid-term — but disclose it immediately.

**Workers comp and equity partners — the nuance [?]:**
- Mississippi generally requires WC at 5+ employees (statutory, verify current threshold with MS Workers' Compensation Commission).
- LLC members/partners can typically **elect out** of WC coverage for themselves — but must file the election, not assume it.
- Tracy and Amy as equity partners are likely exempt *if they elect out*. JP is not finalized and is a gray zone. Contractors can be reclassified as employees by a court if "right to control" tests are met.
- **Action:** Tracy files written election for herself, Amy, and Chase. JP's contractor agreement needs a clean IC clause before any further work is done under it.
- Bearsville changes this: New York is a strict WC enforcement state. Any Bearsville worker triggers a separate NY WC analysis.

**Liquor liability is not optional.** Mississippi is a permit state for on-premises consumption. Both the Blues Room bar and the Inn bar require liquor liability as a practical matter — most landlords, music licensors (ASCAP/BMI/SESAC), and local permit offices will demand proof. This is a hard gate, not a nice-to-have.
