# System Hierarchy

**Status:** The canonical hierarchy of brand surfaces and operating layers in Big Muddy.
**Last revised:** 2026-05-12

This document tells agents what's load-bearing vs. supporting vs. ambient. Use it to prioritize.

---

## The 7 layers, ranked

| Rank | Layer | Role | Economic obligation | Investment priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Big Muddy Inn** | Stable operating engine | Must be profitable | Maintain & marginal improvement |
| 2 | **Chase Pierson Photography** | Founder-funded growth capital | Must be protected | Protect time + grow pipeline |
| 3 | **Big Muddy Touring** | Strategic growth bet | Compounding cultural position toward sustainability | Active investment |
| 4 | **Big Muddy Magazine** | Identity engine | Drive Inn bookings + sponsorship potential | Active investment |
| 5 | **Big Muddy Directory** | Geographic moat | Low-maintenance, search-indexed | Maintain & grow incrementally |
| 6 | **Big Muddy Records** | Artist relationship layer | Cover its own modest costs | Selective signings only |
| 7 | **Big Muddy Radio** | Ambient culture layer | Avoid consuming disproportionate energy | Minimal — defer until audience |

---

## What the ranking means

**Layers 1–2: The cash engines.** These produce real revenue today. They are not subject to disruption or experimentation. Anything that risks Inn revenue or photography revenue is a high-risk proposal that needs strong justification.

**Layer 3: The growth bet.** Big Muddy Touring is where capital and attention concentrate. Its success is measured on a longer horizon (cultural compounding toward sustainability) than the cash engines (quarterly cash performance).

**Layer 4: The identity engine.** The Magazine isn't just marketing — it's how the corridor interprets itself through Big Muddy. It feeds Layers 3, 5, 6 with content + audience + sponsor narrative. Higher priority than its current state implies.

**Layer 5: The moat.** The Directory is the unique-asset differentiator. Low-maintenance, compounding over time. Don't over-invest, don't neglect.

**Layer 6: The optionality.** Records exists to formalize artist relationships and produce ancillary revenue (merch, sessions, sponsor packages). Y1 scope: Amy's projects + Mechanical Bull catalog + Doug Duffy and Badd promo signing if it comes. Don't expand the roster aggressively in Y1.

**Layer 7: The ambient layer.** Radio's job is to exist as atmosphere, not to be a profit center. It does NOT have to justify itself economically — but it does have to **not consume disproportionate energy.** Freeze the 24/7 stack until audience justifies it; keep Spotify playlists + occasional podcast as the ambient presence.

---

## How agents use this hierarchy

When proposing work or evaluating decisions:

1. **Higher-rank layers get higher-priority resources.** When in doubt, choose the investment that serves Layer 1 or 2 over one that serves Layer 6 or 7.
2. **Don't propose investment in Layer 7 (Radio) that increases Chase's maintenance burden.** Doctrine: ambient ≠ deserves engineering effort.
3. **Don't propose Layer 6 (Records) growth that consumes Layer 2 (Photography) time without explicit economics.** Records doesn't fund photography; the opposite is closer to true.
4. **Don't trade Layer 4 (Magazine) for Layer 3 (Touring) — they reinforce each other.** Both serve the cultural-compounding metric for Big Muddy Touring's growth.
5. **Layer 1 (Inn) and Layer 2 (Photography) are the only layers that get to be unambiguously about money.** All other layers' economic obligations are conditional on Layers 1 + 2 remaining healthy.

## Layers that are NOT in the hierarchy

- **"Deep South Directory" (DSD)** — was positioned as a public-facing layer of MBT-as-product. With MBT dropped, DSD's fate is open (probably fold into Big Muddy Directory).
- **"Measurably Better Things"** — dropped as a brand. Internal-only legal entity reference at most.
- **"Bearsville Creative"** — deferred to Y2+. Not currently a Big Muddy layer.
- **"Outsider Economics"** — editorial sibling brand; status open but not part of the BMT hierarchy.
- **"Hillbilly Dreams Inc."** — dead language per CLAUDE.md.
