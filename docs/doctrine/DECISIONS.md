## 2026-05-12 — Strategic pivot to two-umbrella + three-inputs-one-bet model

**Decision:**

Big Muddy operates as two public umbrellas: **Big Muddy Touring** (entertainment + media, containing Radio + Magazine + Records + Directory) and **Big Muddy Inn** (sister hospitality brand). The operating model is "three inputs, one bet": Inn = cash engine #1, Chase Pierson Photography = cash engine #2, Big Muddy Touring = growth bet funded by #1 and #2.

**Reason:**

The previous multi-brand ecosystem framing tried to make seven aspirational businesses behave like equal businesses. They are not. The Inn generates revenue; Chase's photography generates revenue; everything else is aspirational. Reorganizing around what actually makes money matches THE_THESIS lifestyle objectives.

**Would reopen if:**

The Inn becomes structurally unprofitable AND Chase's photography revenue drops more than 30% from baseline, AND no replacement revenue line has emerged. None of those conditions are forecast.

## 2026-05-12 — MBT brand retired

**Decision:**

"Measurably Better Things" is dropped as a brand and framing layer. No "Powered by Measurably Better Things" anywhere. The "Glass Engine" can survive as an internal nickname but is not pitched externally.

**Reason:**

Platform licensing becomes a core business again (i.e., Job Two gets revived). Not currently on the menu.

**Reference:**

- `memory/feedback_drop_mbt_brand.md`

**Note:** the legal entity name (`Measurably Better Things LLC`) is a separate question from the brand. The legal entity may stay MBT LLC operating BMT as a DBA, or may switch to Big Muddy Touring LLC. Decision still pending with Tracy + counsel.

## 2026-05-12 — Job Two (productize the platform) dropped

**Decision:**

The MBT platform's only job is reducing the cost of running existing Big Muddy businesses (Job One). It is not being built toward B2B licensing or productization for other regional ecosystems.

**Reason:**

Three-partner team. No bandwidth for productization in addition to running the operating businesses. The labor cost of maintaining custom infrastructure for an unconfirmed future product market is brutal.

**Would reopen if:**

A real B2B customer materializes unprompted AND the ecosystem revenue grows enough that Chase has bandwidth for productization (likely Y3+ after the Inn hits $250k+ profit milestone).

## 2026-05-12 — No generic festival drift (doctrine)

**Decision:**

Big Muddy Touring is not a volume festival operator. Events stay at 40–150 person scale. Sponsorship is symbolic capital aligned with cultural legitimacy, not commodity event inventory.

**Reason:**

Volume festival operations destroy the moat (intimacy + regional specificity + documentation + aesthetic cohesion). The asset base — Inn at 6 rooms, Blues Room as a listening room, Chase's portrait-photography practice — doesn't support 2,000+ scale.

**Would reopen if:**

This is doctrine, not strategy. It does not reopen lightly. Would require a fundamental strategic re-evaluation across all three equity partners.

**Reference:**

- `memory/feedback_no_generic_festival.md`

## 2026-05-12 — Photography is an explicitly protected revenue pillar

**Decision:**

Chase Pierson Photography (~$100k+/yr) is not invisible support infrastructure. It is the second-most-important revenue pillar in the ecosystem. Photography work gets scheduling priority over BMT tasks.

**Reason:**

Without protection, photography time gets cannibalized by lower-return ecosystem tasks. The "founder energy subsidy" model only works if the source of the subsidy stays healthy.

**Would reopen if:**

BMT becomes self-sustaining (Y3+) AND no longer requires photography revenue to fund operations. At that point photography returns to being a personal creative practice rather than an explicitly protected revenue pillar.

**Reference:**

- `memory/feedback_three_inputs_one_bet.md`

## 2026-05-11 — Big Muddy Radio audio stack: freeze pending audience

**Decision:**

The Big Muddy Radio audio infrastructure (AzuraCast on DigitalOcean + Buzzsprout + ElevenLabs + Restream) is frozen until audience milestones justify the investment. Radio output becomes curated Spotify playlists + occasional podcast features in the interim.

**Reason:**

The stream's SSL is broken (not publicly polished), audience is unmeasured, ongoing cost is $70–140/mo + Chase's maintenance time. Pre-audience infrastructure is the wrong investment.

**Would reopen if:**

A defined audience milestone (e.g., 500 podcast subscribers, sponsorship interest from a regional brand, recurring listener count > 100/day) is met.

- `docs/partners/radio-go-no-go-decision-2026-05-11.md`
