# Big Muddy Touring — 12-Month Plan

*Written 2026-04-29. Voice: admin documentation. Numbers from THE_THESIS, not aspirational.*

---

## What it is

A booking and tour-support operation along the Mississippi corridor and the Blues Highway, anchored in Natchez. Built first to support Amy's career as Arrie Aslin, then opened to other touring acts who need routing, lodging, transport, and promotion through the Big Muddy media stack.

---

## What's working today

- **Amy performs publicly as Arrie Aslin** — artist-in-residence at the Inn and the first artist on the Big Muddy Records roster. Real shows, real audience, weekly cadence.
- **The Big Muddy Inn provides lodging** for visiting acts. That's a legitimate cost advantage no other booking operation in this corridor has.
- **The Blues Room** at the Inn is a working venue. Capacity is small (listening-room scale, not a 500-cap club), but it's a reliable second-night stop for any act routing through.
- **The Sprinter van** is operational this week (per CLAUDE.md). That's tour transport for short corridor runs without renting per-trip.
- **bigmuddytouring.com is live.** Booking inquiries route through the site.
- **Big Muddy Radio + Magazine give us an in-house promotion engine.** Acts get airplay and editorial coverage for being on a Big Muddy bill — that's the trade.
- **Big Muddy Records exists as a label entity.** Catalog is small (Arrie Aslin, plus selected previously-released material — Amy Allen, Scruggs). The label is real, the artist roster is curated, not aspirational.

What is NOT working yet: a published show calendar, a routing playbook for non-Amy acts, a deal sheet template, and a clean booking inquiry flow. All listed below.

## The 12-month plan

**Q1 (May–Jul 2026) — get the operating system in place**
- Publish the Blues Room weekly calendar. Arrie Aslin anchors at least one night/week. Two additional touring acts/week minimum.
- Build the booking inquiry flow on bigmuddytouring.com. One form, one inbox, one response template.
- Write the corridor routing playbook: which venues, which nights, what each pays, what each costs.
- Sprinter van: log every mile, every fuel stop. Set a real per-mile cost number by end of Q1.
- Sign 3 outside touring acts to a Big Muddy corridor run.

**Q2 (Aug–Oct 2026) — push the corridor**
- 6–10 outside acts routed through. Each one stays at the Inn (room-night revenue books to Inn).
- One festival side-date deal (Crescent City Blues or King Biscuit weekend).
- First Big Muddy Records release of the year: a single from Arrie Aslin or a re-released catalog track. Promoted through Magazine + Radio.
- Wedding/private event bookings: target 4–6 Arrie Aslin or Big Muddy house band dates.

**Q3 (Nov 2026–Jan 2027) — record + radio + holiday**
- Holiday programming at the Blues Room: ticketed shows Thanksgiving + NYE.
- Big Muddy Records: second release. Goal is two singles + one EP across the year.
- Big Muddy Radio: lock the regular show schedule. Use it to promote upcoming Blues Room dates and corridor runs.
- January: light booking month. Use the down time to plan Q4 spring corridor + summer festival routing.

**Q4 (Feb–Apr 2027) — book the spring corridor**
- 8–12 outside acts on the calendar.
- One Arrie Aslin tour run — 4 to 7 dates, corridor-only, Sprinter-supported.
- Year-end review: did Touring lift the corridor economy? Did it lift the Inn? Did it lift Records? Where did the dollars actually land?

## The risk

- **The corridor doesn't fill.** Mitigation: Amy's residency at the Blues Room is a controlled floor. We are not dependent on outside acts to make any single week work.
- **Sprinter van breakdown on a tour run.** Insurance + AAA cover the dollars; the bigger risk is a missed show. Mitigation: only route corridor dates Sprinter can reach with a margin for failure (no 14-hour single-day drives).
- **A booked act cancels last-minute.** Standard touring risk. Booking deal sheet must include a cancellation clause.
- **Amy burns out** — same risk as the Inn plan. Same ceiling rule. Tracy and Amy decide.
- **Records release flops.** Pre-revenue Y1 is acceptable per THE_THESIS. The point is the artist roster, not chart position.

## Why it matters to the ecosystem

- Touring is the **engine that fills the Inn** outside of leisure-tourism weeks. Every routed act = room nights + Blues Room door + bar revenue.
- It's the **content engine for Magazine and Radio.** Every show is a story, an interview, a track to spin. Without Touring, the editorial calendar gets thin fast.
- It's the **catalog engine for Records.** Acts that route through the corridor become candidates for the label.
- It's the **proof of the corridor thesis.** If we can route 12+ outside acts in a year and they keep coming back, the Mississippi corridor is real and the rest of the ecosystem has a story to tell.
- It's the place **Amy gets to actually be the artist she is.** That alone is enough reason for the brand to exist; the corridor revenue is the upside.

If Touring works, the Inn fills, the Magazine has subjects, the Records catalog grows, and the corridor's music economy gets a measurable lift. If Touring doesn't work, Amy still plays the Blues Room weekly and we adjust.
