## Panic threshold rules — what trigger fires when

Tracy: this is the part you act on. Don't panic; act.

**Green (on track)** — Month total within 20% of mid-scenario row.
- Action: nothing. Read the report, move on.

**Yellow (attention)** — Month total 20–35% below mid-scenario row, OR Inn line alone 25%+ below mid for two consecutive months.
- Action: 30-min call between Chase + Tracy + Amy. Look at which line missed. Adjust the next 60 days, not the year.
- Examples that trigger Yellow:
  - October Inn comes in at $11k vs. $16k planned. (~30% miss on the load-bearing carrier — investigate why; pricing? booking flow? competitor opened?)
  - CPP zero for two months in a row. (Chase is overcommitted to platform — rebalance.)
  - Studio C overflow surprises by $2k+ on an invoice. (Bucket discipline broke; renegotiate per `plan-studio-c.md` risk #1.)

**Red (act this week)** — Month total 35%+ below mid for two consecutive months, OR cash on hand drops below 60 days of operating cost (~$22k for the Inn alone), OR Inn line drops 40%+ in any peak month (Oct/Nov/Dec/Mar/Apr).
- Action: same-day call. Cut a discretionary spend. Decide whether to pull a wedding deposit forward, run a flash promotion, or pause Records / Radio spend. The $1,500/yr Radio line and the $3,500/yr Records line are the first cuts because they generate $0.
- Hard rule: **no founder gets a distribution in a Red month.** Tracy + Amy + Chase all hold. That rule exists so the partner trust doesn't break the first time the number is bad.

**Blue (above plan)** — Month total 25%+ above mid, sustained 3+ months.
- Action: don't celebrate; recalibrate. Pull the next year's plan forward. Decide whether to capitalize BMA Phase 1, hire the shows-and-programming role, or accelerate the second MBT B2B pilot.

## Where Chase's effort is highest leverage

Inn and CPP carry the year. Records and Radio do not. Touring is small dollars but high ecosystem multiplier. Knowing this, here is where one hour of Chase's time produces the most Y1 dollars:

1. **Whole-Inn buyout funnel (Q2–Q3).** One PDF, one price, one inquiry-to-booked flow. A single $6k buyout = 4% of the Inn's Y1 mid number. Five of these in the year = $30k = the difference between mid and high scenarios on the Inn line. **This is the single highest-leverage line item in the forecast.**
2. **Photography commission outbound.** Every $5k commission Chase books moves Y1 by 1.7%. 2 hours of outbound per week (real estate clients, hospitality clients, editorial pitches) — `chase-pierson-photography.md` voice doc + commission-pitch email template — likely yields 4–6 net new commissions in Y1.
3. **Booking inquiry response time on bigmuddytouring.com.** Fast turnaround on touring inquiries directly grows the Touring line AND the Inn line (because the act stays at the Inn). Auto-template + 24-hour SLA on inquiries.
4. **MBT B2B pilots — close one of the two by end of Q3.** Vicki or Paul Green, doesn't matter which; pick the one closer to ready and push. Either pilot adds $6k–$15k to the year.
5. **The Cloudbeds trailing-12-month pull (Tracy's task, but Chase unblocks).** Until that real number replaces the TBD in `plan-big-muddy-inn.md`, every Inn row in this forecast is an estimate. Once we have the real baseline, recalibrate the whole table — that exercise alone is worth more than any single sales action because it makes every subsequent decision evidence-based.

What is **NOT** highest leverage in Y1: shipping more Records masters (they generate $0 this year), perfecting the Radio rotation (no listeners yet), launching the Magazine subscription model before Q4 (no list yet), opening BMA Phase 1 without the capital decision made.
