# Hillbilly Dreams Ecosystem Directory

**Version:** 2026-04-23
**Status:** Internal draft. Per-person sections excerpted publicly only after Chase, Tracy, and Amy review.
**Public launch target:** 2026-04-27.

This directory maps the people in the Hillbilly Dreams / Big Muddy / Measurably Better Things (MBT) ecosystem to the six principles of Outsider Economics — the working philosophy of the corridor (canonical text in `outsidereconomics.com` and `/outsider-economics-v2/`). The principles are the operating logic of the ecosystem, not branding. Each person below proves one or more of them by what they actually do — not by what they could be marketed as.

The six principles, in the order they appear here:

1. **Community Is the Currency** — relationships are the real asset class. A town with fifty reliable, coordinated people is wealthier than a town with ten thousand strangers.
2. **The Extraction Trap** — eighty-two cents of every small-town dollar leaves the local economy within thirty days. The work is not earning more; it is closing the drain.
3. **The Coordination Premium** — skills coordinated next to other skills are worth roughly 4.5x what they bill alone. The premium goes to whoever owns the coordination layer.
4. **Time as Currency** — money has exit velocity; a neighbor's afternoon does not. Direct skill exchange is structurally un-extractable.
5. **Federation over Scale** — past about 100 people, organizations stop being trust networks and start being bureaucracies. Federate small units; do not grow one big one.
6. **The Task Board** — the most powerful piece of economic infrastructure in a small town is a whiteboard with three columns: NEED, CAN DO, DONE. Visibility is the whole game.

The directory is organized by working group: core team (the three equity partners), the Northeast partner studio (Tuthill Design and its operators), Inn and Blues Room operators, working musicians in the orbit, counsel, legacy advisors and pilot enterprise partners, and a final historical-only entry. Each person carries a bio, a description of the role they actually play in the ecosystem, the principles they prove, and cross-references to others in this directory they connect to.

A small number of biographical details below are flagged as research gaps. Where two source documents disagree about a name, spelling, or affiliation, the conflict is flagged in line rather than resolved silently.
