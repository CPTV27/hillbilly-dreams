## Competitor Homepage Patterns (15 Sites Scraped)

### What Everyone Says
- "All-in-one" — every single one
- "AI-powered" — 11 of 15
- Demo-first CTAs — 10 of 15 hide pricing behind "Get a Demo" or "Watch Demo"
- Social proof by volume: "100,000 businesses," "25 million users," "288,000 customers"
- Pain points are vague: "too many tools," "complexity," "missed leads," "disconnected data"

### What Nobody Says (This Is DSD's Entire Homepage)
- A specific town name
- "We're from here"
- A price on the homepage
- Anything about culture, community, or belonging
- "We own the magazine that'll feature you"

## The Closest Competitors (Why They Lose)

**Townsquare Interactive** ($499/mo) — THE direct competitor. Radio + digital bundle across 300 markets. But: no magazine, no photography, no hotel, no record label. And $499/mo prices out Main Street.

**HighLevel** ($97/mo) — Closest price match with massive feature set. But: agency-focused, not Main Street. No media properties. No local presence.

**Locable** ($99/mo) — Small-town directories. No media production at all.

**Best Version Media** (~$200/mo) — Hyperlocal print magazines. No radio, no digital stack.

## Pain Points (2 Versions)

### The Ghost Town Effect (emotional)
> You're the best at what you do, but if someone searches for you — or drives through town — they'd never know you exist. You're paying for tools you don't use and getting results you can't see.

### The Search Reality (specific/urgent)
> Right now, someone in your town is searching for a restaurant, a hotel, or a shop like yours on Google. If your listing has wrong hours, no photos, or zero reviews — they're picking your competitor. DSD fixes that. Today.
