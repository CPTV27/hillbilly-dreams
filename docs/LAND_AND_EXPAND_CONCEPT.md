# Land & Expand — Concept Analysis
**Source:** Chase Pierson voice memo, April 15, 2026
**Status:** Concept stage — not yet in development pipeline

---

## The Concept in One Paragraph

Buy 30-100 acres near Natchez (Mississippi or Louisiana side) and build a cooperative community with tiny houses, VIP cabins, a common area with commercial kitchen and performance space, a recording studio, a workshop/barn, a pool, and a radio broadcast station. Residents can buy equity in the cooperative instead of paying rent, and units get booked as short-term rentals when owners aren't there. The community feeds into Big Muddy's programming — radio, recording, events, agriculture, workshops. A mirror site does the same thing in the Catskills/Hudson Valley (possibly on Marika's land as a partnership). The whole thing runs on the MBT platform with a new "Born Free" product — a Raspberry Pi in a rugged case that gives residents AI, radio broadcasting, and network connectivity with zero monthly bills.

### 3. Cooperative Ownership Model

**What Chase said:**
> "Instead of offering a traditional mortgage, we can offer ownership in our cooperative. People buy in and they're paying for equity, not just rent. They don't feel bad about paying when they're not there. And then we'll actually work on booking it with rentals when you're not there so it'll actually make you money."

**What this means:**
- Legal structure: housing cooperative (co-op) or LLC with membership interests
- Members buy a share (equity stake), not a unit
- Monthly fees cover maintenance, utilities, shared amenities
- When member isn't occupying their unit, it goes into a rental pool (Airbnb/VRBO/direct booking)
- Rental revenue either offsets member's monthly fees or generates income
- Members can split time between Natchez and Catskills sites

**Legal research needed:**
- Mississippi cooperative housing law
- Louisiana cooperative housing law (if LA side)
- Securities implications of selling ownership interests (may need SEC exemption or state-level registration)
- Tax treatment of co-op member payments vs rental income
- Insurance structure for shared property + short-term rentals
- Comparison: co-op vs LLC vs condo association vs intentional community structures

**Revenue model sketch:**

| Revenue Stream | Monthly | Annual |
|----------------|---------|--------|
| 10 member shares × $800/mo maintenance | $8,000 | $96,000 |
| Rental pool (avg 4 units × 15 nights/mo × $120/night) | $7,200 | $86,400 |
| Event/performance venue rentals | $2,000 | $24,000 |
| Recording studio bookings | $3,000 | $36,000 |
| Workshop/maker space fees | $500 | $6,000 |
| **Total** | **$20,700** | **$248,400** |

### 4. The Snowbird/Dual-Location Program

**What Chase said:**
> "You could have one here and one in Woodstock. We should have a program that makes it easier for people from Woodstock to go back and forth. Kind of like all our groovy people."

**What this means:**
- Two cooperative sites: Natchez + Catskills/Hudson Valley
- Members can buy into one or both
- Seasonal migration program: winter in Mississippi, summer in New York
- Shared member benefits across both sites
- Transportation support (possibly group travel, van service)

**This directly connects to the existing two-region model** (Big Muddy in Natchez, Bearsville in Woodstock). The cooperative becomes the *physical* version of the digital two-region platform.

### 6. Zero-Bills Living / Self-Reliance Model

**What Chase said:**
> "We can make it so you don't have any bills. You can give up your cell phone or get a flip phone, $10/month, but still be connected to all the state-of-the-art stuff through the software platform."

**What this means:**
- Shared utilities (solar? community septic, well water)
- Shared internet (one connection, mesh network)
- Shared tools, kitchen, laundry
- MBT platform provides digital services (communication, AI, content)
- Members trade high individual costs for low shared costs

**Connection to Outsider Economics:**
This IS the Outsider Economics thesis made physical. The magazine/philosophy already talks about alternative economic models. The cooperative is the proof.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Capital requirements ($1M+) | HIGH | Phase it — land first, tiny houses incrementally, studio later |
| Securities law (selling equity interests) | HIGH | Get a lawyer early — co-op structure vs LLC, exemption filings |
| Zoning for tiny houses | MEDIUM | Research before buying — some MS counties are very permissive |
| Construction management | MEDIUM | Tracy's interior design + local contractors |
| Occupancy in early years | MEDIUM | Use for touring bands first, build membership over time |
| Splitting focus from current priorities | HIGH | This is Phase 2+ — don't let it distract from DSD/touring dogfood |
