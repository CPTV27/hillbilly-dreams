# Sanity Schema Design Document: Next Wave

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the design for the next wave of Sanity schemas, expanding our content management capabilities to support new content types, platform features, and editorial workflows. Building upon our existing core schemas (`article`, `location`, `event`, `touringPage`, `contentTemplate`), these new schemas aim to streamline content creation, enhance content discoverability, and empower our editorial and marketing teams (Tracy, Amy, Chase) with a robust and flexible content infrastructure.

Each schema design details its purpose, specific fields with their types and rationale, relationships to existing or new schemas, where the content will be displayed, who is responsible for editing, and its interaction with the Content Creation Wizard (CCW).

### 2.1. Schema Name: `podcastEpisode`

**Purpose:** To manage individual podcast episodes, including audio files, show notes, guest information, and associated metadata. This schema facilitates the publishing and archiving of our audio content, making it accessible through dedicated podcast pages and feeds.

**Fields:**

*   `title` (string): The main title of the podcast episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Essential for identification and display.
*   `slug` (slug): Unique identifier for the episode's URL.
    *   *Rationale:* Provides a clean, human-readable URL for the episode page.
*   `episodeNumber` (number): The sequential number of the episode within its series.
    *   *Rationale:* Helps in ordering and identifying episodes, especially for series-based podcasts.
*   `seriesTitle` (string): The title of the podcast series this episode belongs to.
    *   *Rationale:* While a dedicated `podcastSeries` schema could be a future enhancement, this field allows grouping episodes for display without immediate over-engineering.
*   `publishedDate` (datetime): The date and time the episode was officially released.
    *   *Rationale:* Critical for chronological sorting, RSS feeds, and general content freshness.
*   `episodeImage` (image): A cover image specific to this episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Visual representation for episode listings and social sharing.
*   `audioFile` (file): The actual audio file (e.g., MP3) for the podcast episode.
    *   *Rationale:* The core content of the podcast.
*   `duration` (string): The length of the episode (e.g., "30:45").
    *   *Rationale:* Informative for listeners.
*   `description` (block content): A rich text summary or show notes for the episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Provides context, links, and detailed information for listeners.
*   `guests` (array of references to `staff` or a new `guest` schema): List of individuals featured as guests on the episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Acknowledges contributors and allows linking to their profiles. A dedicated `guest` schema might be useful for external guests not on staff.
*   `hosts` (array of references to `staff`): List of staff members who host the episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Identifies regular hosts and links to their profiles.
*   `transcript` (block content): A full text transcript of the episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Improves accessibility and SEO.
*   `relatedArticles` (array of references to `article`): Links to relevant articles mentioned or discussed in the episode.
    *   *Rationale:* Cross-promotes content and provides additional resources.
*   `seo` (object): Standard SEO fields (title, description, image, keywords).
    *   *Rationale:* Optimizes discoverability in search engines.

**Relationships:**

*   **References:** `staff` (for hosts and guests), `article` (for related content).
*   **Referenced By:** Potentially a `podcastSeries` schema in the future, or `contentTemplate` for featuring episodes.

**Page References:**

*   `/podcast/[slug]` (Dedicated episode page)
*   `/podcast` (Podcast listing page, displaying all episodes)
*   RSS Feed generation for podcast platforms.

**Editor:** Tracy (primary), Amy (secondary for content review).

**Content Creation Wizard (CCW) Interaction:**
The CCW will include a "New Podcast Episode" workflow. This workflow will guide Tracy through uploading the audio file, auto-populating duration (if possible), entering title, description, and selecting hosts/guests. It could also suggest related articles based on keywords in the description, simplifying cross-linking.

### 2.2. Schema Name: `showEvent`

**Purpose:** To manage and publish details for Blues Room shows that are publicly promoted on the `/shows` page. This is distinct from the more general `event` schema, which might cover internal meetings or non-public events. `showEvent` focuses on public-facing performances, often tied to `location` and `partnerArtist` schemas.

**Fields:**

*   `title` (string): The name of the show/performance.
    *   *Rationale:* Primary identifier for the event.
*   `slug` (slug): Unique identifier for the show's URL.
    *   *Rationale:* Clean URL structure for individual show pages.
*   `eventDate` (datetime): The date and time the show takes place.
    *   *Rationale:* Crucial for scheduling, display, and chronological sorting.
*   `location` (reference to `location`): The venue where the show is held.
    *   *Rationale:* Links to our existing location data, providing address, map, and venue details.
*   `headliner` (reference to `partnerArtist`): The main performing artist for the show.
    *   *Rationale:* Connects to our artist profiles for detailed information.
*   `supportActs` (array of references to `partnerArtist` or string): Other artists performing at the show.
    *   *Rationale:* Allows for multiple performers, supporting both `partnerArtist` and external artists by name.
*   `description` (block content): Detailed information about the show, artists, and any special notes.
    *   *Rationale:* Provides rich promotional content.
*   `ticketLink` (url): URL where tickets can be purchased.
    *   *Rationale:* Direct call to action for attendees.
*   `priceRange` (string): E.g., "$25 - $50" or "Free".
    *   *Rationale:* Essential information for potential attendees.
*   `showImage` (image): Promotional image for the show.
    *   *Rationale:* Visual appeal for listings and social sharing.
*   `status` (string, enum: "on-sale", "sold-out", "cancelled", "postponed"): Current status of the show.
    *   *Rationale:* Communicates critical updates to the audience.
*   `seo` (object): Standard SEO fields.
    *   *Rationale:* Improves search engine visibility for show listings.

**Relationships:**

*   **References:** `location` (existing schema), `partnerArtist` (new schema for artists).
*   **Referenced By:** Potentially `touringPage` if a show is part of a larger tour, or `contentTemplate` for featuring specific shows.

**Page References:**

*   `/shows/[slug]` (Dedicated show event page)
*   `/shows` (Main public shows listing page)
*   `/records/[artist-slug]` (Artist profile pages, listing their upcoming shows).

**Editor:** Amy (primary for scheduling and details), Chase (secondary for promotional copy/images).

**Content Creation Wizard (CCW) Interaction:**
The CCW will offer a "New Public Show Event" workflow. This would prompt for key details like headliner (with a search/select from `partnerArtist`), date, time, location, and ticket link. It could also suggest `partnerArtist` images for the `showImage` if available, and allow quick status updates.

### 2.5. Schema Name: `socialPost`

**Purpose:** To serve as a central repository for generating social media content and archiving published versions. This schema aims to standardize social messaging, enable multi-platform publishing (manual or automated), and maintain a historical record of our social presence.

**Fields:**

*   `internalName` (string): An internal identifier for the post (e.g., "Announce New Album - Artist X").
    *   *Rationale:* For internal organization and easy lookup in Sanity. Not public-facing.
*   `primaryText` (text): The main text content of the social post.
    *   *Rationale:* The core message, adaptable for different platforms.
*   `callToAction` (string): A short, compelling call to action (e.g., "Listen Now!", "Get Tickets!").
    *   *Rationale:* Standardized CTA for various platforms.
*   `targetLink` (url): The URL the post should direct users to (e.g., album page, ticket link).
    *   *Rationale:* The primary destination for the social post's engagement.
*   `media` (array of images or files): Images or video clips to accompany the post.
    *   *Rationale:* Visuals are critical for social engagement.
*   `platforms` (array of objects): Details specific to each social media platform.
    *   *Rationale:* Allows customization for platform-specific requirements and archiving publication details.
    *   *Nested Fields (object):*
        *   `platformName` (string, enum: "Instagram", "Facebook", "X", "LinkedIn", "TikTok"): The social media platform.
        *   `platformText` (text): Customized text for this specific platform (optional, defaults to `primaryText`).
        *   `platformMedia` (array of images/files): Customized media for this platform (optional, defaults to `media`).
        *   `publishedDate` (datetime): Date/time this specific post was published on this platform.
        *   `publishedURL` (url): The direct URL to the published post on this platform.
        *   `status` (string, enum: "draft", "scheduled", "published", "failed"): Publication status.
*   `keywords` (array of strings): Relevant keywords or hashtags.
    *   *Rationale:* For internal search, content grouping, and hashtag suggestions.
*   `author` (reference to `staff`): The staff member who created/drafted the post.
    *   *Rationale:* For internal accountability.
*   `relatedContent` (array of references to `article`, `event`, `podcastEpisode`, `partnerArtist`, etc.): Links to internal content the post promotes.
    *   *Rationale:* For tracking which social posts promoted what internal content.

**Relationships:**

*   **References:** `staff` (for author), `article`, `event`, `podcastEpisode`, `partnerArtist` (for promoted content).
*   **Referenced By:** `yearEndRecap` (for highlighting key social moments).

**Page References:**

*   Internal Social Media publishing tools (e.g., Hootsuite, Buffer, or custom integration).
*   Archived versions visible in Sanity Studio.
*   Could be displayed on an "In the News" or "Social Feed" section on the website, pulling published URLs.

**Editor:** Chase (primary for content creation and scheduling), Tracy/Amy (for approval and strategic oversight).

**Content Creation Wizard (CCW) Interaction:**
The CCW will have a "Create Social Post" workflow. This workflow will ask for primary text, target link, and media. It will then allow Chase to select target platforms and customize text/media for each. It could also suggest hashtags based on keywords in related content. Upon "publishing" (either manual trigger or API integration), it would update the `publishedDate` and `publishedURL` for each platform.
