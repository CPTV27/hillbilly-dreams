# MBT Offer Architecture

*One-page reference for the MBT licensed program. Updated 2026-04-08.*

---

## Buyer

Anyone responsible for activating a local economy who needs coordination capacity. Multiple stakeholders can buy into the same market — their programs stack, not compete.

---

## The Multi-Stakeholder Model

MBT is designed for multiple institutions to operate in the same market simultaneously. A business can participate in more than one program. Each stakeholder buys in for their own reasons, but the system is shared.

### How It Stacks (Natchez Example)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LAYER 1: MUNICIPAL                                     │
│  City of Natchez / Visit Natchez                        │
│  Buys: City-wide program                                │
│  Gets: Directory, reporting, tourism metrics,           │
│        grant/ARPA justification, economic dev proof      │
│  Incentive: Tourism dollars, funder ROI, civic pride     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 2: BUSINESS LEADERS / BROKERAGES                 │
│  Local broker, Realtor association, Main Street org      │
│  Buys: Sponsor program (pays to upgrade businesses)     │
│  Gets: Monthly reports on street vitality, foot traffic, │
│        search visibility for sponsored businesses        │
│  Incentive: Vibrant Main Street = higher property values │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 3: VENUE / HOSPITALITY CLUSTERS                  │
│  Inn operators, restaurant groups, venue networks        │
│  Buys: Cluster program for their portfolio              │
│  Gets: Coordinated promotion, event-to-listing pipeline, │
│        shared audience across properties                 │
│  Incentive: Cross-promotion, tourism capture, show ROI   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PARTICIPANTS: INDIVIDUAL BUSINESSES                     │
│  Restaurants, shops, studios, musicians, services        │
│  Join: One or more programs above (or self-serve)        │
│  Get: Listing, promotion, media, measurement             │
│  Cost: $0 (sponsored) or self-serve ($99/$250)           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Buyer Profiles

| Buyer Type | Why They Buy | What They Get |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Town / City** | Economic development metrics, tourism proof, grant justification | City-wide program, outcome reporting for funders |
| **Tourism Bureau / CVB** | Visitor engagement, destination marketing | Business directory, event promotion, visitor-facing content |
| **Real Estate Brokerage** | Property values tied to street vitality | Sponsor businesses, monthly vitality reports |
| **Chamber / Main Street Org** | Member value, community activation | Onboarding, listing management, member reporting |
| **Hospitality Portfolio** | Cross-promotion across properties | Cluster program, shared audience, event pipeline |
| **Venue Cluster** | Coordinated booking, shared promotion | Touring integration, media coverage, directory presence |
| **District Developer** | Attract tenants, prove concept to investors | Program metrics, media proof, occupancy correlation |

### The Broker Play

A broker sponsors 20 businesses on Main Street at $99/mo each through the program. Those businesses get listed, promoted, and measured — without paying a dime. The broker gets a monthly report: foot traffic trends, search visibility, new listings, participation rate. Their properties are worth more because the street is alive and provably active.

That's a real estate play disguised as a media buy.

## Problem

Small-town businesses are invisible to travelers and search. Local organizations lack tools to aggregate, promote, and measure their business ecosystem. The gap isn't technology — it's organization.

Individual businesses can't afford real marketing. Chambers can't prove ROI to funders. Tourism bureaus can't show what's actually happening on the ground. Brokers know a vibrant street is worth more, but have no way to invest in that vitality directly. Everyone is running separate, disconnected efforts.

---

## Promise

A turnkey program that makes every participating business findable, promotable, and measurable — with real reporting that proves ROI to funders, stakeholders, and investors.

One system. Multiple stakeholders. Programs that stack. One report per stakeholder that shows: here are your businesses, here's who's participating, here's the traffic, here's the proof.

## Pricing Model

| Component | Structure |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Setup/Implementation** | One-time fee. Scope depends on market size and business count. |
| **Annual Platform License** | Recurring. Covers admin access, modules, reporting, publishing. |
| **Content/Media Package** | Optional. Studio C photography, video, editorial, training. |
| **Merchant Onboarding** | Optional. Per-business onboarding at scale (bulk import, asset collection). |

This is not a tier chart. Each engagement gets a tailored proposal based on market size, business count, and modules needed.

MBT is a licensed local growth system for towns, districts, brokerages, and cultural operators — combining directory infrastructure, publishing, media distribution, business onboarding, and service delivery into one operating model.

## What MBT Is NOT

- Not a self-serve SaaS tool individuals sign up for
- Not a website builder
- Not a CRM
- Not a generic "business platform"

It is a community operating agreement. A local economic activation program. A directory + publishing + distribution + business enablement layer — implemented with services, relationships, and software.
