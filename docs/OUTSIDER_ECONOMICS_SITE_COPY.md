Eighty cents of every dollar earned in a small Southern town

Outsider Economics is the theory that says: if you build the
infrastructure locally — the technology, the media, the payment
rails, the content engine — you can change the direction of flow.
Not by blocking the outside. By making the inside strong enough

### The Four Principles (tighten the copy)
```
1. SOVEREIGNTY
Own your data. Own your tools. Own your story.
When the platform is yours, the decisions are yours. When the
decisions are yours, the money follows.

2. DENSITY
One platform runs everything. Every new node makes the network
smarter. A hotel booking feeds the magazine. A radio show feeds
the touring route. A directory listing feeds the dashboard.
Nothing runs alone.

3. COMPOUNDING
Every dollar creates a permanent asset. An article written today
markets the town forever. A radio episode lives in the catalog
permanently. Content compounds. Nothing is disposable.

4. LOCAL VELOCITY
A dollar that recirculates three times in-county creates three
times the economic impact of a dollar that leaves on day one.
Technology dollars, content dollars, entertainment dollars —
keep them moving inside the community.
```

## HOW THE FOUR LEVELS CONNECT (for any writer or agent)

| Level | What someone sees | What makes them curious about the next level |
|-------|------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| 4. Outsider Economics | A compelling idea about local economies | "Wait, someone is actually building this?" |
| 3. Big Muddy | A real hotel, radio station, magazine | "How do they run all this with 4 people?" |
| 2. Measurably Better | The technology platform | "Could this work for my business / my city?" |
| 1. Hillbilly Dreams | The corporate structure | "This is investable." |

Each level answers the question the previous level raises. The book creates the believers. Big Muddy proves it's real. Measurably Better makes it available. Hillbilly Dreams makes it investable.

That's the narrative. Every page, every pitch, every conversation is somewhere on this ladder.
