## How We Make Money

Six revenue streams, in order of what's real today versus what's coming.

### 1. The Inn — rooms and bar ($3,000-6,000/month target)

**What it is:** The Big Muddy Inn at 411 North Congress Street, Natchez. Six rooms. A bar. The Blues Room for live shows.

**How the money comes in:**
- Room bookings via Cloudbeds → Airbnb, VRBO, direct. Average $125-169/night depending on season.
- Bar revenue on show nights. $300-500 per show night in drinks.
- Event rentals (future — not yet priced).

**Who owns it:**
- **Amy** runs front-of-house. Guest experience, bar operations, show night logistics.
- **Tracy** runs back-of-house. Bookkeeping, reservations management, pricing strategy, liquor compliance.

**What's working:** Cloudbeds is set up. Airbnb is live. Rooms are bookable.
**What's not:** VRBO may be inactive. Booking.com and Expedia applications haven't been submitted. No post-stay review email is set up. No show-night packages on Airbnb yet.

**Target:** Break even on the Inn first. Then shows generate the premium that makes the Inn profitable.

### 2. Deep South Directory — business subscriptions ($1,000-3,000/month target)

**What it is:** A marketing product for small businesses in Natchez and the Mississippi corridor. We manage their Google listing, reviews, social media, and analytics. They pay monthly.

**Pricing (locked):**
- Free — basic listing
- Essentials — $25/month
- Pro — $50/month
- Marketing — $99/month
- Engine — $250/month

**How the money comes in:** Stripe recurring subscriptions. Chase walks into businesses on Main Street, shows them what we can do, they sign up. Eventually Tracy and Amy can pitch too.

**Who owns it:**
- **Chase** does the walk-in sales and the platform.
- **Tracy** tracks the subscriptions and revenue.
- **Amy** knows the Natchez business owners personally — she's the door-opener for relationships.

**What's working:** The product exists. The onboarding flow exists. The admin dashboard exists.
**What's not:** Zero paying external customers yet. Regina Charboneau is the first dogfood client. Friends-and-family onboarding (10 businesses) is the next milestone.

**Target:** 10 paying customers by the end of the month. That's $250-2,500/month depending on tier mix.

### 3. Shows at the Inn — door revenue + the multiplier ($1,000-2,000/month target)

**What it is:** Live music at the Blues Room. Cover charge + bar sales + the downstream content that feeds everything else.

**How the money comes in:**
- Door charge: $10-20/person, capacity ~50.
- Bar sales during the show: $300-500/night.
- **The 2:1 multiplier:** every show also generates a radio recording, a magazine article, social content, and brings out-of-town guests who book rooms. One $1,000 show night generates ~$1,500-2,000 in total ecosystem value.

**Who owns it:**
- **Amy** books the acts, programs the music, runs the bar on show nights.
- **JP** handles show logistics and programming support (his role is not finalized — Chase manages that relationship).
- **Chase** photographs, records, and produces the content from each show.

**What's working:** The Blues Room is a real venue. Acts have played there.
**What's not:** No regular show calendar yet. No standardized booking flow. Need the first three consistent weekly/biweekly shows booked.

**Target:** 4 shows/month minimum. Build from there.

### 4. Photography and gallery — print sales ($500-1,000/month target)

**What it is:** Chase has 17,956 photographs. Some are gallery-worthy art. We sell prints online via the gallery page with Stripe checkout and print-on-demand fulfillment.

**How the money comes in:** Customer buys a print on the website → Stripe processes payment → print-on-demand lab (Bay Photo, Printful, or WHCC) prints and ships → we keep 70-85% margin.

**Pricing:**
- 8x10: $25
- 16x20: $75
- 24x36: $150
- 40x60: $350

*(Tracy — you set the final pricing. These are starting points.)*

**Who owns it:**
- **Chase** shoots the photos.
- **Tracy** curates the gallery (picks which photos sell), sets pricing, manages the Stripe account.
- The system handles fulfillment automatically.

**What's working:** The gallery page exists. Photos are in GCS.
**What's not:** Stripe checkout isn't wired to the gallery buy buttons yet. Tracy hasn't picked the first 20 prints. No print-on-demand partner is selected.

**Target:** First print sale this month. 10 prints/month at $75 average = $750/month.

### 5. Big Muddy Records — music revenue ($200-500/month target, growing)

**What it is:** Amy's music catalog (and future artists) released under Big Muddy Records. Non-exclusive deals. Artists keep their masters. We take 20% of streaming/sync revenue.

**How the money comes in:** DistroKid distributes to Spotify, Apple Music, etc. Streaming royalties trickle in. Sync licensing (music in ads/film) is the bigger play long-term.

**Who owns it:**
- **Amy** is the first artist AND the A&R person — she decides what gets released.
- **Chase** handles the production and distribution infrastructure.
- **Tracy** tracks the royalty income.

**What's working:** Amy has recorded tracks. The Mechanical Bull catalog exists.
**What's not:** Nothing is on DistroKid yet. No BMI/ASCAP registration. No sync licensing submissions.

**Target:** Get Amy's catalog and the Mechanical Bull re-releases on all platforms this month.

## What You Do Every Day

### Amy's day

**Morning (before the Inn opens):**
- Check Asana on your phone → the Big Muddy shared seat. See what's on today's list.
- Check Cloudbeds for arrivals and departures.
- Walk the Inn — rooms are ready, bar is stocked, common areas are clean.

**Afternoon:**
- Station programming — when AzuraCast is live, open it on your phone or iPad and review today's playlist. Change anything that doesn't feel right. Schedule your live segments.
- Content — if Chase shot something last night, review the clips. Approve or reject for social posting.
- DSD outreach — if you're downtown, mention the Directory to business owners you know. You're not selling — you're introducing. Chase closes.

**Show nights:**
- Run the bar. Run the vibe. Be the host.
- Chase handles recording and photography.
- After the show: make sure the door count and bar receipts are logged (Asana task or a simple text to Tracy).

**Weekly:**
- Saturday: check the radio programming for next week. Are the playlists right? Any new music to add?
- Listen to Mechanical Bull mixes if JP delivers any — flag what's release-ready.

### Tracy's day

**Morning:**
- Check Asana on your phone → the Big Muddy shared seat. See what's on today's list.
- Check Cloudbeds dashboard — revenue from last night, upcoming bookings, any cancellations.
- Check email for Stripe notifications (when wired) — any new DSD subscriptions, print sales, or charges.
- Quick scan of the bank account — any unexpected charges, is the IRS installment on track.

**Midday:**
- Editorial — review any new magazine articles or photos Chase submits. Approve or send back.
- Gallery — if Chase flags new photos for the gallery, review and approve for the storefront.
- Finance — log any expenses, track hours if applicable, keep the P&L current.

**Weekly:**
- Friday: pull the weekly numbers. Rooms, bar, DSD, gallery, records. Put them in the weekly check-in template on Asana.
- Review the directory listings for accuracy — are the Natchez businesses still open, still correctly described?

**Monthly:**
- Close the books. Tracy should be able to run the P&L without Chase.
- Review the IRS installment plan — payment by the 28th.
- Review any grants or legal deadlines on the calendar.

### Chase's day

**Morning:** Architecture, product, photography. The things only Chase can do.
**Afternoon:** Walk-in sales on Main Street (5 businesses/day when active). Meetings. Deals.
**Evening:** If there's a show, Chase photographs and records. If not, Chase edits.
**Night:** The agents work. Chase sleeps.

**The goal is that Chase does NOT do:**
- Restart Docker
- Edit playlist files
- Answer "how do I log in" questions
- Relay messages between agents
- Debug 404s on pages Tracy clicked

All of that is either automated, documented in the manual, or handled by the agents.

## The Weekly Cadence

### Monday morning — 15-minute standup (all three, coffee, no screens for the first 5 minutes)

- What happened last week? (Tracy: numbers. Amy: shows + guests. Chase: product + sales.)
- What's happening this week? (Any shows booked? Any DSD pitches? Any deadlines?)
- What's blocking anyone? (If someone says "I can't do X because Y" — solve Y right now.)

### Friday afternoon — 15-minute revenue review (Tracy leads, all three attend)

Tracy pulls the numbers:
- Inn revenue (rooms + bar) this week
- DSD subscriptions (new + churned)
- Gallery print sales
- Records royalties (when active)
- Show door revenue
- Expenses
- Net

Chase and Amy listen. If a number is off, discuss why. If a number is good, acknowledge it. No action items — just awareness.

### Saturday morning — 30-minute content planning (Chase leads)

- What content goes out next week? (Magazine article, social posts, radio features)
- What photos need shooting? (Inn, Natchez, shows, directory clients)
- What does Amy want on the radio next week? (Programming changes, live segments, new music)
- What does Tracy want in the magazine? (Editorial picks, story ideas, photo features)
