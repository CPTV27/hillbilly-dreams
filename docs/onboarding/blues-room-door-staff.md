# Blues Room Door Staff Guide

This guide outlines essential procedures for Blues Room door staff, ensuring efficient entry, accurate record-keeping, and a safe environment for all guests. Adhere strictly to these guidelines.

---

## 1. Pre-show Checklist (30 min before doors)

Prepare the entry area for smooth operation.

*   **Scanner Device:** Obtain designated Blues Room scanning phone/tablet.
*   **App Login:** Log into the `/admin/booking/tickets/scan` application.
*   **Connectivity:** Confirm strong Wi-Fi or cellular data connection.
*   **Power:** Verify device battery is above 75%. Have a charged power bank available.
*   **Entry Path:** Ensure entry/exit route is clear, well-lit, and free of obstructions.
*   **Cash Box:** If walk-up sales are anticipated, secure the cash box with starting float.
*   **"SOLD OUT" Sign:** Have the sign ready for display if capacity is reached.
*   **Show Details:** Review tonight's specific show notes, capacity, and scheduled times.
*   **Emergency Contacts:** Confirm location of the emergency contact list (Amy, Tracy, security).
*   **Amy Check-in:** Briefly confirm Amy's availability for immediate questions.

---

## 2. Ticket Scanning

The `/admin/booking/tickets/scan` application is your primary tool. One scan equals one entry.

*   **Process:**
    1.  Request guest's digital ticket (QR code or barcode).
    2.  Scan using the device camera.
    3.  Confirm green "SUCCESS" message.
    4.  Admit guest.
*   **Scan Rejection Handling:**
    *   **"ALREADY USED":**
        *   Politely inform guest their ticket has already been scanned.
        *   Ask if they've already entered and exited, or if someone else in their party entered.
        *   Verify guest ID against the ticket name if possible.
        *   If no clear explanation, escalate to Amy/Tracy. Do NOT admit.
    *   **"REFUNDED / VOID":**
        *   Inform guest the ticket is invalid.
        *   They must purchase a new ticket (if available) or contact customer support for resolution. Do NOT admit.
    *   **"WRONG SHOW":**
        *   Inform guest the ticket is for a different event.
        *   Direct them to the correct event if applicable, or offer walk-up purchase for the current show if capacity allows. Do NOT admit on an incorrect ticket.
    *   **Technical Issue (Scanner/App):**
        *   Restart the `/admin/booking/tickets/scan` app.
        *   Check Wi-Fi/data connection.
        *   If persistent, manually search guest's name/email in the app (last resort, slower).
        *   If still unable to scan or find, escalate to Amy.

## 3. Walk-up Sales

Only permitted if the show is NOT sold out. Confirm capacity with Amy before initiating.

*   **Payment Methods:**
    *   **Stripe Terminal (Preferred):** Connect the terminal, enter ticket price, process card payment.
    *   **Phone Link:** Generate a payment link via the POS system, send to guest's phone, confirm payment notification.
    *   **Cash:** Accept exact change only. Place cash immediately into the secure cash box.
*   **Issuing Walk-up Ticket/Entry:**
    *   After payment, use the `/admin/booking/tickets/scan` app to create a "Walk-up" entry or immediately scan a generated walk-up QR code. This ensures the guest is accounted for in the system's capacity count.
    *   Record walk-up details (time, payment method, amount) on the designated walk-up log sheet.

## 4. Common Door Issues

Handle these situations with professionalism and clear communication.

*   **"I have a ticket but I'm not on the list":**
    1.  Politely ask for their confirmation email or QR code.
    2.  Attempt a manual search by full name and email in the scanner app.
    3.  Check the "Will Call" list if provided.
    4.  If still no record, and the guest is insistent, politely explain the system shows no record. Offer to check with Amy/Tracy. Do NOT admit without a valid record or explicit override from Amy/Tracy.
*   **Transferred Ticket:**
    1.  Request the original purchaser's name/email AND the recipient's name/ID.
    2.  Check for transfer notes in the system (if visible).
    3.  If a valid transfer is confirmed (e.g., new QR code issued to recipient), scan the new ticket.
    4.  If unclear or suspicious, escalate to Tracy.
*   **Comp Tickets (Partners/Staff):**
    1.  All comp tickets MUST be on the pre-approved "Comp List" provided by Amy.
    2.  Verify guest's ID against the list.
    3.  Do NOT admit anyone claiming a comp ticket who is not on the list without direct, explicit approval from Amy or Tracy.
*   **Press List:**
    1.  Similar to comps, press entries MUST be on the "Press List" provided by Amy.
    2.  Verify guest's ID against the list.
    3.  Do NOT admit without being on the list or direct approval.

## 5. Capacity + Safety

Maintaining safe capacity is paramount.

*   **Room Max:** The Blues Room maximum capacity is **150 people**.
*   **Stopping Sales:**
    *   When the scanned ticket count approaches **140**, immediately notify Amy.
    *   Prepare the "SOLD OUT" sign.
    *   Once Amy confirms, cease all walk-up sales. Display the "SOLD OUT" sign prominently.
*   **Pausing Entry:**
    *   If the room *feels* overcrowded, regardless of the scan count, or if directed by Amy/security, immediately pause entry.
    *   Politely inform waiting guests there is a temporary hold due to capacity.
    *   Only resume entry when directed by Amy or when space visibly clears.
*   **Emergencies:** In case of fire alarm, fight, medical emergency, or other critical incidents, immediately follow the Blues Room Emergency Protocol (posted near the door). Prioritize guest and staff safety. Escalate to Tracy and Amy immediately.

## 7. End-of-Night Checklist

Ensure all entries are reconciled and the venue is secured.

*   **Count Tickets Scanned:** Use the `/admin/booking/tickets/scan` app to retrieve the final "Total Scanned" count.
*   **Reconcile Walk-ups:**
    *   Count all cash from walk-up sales.
    *   Verify cash total against the walk-up log sheet and any digital walk-up entries.
    *   Secure cash box.
*   **Lock Door:** Ensure the main entry door is securely locked after all guests and non-essential staff have exited.
*   **Send Recap to Amy:** Email a concise summary to Amy, including:
    *   Total tickets scanned from the app.
    *   Total number of walk-up sales (broken down by payment type: cash, card, link).
    *   Total cash collected from walk-ups.
    *   Any significant issues or incidents encountered during the night.

## 8. When to Escalate

Know who to contact for different types of issues.

*   **Amy (Operational / Booking):**
    *   Capacity concerns / decision to stop sales.
    *   Significant technical issues with the scanner or POS system.
    *   Unresolved discrepancies on comp/press lists.
    *   General operational questions or issues impacting smooth entry flow.
    *   Any situation not explicitly covered that affects the show's operation.
    *   *Contact via:* Phone call / text.
*   **Tracy (Guest Experience / Security):**
    *   Disruptive, aggressive, or confrontational guests.
    *   Unresolved "not on list" issues where a guest is becoming agitated.
    *   Any medical incidents involving guests or staff.
    *   Security concerns (e.g., suspicious activity, potential threats).
    *   Any situation threatening guest or staff comfort, safety, or well-being.
    *   *Contact via:* Phone call / radio (if available).
*   **Chase (Rare Strategic):**
    *   Only for *extremely* unusual, high-level strategic issues that threaten the entire event or venue's reputation (e.g., major artist no-show, significant PR crisis).
    *   **Do NOT contact Chase for routine operational or guest issues.** Amy or Tracy will typically handle escalations before Chase is involved.
    *   *Contact via:* Amy or Tracy will initiate if necessary.
