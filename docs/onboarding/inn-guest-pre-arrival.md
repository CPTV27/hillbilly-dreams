### What to Do in Natchez

Natchez offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and Southern charm. Here are a few of my personal favorites that I believe truly capture the spirit of our town:

1.  **A Stroll Along the River Overlook:** You simply must take time to stand at the bluff overlooking the mighty Mississippi. The views, especially at sunrise or sunset, are breathtaking. It’s a perfect spot for quiet reflection or a leisurely walk, watching the barges glide by. You can even walk down to the "Under-the-Hill" area for a glimpse into Natchez's wilder past.
2.  **Antebellum Tour Walk:** Natchez is renowned for its magnificent antebellum homes. Instead of just driving by, I highly recommend a guided walking tour. You'll hear the fascinating stories behind these grand mansions and gain a deeper appreciation for the town's rich history. Many of these homes are still privately owned and beautifully preserved. My personal favorites to recommend for a tour are **Longwood** (for its unfinished, poignant story) and **Stanton Hall** (for its sheer grandeur).
3.  **Breakfast at The Little Easy:** Just a short walk from the Inn, The Little Easy offers the most delightful breakfast and brunch. Their pecan pancakes are legendary, and their coffee is strong enough to get your day started right. It’s a cozy, local spot with a warm atmosphere.
4.  **Lunch at Pearl Street Pasta:** For a delicious midday meal, Pearl Street Pasta is a local gem. Don't let the name fool you; while their pasta dishes are excellent, they also have fantastic salads, sandwiches, and daily specials that incorporate fresh, local ingredients. Their outdoor seating is lovely on a nice day.
5.  **Dinner at The Carriage House Restaurant:** For a truly special Natchez dining experience, The Carriage House, located on the grounds of Stanton Hall, offers classic Southern cuisine in an elegant setting. Their fried chicken is a standout, and their pecan pie is simply divine. It's perfect for a celebratory evening.
6.  **Dinner at Fat Mama's Tamales:** If you're looking for something more casual and uniquely Natchez, you absolutely have to try Fat Mama's Tamales. Their "Knock-You-Naked" margaritas are famous, and their hot tamales are a local institution. It's a lively, no-frills spot that's always a good time.
7.  **Explore the Natchez Trace Parkway:** For those who love a scenic drive, hop on the Natchez Trace Parkway. It's a historic, 444-mile parkway stretching from Natchez to Nashville, Tennessee. Even a short drive will immerse you in serene natural beauty and offer glimpses into ancient history with various historical markers and walking trails along the way.
