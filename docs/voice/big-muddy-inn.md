# Big Muddy Inn: Brand Voice Guide

## Voice in a Nutshell

Big Muddy Inn speaks directly and authentically. Our voice is grounded in Natchez and its music. We invite guests to a real experience, not a manufactured one. We are warm but never sentimental, Southern without being folksy. We state facts clearly and with confidence, reflecting the genuine nature of our hospitality and the energy of the Blues Room. We cut the fluff.

## Voice Attributes

1.  **Direct:** We get to the point. No flowery language. We state what is, clearly.
2.  **Authentic:** Our words reflect the genuine experience of Natchez and the Inn. We don't oversell or invent.
3.  **Grounded:** Our language connects to the history, place, and music of Natchez, Mississippi. We speak from a place of local knowledge.
4.  **Confident:** We know what we offer. Our voice holds authority without arrogance.

## Tone

Our tone is **warm but not sentimental.** We welcome guests genuinely. We are not saccharine or overly emotional. We are hospitable without being cloying.

Our tone is **Southern but not folksy.** We acknowledge our roots in Natchez, Mississippi. We avoid clichés, stereotypes, or cutesy phrasing. Our Southern identity is about heritage, rhythm, and atmosphere, not contrived charm.

## Word List: Do's and Don'ts

### Do Use

*   Natchez, Mississippi, Delta, river
*   Blues, music, live, venue, stage, sound
*   Inn, stay, room, guest, hospitality
*   Authentic, genuine, real, historic, original
*   Experience, atmosphere, intimate, local
*   Book, visit, explore, discover, hear

### Don't Use

*   Leverage, synergy, unlock, elevate, transform, journey (marketing jargon)
*   Curated, bespoke, unique offerings, quaint, charming (overused, sentimental)
*   Delightful, enchanting, magical (too sentimental, not direct)
*   Y'all, bless your heart (too folksy, stereotypical)
*   Strive to, endeavor to, aim to (lacks confidence, indirect)

## Example Sentences

1.  The Blues Room hosts live music every weekend.
2.  Stay at Big Muddy Inn. Experience Natchez.
3.  Our rooms offer comfort after a night of music.
4.  Tracy Alderson-Allen is your innkeeper.
5.  Big Muddy Magazine covers the music scene here.

## Anti-Examples

1.  Embark on a transformative journey through the rich tapestry of Natchez's storied past, where our unique accommodations unlock unparalleled experiences. (Marketing jargon, sentimental, indirect)
2.  We strive to provide a delightful and charming stay for all our cherished guests. (Lacks confidence, sentimental, folksy)
3.  Come on down, y'all, and let us bless your heart with some true Southern hospitality. (Folksy, stereotypical, sentimental)
4.  Our bespoke rooms are thoughtfully curated to elevate your stay to new heights of luxury. (Marketing jargon, overblown)
5.  Big Muddy Magazine endeavors to bring you the most compelling stories from the music world. (Lacks confidence, indirect)

## Inn Voice vs. Touring/Records/Radio Voice

The Big Muddy Inn voice is guest-facing. It focuses on hospitality, the stay, the experience of Natchez, and the atmosphere of the Blues Room. It is inviting, informative, and direct, aimed at travelers considering a visit.

The Touring/Records/Radio voice is industry-facing. It addresses musicians, agents, labels, and media. Its purpose is professional communication regarding bookings, releases, and industry news. It is more formal, transactional, and focused on the business of music. It carries the same bluntness but prioritizes clarity for professional engagement over guest invitation.
