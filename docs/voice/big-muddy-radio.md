## Big Muddy Radio: Brand Voice Document

### Voice Attributes

1.  **Enthusiastic & Genuine:** We don't just *like* the music; we *feel* it, deeply. Our passion is palpable and infectious, born from a genuine love for the artists and their craft. This isn't a job; it's a calling.
2.  **Curious & Exploratory:** We're always digging, listening, and discovering. Our voice reflects an eagerness to share new finds, uncover forgotten gems, and explore the connections that weave through the music we play. We're on a journey, and we invite our listeners along.
3.  **Discerning & Contextual:** We have an ear for what's truly compelling, and we can articulate *why* a song or artist resonates. We provide rich context—historical, cultural, personal—that elevates the listening experience without being academic or elitist.
4.  **Rooted & Resonant:** Our sound is deeply connected to the geography and spirit of the Mississippi corridor, from its Delta blues origins to its contemporary echoes. This regional consciousness adds a unique flavor and depth, making our voice feel authentic and grounded without ever being insular or parochial.
5.  **Intimate & Conversational:** We speak directly to our listeners, as if sharing a favorite record with a trusted friend. It's a one-on-one exchange, respectful and engaging, treating every listener as a fellow obsessive with a shared love for the music.

### Tone

Our tone is consistently **warm**, inviting listeners into a shared space of discovery and appreciation. We are **knowledgeable**, offering insights and context that enrich the music, but always approachable. We are **opinionated**, not afraid to champion an artist or explain *why* a particular track hits different, yet we are **never preachy**. Our opinions are offered as invitations to deeper listening, not as definitive pronouncements. We aim to inspire curiosity and connection, not deliver lectures.

### Vocabulary: Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
*   Use evocative, sensory language to describe music: "that guitar line just *sings*," "a rhythm that feels like the river itself," "lyrics that cut straight to the bone."
*   Connect music to its origins, influences, and impact: "you can hear the echoes of [earlier artist] in that," "this track feels like a humid summer night in Memphis."
*   Emphasize craft, storytelling, and the human element behind the sound.
*   Use terms that reflect deep listening and appreciation: "nuance," "texture," "groove," "soul," "raw energy."
*   Incorporate regional flavor naturally: "downriver sound," "up Highway 61," "Delta heartbeat," when it genuinely serves the context.

**DON'T:**
*   Use corporate radio jargon: "today's hottest hits," "all the hits," "non-stop music," "your favorite tunes."
*   Employ algorithmic-playlist-speak: "songs you might like," "similar to X," "trending now," "perfect for your commute."
*   Offer generic, bland descriptors: "good song," "great artist," "nice track."
*   Focus on commercial metrics: "millions of streams," "chart-topping," "critically acclaimed" (unless it's to highlight *why* it's acclaimed, not just *that* it is).
*   Sound like a machine or an algorithm. We are human, passionate, and imperfectly real.
*   Name-drop without providing meaningful context or insight.
