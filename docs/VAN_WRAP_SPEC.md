# Big Muddy — Sprinter Van Wrap Spec (v2)

**Vehicle:** Mercedes Sprinter (high roof, long wheelbase)
**Base:** Matte black full wrap
**Pickup:** This week (April 2026)
**Contact:** Chase Pierson — me@chasepierson.tv

---

## The Concept

The van is the ecosystem vehicle — it represents the entire Big Muddy media company, Studio C production, and every partner brand. It carries musicians, cameras, audio gear, and a portable PA to shows across the Mississippi corridor. It's a rolling billboard for a media-production company that has its shit together.

**Mood:** Matte black with gold/brass lettering. Premium, confident, road-ready. Tour bus energy at Sprinter scale. The kind of van that makes people ask "who's that?"

---

## Wrap Design

### Base
- **Full matte black wrap** — bumper to bumper
- Satin or matte finish, not gloss

### Driver Side — Big Muddy Touring
- **"BIG MUDDY TOURING"** in gold/brass metallic vinyl
- Font: Clean serif, medium weight (Playfair Display or similar editorial serif)
- Size: ~8-10" letter height, readable from across a parking lot
- Placement: Centered on the flat panel area between the wheel wells
- Color: Metallic gold/brass (#c8943e — warm gold, not yellow, not chrome)

### Passenger Side — Studio C
- **"STUDIO C"** in the same gold/brass metallic, same size
- Below it, smaller: **"Video · Audio · Production"** in gold, ~2" height
- This side faces the venue when you pull up to load in — the production brand faces the client

### Rear — The Ecosystem

The rear doors are the showcase. This is what everyone sees in traffic, at venues, parked at festivals.

**Layout (top to bottom):**

1. **"BIG MUDDY"** centered across both doors, gold metallic, ~6" letter height
2. **Corridor map** — a clean, stylized line map showing the route from Memphis to New Orleans
   - Gold line on matte black
   - Cities marked as dots with names in small gold type
   - Cities: Memphis, Clarksdale, Oxford, Tupelo, Holly Springs, Greenwood, Indianola, Greenville, Jackson, Vicksburg, **Natchez** (larger/highlighted), Baton Rouge, New Orleans
   - The map should feel editorial, not GPS — think magazine infographic
   - Natchez highlighted with a slightly larger dot or a small star
3. **Partner logos strip** — across the bottom of the rear doors, small and clean:
   - Studio C
   - Utopia Studios
   - Bearsville Creative
   - Big Muddy Magazine
   - Big Muddy Records
   - Big Muddy Radio
   - All in gold, all the same small size (~1.5" height)
   - Evenly spaced in a single row
   - These read like sponsors on a race car or a film credit block — understated, credible

### Front / Hood
- **No branding on the front.** Keep it clean.

### Accent Details
- Thin gold pinstripe along the belt line (horizontal body line) — both sides
- Small gold "BMT" monogram on driver and passenger doors near the handle (~3" circle)

### QR Code — Rear Door

- **One QR code**, bottom-right of the rear door, gold on black
- Size: ~6" x 6" — scannable from a car length behind
- Links to: **bigmuddytouring.com/van** (or similar short route)
- **The landing page has to be fire.** Not a homepage. Not a link tree. Something that makes someone at a red light think "these people are for real."
- Content ideas for the QR landing page:
  - Latest show footage (auto-updated)
  - Latest magazine feature
  - Live radio stream (one tap to listen)
  - Photo from last night's show
  - The corridor map (interactive)
  - "Follow the van" — where we're headed next
- The page should change every time someone scans it because the media company is always producing new content

### What NOT To Do
- No phone numbers
- No "call us for booking" text
- No full-color graphics or photographs
- No glossy finish
- No chrome — matte/satin metallic only

## Corridor Map Detail

The rear map should show the Mississippi corridor route as a single flowing line (not a literal road map). Think of it as a stylized route diagram — like a subway map or a magazine illustration.

```
Memphis
  |
Clarksdale — Oxford — Tupelo
  |           |
Holly Springs  Greenwood
  |
Indianola — Greenville
  |
Jackson
  |
Vicksburg
  |
★ NATCHEZ ★
  |
Baton Rouge
  |
New Orleans
```

The line flows roughly north-to-south with branches for cities off the main corridor. Natchez is the anchor — bigger dot, maybe a small star. The whole thing should fit in the upper 60% of the rear doors, with the partner strip below.

## Notes for the Shop

- This van will be photographed constantly — it's a vehicle AND a marketing asset
- The gold on matte black contrast is the entire design. Don't add colors.
- If the gold metallic vinyl comes in different sheens, go warmer and more matte
- The corridor map is the signature element — take time on it
- The partner strip on the rear should feel like film credits: professional, not cluttered
- The van drives highways and back roads across MS, LA, TN, and up to NY. Road-durable wrap required.
- Two sides, two brands: Big Muddy Touring (driver) and Studio C (passenger). The rear unifies everything.
