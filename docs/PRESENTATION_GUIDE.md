## The Flow (25 minutes)

### Slide 1: Title (30 seconds)
- "I want to show you where we are and where we're going."
- Don't explain anything yet. Let the title breathe.

### Slide 2: The Thesis (2 minutes)
- "This is from the Outsider Economics book I wrote. It's the whole company in one sentence."
- Read the quote aloud. Slowly.
- "That's what Big Muddy is. We're building a new system next to the old one."
- **Tracy hook:** "Tracy, this is why I keep saying the directory isn't just a website. It's the economic infrastructure."

### Slide 3: What We Are (2 minutes)
- Read the one-sentence description aloud.
- "No jargon. No corporate structure. This is what a stranger needs to know."
- Emphasize: "We OWN the whole stack. No landlords. No platform taking 70%."

### Slide 4: The OE Connection (3 minutes)
- "The book isn't just theory. Every chapter maps to something we've already built."
- Walk through the three mappings.
- **Key moment:** "The Coordination Premium — a $1,000 show generates $500 more at the Inn. That's not a projection. That's math."

### Slide 5: The Numbers (3 minutes)
- Let the three numbers land: $321K → $871K → $1.53M
- "We're at $7,500 a month right now. Break-even is $15,000. We're halfway there."
- "The moat is real: no competitor owns a magazine AND a radio station AND a record label AND a hotel. Nobody can copy this because nobody else has all the pieces."
- **Tracy hook:** "This is why the directory matters. $10K/month from subscriptions alone by year-end."

### Slide 6: Roles (5 minutes — the most important slide)
- Go through each person's card.
- **For Tracy:** "You're Editor-in-Chief of the magazine now. AI drafts articles in your voice. You approve them from your phone. You also run finance and the Inn. And you're Delta Dawn's first power user."
- **For Amy:** "You're the talent AND the operator. Your music is on Big Muddy Records. You host the radio. You run the bar and the shows. You're the energy that makes people want to come to Natchez."
- End with: "Equal thirds. Equal say. Different superpowers."

### Slide 7: What We Built Tonight (2 minutes)
- "While you were driving back from New Orleans, we built all of this."
- Let the grid speak for itself.
- "One night. Four AI agents. This is the machine working."

### Slide 8: This Week (3 minutes)
- Go through each person's action items.
- **For Tracy:** Lead with the 1065 filing. "$440 a month penalty. We need to file this week."
- **For Amy:** "Tuesday morning priorities before you leave at 1 PM."
- End with the closing line.

## What NOT to say

- Don't say "ecosystem" or "platform" — say "the company" or "the machine"
- Don't explain n8n, Prisma, or database tables
- Don't mention Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity by name — say "the AI tools"
- Don't say "sovereign stack" or "civic-commerce"
- Don't say "we're building a Viacom" — say "we own a radio station, a magazine, a record label, a directory, and a hotel"
- Don't lead with technology. Lead with the story. Lead with the numbers. Lead with their roles.
