# THE BIG MUDDY: FINANCIAL WATERFALL & CAPITAL STRUCTURE
## Investor-Grade Financial Architecture

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document presents a comprehensive financial architecture for The Big Muddy, a vertically integrated hospitality and media venture in Natchez, Mississippi. The structure prioritizes capital preservation while creating aligned incentives across all stakeholders.

**Core Principles:**
1. Capital Partners receive first claim on all cash flows
2. Real estate asset protection is paramount
3. Operator incentives align with long-term value creation
4. Talent compensation drives performance
5. Sustainable reinvestment ensures growth

## SECTION 2: CAPITAL PARTNER PROTECTION MECHANISMS

### 2.1 Structural Protections

#### A. Real Estate Asset Protection

| Mechanism | Description | Implementation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Separate Legal Entity** | Real estate held in LLC distinct from operating entity | Property LLC leases to Operating LLC |
| **First Mortgage Position** | Capital Partners hold first lien on real property | Recorded deed of trust |
| **Title Insurance** | Protects against title defects | Owner's policy at closing |
| **Property Insurance** | Comprehensive coverage including business interruption | Minimum $2M coverage |
| **UCC Filings** | Secured interest in equipment and FF&E | Filed on all major assets |

#### B. Financial Covenants

| Covenant | Threshold | Testing Frequency | Remedy for Breach |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) | ≥ 1.25x | Quarterly | Cash sweep, cure period |
| Loan-to-Value (LTV) | ≤ 65% | Annually | Additional equity contribution |
| Minimum Liquidity | 3 months OpEx | Monthly | Operating restrictions |
| Capital Expenditure Reserve | 5% of revenue | Monthly | Mandatory funding |
| Insurance Compliance | 100% maintained | Monthly | Force-placed insurance |

#### C. Control Rights

| Matter | Capital Partner Approval Required |
|--------|-----------------------------------|
| Sale of real estate asset | YES - unanimous |
| Additional debt encumbrance | YES - unanimous |
| Major capital expenditures >$25K | YES - majority |
| Change in business model | YES - unanimous |
| Operator removal | YES - majority |
| Annual budget | YES - majority |
| Operating reserve deployment >$10K | YES - majority |

### 2.2 Yield Expectations & Structure

#### Preferred Return Structure

| Component | Rate | Terms |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Current Preferred Return | 8% annually | Paid monthly from CAD |
| Cumulative Preferred Return | 10% annually | Accrues if not paid current |
| Compounding | Annual | Unpaid amounts compound |
| Catch-Up Provision | 100% | All CAD goes to Capital Partners until caught up |

#### Total Return Target

| Scenario | Year 1-3 | Year 4-7 | Year 8+ |
|----------|----------|----------|---------|
| Conservative | 8-10% | 10-12% | 12-15% |
| Base Case | 10-12% | 12-15% | 15-18% |
| Upside Case | 12-15% | 15-20% | 20-25% |

### 2.3 Exit & Liquidity Provisions

| Provision | Terms |
|-----------|-------|
| **Put Option** | Capital Partners may force sale after Year 5 |
| **Call Option** | Operating entity may buy out Capital Partners at FMV after Year 3 |
| **Tag-Along Rights** | Capital Partners participate in any operator sale |
| **Drag-Along Rights** | If 75% want to sell, all must sell |
| **Right of First Refusal** | On any transfer of interests |
