## DeepSouthDirectory.com

---

## WHAT THIS SITE IS

DeepSouthDirectory.com is where businesses sign up. It's not measurablybetter.life — that's the platform page for operators and institutions. This is the neighborhood.

A business owner lands here because their chamber told them about it, their mayor sponsored it, or the guy who runs the hotel down the street said "you should be on this."

The voice is local. Warm. Unhurried. Not a tech company talking. A neighbor talking.

---

## VOICE RULES (different from MB)

- First person plural: "we" = the businesses on Main Street, not the company
- No jargon. No "AI-powered." No "platform."
- Talk about the business, not the technology
- The technology is invisible. The value is obvious.
- Sounds like a chamber of commerce that actually gets it — not a Silicon Valley startup

## DESIGN NOTES

- Warm paper background (#FAFAF8) — same as MB but friendlier
- Abril Fatface headlines, Inter body — consistent with the system
- Amber accent (#B45309) for CTAs
- NO dark hero sections. This isn't a tech product page. Keep it light, open, welcoming.
- Photography would make this sing — placeholder with solid color cards until real photos exist
- Mobile-first. Most business owners will see this on their phone.
- The search bar should be prominent. People come here to find a business or list theirs.

---

## WHAT THE DIRECTORY IS NOT

- Not Yelp. We don't have user reviews on the directory. Reviews come from Google.
- Not a marketplace. We don't take a cut of transactions.
- Not an advertising platform. No promoted listings, no pay-to-rank.
- Not a national play. This is the Deep South corridor. Memphis to New Orleans.

---

## THE FLOW

1. Business owner hears about it (chamber, CVB, neighbor, Chase walking in with a tablet)
2. Goes to DeepSouthDirectory.com
3. Types business name → we auto-populate from Google
4. Listing is live in 3 minutes, free
5. They see their reviews summarized, their rating, their competition
6. They think "I want more"
7. They connect QuickBooks ($20/mo, 7-day free trial)
8. Daily briefings start. Review monitoring starts. They're hooked.
9. They outgrow $20, upgrade to $99 for marketing automation and alerts
10. The directory is the top of the funnel. MB is what they grow into.
