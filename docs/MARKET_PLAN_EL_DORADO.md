## Why El Dorado

El Dorado is a 17,000-person town that invests like a city ten times its size. The Murphy family (Murphy Oil, Murphy USA) built a $54M arts district, funded a universal college scholarship ($50M), endowed a hospital, and seeded a $25M charitable foundation — all in the same town. Voters just passed a 1% sales tax generating $1M+/month for economic development. A $1.5B lithium plant starts construction mid-2026. Commercial air service returned via Contour Airlines.

This town already does what MBT systematizes. They have the venues, the funding, the institutional will, and the cross-sector collaboration. What they don't have is the digital spine connecting it all into one measurable system.

Tracy has deep high-level relationships here. That's the entry point.

## The Pitch by Stakeholder

### To the El Dorado Wins Board
"You're spending $12M+ a year of voter-approved tax money on economic development. You need to show voters where it went and what it did. MBT gives you a live dashboard: how many businesses are active downtown, how many are getting found online, what the foot traffic looks like, what shows are driving commerce. Your next report to voters has real numbers, not ribbon-cutting photos."

### To Steven Jones / Chamber
"You just took over as CEO with a $400K mandate and a town about to absorb a $1.5B lithium plant. The workers coming for Standard Lithium need places to eat, shop, and live. The businesses serving them need to be findable. MBT onboards every business in town into one directory with promotion, media, and measurement built in. You show the board a participation dashboard, not a membership spreadsheet."

### To Pam Griffin / MAD
"MAD spent $54M building the best small-town cultural scene in America. Every show at the Amphitheater or Music Hall should fill downtown restaurants and shops. Right now that connection is invisible. MBT makes it visible — every show promotes the businesses around it, every business listing links to the events happening this week. The 2:1 multiplier we proved in Natchez works here too."

### To MainStreet El Dorado
"You've been an accredited Main Street program since 1987. You won the Great American Main Street Award. MBT is the digital operating system for that program. Every merchant gets listed, promoted, and measured. You get a monthly report showing downtown vitality by the numbers. Your funders see real data."

### To Tracy's High-Level Contacts
"We built this in Natchez. It works. The Inn, the touring circuit, the magazine, the radio, the directory — all one system. Natchez has 400 new businesses since 2020 and we can measure every one. El Dorado has the same ingredients — better funding, bigger venues, a generational investment thesis from Murphy. What you don't have is the connective tissue. That's what MBT is."

## The Standard Lithium Angle

A $1.5B commercial lithium plant starts construction mid-2026 in nearby Lafayette County. This will:
- Bring hundreds of construction workers, then permanent employees
- Create demand for housing, food, retail, services
- Transform the regional economy

El Dorado needs commercial infrastructure to capture that growth for Main Street — not just the industrial corridor. MBT is how you make sure the lithium boom feeds downtown, not just the plant.

The pitch: "Standard Lithium is going to change this region. The question is whether that change flows through downtown El Dorado or bypasses it. MBT connects the new workers to the businesses, the businesses to the directory, the directory to the visitors. You capture the boom instead of watching it drive past."
