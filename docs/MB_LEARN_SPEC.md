## 1. FUNDING REALITY CHECK

### E-Rate: NOT a fit for the software product

E-Rate funds **infrastructure only** — pipes, switches, access points, managed network services. AI-powered educational software is explicitly ineligible under all current categories.

- Category 1: Internet connectivity (ISP, WAN)
- Category 2: Internal connections, BMIC, managed internal broadband services

**Bottom line:** E-Rate will not pay for MB Learn. Do not position against E-Rate. However, E-Rate savings on infrastructure *free up* other budget dollars districts can redirect to software.

### Title IV-A: THIS IS THE PLAY

Title IV-A (Student Support and Academic Enrichment Grants) under ESSA is the primary funding vehicle.

**Three content pillars:**
1. Well-Rounded Education (20% minimum spend for districts >$30K)
2. Safe and Healthy Students (20% minimum spend for districts >$30K)
3. **Effective Use of Technology** — MB Learn lives here

**Key authority:** The U.S. Department of Education issued a **Dear Colleague Letter on July 22, 2025** explicitly affirming that Title IV-A funds can be used to:
- Procure AI instructional tools
- Expand access to personalized AI learning materials
- Train educators on AI use
- Enhance tutoring, curriculum, and career advising with AI

This DCL is the single most important document in our sales kit. It removes all ambiguity.

**Mississippi Title IV-A allocation:** ~$15-18M statewide across ~148 districts. Small districts typically receive $10K-$30K each.

**Key rule for small districts (<$30K allocation):** They are NOT bound by the 20%/20% content area minimums. They can spend 100% of their Title IV-A on Effective Use of Technology. This is huge for us — most of our target districts are small.

### Blended Funding Strategy (the real play)

| Source | What it pays for | Est. per district |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Title IV-A (EUT) | Software subscription | $10K-$30K/yr |
| Title I | Supplemental AI tutoring for low-income students | Varies (large pot) |
| Title II-A | Teacher PD on AI integration | $5K-$15K/yr |
| State/district discretionary | Gap fill | As needed |

**Recommended positioning:** "MB Learn is a grant-funded solution. We help you write the application."

## 2. WHAT MB LEARN DELIVERS

### Product Definition

MB Learn is an AI-powered adaptive learning platform for K-12 school districts. It delivers:

**Core:**
- AI tutoring engine — personalized, adaptive instruction across core subjects (ELA, Math, Science)
- Digital literacy curriculum — students learn *with* AI and *about* AI
- Teacher dashboard — real-time student progress, intervention alerts, standards alignment reporting

**Grant Compliance Layer (the differentiator):**
- Pre-built alignment maps to Mississippi College and Career Readiness Standards
- Automated Title IV-A reporting — activity logs, usage data, outcome metrics formatted for ESSA Consolidated Application reporting
- Comprehensive Needs Assessment template pre-populated with district data
- Annual impact report generator for grant renewal

**Professional Development Module:**
- Teacher training on AI integration (Title II-A fundable)
- Administrator training on AI governance and policy
- Certification pathway — "AI-Ready Educator" credential

### Why This Wins

Most ed-tech vendors sell software and leave districts to figure out funding and compliance. MB Learn sells the complete package: software + grant application support + compliance reporting. The district's total out-of-pocket is near zero when grants are stacked correctly.

This is the sales weapon. We don't just sell software — we hand the district a ready-to-submit grant application.

- Pre-written narrative linking MB Learn to ESSA Section 4109 (Effective Use of Technology)
- Direct quotes from the July 2025 Dear Colleague Letter
- Alignment to Mississippi College and Career Readiness Standards
- Cost-benefit analysis template (personalized per district)

**Document 2: Comprehensive Needs Assessment Insert**
- Technology needs section pre-populated with:
  - District's current device-to-student ratio (from Mississippi Connects data)
  - Achievement gap data (from MDE report cards)
  - Digital literacy gap analysis
  - AI readiness assessment

**Document 3: Budget Narrative Template**
- Line-item budget showing MB Learn as Title IV-A EUT expenditure
- Blended funding model showing Title I + Title II-A + Title IV-A stacking

| Title IV-A allocations too small (<$12K) for some districts | High | Blended funding model (stack Title I + Title IV-A + Title II); consortium option for tiny districts |
