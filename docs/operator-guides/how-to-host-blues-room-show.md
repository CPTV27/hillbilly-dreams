# Blues Room Show Operator Guide: From Booking to Encore

This guide outlines the end-to-end process for successfully hosting a Blues Room show, designed for Amy as the primary Showrunner, with Tracy providing essential Innkeeper backup. Our aim is to deliver exceptional experiences for both our esteemed artists and our cherished guests, upholding the Blues Room's reputation for quality music and unparalleled hospitality.

## Introduction

Welcome to the Blues Room Show Operator Guide. This document serves as your comprehensive playbook for managing a show from its inception to its final curtain call. As Showrunner, Amy, you are the primary orchestrator, responsible for the artistic and operational success of each event. Tracy, as Innkeeper Backup, plays a crucial role in ensuring seamless integration with the Inn's hospitality standards and resources.

Our philosophy is built on two pillars: **Artist Care** and **Guest Experience**. Every step of this workflow is designed to uphold these values, ensuring artists feel valued and supported, and guests leave with unforgettable memories.

Let's make some magic happen.

## 1. Booking the Act (via Big Muddy Touring)

The journey begins with securing the talent that defines the Blues Room experience.

**Amy's Role:** Lead the artist selection and booking process.

1.  **Talent Identification & Research:**
    *   Continuously research emerging and established blues, roots, and Americana artists who align with the Blues Room's aesthetic and our audience's preferences.
    *   Attend industry showcases, listen to new releases, and monitor social media buzz.
    *   Consider artists with a strong live presence and a genuine connection to their craft.

2.  **Initial Contact & Inquiry (via Big Muddy Touring):**
    *   **Primary Channel:** All booking inquiries for the Blues Room are routed through our trusted partner, **Big Muddy Touring**. They manage artist availability, routing, and initial financial discussions.
    *   **Communication:** Amy will initiate contact with Big Muddy Touring's designated representative for potential acts. Provide them with preferred dates, our venue capacity, and a brief overview of the Blues Room's unique offering.
    *   **Information Exchange:** Request artist press kits, technical riders (tech riders), hospitality riders, and current touring schedules.

3.  **Contract Negotiation & Offer:**
    *   **Review Riders:** Carefully examine the artist's tech rider (stage plot, input list, backline requirements) and hospitality rider (catering, accommodation, green room needs). Assess feasibility and potential costs.
    *   **Financial Terms:** Negotiate the artist fee. This typically involves a guarantee, a percentage of ticket sales (or a combination thereof), and sometimes a merch split. Big Muddy Touring will facilitate this.
    *   **Hospitality & Logistics:** Confirm our ability to meet hospitality requests (e.g., complimentary rooms at the Inn, specific dietary needs). If a request is outside our standard offering, discuss alternatives with Big Muddy Touring.
    *   **Drafting:** Once terms are verbally agreed upon, Big Muddy Touring will issue a formal contract. Amy reviews this thoroughly, ensuring all agreed-upon details are accurately reflected.

4.  **Confirmation & Internal Communication:**
    *   **Signed Contract:** Upon receipt of the signed contract from the artist's representative, the booking is confirmed.
    *   **Notify Tracy:** Immediately inform Tracy of the confirmed show, including artist name, date, anticipated load-in/soundcheck times, and any specific accommodation or catering requests from the hospitality rider. This allows Tracy to preemptively block rooms or arrange kitchen support.
    *   **Add to Master Calendar:** Input the show details into the Blues Room master calendar and the Inn's shared events calendar.

**Key Principle:** Maintain open, clear, and professional communication with Big Muddy Touring. They are our conduit to the artists and crucial to securing top-tier talent.

## 2. Setting Up the Show in `/admin/booking/resources`

Once the act is booked, the next step is to configure the event within our internal system, making it available for ticketing and management.

**Amy's Role:** System setup and configuration.

1.  **Access the Admin Panel:**
    *   Log in to the `/admin/booking/resources` portal using your showrunner credentials.

2.  **Create a New Event:**
    *   Navigate to "Events" or "New Booking."
    *   Select "Blues Room Show" as the event type.

3.  **Input Core Event Details:**
    *   **Event Name:** Use a clear, consistent format (e.g., `[Artist Name] - [Date]`).
    *   **Description:**
        *   Copy/paste the artist's official bio.
        *   Include genre, key selling points, and a captivating hook.
        *   Embed links to the artist's website, social media, and music streaming platforms.
    *   **Date & Time:**
        *   **Start Time:** The official show start time (e.g., 8:00 PM).
        *   **End Time:** Estimated show end time (allow for encores, typically 2 hours after start).
        *   **Door Open Time:** When guests are permitted entry (typically 1 hour before start).
        *   **Load-in Time:** (Internal Note) Add this to the event notes for operational planning, but not public-facing.
        *   **Soundcheck Time:** (Internal Note) Also for internal planning.
    *   **Venue/Resource:** Select "Blues Room" from the available resources.

4.  **Configure Capacity & Ticketing:**
    *   **Maximum Capacity:** Set this according to the Blues Room's fire code and comfortable guest experience (e.g., 80 seated, 100 standing). This is critical for safety and compliance.
    *   **Ticket Price(s):**
        *   **General Admission (GA):** Standard ticket price.
        *   **VIP/Premium (if applicable):** Higher price for special seating or perks.
        *   **Door Price:** Often slightly higher than advance online price to encourage pre-sales.
    *   **Ticket Types:**
        *   **Online Sales:** Primary sales channel.
        *   **Door Sales:** For walk-ups on the night.
        *   **Comp Tickets:** For artist guests, staff, partners, press.
    *   **Availability:** Set the date when tickets go on sale.

5.  **Add Visual Assets:**
    *   Upload high-resolution artist photos and approved promotional artwork provided by Big Muddy Touring. This will be used across our website and marketing.

6.  **Review and Publish:**
    *   **Crucial Step:** Before publishing, thoroughly review *all* entered information for accuracy (dates, times, prices, spellings).
    *   Click "Save" or "Publish" to make the event live and tickets available for purchase on our website.
    *   **Internal Calendar Sync:** Confirm the event automatically populates the Inn's public and internal event calendars.

**Key Principle:** Accuracy and attention to detail in this stage prevent errors and ensure a smooth booking and marketing process.

With the show configured, it's time to generate excitement and drive ticket sales.

**Amy's Role:** Lead marketing strategy and execution.

1.  **Launch Strategy:**
    *   **Timing:** Announce the show and open ticket sales 6-8 weeks in advance for established artists, or 3-4 weeks for local/emerging acts, depending on artist touring schedule and market saturation.
    *   **Teaser Campaign (Optional):** A brief "save the date" announcement can precede the full launch.

2.  **Social Media Wizard Integration:**
    *   **Access:** Navigate to the "Marketing" section within the `/admin` panel and select "Social Media Wizard."
    *   **Input Data:** The wizard will pull event details directly from your `/admin/booking/resources` setup. You may need to add specific talking points, artist quotes, or unique aspects of the show.
    *   **Content Generation:** The wizard will automatically generate draft posts tailored for:
        *   **Instagram:** Feed posts (captions, relevant hashtags, emoji suggestions), Story templates (swipe-up links, poll ideas).
        *   **TikTok:** Short video script ideas (e.g., "behind the scenes" of the Blues Room, artist sound clips), trending audio suggestions.
        *   **Facebook:** Event page creation (pulling all details), post drafts for sharing to our main page and local groups.
    *   **Customization & Review:** Amy must review, refine, and personalize these drafts. Add a personal touch, ensure brand voice consistency, and select the best visuals.
    *   **Scheduling:** Use the wizard's scheduling feature to stagger posts across various platforms and times for maximum reach.

3.  **Magazine Cross-Promotion:**
    *   **Identify Opportunities:** For high-profile acts or unique stories, consider featuring the artist in the Inn's quarterly Magazine.
    *   **Content Coordination:** Liaise with the Magazine editor. Provide artist bios, high-res photos, and potential interview questions for the artist.
    *   **Types of Promotion:** Full feature article, interview, event listing, "Artist Spotlight" section.

4.  **Website Listing & Email Marketing:**
    *   **Website:** The show will automatically appear on the Blues Room and Inn's "Events" pages once published in `/admin`.
    *   **Email List:** Segment our email subscribers to target those interested in music events. Craft compelling email campaigns with direct links to ticket sales. Schedule multiple sends (announcement, reminder, "last chance").

5.  **Local & Regional Outreach:**
    *   Submit event details to local tourism boards, community calendars, music blogs, and relevant online platforms.
    *   Consider partnerships with local businesses for cross-promotion.

**Key Principle:** Consistent, engaging, and multi-platform marketing is crucial for reaching our target audience and ensuring a well-attended show.

## 4. Pre-Show Day-of

The day of the show is a whirlwind of activity, requiring meticulous coordination and a focus on hospitality.

**Amy's Role:** Primary operational lead, artist liaison.
**Tracy's Role:** Inn coordination, accommodation, kitchen support.

1.  **Morning Briefing (Amy & Tracy):**
    *   **Review Schedule:** Go over the detailed timeline for the day: artist arrival, load-in, soundcheck, door time, showtime.
    *   **Artist Needs:** Confirm all hospitality rider details (meals, beverages, green room setup, accommodation).
    *   **Staffing:** Confirm all Blues Room and Inn staff are briefed on their roles and the day's schedule.
    *   **Potential Issues:** Discuss any known variables or potential challenges.

2.  **Blues Room Prep:**
    *   **Stage Setup:** Ensure the stage is clean, clear, and ready for the artist's tech rider.
    *   **Seating Arrangement:** Set up tables and chairs according to the planned layout (e.g., cabaret, theater style).
    *   **Bar Stocking:** Ensure the Blues Room bar is fully stocked, ice is abundant, and all necessary glassware and supplies are ready.
    *   **Restrooms:** Verify cleanliness and stocking.

3.  **Artist Arrival & Load-in:**
    *   **Scheduled Time:** Adhere strictly to the agreed-upon load-in time. Amy will be present to greet the band.
    *   **Access:** Ensure clear and accessible pathways for equipment. If there are stairs or tight corners, have staff ready to assist.
    *   **Initial Welcome:** Amy or Tracy (if Amy is occupied) will personally welcome the band, offering a brief tour of their green room and the venue.

4.  **Soundcheck:**
    *   **Dedicated Engineer:** Our professional sound engineer will lead the soundcheck. Amy facilitates communication between the artist and engineer.
    *   **Artist Requirements:** Ensure the tech rider is being followed. Address any issues promptly and professionally.
    *   **Timeline:** Keep soundcheck on schedule to avoid delays later in the evening.

5.  **Hospitality for the Band:**
    *   **Green Room/Artist Suite:**
        *   Ensure it's clean, comfortable, and private.
        *   Stock with requested beverages, snacks, and any specific rider items.
        *   Provide fresh towels, mirrors, and adequate lighting.
        *   Ensure reliable Wi-Fi access.
    *   **Meals/Refreshments:**
        *   Provide meals as per the hospitality rider, or offer a selection from the Inn's kitchen menu.
        *   Confirm any dietary restrictions have been addressed.
        *   Have water, coffee, and tea readily available throughout the day.
    *   **Accommodation (if applicable):** Tracy confirms that any complimentary rooms at the Inn are clean, ready, and accessible for the band members. Provide room keys and necessary Inn information.
    *   **Personal Touch:** A brief check-in from Amy or Tracy to ensure the band has everything they need.

6.  **Comp List & Press List:**
    *   **Compilation:** Amy is responsible for compiling the definitive comp and press lists, including full names.
    *   **Distribution:** Share the final list with the door staff well before doors open.
    *   **Instructions:** Brief door staff on how to handle entries from these lists (e.g., check ID, mark off names, provide wristbands if applicable).
    *   **Guest Experience:** Ensure a welcoming and efficient entry for all guests, including those on lists.

**Key Principle:** Proactive communication and meticulous preparation are paramount to a smooth and stress-free pre-show experience for both artists and staff.

## 5. Door

The door is the first point of contact for our guests and sets the tone for their evening. Efficiency, friendliness, and accuracy are key.

**Amy's Role:** Oversee door operations, troubleshoot.
**Door Staff's Role:** Execute scanning, sales, guest relations.

1.  **Staff Briefing (30 minutes before doors open):**
    *   **Roles:** Assign clear roles: Ticket Scanner, Walk-up Sales, Floater/Problem Solver.
    *   **Show Details:** Reiterate artist name, start time, door time, and any special announcements.
    *   **Ticket Pricing:** Confirm door price for walk-ups.
    *   **Comp/Press List:** Review the list with staff, emphasizing name verification and special instructions.
    *   **Capacity:** Remind staff of the Blues Room's maximum capacity and the importance of monitoring it.
    *   **Customer Service:** Emphasize a friendly, welcoming, and problem-solving approach.
    *   **Emergency Procedures:** Briefly review evacuation routes and contact protocols.

2.  **Ticket Scanning Workflow (`/admin/booking/tickets/scan`):**
    *   **Device Setup:**
        *   Ensure tablets or smartphones are fully charged and connected to reliable Wi-Fi.
        *   Log in to the `/admin/booking/tickets/scan` portal.
        *   Select the current show from the event list.
    *   **Scanning Process:**
        *   Guests present their digital or printed tickets.
        *   Use the device camera to scan the QR or barcode.
        *   A "green light" confirmation means the ticket is valid and scanned.
        *   A "red light" or error message indicates an issue.
    *   **Troubleshooting Common Issues:**
        *   **Invalid Ticket:** Politely inform the guest. Ask them to re-check their purchase confirmation.
        *   **"Already Scanned":** Indicates a duplicate. Politely investigate. Could be a screenshot, a shared ticket, or an accidental double scan. Refer to Amy if unresolved.
        *   **No Ticket/Confirmation:** Direct to walk-up sales or Amy for assistance.
        *   **Guest Name Not Found on List:** For comps/press, verify spelling. If still not found, refer to Amy or request the guest purchase a ticket.
    *   **Walk-up Sales:**
        *   **Process:** For guests purchasing at the door, use the `/admin/booking/resources` interface to process sales.
        *   **Payment:** Accept credit/debit cards via the POS system and cash.
        *   **Issuance:** Issue a digital ticket via email or a physical ticket/receipt.
        *   **Capacity Check:** Always check remaining capacity before selling walk-up tickets.

3.  **Cash Handling:**
    *   **Secure Float:** Ensure a secure cash float is available at the start of the night.
    *   **Reconciliation:** All cash transactions must be accurately recorded in the POS system.
    *   **Security:** Cash should be stored securely and reconciled at the end of the night according to Inn procedures.

**Key Principle:** The door team is the face of the Blues Room. A well-briefed, friendly, and efficient team ensures a positive start to the guest experience.

Once the doors are open and the music begins, the focus shifts to maintaining a vibrant, safe, and comfortable environment.

**Amy's Role:** Overall venue management, artist care, guest satisfaction.
**Bar Staff:** Beverage service, responsible alcohol service.
**Floor Staff:** Guest assistance, monitoring environment.

1.  **Bar Operations:**
    *   **Staffing:** Ensure adequate bar staff to handle demand without excessive wait times.
    *   **Stock & Replenishment:** Monitor inventory and replenish as needed from the backstock.
    *   **Responsible Service:**
        *   **ID Checks:** Continue to verify age for all alcohol purchases.
        *   **Intoxication:** Staff must be trained to identify and politely refuse service to intoxicated patrons. Refer to Inn's responsible service policy.

2.  **Room Temperature:**
    *   **Monitoring:** Regularly check the Blues Room temperature. As the room fills, it will warm up.

    *   **Monitoring:** Amy should periodically check the sound levels from different parts of the room.

    *   **Decibel Meter:** The sound engineer should use a decibel meter to ensure levels remain within acceptable limits, protecting both artist and audience hearing.
