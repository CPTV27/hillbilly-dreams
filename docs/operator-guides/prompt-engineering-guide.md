# MBT Prompt Engineering Guide

This guide is for anyone at MBT who interacts with AI tools – whether it's the high-level "Wizard" for Sanity-published content, direct "manual Gemini batches," or specialized "Claude Code agents." Our goal is to harness the power of AI while maintaining the unique voice, accuracy, and quality that defines MBT.

AI is a powerful assistant, not a creative director. Your role as a prompt engineer is to provide clear, constrained, and concrete instructions that guide the AI to produce outputs indistinguishable from human-crafted content.

---

## Core Principles of MBT Prompt Engineering

These principles form the foundation of all AI interactions at MBT. Adhere to them rigorously.

### 1. The Voice Firewall: Enforcing Brand Identity

**Principle:** Every piece of content MBT produces must adhere to our established brand voice. AI models are prone to generating generic, "AI-sounding" text if not explicitly constrained. We never let an AI pick a voice freely.

**How to Apply:** Always reference the relevant brand voice profile. Our central repository for voice profiles is located at `docs/voice/`. When prompting, you must either embed the relevant voice guidelines directly or explicitly instruct the AI to consult a predefined persona based on those guidelines.

**Example Prompt Integration:**

```prompt
You are a content writer for MBT. Your primary goal is to write engaging, informative, and authentic content for our audience.

**Voice Profile:**
Adhere strictly to the "MBT Blues Room" voice profile. Key characteristics:
-   **Authentic & Passionate:** Speak with genuine enthusiasm for the music and artists. Avoid overly academic or detached tones.
-   **Knowledgeable but Accessible:** Assume the reader has some interest in blues/jazz but avoid jargon without explanation. Share insights.
-   **Respectful & Celebratory:** Highlight the artists' skill, history, and contribution. Foster a sense of community.
-   **Direct & Clear:** Get to the point. Use active voice. Avoid flowery language or clichés (unless used ironically and intentionally).
-   **Slightly Informal but Professional:** Conversational, but always polished. No slang.
-   **Focus on the Experience:** Describe the atmosphere, the sound, the feeling of live music.

**Forbidden Phrases (Humanizer Rule):** [See Section 2]

Write a short promotional blurb for the upcoming "Tracy Lake Trio" show.
```

### 2. The Humanizer Rule: Eliminating AI Jargon

**Principle:** AI models often default to a set of overused, corporate, or generic phrases that dilute authenticity and sound unmistakably "AI-generated." The Humanizer Rule is a strict filter to remove these.

**How to Apply:** Every AI text output must run through this forbidden-phrases check. You *must* include a list of these forbidden phrases in your prompt and instruct the AI to avoid them explicitly. This list extends per-brand and content type but always includes our universally forbidden terms.

**Universally Forbidden Phrases:**
*   leverage
*   synergy
*   unlock
*   elevate
*   transform (unless referring to a physical change, e.g., "the caterpillar transformed into a butterfly")
*   journey (unless referring to actual travel)
*   cutting-edge
*   seamless
*   unparalleled
*   innovative
*   dynamic
*   robust
*   paradigm
*   optimize
*   curated (unless literally referring to an art curation process)
*   delve into
*   ecosystem
*   holistic
*   empower
*   revolutionize
*   pivot
*   scalable
*   impactful
*   thought leadership

**Example Prompt Integration:**

```prompt
[Previous Voice Profile and context...]

**Humanizer Rule: STRICTLY AVOID the following words and phrases:**
leverage, synergy, unlock, elevate, transform (unless physical), journey (unless travel), cutting-edge, seamless, unparalleled, innovative, dynamic, robust, paradigm, optimize, curated (unless literal), delve into, ecosystem, holistic, empower, revolutionize, pivot, scalable, impactful, thought leadership.

If you use any of these, I will reject the output. Rephrase to be more direct, authentic, and human.

[Content generation task...]
```

### 3. Concrete > Vague Topics: Specificity Drives Quality

**Principle:** AI models perform best when given precise, detailed instructions. Vague topics lead to generic, uninspired, or inaccurate outputs. The more specific you are, the better the AI can focus its knowledge.

**How to Apply:** Always provide as many concrete details as possible. Instead of broad categories, give specific names, dates, times, locations, and unique characteristics.

**Vague vs. Concrete Examples:**

| Vague Prompt Input           | Concrete Prompt Input                                         |
| :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "Write about music coverage." | "Write a promotional blurb for 'The Blues Room' shows in May, focusing on the 'Tracy Lake Trio' performing Friday, May 17th at 8 PM." |
| "Describe a local business." | "Describe 'The Daily Grind Coffee Shop' at 123 Main St. Highlight its artisanal pastries, live acoustic sets on weekends, and fair-trade coffee." |
| "Generate social media posts."| "Create three Instagram captions for the 'Summer Jazz Festival.' Focus on the headline act, 'Ella Fitzgerald Tribute Band,' playing August 10th. Include hashtags: #SummerJazz #LiveMusic #EllaTribute." |

**Example Prompt:**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule...]

**Task:** Write a 150-word event description for our website's "Events" page.
**Topic:** The upcoming "Blues & Brews Night" at The Old Brick Tavern.
**Specific Details:**
-   **Date:** Saturday, June 22nd
-   **Time:** Doors at 7:00 PM, Music starts at 8:00 PM
-   **Featured Artist:** The Delta Roamers (a 4-piece blues band known for their harmonica solos and gritty vocals)
-   **Special Offer:** Local craft beer tasting flight included with ticket purchase.
-   **Venue:** The Old Brick Tavern, 456 Elm Street. Known for its rustic ambiance and excellent acoustics.
-   **Ticket Price:** $25 advance, $30 at door.
-   **Website for Tickets:** www.oldbricktavern.com/bluesbrews

Focus on the atmosphere, the unique sound of The Delta Roamers, and the craft beer experience.
```

### 4. Entity Grounding: Preventing Hallucinations

**Principle:** AI models can invent businesses, artists, or locations that don't exist (hallucinations). This is unacceptable. All entities mentioned in AI-generated content *must* be real and verifiable.

**How to Apply:** Always pull real entity data from our internal directory or verified sources. Provide the AI with the specific names, descriptions, and relevant details of these entities. Never allow the AI to "imagine" an entity.

**Example Prompt Integration:**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule, Concrete Topic...]

**Entity Data (from MBT Directory):**
-   **Artist:** Tracy Lake Trio
    -   **Genre:** Contemporary Blues, Soul
    -   **Members:** Tracy Lake (vocals, guitar), Marcus "Groove" Johnson (bass), Sarah Chen (drums)
    -   **Known For:** Powerful vocals, improvisational guitar solos, high-energy stage presence, recent album "Midnight Howl."
    -   **Website:** tracylaketrio.com
-   **Venue:** The Blues Room
    -   **Location:** 789 Music Alley, Downtown
    -   **Capacity:** 200
    -   **Atmosphere:** Intimate, dimly lit, excellent sound system, known for hosting established and emerging blues acts.
    -   **Features:** Full bar, limited standing room.

**Task:** Write a 200-word blog post introducing the Tracy Lake Trio's upcoming performance at The Blues Room. Emphasize their unique sound and why fans shouldn't miss this live experience. Integrate details about the venue's atmosphere.
```

### 5. Photo Grounding: Accurate Visual Descriptions

**Principle:** Just as with entities, AI models can invent image descriptions. This leads to misrepresentation and poor content. All photo descriptions or accompanying text *must* accurately reflect real assets from our image library.

**How to Apply:** Always pull real asset information (e.g., file names, existing descriptions, metadata) from Immich. Provide the AI with these concrete details. Never ask the AI to "imagine a photo" or describe a generic scene.

**Example Prompt Integration:**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule, Entity Grounding, etc...]

**Photo Asset Data (from Immich):**
-   **File Name:** tracy-lake-trio-live-2023.jpg
-   **Existing Description:** Tracy Lake (center) passionately sings into a vintage microphone, illuminated by blue stage lights, with Marcus Johnson on bass to her left and Sarah Chen on drums in the background. The audience is visible as blurred silhouettes.
-   **Keywords:** blues, live music, concert, Tracy Lake Trio, performance, microphone, stage lights, bass, drums.

**Task:** Write an Instagram caption (max 60 words) for the provided image (`tracy-lake-trio-live-2023.jpg`). Focus on the energy captured in the photo and encourage ticket sales for their show.
```

### 6. Length Targets: Respecting Content Constraints

**Principle:** Different content types have specific length requirements (e.g., tweet, blog post, email subject line). AI models will often generate arbitrary lengths if not explicitly constrained.

**How to Apply:** Always specify a minimum and/or maximum word or character count for the desired output. Be clear about what constitutes a "word" or "character" if there's ambiguity (though usually standard definitions suffice).

**Example Prompt Integration:**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule, Entity Grounding, Photo Grounding...]

**Task:** Write a short, punchy promotional email subject line.
**Length Target:** Maximum 50 characters.

---

**Task:** Generate a comprehensive artist biography for our website.
**Length Target:** Between 400 and 500 words.

---

**Task:** Draft a tweet for the upcoming event.
**Length Target:** Maximum 280 characters, including hashtags.

---
**Example Prompt:**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule, Concrete Topic, Entity Grounding...]

**Task:** Write a short social media post for Instagram announcing the "Blues & Brews Night."
**Length Target:** 70-90 words.
**Call to Action:** Encourage followers to tag a friend they'd bring.
```

### 7. Few-Shot Examples: Guiding Output When it Drifts

**Principle:** Sometimes, despite clear instructions, an AI's output might drift from the desired tone, style, or structure. Providing one or two high-quality examples can "re-calibrate" the AI.

**How to Apply:** When you notice the AI struggling to meet your expectations, provide 2 example drafts that perfectly match the desired voice, style, and format. Place these examples *before* the main content generation task.

**Example Prompt Integration:**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule, Concrete Topic, Entity Grounding, Length Target...]

**Context:** We need a short, engaging blurb for our weekly newsletter, highlighting a specific track from a featured artist's new album. The tone should be enthusiastic and evocative, making the reader want to click and listen.

**Example 1 (Desired Style):**
"Dive deep into the soulful depths of 'Riverbed Blues' from Maggie Mae's latest album, 'Delta Echoes.' Maggie's haunting vocals and a searing slide guitar riff paint a vivid picture of longing and resilience. This track isn't just music; it's a journey through the heart of the blues. Don't miss it!"

**Example 2 (Desired Style):**
"Get ready to tap your feet to the infectious rhythm of 'City Lights Shuffle' by The Uptown Horns! This track, off their new release 'Brass & Grit,' explodes with vibrant horns and a driving bassline that’ll have you dancing in your seat. Pure, unadulterated funk-infused blues joy!"

**Task:** Write a similar 50-70 word blurb for our newsletter, focusing on the track "Sunset Serenade" from the new album "Horizon Hues" by the artist "Luna & The Drifters." Describe its smooth jazz-blues feel and laid-back vibe.
```

### 8. The Gemini Batch Dispatcher (`scripts/ai/gemini-batch.mjs`)

**Purpose:** The Gemini Batch Dispatcher is our workhorse for generating large volumes of structured content efficiently. It allows us to process multiple prompts in a single run, making it ideal for tasks like generating descriptions for an entire season of shows, artist bios for a festival lineup, or social media posts for a month.

**When to Use:**
*   When you need to generate 5 or more similar content pieces that share a common structure or prompt template, but have different specific data points.
*   For tasks that are repetitive and would be tedious to do manually in the Wizard.
*   For content that *does not* require immediate publication to Sanity or direct content management system integration.

**Batch Size Norms:**
*   **Small Batches (5-20 items):** Ideal for testing new prompt structures or generating content for a single event series.
*   **Medium Batches (20-100 items):** Suitable for festival lineups, seasonal programming, or extensive social media calendars.
*   **Large Batches (100+ items):** Use with caution. Ensure your prompt is extremely robust and your input data is clean. Large batches require more processing time and meticulous review.
*   **Recommendation:** Start small. Test your prompt with 2-3 items, then scale up.

**Output Path Conventions:**
*   All `gemini-batch.mjs` outputs **must** be directed to a clearly defined and descriptive subdirectory within `outputs/ai_generated/`.
*   The directory name should indicate the project, date, and purpose.
*   **Format:** `outputs/ai_generated/[PROJECT_NAME]/[YYYY-MM-DD]_[CONTENT_TYPE]/`
    *   **Example:** `outputs/ai_generated/blues_room_may_2024/2024-05-15_show_descriptions/`
    *   **Example:** `outputs/ai_generated/summer_jazz_fest/2024-07-01_artist_bios/`
*   Each output file should be clearly named, e.g., `tracy_lake_trio_blurb.md`, `delta_roamers_bio.json`.

**Voice + Format Enforcement in System Prompts:**
The power of batch processing comes from a well-crafted *system prompt* that applies consistently to all items in the batch. This is where you embed all the core principles:

*   **Voice Profile:** Copy-paste the relevant `docs/voice/` profile directly into the system prompt.
*   **Humanizer Rule:** Include the full list of forbidden phrases.
*   **Output Format:** Explicitly define the desired output format (e.g., JSON, Markdown, plain text, YAML). If JSON, provide a schema.
*   **Placeholders:** Clearly define how input variables will be used.

### 10. Common Prompt Failures + Fixes

Understanding common pitfalls helps you quickly diagnose and correct AI output.

#### Failure 1: Generic, "AI-Blog" Tone

**Symptom:** Output sounds bland, uses corporate jargon, lacks personality, or is indistinguishable from any other AI-generated article online.

**Fix:**
*   **Enforce The Voice Firewall:** Always embed the specific voice profile.
*   **Apply The Humanizer Rule:** Explicitly list forbidden phrases.
*   **Use Few-Shot Examples:** Provide 1-2 examples of desired output that perfectly embody the MBT voice.

**Example Prompt (Fixing Generic Tone):**

```prompt
You are a passionate music journalist for MBT, writing for an audience that loves authentic blues music.

**Voice Profile:**
-   Authentic & Passionate: Speak with genuine enthusiasm.
-   Knowledgeable but Accessible: Share insights without jargon.
-   Respectful & Celebratory: Highlight artists' skill.
-   Direct & Clear: Active voice, no flowery language.
-   Slightly Informal but Professional: Conversational, polished.

**Humanizer Rule: STRICTLY AVOID:** leverage, synergy, unlock, elevate, transform (unless physical), journey (unless travel), cutting-edge, seamless, unparalleled, innovative, dynamic, robust, paradigm, optimize, curated (unless literal), delve into, ecosystem, holistic, empower, revolutionize, pivot, scalable, impactful, thought leadership.

**Example of Desired Tone:**
"When the lights dim and Sarah 'Blues Mama' Jenkins takes the stage, you don't just hear music; you feel the history. Her guitar weeps and wails, telling stories that resonate deep in your soul. It's raw, it's real, and it's an experience that reminds you why the blues endures."

**Task:** Write a 100-word social media post about the upcoming "Harmonica Hounds" show at The Blues Room on July 5th. Focus on their energetic Chicago blues sound and their legendary harmonica player, "Fast Eddie" Miller.
```

#### Failure 2: Fictional Entities or Information (Hallucinations)

**Symptom:** The AI invents artist names, venue details, album titles, dates, or facts that are not real or accurate.

**Fix:**
*   **Enforce Entity Grounding:** Always provide the AI with explicit, verified data for all entities.
*   **Explicitly Forbid Invention:** Add a direct instruction not to invent.

**Example Prompt (Fixing Fictional Entities):**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule...]

**CRITICAL INSTRUCTION:** You MUST NOT invent any artist names, album titles, or venue details. Only use the information provided.

**Entity Data (from MBT Directory):**
-   **Artist:** The Midnight Drifters
    -   **Genre:** Swamp Blues, Delta Blues
    -   **Known For:** Gritty slide guitar, raw vocals, energetic live shows.
    -   **Latest Album:** "Bayou Echoes" (released April 2024)
-   **Venue:** The Cypress Club
    -   **Location:** 101 Riverfront Drive
    -   **Atmosphere:** Intimate, cozy, known for hosting roots music.

**Task:** Write a 150-word announcement for The Midnight Drifters' show at The Cypress Club on August 20th. Highlight their new album "Bayou Echoes" and their signature sound.
```

#### Failure 3: Incorrect Length

**Symptom:** The output is significantly too long or too short for the intended content type.

**Fix:**
*   **Enforce Length Targets:** Always specify a precise word or character count. Be clear about min/max.
*   **Iterate and Refine:** If the first attempt is off, explicitly tell the AI to "shorten this to 75 words" or "expand this to 300 words."

**Example Prompt (Fixing Incorrect Length):**

```prompt
[Voice Profile, Humanizer Rule, Entity Grounding...]

**Task:** Write a promotional email body.
**Length Target:** Strictly between 250 and 300 words. Do not go over 300 words.

[Content details...]
```

#### Failure 4: Wrong Voice or Tone

**Symptom:** The output doesn't match the desired brand persona – it might be too formal, too casual, too academic, or simply "off."

**Fix:**
*   **Specify Brand Profile:** Reiterate or refine the voice profile in the prompt.
*   **Use Few-Shot Examples:** This is particularly effective for nuanced tone issues. Provide examples that perfectly capture the desired voice.
*   **Negative Constraints:** Sometimes, telling the AI what *not* to do can be as helpful as telling it what *to* do (e.g., "Avoid overly academic language," "Do not sound overly corporate").

**Example Prompt (Fixing Wrong Voice):**

```prompt
[Humanizer Rule, Entity Grounding, Length Target...]

**Voice Profile:**
You are writing for MBT's "Jazz Lounge" section. The tone should be sophisticated, appreciative, and slightly nostalgic, but still engaging and accessible. Imagine speaking to a discerning audience at a dimly lit jazz club. Avoid overly casual language or hyperbole.

**Example of Desired Voice:**
"The velvet hush of the evening settled as Miles Davis's 'Kind of Blue' began to play. Each note, a brushstroke of genius, invited introspection and quiet awe. It's a testament to the timeless elegance of jazz, a masterpiece that continues to captivate generations."

**Task:** Write a 120-word social media post celebrating the anniversary of John Coltrane's album "A Love Supreme." Focus on its spiritual depth and enduring influence on jazz.
```
