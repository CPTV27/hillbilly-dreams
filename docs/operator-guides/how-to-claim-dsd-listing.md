### Step 3: Verify Ownership (Email Confirmation or Phone Verification)

Verifying ownership is a crucial step to ensure that only legitimate business owners can control a listing. This protects businesses from unauthorized changes and maintains the integrity of our directory. We offer a couple of straightforward methods:

#### Option A: Email Confirmation (Most Common)

1.  **Check Your Inbox:** We will attempt to send a verification email to the email address we have on file for your business. This is typically an email address found on your public website, business registrations, or other publicly accessible records.
2.  **Look for Our Email:**
    *   **Subject Line:** The email will have a clear subject line, something like "Deep South Directory: Verify Your Business Ownership."
    *   **Sender:** It will come from an official Deep South Directory email address.
    *   **Check Spam/Junk:** Sometimes, these automated emails can end up in your spam or junk folder, so be sure to check there if you don't see it in your main inbox within a few minutes.
3.  **Click the Verification Link:** Inside the email, you'll find a unique link. Click this link to confirm your ownership. This link is time-sensitive, so it's best to click it as soon as you receive it.

**What if the Email Address is Old or Inaccessible?**
We understand that business contact information can change! If the email address we have on file is no longer active, or if you simply can't access it, don't worry. You'll typically be given an option to try an alternative verification method or to contact our support team for manual verification.

#### Option B: Phone Verification

In some cases, or if email verification isn't successful, we may offer phone verification:

1.  **Receive a Call or Text:** We will send a verification code to the phone number we have on file for your business. This might be a direct call with an automated message or a text message (SMS).
2.  **Enter the Code:** You'll be prompted to enter this code into a field on our verification page.
3.  **Confirm:** Once the code is entered correctly, your ownership will be verified.

**What if the Phone Number is Old or Inaccessible?**
Similar to email verification, if the phone number is outdated or unavailable, you'll have options to explore alternative verification methods or reach out to our customer support.

#### Alternative Verification (If Primary Methods Fail)

If both email and phone verification aren't possible, we have a manual process to help. You'll be prompted to contact our support team, who may ask you to provide documentation proving your ownership. This could include:

*   A recent utility bill addressed to your business at the listed location.
*   A business license or registration document.
*   A photo of your storefront with visible signage.
*   Other official business documentation.

Our team will guide you through this process to ensure your claim is handled securely and efficiently.

## What Else You Should Know

### What if my business isn't listed yet? (Add it free)

1.  **Go to the "Add a Business" Page:** On [deepsouthdirectory.com](https://deepsouthdirectory.com), look for a link like "Add My Business," "Submit Listing," or "Get Listed."
2.  **Fill Out the Form:** You'll be prompted to enter all the essential information about your business:
    *   Business Name
    *   Address
    *   Phone Number
    *   Website
    *   Categories
    *   A brief description
    *   Your contact email (for verification and communication)
3.  **Initial Submission:** Once you've filled out the required fields, submit the form.
4.  **Verification & Moderation:** Your newly submitted listing will then go through a similar verification and moderation process as outlined in Steps 3 and 7. We'll verify the information and ensure it meets our quality standards.
5.  **Claim After Approval:** Once your newly added listing is approved and goes live as a basic entry, you can then follow the steps from the beginning of this guide to "Claim this listing" and enhance it further!

### What if someone else claimed my listing? (Dispute Process)

While rare, it's possible that an unauthorized individual might have attempted to claim your business listing. We take such incidents very seriously and have a clear dispute process to protect legitimate business owners.

1.  **Identify the Issue:** If you find your listing shows as "claimed" but you haven't claimed it yourself, or if you notice unauthorized changes, look for a "Report an Issue" or "Dispute Claim" button on your business's listing page.
2.  **Initiate Dispute:** Click this button. You'll be asked to provide:
    *   Your contact information.
    *   A detailed explanation of why you believe the listing is incorrectly claimed.
    *   Crucially, **proof of ownership** (e.g., a business license, utility bill in your business name at the listed address, a photo of your storefront, legal documentation, etc.).
3.  **Our Investigation:** Our dedicated support team will thoroughly investigate your claim. This may involve:
    *   Contacting the individual who initially claimed the listing.
    *   Reviewing all submitted documentation.
    *   Potentially requesting additional information from you.
4.  **Resolution:** We aim to resolve disputes promptly and fairly. You will be contacted by our team with updates and the outcome of the investigation. If your claim is validated, the unauthorized claim will be revoked, and you will be guided through the proper ownership verification process.

You'll be notified if moderation is required.

### How to upgrade/downgrade tier

Your business needs evolve, and so can your Deep South Directory subscription! Changing your tier is simple.

1.  **Log In:** Access your Deep South Directory business account.
2.  **Go to Subscription Settings:** Navigate to your "My Listings" or "Subscription Settings" area within your dashboard.
3.  **Select New Tier:** You'll see your current tier highlighted and options to upgrade or downgrade to other available tiers. Click on the tier you wish to switch to.
4.  **Confirm Changes:**
    *   **Upgrading:** If you upgrade to a higher-priced tier, you'll typically be charged a prorated amount for the remainder of your current billing cycle, and your new tier benefits will become active immediately.
    *   **Downgrading:** If you downgrade to a lower-priced tier, your current tier's benefits will remain active until the end of your current billing cycle. The new, lower price will then apply from your next billing period.
5.  **Confirmation:** You'll receive an email confirming your tier change and details about your billing adjustment.
