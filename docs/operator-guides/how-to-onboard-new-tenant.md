## 1. Pre-call Checklist: Gathering Customer Information

Before your kickoff call with a new tenant, it's essential to collect all necessary information. This ensures a productive conversation and minimizes back-and-forth later.

**Goal:** Gather essential information from the customer *before* the kickoff call.

**Actions:**

1.  **Confirm Contract:** Ensure the customer's service agreement or contract is fully signed and executed.
2.  **Collect Tenant Details:** Request the following information from the customer (ideally via a simple online form or email):
    *   **Full Name:** The primary contact person (e.g., Vicki Wolpert).
    *   **Business Name:** Their real estate business name (e.g., "Vicki Wolpert Real Estate").
    *   **Primary Contact Email:** For platform access, notifications, and communication.
    *   **Primary Contact Phone:** For urgent communications.
    *   **Existing Website URL (if any):** To understand their current online presence.
    *   **Social Media Links:** Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc. (we'll need these for platform integration).
    *   **Brand Colors:** Hex codes for their primary and secondary brand colors (e.g., `#007bff`, `#6c757d`).
    *   **Logo:** High-resolution SVG or PNG file.
    *   **Headshot:** High-resolution professional photo.
    *   **Bio/About Me Text:** A brief professional biography.
    *   **Target Audience/Niche:** Who do they primarily serve? (e.g., first-time homebuyers, luxury properties, specific neighborhoods).
    *   **Initial Listings:** Details and photos for 3-5 current or recent listings they want to showcase immediately.
    *   **Payment Method:** Confirm if they've set up their billing details with Stripe (if self-service) or if you need to collect this information securely.
3.  **Domain Details:** This is critical for getting their website live.
    *   **Desired Subdomain:** If they want to use a subdomain on our platform (e.g., `vicki.mbt.com`).
    *   **Custom Domain:** If they want to use their own domain (e.g., `vickiwolpert.com`):
        *   Ask them to identify their **DNS provider** (e.g., GoDaddy, Cloudflare, Namecheap, Google Domains).
        *   Explain that we'll need either **direct access** to their DNS settings (with their explicit permission) or their **cooperation** to make specific changes.
        *   **Crucial:** Emphasize that this step is essential for their website to go live on their custom domain.
4.  **Content Preferences:**
    *   Any specific content ideas or topics they're passionate about?
    *   Preferred posting frequency for social media and blog?
    *   Any existing content assets (photos, videos, articles) they want to migrate?

**Deliverable:** A complete `Tenant Information Sheet` (either a digital document you fill out or notes you've compiled) with all required details.

## 2. The Kickoff Call: Setting Expectations and Confirming Details

The kickoff call is your opportunity to welcome the customer, confirm details, and set clear expectations for the onboarding process.

**Goal:** Set clear expectations, confirm all gathered details, and obtain necessary authorizations.

**Agenda:**

1.  **Introductions & Welcome:**
    *   Warm welcome to the MBT platform.
    *   Briefly reiterate the value proposition and how we'll help them grow their business.
2.  **Review Platform Benefits:**
    *   Quick overview of the personalized website, content management, and social media scheduling features.
3.  **Confirm Gathered Details:**
    *   Go through the information collected in the Pre-call Checklist.
    *   Verify their Business Name, Contact Info, desired Domain, Brand Colors, etc.
    *   Confirm the initial 3-5 listings for immediate setup.
4.  **Discuss Content Strategy (Initial):**
    *   Talk about their target audience and niche.
    *   Brainstorm initial content ideas (e.g., neighborhood guides, market updates, personal stories).
    *   Discuss preferred posting frequency for social media and their blog.
    *   Explain how we'll work together to create and schedule content.
5.  **Explain Onboarding Timeline:**
    *   **Day 1:** Platform provisioning (getting their website live, setting up accounts).
    *   **Day 2-7:** Initial content preparation (populating the site, scheduling first social posts).
    *   **First Week Check-in:** A brief call to review everything.
    *   **First Month Review:** A deeper dive into performance.
    *   **Quarterly Reviews:** Ongoing strategy sessions.
6.  **Required Access & Authorizations:**
    *   **DNS Access (if custom domain):**
        *   **Crucial:** Explain that for their custom domain (e.g., `vickiwolpert.com`) to point to their new MBT website, we need to make specific changes to their DNS records.
        *   **Option 1 (Preferred):** Ask them to grant you temporary access to their DNS provider (e.g., GoDaddy, Cloudflare).
        *   **Option 2:** If they prefer, you can provide them with the exact records, and they can make the changes. Explain the importance of accuracy.
        *   **Obtain Written Authorization:** Get explicit written (email is fine) authorization for MBT to make or guide them through these DNS changes. Example wording: *"I, [Tenant Name], authorize MBT to configure DNS records for [their domain, e.g., vickiwolpert.com] to point to the MBT platform as part of my onboarding process."*
    *   **Social Media Access (if we're managing posts):** If MBT will be scheduling posts on their behalf, explain that you'll need access to their Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn accounts. Guide them on the secure way to provide this (e.g., adding you as an editor, or using a secure password manager).
7.  **Q&A:** Address any questions or concerns.
8.  **Next Steps:** Inform them that you'll begin the provisioning process immediately after the call.

**Deliverable:** Confirmed details for all onboarding steps, and explicit written authorization for DNS changes (if applicable).

### 3.2. Add Domain to Vercel + Cloudflare

This step links the tenant's chosen domain to their new MBT website.

*   **Scenario A: Using an MBT Subdomain (e.g., `vicki.mbt.com`)**
    *   **No action needed.** Vercel automatically handles this when the `domain` field is set to `[tenant-id].mbt.com` in `tenants.ts` and pushed to `main`.
    *   **Verify:** After Vercel deployment, confirm the site loads at `https://vicki.mbt.com`.

*   **Scenario B: Using a Custom Domain (e.g., `vickiwolpert.com`)**
    This requires actions in both Vercel and the tenant's DNS provider (often Cloudflare).

    1.  **In Vercel:**
        *   Go to the Vercel Dashboard -> Select the `mbt-web` project.
        *   Navigate to **Settings -> Domains**.
        *   Click **"Add New Domain"**.
        *   Enter the tenant's custom domain (e.g., `vickiwolpert.com`).
        *   Vercel will provide you with specific DNS records (typically an `A` record and a `CNAME` record) that need to be added to the tenant's DNS provider. **Copy these records down carefully.**

    2.  **In Cloudflare (or their DNS provider like GoDaddy, Namecheap):**
        *   Log into the tenant's Cloudflare account (or their specific DNS provider).
        *   Select their domain (e.g., `vickiwolpert.com`).
        *   Go to the **DNS -> Records** section.
        *   **Add the records provided by Vercel:**
            *   **For the root domain (`vickiwolpert.com`):**
                *   Type: `A`
                *   Name: `@` (or leave blank, depending on provider)
                *   Value: `76.76.21.21` (Vercel's IP address)
                *   TTL: Automatic or 1 hour
            *   **For the `www` subdomain (`www.vickiwolpert.com`):**
                *   Type: `CNAME`
                *   Name: `www`
                *   Value: `cname.vercel-dns.com`
                *   TTL: Automatic or 1 hour
            *   **Important Cloudflare Note:** Ensure the "Proxy status" for these records is set to **"DNS only"** (gray cloud) initially. Once verified, you can change it to "Proxied" (orange cloud) for Cloudflare's performance benefits.

        *   **Crucial Step (if using Cloudflare but Cloudflare isn't their registrar):** If the tenant's domain registrar (e.g., GoDaddy) is *not* Cloudflare, you must ensure that the domain's nameservers at the registrar are pointing to Cloudflare's nameservers. If not, provide the tenant with Cloudflare's nameservers and instruct them to update this at their registrar. This is a common point of failure.

    3.  **Verify DNS Propagation:**
        *   DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours to propagate globally, though often it's faster.
        *   You can use online tools like `whatsmydns.net` or `dig vickiwolpert.com` in your terminal to check if the new DNS records are resolving correctly.
        *   Once propagated, the tenant's custom domain (`https://vickiwolpert.com`) should display their new MBT website.
