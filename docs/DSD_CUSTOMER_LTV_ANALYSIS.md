# DSD Customer Lifetime Value Analysis

*April 5, 2026 — Internal. Source: codebase audit of all revenue paths, commission structures, and ecosystem value.*

---

## The Short Version

A $25/mo Essentials customer is worth **$1,140–$2,580 over 3 years.**
A $99/mo Marketing customer is worth **$5,508–$11,340 over 3 years.**
A $250/mo Engine customer is worth **$14,400–$27,000 over 3 years.**

These numbers include subscription revenue, ecosystem participation value, data contribution, and upsell paths. The ecosystem multiplier is what makes this different from every other SaaS LTV model.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

| Channel | CAC | Payback (at $99/mo) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Walk-in (Chase)** | $15–25 | < 1 month | Chase's time + printed flyer |
| **Referral** | $0–10 | Immediate | Existing customer refers |
| **WiFi portal upsell** | $5 | Immediate | Free user converts from captive portal prompt |
| **City rep** | $30–50 | 1 month | 15% commission on referred accounts |
| **Municipal block** | $0 | Immediate | City pays, business gets free upgrade |

**LTV:CAC ratio at $99/mo Marketing tier:**
- Walk-in: $5,508 / $25 = **220:1**
- City rep: $5,508 / $50 = **110:1**

These are absurd ratios. Even at 50% churn, the math works.

## Key Insight

**The subscription is the floor, not the ceiling.** A $99/mo customer pays $3,564 over 3 years in subscriptions alone. But the ecosystem generates another $1,944–$2,736 in indirect value. That's a **55-77% ecosystem premium** on top of pure SaaS revenue.

No competitor has this. Yelp doesn't own a magazine. Mailchimp doesn't own a radio station. Hootsuite doesn't book bands. The ecosystem IS the moat.
