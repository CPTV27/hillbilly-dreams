## Why this decision before everything else

Destination events are the convergence point. They are where Touring + Inn occupancy + Magazine content + Photography + sponsorship + regional identity collapse into a single economic object. Each event drives:

- **Room nights** at the Inn (the cash engine)
- **Bar / F&B revenue** at the Inn (margin)
- **Ticket revenue** for Big Muddy Touring
- **Editorial content** for the Magazine (recurring annual coverage)
- **Photography inventory** for Chase's archive + brand
- **Mailing-list growth** at every guest touch point
- **Regional reputation** that compounds year over year
- **Artist relationships** with the acts we put on stage
- **Sponsor narrative** for whoever buys into the event

Every other operational decision (infrastructure, software, branding, scheduling) becomes implementation detail once the three events are picked.

**The shift in mindset:** stop thinking of events as "programming." They are ecosystem-shaping rituals — recurring annual rituals that build the cultural institution Big Muddy Touring is becoming.

## The scale guardrail

**Avoid:** 2,000-person festival scale. Bonnaroo / Newport Folk shapes. Commodity positioning. Operationally brutal.

**Target:** **40–150 person destination experiences.** Intimacy + aesthetic cohesion + regional specificity is the actual moat. The Inn can host 40 guests; the Blues Room is a listening room; Chase's photography practice is built for portraiture and small-scale documentary. The asset base aligns with intimate.

**If an event idea requires 500+ attendees to make economic sense, it's the wrong event for us.**

## Sponsorship — symbolic capital, not commodity inventory

A bank sponsoring "another festival" is ordinary inventory. A sponsor attached to "a documentary-driven Mississippi corridor cultural institution" is a different psychological object — sponsor alignment with cultural legitimacy.

That changes:
- **Sponsor pricing** (premium for legitimacy alignment, not per-impression rate)
- **Institutional partnerships** (museums, universities, heritage orgs > generic brand sponsors)
- **Press positioning** (cultural press > event-listings press)

When designing events, design the sponsor architecture for symbolic capital. The wrong sponsor at the right event damages the moat. The right sponsor at the right event compounds it.

## The scoring criteria

Each candidate event is scored against ten tests. **An event should hit at least six of the ten to qualify as one of the three.**

| # | Test | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Creates travel intent | Drives Inn occupancy |
| 2 | Generates documentary / editorial material | Feeds Magazine |
| 3 | Reinforces Mississippi corridor identity | Builds moat |
| 4 | Attracts artists we want long-term relationships with | Improves roster / network |
| 5 | Works at small scale initially | Lowers risk; allows Y1 launch |
| 6 | Can recur annually | Creates compounding recognition |
| 7 | Produces strong photography / video | Amplifies authority |
| 8 | Has sponsor narrative potential | Future leverage |
| 9 | Feels culturally specific to Big Muddy | Prevents generic-festival syndrome |
| 10 | **Creates reusable long-tail media / IP assets** | Extends event value year-round (documentaries, books, archives, sponsor reels, editorial franchises, monetizable libraries) |

**Test #10 is distinct from #7.** Beautiful photos at an event is one thing. An event that generates a year's worth of editorial inventory, sponsor decks, documentary footage, archive material, and ongoing marketing assets is something else entirely. That distinction is where Big Muddy's structural differentiation lives.

## Candidate event concepts

Six concepts to evaluate. Three (A, B, C) are the strongest initial set; D, E, F are additional candidates worth at least considering before committing.

### Candidate A — Mississippi Corridor Weekender

**Shape:** 3-day curated cultural immersion. Friday–Sunday. 80–120 guests. Inn stays, curated live performances, guided regional experience, local food/drink, documentary/photo component, partner venue collaborations.

**The flow:** guests arrive Friday afternoon. Welcome dinner + acoustic set at the Inn. Saturday = guided morning ride along Highway 61 (Vicksburg or Helena-West Helena loop), afternoon return + Blues Room set, evening at a partner venue (Arcade, Ground Zero, Club Ebony depending on the year). Sunday brunch + farewell concert.

**Score against the 10 tests:** likely 8/10. Hits everything except possibly #4 (artist relationship depth — depends on who's curated) and #10 (produces good event-time media but lower long-tail IP than C).

**Revenue mechanics:** ticket price ~$650–950 all-in per guest (lodging included). 80 guests at $750 avg = $60k gross. Net of artist guarantees + meals + transport + production = ~$25–35k margin per event.

**⚠ Hidden operational creep risk:** the moment you add buses + guided routes + multiple venues + lodging coordination + meals + artist transport + tourism logistics, you are quietly approaching boutique travel-company complexity. Three partners cannot carry that without breaking.

**Discipline for Version 1:** **one venue + one partner venue + one route experience + one dinner + one brunch.** NOT the full Deep South experiential tourism package. Add complexity Y2+ after Y1 proves.

**Best fit if:** the goal is broad accessibility + Inn occupancy + a flagship annual event everyone in the region knows about. **Probably the eventual flagship public-facing ritual — but also the highest operational surface area.**

### Candidate B — Songwriter Residency / Retreat

**Shape:** 5-day artist residency. Monday–Friday. 12–20 invited songwriters (mix of established + emerging). All-inclusive at the Inn. Songwriting sessions, performance evenings, listening rooms, mentorship from Amy + visiting artists.

**The flow:** invitation-only. Tracy curates the cohort. Daily structure: morning writing sessions, afternoon workshops or mentorship, evening informal Blues Room performance for invited locals. Sunday night closing concert open to the public.

**Score against the 10 tests:** likely 7/10. Strong on artist relationships (#4), cultural specificity (#9), and recurring annual potential (#6). Moderate on long-tail media (#10) — produces material but the invite-only nature limits public-facing IP. Weaker on travel intent (#1) since it's invite-only, and on sponsor narrative (#8) unless structured intentionally.

**Revenue mechanics:** could be free to artists (Big Muddy invests in the relationship) OR $1,500–2,500/artist ticketed (workshop-style). The closing public concert sells ~120 tickets at $40 = $4.8k. Net: probably break-even or modest loss in Y1; significant relationship + reputation value.

**Best fit if:** the goal is artist-relationship depth, cultural prestige, and a long-term "the thing serious regional writers quietly want to get invited into." **Likely matters more after the ecosystem has stronger gravity — Y3+ as a flagship.**

### Candidate C — Photography + Music Documentary Weekend

**Shape:** 4-day intensive. Mix of paying participants (photographers / writers) + invited musicians. Documentary-style coverage of musicians at work, intimate performances, regional shoots. Chase teaches / leads.

**The flow:** participants arrive Thursday. Welcome session + meet-the-musicians dinner. Friday + Saturday = field documentary work — shoot at the Inn, at corridor venues, at locations (cotton field portraiture, riverfront, juke joints). Saturday night = featured live performance documented by the cohort. Sunday = portfolio review + closing.

**Score against the 10 tests:** **10/10.** This may be the only candidate that hits every test cleanly. Test #10 (long-tail media / IP) is the highest of any candidate — by structural design.

**Revenue mechanics:** 15–25 paying participants at $1,800–2,800 per person all-in = $30–60k gross. Net of artist talent + production + meals = ~$15–30k margin per event.

**The hidden leverage — this is not just an event.** Candidate C is simultaneously:
- An **event** (ticket revenue + room nights + bar)
- A **content engine** (year's worth of Magazine inventory)
- A **documentation engine** (long-tail editorial franchise, archives, sponsor reels)
- A **photography business extension** (CPP teaching revenue + portfolio building)
- A **sponsor narrative** (documentary-driven cultural institution → premium sponsor pricing)
- An **artist relationship deepener** (musicians get documented by serious photographers; mutual upside)
- An **identity proof point** (signals that Big Muddy is the cultural operating layer, not just a venue operator)

**Differentiation note:** A lot of people produce "music weekends" or "songwriter festivals." Very few can authentically combine documentary photography + editorial storytelling + Southern music culture + hospitality + live performance access + guided visual narrative into one coherent experience. **This is the candidate that only Big Muddy can do.** That matters more than scoring — it's the difference between a flagship event and a unique cultural artifact.

**Best fit if:** the goal is maximum differentiation + Chase's photography pillar made visibly central + producing flagship content that fuels the rest of the year + signaling that BMT is a cultural institution in formation, not a venue operator.

### Candidate D — Mechanical Bull 20-Year Retrospective

**Shape:** 2–3 day flagship event celebrating Mechanical Bull's 20-year catalog. Concerts at the Inn + Arcade (or another corridor venue). Live album recording. Book release / catalog retrospective.

**Score against the 9 tests:** 6/9. Strong on cultural specificity, photography, sponsor narrative, recurring potential (could become "Mechanical Bull weekend" annually). Weaker if Mechanical Bull's brand isn't strong enough to anchor an event.

**Best fit if:** Mechanical Bull is a real anchor artist for Big Muddy Records and the catalog can carry the weight of a flagship.

### Candidate E — Big Muddy Season Finale

**Shape:** the closing weekend of the Big Muddy Inn season. Multi-night farewell show across the Blues Room + a partner venue. Mix of season highlights + special guests. Photo retrospective + catered dinner + auction or fundraiser component.

**Score against the 9 tests:** 6/9. Strong on Inn integration, recurring rhythm, photography/editorial. Weaker on differentiation (lots of "season finale" events exist generically) unless paired with something specific (e.g., a documentary screening, an album release).

**Best fit if:** we want a natural end-of-season ritual that doesn't require inventing a new concept.

### Candidate F — Spring Pilgrimage Concert Series

**Shape:** Natchez already hosts the **Natchez Spring Pilgrimage** annually (a 3-week heritage tourism event). Big Muddy Touring could anchor a music programming component that runs in parallel — concerts at the Inn + partner venues for visiting Pilgrimage tourists.

**Score against the 9 tests:** 7/9. Piggybacks on an existing travel-intent event (#1 is solved for free). Strong on Inn occupancy, photography, sponsor narrative. Weaker on cultural specificity if it just attaches to an existing tourism event.

**Best fit if:** we want to leverage existing tourism without inventing new travel intent.

## The sequencing question

**Three events at once vs. Y1 launch + Y2 + Y3 stagger?**

Picking three doesn't mean launching three in Y1. The team can probably produce ONE flagship event in Y1 to high quality, with the other two committed as Y2 / Y3 additions to the calendar.

**Likely natural sequence based on the scoring + operational reality:**

| Year | Event | Why this year |
|---|---|---|
| **Y1 (2026)** | **Candidate C** — Photography + Music Documentary Weekend | Highest scoring (10/10). Fits existing strengths. Cleanest differentiation. Leverages Chase's protected revenue pillar. Works at intimate scale. Produces immediate media value. Likely requires the least partner-operations complexity. |
| **Y2 (2027)** | **Candidate A** — Mississippi Corridor Weekender | Strong public-facing flagship potential. But largest operational surface area — better to build after Y1 has proven the team's capacity for multi-day events. Tourism logistics complexity needs Y1 reps first. |
| **Y3 (2028)** | **Candidate B** — Songwriter Residency / Retreat | Highest-prestige play; the "quiet authority" event. Matters more after the ecosystem has stronger gravity. Y3 is the right year to add an invite-only artist event because by then the BMT name will mean something to the artists you want to invite. |

**The doc doesn't prescribe this sequence — the scoring session should derive it.** But the scoring is likely to land here.

**Alternative:** commit to launching all three in Y1 if Tracy / Amy / Chase want to maximize coverage. Higher risk; more operational load; more learning per year. Probably wrong for a three-partner team that's also running the Inn and a touring operation.

**Hybrid:** Y1 = C + a low-key version of A (e.g., a single show + dinner under the "Weekender" name, without the full multi-venue tourism package). Test both. Defer B to Y3 regardless.

## What gets decided in the session

1. **Which 3 concepts** become the strategic destination-events portfolio? (A through F, or new ones you generate during the session)
2. **Which 1 launches first** in Y1? Specifically — name the month, name the rough format.
3. **Who owns what** for the Y1 event? Tracy + Amy + Chase split the responsibilities.
4. **Budget envelope** for the Y1 event — what's the maximum BMT will invest (out of stabilization capital from the Inn + CPP)?
5. **Success criteria** for the Y1 event — what tells us it's worth committing to as an annual?

## What happens after the decision (30-day action list)

Once the three are picked + Y1 flagship is named:

1. **Block the Inn calendar** for the Y1 event weekend. Don't accept other bookings that conflict.
2. **Draft a one-page concept brief** for the Y1 event. Used for outreach, sponsor conversations, artist invites.
3. **Identify 3–5 sponsors** who fit the event narrative. Initial outreach within 30 days.
4. **Identify the headliner / featured artists** for the Y1 event. Confirm availability and rough terms.
5. **Open a registration page** (Squarespace + Eventbrite or similar). Even with no specific date locked, capture interest list.
6. **Build the editorial calendar** for the Magazine that ladders up to the event (pre-event coverage, day-of, post-event).
7. **Brief Chase Pierson Photography** on the documentary plan for the event.

## Decision-session format

**Recommended:** 90 minutes, all three partners present, OR Chase alone if Tracy + Amy can't make it (Chase brings them their input separately).

**Agenda:**
- 0–10 min: re-read this doc + the "three inputs, one bet" memory together. Get aligned on the operating model.
- 10–25 min: read through candidates A–F. Add any new candidates that surface in conversation.
- 25–55 min: score each candidate against the 9 tests. Eliminate anything scoring <5.
- 55–75 min: pick 3 from the surviving set. Discuss the sequencing question — three in Y1 or staggered launch?
- 75–85 min: assign Y1 flagship ownership + initial budget envelope + success criteria.
- 85–90 min: schedule the next checkpoint (30-day review).
