## 1. THE CORRIDOR

### What It Is

The Mississippi music corridor runs roughly 400 miles from Natchez in the south to Memphis in the north, tracing the Mississippi River bluff country through the heart of the American Delta. This is the geography that produced blues, gospel, soul, rock and roll, and country — in that order. B.B. King, Robert Johnson, Muddy Waters, Elvis Presley. The corridor is not a marketing concept. It is the origin point of American popular music.

The operational route as documented: **Memphis to New Orleans and 14 cities in between — 18 total stops across five states.** [PLANNED — route documented, venues not all contracted]

### Why It Matters for Touring

Three structural advantages:

1. **Cultural gravity.** The story sells itself. Blues tourism draws hundreds of thousands of visitors annually — 700,000+ to Natchez alone. The audience exists and already travels.

2. **Underserved live music infrastructure.** Outside Memphis and New Orleans, mid-corridor cities (Clarksdale, Greenville, Natchez, Vicksburg, Baton Rouge) have real music heritage and insufficient live programming. No dominant regional touring operator fills this gap.

3. **The Snowbird Circuit.** Seasonal migration from northern states to the Gulf South in winter creates a ready-made touring audience between October and April. People are already on this road. The circuit captures them.
