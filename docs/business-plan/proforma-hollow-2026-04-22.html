⚠ Calibration note on the Ardent line

The $300,000 above is the gross headline number from the original pasted analysis. A second pasted analysis (the "v2" paste, captured later the same evening in the Ardent brief) walked the math down significantly. The amount Chase personally receives is almost certainly far less than $300K.

Three drivers:

  • Plaintiff structure. If Tracy and Amy are co-plaintiffs, the settlement gets split three ways before anything else happens. $300K → $100K each.
  • Legal fees. Roughly 33% to counsel on a contingency-style settlement.
  • Tax characterization. If the proceeds are damages on a personal return, federal + state tax could be ~35%.

Conservative path (3-way split → 33% legal → 35% tax on personal damages): Chase nets roughly $43,000. Aggressive path (sole plaintiff → entity-routed → favorable characterization): Chase or MBT could see $200K+.

The "Ardent ON" toggle below still shows the gross $300K landing in the Phase 1 stack — that's the optimistic ceiling, not the expected case. This proforma should not be read as "if Ardent settles, Phase 1 funds itself." The honest read is: even in a favorable Ardent outcome, additional funding sources are likely still required. The recommended posture remains treat Ardent as contingent upside, not base funding.

Scenario summary

Total sources$730,000

Surplus to operating reserve$103,000

What the surplus unlocks (Ardent ON)

  • Option A — Buffer. Hold the $103K as cost-overrun and weather-delay reserve. Safest play.
  • Option B — Accelerate Y2 builds. Use the surplus to put up 2–3 additional tiny units immediately after the barndo completes, getting to operating cash flow faster.
  • Option C — Drop the construction loan. Net surplus after dropping the $60K loan = $43K. Debt-free operation, no bank covenants. The pasted analysis flagged this as probably the right move. Note: this $43K is a Phase 1 surplus number, unrelated to (and coincidentally identical to) the ~$43K conservative-case net Chase might personally receive from Ardent. Do not double-count them.
  • Option D — Kate's farmhouse upgrade. Chunk ~$50K into immediate roof / HVAC / paint to make the move-in smooth. Trust signal.

What changes if Ardent doesn't land (Ardent OFF)

  • Funding gap: $197,000. Phase 1 cannot fully fund as scoped without Ardent (or a substitute source).
  • Lever 1 — More debt. Grow the construction loan from $60K to ~$257K. Adds debt service and bank covenants.
  • Lever 2 — More founder buy-ins. Add ~5–6 more founder buy-ins at $35K each. Requires identifying and recruiting those people in Y1.
  • Lever 3 — Scope reduction. Defer studio buildout (-$40K), solar (-$35K), and full site work (-$40K) into Phase 2. Get the barndo + first unit + minimal infrastructure in. ~$115K of deferrable scope; still leaves an ~$80K gap.
  • Lever 4 — Tracy's bankroll absorbs more. Tracy's bankroll is the canonical financing source per the handoff; the $180K (Chicken's house) + $120K (MBT equity) + $300K (Ardent-as-substitute-bridge) could conceivably all come from her if she wants to take that exposure. That's a partnership conversation, not a model decision.
  • Recommended posture. Per the pasted analysis: treat Ardent as contingent upside, not base funding. Run Phase 1 on a fundable stack. If Ardent lands, deploy it to retire debt + accelerate Y2.

None of these scenarios show the project becoming a profit center quickly — that's not what the Hollow is. It's a creation infrastructure with modest operating economics.

What this proforma is not

  • It is not a forecast.
  • It is not a basis for any commitment to Tracy, Chicken, founders, or a property.
  • It is not validated against an actual property survey, build estimate, or zoning check.
  • It does not include any income from the Theater plan, the Inn, Tuthill, the magazine, Big Muddy Touring, or any other line of MBT business — those are separate proformas to build later.
  • It does not include Chase's salary, partner distributions, or income tax.
  • It does not model what happens if the Ardent settlement lands net of significant tax (the pasted analysis flagged a possible $120–140K tax bite on $300K) — see the calibration note in the Sources section above.
  • The Ardent ON scenario uses gross $300K. The v2 calibration (captured in docs/decision-briefs/ardent-settlement-funding-scenario-2026-04-22.md) shows Chase's personal net could be as low as ~$43K conservative, ~$200K aggressive. A future version of this proforma should add a third toggle state for "Ardent (net to Chase, conservative)" once the actual claim structure and characterization are known.
