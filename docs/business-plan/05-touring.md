## 1. The Corridor

The Mississippi music corridor runs from Memphis, Tennessee to New Orleans, Louisiana — roughly 450 miles of river road through five states. This is not a marketing invention. It is the physical geography of American popular music: blues, gospel, jazz, soul, and rock and roll all trace their origin stories to stops along this river.

Big Muddy Touring operates an 18-city route along this corridor. The anchor is the Big Muddy Inn at 411 North Commerce Street, Natchez, Mississippi — a 6-suite boutique hotel with a 50-seat music venue (the Blues Room) sitting on a bluff above the Mississippi River.

**What makes the corridor special:**

- **Density of music heritage sites.** Clarksdale (Ground Zero Blues Club, the Crossroads), Indianola (B.B. King Museum), Memphis (Beale Street, Sun Studio, Stax), Natchez (the oldest settlement on the Mississippi), New Orleans (self-evident). These are not competing destinations — they are stops on one continuous road.
- **Underserved touring infrastructure.** The $19.7 billion U.S. live music market (2026) is concentrated in major metros. The corridor towns have the audiences and the venues but lack the routing infrastructure that connects them into a viable circuit. That gap is the business.
- **82% of independent artists cannot afford to tour.** The economics break when an artist has to self-fund lodging, transport, and promotion for every stop. A circuit that bundles those costs into the routing changes the math.

**The route (confirmed cities on Big Muddy Touring):**

| State | Cities |
|-------|--------|
| Tennessee | Memphis |
| Mississippi | Clarksdale, Indianola, Greenville, Vicksburg, Natchez |
| Louisiana | St. Francisville, Baton Rouge, New Orleans |
| Alabama | Muscle Shoals, Tuscaloosa |
| Arkansas | Helena, Little Rock |
| + additional corridor stops | 18 total cities mapped |

### 2.3 The Snowbird Circuit (Phase 3) -- ASPIRATIONAL

The full Snowbird Circuit is the scaled touring route — New Orleans to Chicago, with stops at corridor venues across five states. It follows the migration pattern of Northern retirees wintering in the South and Southern travelers heading north in summer.

**Seasonal routing concept:**

| Season | Direction | Route |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Fall/Winter (Oct-Mar) | Northerners heading south | Memphis down to New Orleans, with corridor stops |
| Spring/Summer (Apr-Sep) | Southerners heading north | New Orleans up to Memphis and beyond to Nashville, St. Louis, Chicago |

**Snowbird Circuit timing aligns with:**
- Natchez Fall Pilgrimage (October)
- Natchez Spring Pilgrimage (March-April)
- Wedding peak season (October-May)
- Blues Room programming calendar

**Circuit expansion targets (Phase 3, Year 3):**
- Full NOLA-to-Chicago routing
- Tourism board partnerships at each stop
- Municipal lodging offsets negotiated per city
- Subscription model (Muddy Waters Society) for circuit pass holders

> **Status flag:** The Snowbird Circuit is a Year 3 initiative. No commitments, no deposits, no contracts until Golden Triangle proves out in Year 1-2. This section is aspirational and clearly flagged as such.

### 2.4 Venue Along the Corridor -- What We Need

### 4.4 Revenue Per Show -- The 2:1 Multiplier

**This is the most important economic concept in the touring operation.**

A $1,000 show does not generate $1,000 in value. It generates $1,500, because every show triggers downstream revenue across the ecosystem.

| Revenue Layer | Amount | Source |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| **Direct show revenue** | $1,000 | Ticket sales + merch + cover charge |
| **Downstream Inn revenue** | $300-$500 | Concert attendees who book rooms, return visits, wedding inquiries from event exposure |
| **Downstream Directory revenue** | $100-$200 | Businesses along the corridor who see Big Muddy audience traffic and subscribe to MBT |
| **Content value** | Unquantified | Radio recording, Magazine article, social clips — these compound over time |
| **Total ecosystem value** | ~$1,500 | 2:1 multiplier on direct show revenue |

> **Rule:** Never evaluate a show by ticket revenue alone. Finance tracks "show-attributed revenue" as a separate metric.

**Where the downstream revenue comes from:**

1. Show attendee books a room at the Inn for the next show (or a return trip)
2. Magazine writes the city guide for the tour stop — drives tourism traffic
3. Radio plays the live recording — builds artist and venue awareness
4. Local businesses at the tour stop see the audience and want access via MBT Directory
5. Wedding couples discover the Inn through a show and book a buyout weekend

One artist booking triggers seven revenue events (from the Master Plan):
1. They play the Inn's stage
2. The show gets recorded for Radio
3. Magazine writes the city guide
4. The artist goes on Rise Up's roster
5. Their catalog lives on Records
6. The touring route adds the venue
7. Kiosk mode sells the ticket at the front desk

## Summary

Big Muddy Touring is a Phase 1 operation with a Phase 3 vision. The corridor is real. The venues exist. The audience is there. The economics work at a 50-person room with $40/head and the 2:1 multiplier.

What's missing is execution infrastructure: JP's deal needs to close, Newco needs to form, the Transit needs fleet insurance, and 2-3 venue partnerships need to be confirmed on the Golden Triangle.

Year 1 is about proving the model with 3-5 regional shows on rented or existing vehicles. Year 2 is about establishing the Golden Triangle as a regular circuit with a Sprinter. Year 3 is when the Prevost rolls and the full Snowbird Circuit activates.

The flywheel is simple: **book the shows, and everything else follows.** Shows create content. Content builds audience. Audience fills rooms. Rooms fund more shows. The 2:1 multiplier means every dollar spent on a show generates $1.50 in ecosystem value. The touring operation is not a cost center — it is the engine that powers the entire Big Muddy ecosystem.
