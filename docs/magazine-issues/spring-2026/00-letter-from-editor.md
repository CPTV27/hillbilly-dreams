Letter from the editor — Spring 2026

Welcome to the inaugural issue of Big Muddy Magazine. If you’re holding this, chances are you’re either already settled into the quiet comfort of Big Muddy Inn, or you’re contemplating the journey that will bring you here. Either way, we’re glad you’ve found us.

This publication is a companion piece to your visit, a guide to the unhurried grace of Natchez and the enduring spirit of the Mississippi River. Consider it an extension of our hospitality, a curated collection of insights and stories designed to deepen your experience of this remarkable corner of the South. Let me be clear from the outset: this is not a music-industry periodical. While the rich tapestry of Southern sound will undoubtedly echo through these pages, our focus remains squarely on the travel and hospitality that define a stay at Big Muddy Inn. We aim to illuminate the character of this place, not the charts.

Natchez, as you will soon discover, possesses a certain cadence, a rhythm set not by clocks, but by the slow drift of the river and the long shadows cast by ancient oaks. It’s a town where history isn’t just preserved; it breathes. From the stately antebellum homes that line the bluffs to the hidden courtyards whispering tales of centuries past, there’s an authenticity here that is increasingly rare. We hope Big Muddy Magazine will lead you to some of these quieter discoveries, to the nuances that make Natchez more than just a destination, but an immersion.

And then there is the river. The Big Muddy itself. It is more than a geographic feature; it is the lifeblood of this region, shaping its landscape, its economy, and its very soul. Its presence is a constant, a powerful, silent force that grounds everything around it. Our stories will often return to the river, exploring its influence on our culture, our cuisine, and the very air we breathe.

At Big Muddy Inn, our understanding of hospitality runs deep. It’s about more than just a comfortable bed or a well-prepared meal; it’s about creating an atmosphere where you can truly unwind, connect with your surroundings, and perhaps even reconnect with yourself. This magazine reflects that ethos. We want to introduce you to the artisans, the chefs, the historians, and the musicians who embody the local spirit, allowing you to engage with Natchez on a more personal level.

Music, when it appears in these pages, will be in its natural habitat: the blues wafting from a porch down by the river, the gospel harmonies rising from a Sunday service, the soft jazz providing the backdrop to an evening drink. It’s the soundscape of our lives here, a cultural inheritance rather than a commercial enterprise.

As you turn these pages, we hope you’ll find inspiration for your explorations, a new perspective on the familiar, and a deeper appreciation for the genuine warmth that defines our Southern welcome. Settle in. The river is waiting.

Tracy Alderson-Allen
Editor, Big Muddy Magazine
