# Big Muddy Magazine — Brand Identity Guidelines

**Spec version:** 2026-04-21
**Brand owner:** Hillbilly Dreams Inc. (Big Muddy Magazine — editor: Tracy Alderson-Allen)
**Voice doc:** `docs/voice/big-muddy-magazine.md` (Tracy-reviewed, final sign-off required on everything published)
**Intended designer:** Rosemary Hallmark Creative (re-engagement — already produced the Inn guideline this extends)
**Template reference:** Rosemary Hallmark Creative / The Big Muddy Inn + Blues Room, March 2023

> **Editorial premise, verbatim from the voice doc:** *"This magazine exists to sell rooms at Big Muddy Inn. Period."* The magazine is a **heritage journal** in the Garden & Gun / Oxford American tradition — long-form, photo-first, slow, observational. The guideline below reflects that discipline.

---

## 1. Cover concept

- Background: **Cloud Dancer** (cream), full bleed. This is the one brand in the family whose cover is NOT a dark ground — the magazine feels like fine stationery in the hand.
- Primary mark: the **masthead wordmark** `BIG MUDDY` in Abril Fatface all caps, Indigo Deep, large and centered, with a single Nathan-script `magazine` tucked under the "Y" at a slight tilt.
- A full-width hero photograph (Chase Pierson original, approved/ only) printed with a gentle Indigo Deep duotone wash, 60% of page height, positioned below the masthead.
- Issue metadata: `VOLUME I · ISSUE 01 · SPRING 2026 · NATCHEZ · MISSISSIPPI` set in Citrus Gothic small caps along a bottom-edge bar.
- Bottom-left of the inside spread: `BRAND IDENTITY GUIDELINES`
- Bottom-right: `BY ROSEMARY HALLMARK CREATIVE`

## 3. Primary logo — the Masthead

Three structural elements, always locked together at the same proportion:

- **`BIG MUDDY`** in Abril Fatface, Indigo Deep, set as the anchor.
- **`magazine`** in Nathan script, smaller, overlapping the underside of the wordmark, Indigo Deep or Natchez Magenta (context-dependent).
- **A thin hairline rule** below, 0.5pt, the full width of `BIG MUDDY`, Indigo Deep.

This masthead is the single most important asset in the brand. It sits at the top of every print issue, every web page, every newsletter. Never scale below 110px width on screen.

## 4. Secondary logo — the Pocket Mark

A compact lockup for anywhere the Masthead won't fit:

- `BMM` in Abril Fatface tight-set, Indigo Deep, with an em dash and `magazine` in Nathan script on the same baseline.
- Used for: email signatures, page footers, social avatars (where the full masthead is illegible), merch labels.
- Color treatments: Indigo Deep on cream (primary), cream on Indigo Deep (inverse), Natchez Magenta on cream (accent — only for social profile artwork).

## 5. Brand colors

| Role | Name | Pantone | Hex | CMYK | RGB |
|------|------|---------|-----|------|-----|
| Primary dark | **Indigo Deep** | 2119 C (inherited from Inn) | `#1A1A3E` | 100 / 95 / 35 / 45 | 26 / 26 / 62 |
| Primary light | **Cloud Dancer** | 11-4201 TPG | `#F1F0EC` | 4 / 3 / 6 / 0 | 241 / 240 / 236 |
| Feature accent | **Natchez Magenta** | 682 C (inherited) | `#994878` | 35 / 85 / 30 / 15 | 153 / 72 / 120 |
| Editorial gold | **Masthead Gold** | 871 C | `#8C7B4E` | 40 / 45 / 75 / 25 | 140 / 123 / 78 |
| Body black | **Ink Black** | Black 6 C | `#0A0D12` | 85 / 75 / 60 / 85 | 10 / 13 / 18 |

Rationale: The Magazine inherits the **entire Inn palette** — it is the narrative publication for the Inn — and adds **Masthead Gold** as the accent tone for pull quotes and issue numbers. Magenta is reserved for feature openers and issue covers; day-to-day running text and website body use Ink Black on Cloud Dancer.

Proportions in a typical 16-page spread: 50% Cloud Dancer (body), 25% Indigo Deep (masthead + display), 10% Ink Black (body type), 10% Natchez Magenta (features), 5% Masthead Gold (accents).

## 6. Incorrect usage

- Do **not** set body copy in anything other than Montserrat or the prescribed editorial serif.
- Do **not** use the Nathan script `magazine` without `BIG MUDDY` above it — the two are locked.
- Do **not** print the cover on gloss stock — this is a matte, soft-touch, heritage-feel publication.
- Do **not** use the Magazine wordmark for anything promotional that isn't editorial (no "Big Muddy Magazine presents" event posters — that's Big Muddy Touring's job).
- Do **not** break the voice rules from `docs/voice/big-muddy-magazine.md` — if a headline or pull quote drifts into press-release or Pitchfork territory, it doesn't run.
- Do **not** use AI-generated images of people. Chase Pierson originals only.

## 7. Typography

| Role | Typeface | Weights | Usage |
|------|----------|---------|-------|
| Display (masthead) | **Abril Fatface** | Regular | `BIG MUDDY` masthead only |
| Display (accent) | **Nathan** | Regular | The `magazine` underline; feature callouts |
| Headline (feature) | **Abril Fatface** | Regular | Article titles — set large, one line when possible |
| Subhead / decks | **Citrus Gothic** | Black / Regular | Decks under feature titles, section dividers, small caps runners |
| Body | **Montserrat** | Light / Regular / Medium | Feature body, web body, newsletter body |
| Editorial serif (optional) | **Source Serif Pro** (or Caslon) | Regular / Italic | Long-form features that want a print-serif feel — subs for Montserrat on print only |
| Small metadata | **Citrus Gothic** small caps | Regular | Issue numbers, datelines, photo credits |

Rule: every feature opens with a **drop cap** in Abril Fatface, Natchez Magenta, 4 lines deep.

## 8. Submark + editorial marks

1. **BMM pocket mark** (see §4).
2. **Dateline mark:** `NATCHEZ · MS · YYYY` in Citrus Gothic small caps, used at the foot of every feature.
3. **Pull-quote mark:** an oversized Abril Fatface open-quote in Natchez Magenta, 120pt, anchoring pull quotes.
4. **End-of-article mark:** a single Masthead Gold bullet `•` — replaces "THE END" or "—30—" at the close of each feature.
5. **Issue sigil:** a two-letter abbreviation inside a circle for each issue's theme — "SP" for Spring, "SU" for Summer, etc. — in Masthead Gold on Cloud Dancer. Lives on the spine.

## 9. Patterns

- **Pattern A — Masthead Stripe:** a single thin horizontal hairline in Indigo Deep on Cloud Dancer, sitting 10mm below the masthead on every page. Like a heritage journal's running rule.
- **Pattern B — River Weave:** a low-contrast halftone texture (0–10% Ink Black on Cloud Dancer) used behind pull quotes on feature openers, suggesting the fabric of a book end-paper.
- **Pattern C — Magenta Bar:** a solid Natchez Magenta bar used as the top edge of any feature opener — a visual "chapter marker" the reader learns to recognize.

## 11. Application examples (sketches for the finished PDF)

1. **Print magazine cover (8.5×11, 4-color process, matte stock):** cream ground, masthead top, single hero photo at ~60% page height with Indigo Deep duotone, one feature title overlaid bottom-left in Abril Fatface cream, `VOLUME I · ISSUE 01 · SPRING 2026` bar at the foot.
2. **Feature article opener (magazine spread):** left-hand page is a full-bleed Chase Pierson photo, right-hand page is cream with `Natchez Magenta` bar across the top, Abril Fatface article title, Citrus Gothic deck, drop-cap body opener.
3. **Web article (bigmuddymagazine.com):** Masthead Stripe at top, article title in Abril Fatface, hero image below, Montserrat body at 18px/1.65 line-height, pull quotes in Abril Fatface Natchez Magenta. Maximum line length 68 characters.
4. **Instagram story (1080×1920):** Pattern A + hero photo bottom two-thirds, Abril Fatface feature title top-center, Pocket Mark bottom-center. Swipe-up CTA: "Read on bigmuddymagazine.com."
5. **Editor's newsletter header (600px):** Masthead + hairline rule + `FROM THE EDITOR'S DESK — TRACY ALDERSON-ALLEN` in Citrus Gothic small caps. Footer: Pocket Mark + unsubscribe.
6. **Subscription card (6×4 insert for print issue):** cream, masthead top, "ONE YEAR — FOUR ISSUES — $60" in Abril Fatface, subscribe.bigmuddymagazine.com in Citrus Gothic, Magenta Bar at foot.
