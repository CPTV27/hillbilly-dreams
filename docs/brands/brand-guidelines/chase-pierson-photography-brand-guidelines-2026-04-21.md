# Chase Pierson Photography — Brand Identity Guidelines

**Spec version:** 2026-04-21
**Brand owner:** Chase Pierson (individual practitioner brand)
**Voice doc:** `docs/voice/chase-pierson-photography.md`
**Intended designer:** Chase + Tuthill Design (minimal, in-house execution — this brand is consciously modest)
**Template reference:** Rosemary Hallmark Creative / The Big Muddy Inn + Blues Room, March 2023

> **Positioning premise from the voice doc:** *"Unsentimental. Observational. Tactile. The image is the primary statement. My words support its existence, not interpret it."* This guideline reflects that — the identity is as close to zero-design as it can be while still being a system. The photography is the brand; the mark gets out of the way.

---

## 1. Cover concept

- Background: **Silver Gelatin** (see §5), full bleed — a soft neutral off-white, 2% warm.
- Primary mark: `CHASE PIERSON` in a plain sans-serif (Söhne or GT America), small, bottom-left, Ink Black.
- `PHOTOGRAPHY` directly under, letter-spaced wide, small, Ink Black.
- The rest of the cover is **one photograph** — a Chase Pierson original, full-bleed behind the type, selected to represent the range of the practice (musician backstage, architectural interior, aerial, whichever best represents the year). The image carries the cover.
- Bottom-right corner: `BRAND IDENTITY GUIDELINES · [YEAR]`

## 3. Primary logo — the Wordmark

A single-line wordmark, intentionally minimal:

- `CHASE PIERSON` in **GT America Mono Medium** or **Söhne Buch**, all caps, tight letter-spacing (tracking 20/1000), Ink Black.
- Optionally: a thin Ink Black hairline rule (0.5pt) drawn the full width of the wordmark, 4mm below it, and the word `PHOTOGRAPHY` underneath in the same typeface, small caps, wider letter-spacing (tracking 200/1000).
- No flourishes. No monogram shield. No camera icon.

The discipline here is that **the photographs are the visual system.** The mark only labels them.

## 4. Secondary logo — the Initials Mark

For favicons and social avatars where the wordmark is illegible:

- `CP` in GT America Mono Medium, Ink Black on Silver Gelatin, set inside a 1px Ink Black square frame.
- That's it. The simplest mark in the entire family.

Color treatments: Ink Black on Silver Gelatin (primary), cream on Ink Black (inverse), the square frame is always present.

## 5. Brand colors

| Role | Name | Pantone | Hex | CMYK | RGB |
|------|------|---------|-----|------|-----|
| Primary dark | **Ink Black** | Black 6 C | `#0A0D12` | 85 / 75 / 60 / 85 | 10 / 13 / 18 |
| Primary light | **Silver Gelatin** | Warm Gray 1 C | `#F4F1EC` | 4 / 5 / 8 / 0 | 244 / 241 / 236 |
| Photo duotone (warm) | **Fixer Sepia** | 7530 C | `#A79478` | 30 / 35 / 50 / 20 | 167 / 148 / 120 |
| Utility mid-gray | **Contact Sheet Gray** | Cool Gray 8 C | `#8E8E8E` | 25 / 20 / 20 / 50 | 142 / 142 / 142 |
| Accent (sparingly) | **Amber Safe-light** | 1385 C | `#C8852A` | 20 / 50 / 100 / 5 | 200 / 133 / 42 |

Rationale: The palette is a **dark room**. Ink Black and Silver Gelatin are the only two colors that appear on 99% of pages. Fixer Sepia is a photo duotone option when a single tint is needed. Amber Safe-light is used for nothing except the `SHOP` button on the portfolio and hyperlink hovers — the faintest possible indicator that the site is interactive. Contact Sheet Gray is for secondary text and dividers. There is no brand signature color, and that's the point.

Proportions: 85% Silver Gelatin, 10% Ink Black, 3% Contact Sheet Gray, 1% Fixer Sepia (duotone treatments only), 1% Amber Safe-light (links only).

## 6. Incorrect usage

- Do **not** add a camera shutter icon, lens icon, aperture icon, or any other "I am a photographer" visual cliché to the wordmark.
- Do **not** use a script font anywhere — this brand never scripts.
- Do **not** use saturated colors. If a photo is rendered in the portfolio, it renders at full color, but the brand system around it is always neutral.
- Do **not** use phrases like "capturing moments," "telling your story," or any anti-example from the voice doc.
- Do **not** use AI-generated content to fill photo slots — portfolio pieces are only real Chase Pierson originals, gallery-ready.
- Do **not** set body copy above 11pt on a portfolio page (the photographs do the heavy lifting, not the words).
- Do **not** center-align wordmarks in the logo — they are always left-aligned or bottom-aligned; never center-stacked.

## 7. Typography

| Role | Typeface | Weights | Usage |
|------|----------|---------|-------|
| Display / wordmark | **GT America Mono** | Medium | Wordmark only |
| Headline | **GT America** | Regular / Medium | Project titles, page headings |
| Body | **GT America** | Regular | All body copy, captions, commission proposals |
| Caption / metadata | **GT America Mono** | Regular | File names, EXIF info, edition numbers |
| Long-form (optional) | **Söhne Buch** | Regular / Kursiv | Essay-length artist statements or the Bearsville book foreword |

A single typeface family (GT America) does 95% of the work. Söhne is the optional long-form subs when an editorial essay needs a different feel. No serifs except in body-copy italics (which share the GT America family).

## 8. Submark + portfolio marks

1. **CP initials mark** (see §4).
2. **Edition mark:** a format like `5/25` set in GT America Mono, 8pt, bottom-right of any print label — the print-edition notation as a quiet badge.
3. **Project mark:** a fixed-width folder-label template — project title in GT America Regular, date range in GT America Mono, client in Contact Sheet Gray. Used in portfolio navigation and on the back of every print.
4. **Signature mark:** Chase's handwritten signature scanned at high-dpi, reproduced in Ink Black only for editions, certificates of authenticity, and book plates. Never used as a logo.

## 9. Patterns

Deliberately: **none.**

This brand has no pattern. Backgrounds are Silver Gelatin or Ink Black. If visual complexity is wanted, it comes from the photograph, not from decoration. The absence of pattern is itself a signature — it reinforces that the photography is the visual system and the identity is the frame.

If a supporting layout needs visual rhythm, use a **6-column grid** with 24px gutters at breakpoint 1440 — the grid is the pattern.

No JPGs unless specifically required by a vendor (catalogs, art fair kits). The wordmark PNG renders correctly on Silver Gelatin backgrounds because the file contains its own margin.

## 11. Application examples (sketches for the finished PDF)

1. **Portfolio website (chasepierson.tv):** Silver Gelatin ground, wordmark top-left small, photo grid with generous gutters, project titles in GT America Mono. Click-through opens a full-bleed image lightbox with captions in 10pt GT America Mono Contact Sheet Gray.
2. **Gallery print label (3×5, for exhibition or sale):** Silver Gelatin, wordmark top, project title in GT America Regular, date + medium in GT America Mono, edition mark lower right, Chase's signature below, Amber Safe-light hairline rule at foot.
3. **Commission proposal (letter size PDF):** Silver Gelatin, wordmark top-left, `COMMISSION PROPOSAL FOR [CLIENT]` in GT America Mono small caps, body in GT America Regular 10.5pt, line spacing 15pt. Rate sheet in a clean table. One representative photograph appears on page 2 at 60% page width.
4. **The Bearsville Studios book (hardback monograph):** dust jacket in Silver Gelatin, single Chase photograph full-bleed on front (no type), spine has wordmark and `THE BEARSVILLE STUDIOS` in GT America Mono small caps. Interior is cream stock, body in Söhne Buch, photos full-bleed on right-hand pages with captions on left-hand.
5. **Business card (2×3.5, Mohawk Superfine Eggshell stock):** Silver Gelatin, wordmark top, name (individual contact) in GT America Regular, phone + email in GT America Mono, CP initials mark on reverse. Nothing else.
6. **Email signature:** wordmark 180px wide, three lines — Chase Pierson / Photographer / chasepierson.tv — in GT America Mono 10pt Ink Black.
