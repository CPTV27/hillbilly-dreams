## The Proof: WQRZ-LP Bay St. Louis, MS

During Hurricane Katrina, WQRZ-LP was one of only 4 out of 41 broadcast stations that survived — and the **only station at ground zero.** It was the area's sole point of contact for ten days. FEMA distributed food, water, and WQRZ-tuned radios. The owner won the SBA Phoenix Award.

**This is exactly what Big Muddy Radio + Sovereign Pi is.** Community-scale broadcast infrastructure that survives when commercial systems fail.

## The Gap: Rolling Fork 2023

The Rolling Fork EF4 tornado killed 13+ people and destroyed 78% of the town. Key failures:
- No cell service during the storm — text warnings never arrived
- Residents questioned whether sirens went off at all
- City hall, hospital, and emergency response stations all destroyed
- No redundant communication systems existed

**MBT fills this gap:** Radio broadcasting (Icecast), WiFi captive portals, digital signage, offline Sovereign Pi — multiple channels that don't depend on cell infrastructure.

## The Sovereign Pi as Emergency Node

Comparable devices in the emergency mesh space:
- SafeLink: Solar-powered LoRa mesh node
- Disaster Radio: Open-source off-grid mesh
- HELPER: Raspberry Pi-based pop-up WiFi in disaster areas

**Sovereign Pi advantages:**
- $165 COGS (cheaper than any commercial emergency node)
- Offline AI — works without internet
- Solar + battery = works during power outages
- WiFi creates ad-hoc network for smartphones
