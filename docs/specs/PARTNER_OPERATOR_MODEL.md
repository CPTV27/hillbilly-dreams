# Partner-Operator Model (Northeast / Studio C)

This document establishes the commercial framework for operators running regional nodes of Hillbilly Dreams Inc. (HDI), specifically addressing Elijah and Miles for the Bearsville Creative / Studio C instance.

## The Precedent (The Natchez Deal)
Tracy and Amy operate the Natchez structural anchor (The Big Muddy Inn, Mechanics, F&B). 
*   **The Framework:** They are pure equity partners in the overarching holding entity, bypassing employee constraints.
*   **The Math:** They do not draw an hourly wage. They take a direct profit-share of Net Operating Income (NOI) generated from the physical locations they manage.

## Bearsville Strategy: Equity vs. Rev-Share

Holding company equity works for real-estate backed operators (Tracy/Amy). However, creative services (Studio C) operate differently. If we grant Elijah and Miles blanket HDI equity, their production effort subsidizes completely unrelated operations (e.g. the Inn). 

### The Recommendation: The Regional Franchise Deal
For Elijah and Miles, we do not issue master HDI equity. Instead, we structure a high-yield Revenue Share against the specific regional P&L of *Bearsville Creative*.

*   **Gross Margin Split (70/30):** All production revenue (video shoots, touring sessions at Utopia) routed through Studio C / Bearsville Creative is split:
    *   **70%** flows directly to Elijah and Miles to cover local overhead, hardware maintenance, and their personal capital extraction.
    *   **30%** flows upstream to the HDI Master node as the "Software/Brand Royalty" to cover Vercel hosting, the Master DSD platform instance, brand IP, and centralized Stripe billing logic.

### Base Retainer Mechanism
To prevent independent operator starvation during the "Summer Prep" phase (June):
*   Provide a finite, 90-day base retainer (e.g., $2k/mo flat) acting as a non-recourse draw against future production revenue, exclusively designed to subsidize their time while building the B2B Walk-In pipeline and onboarding the first 10 DSD clients on Tinker Street. 

## The Conversation Script
*"You two possess absolute operational autonomy in the Northeast to run Studio C and Bearsville Creative. Identical to Tracy and Amy in the south, you are not employees. We issue you a 70% rev-share on all regional production. HDI takes 30% for maintaining the global tech plumbing and distribution layer. If you work twice as hard, you keep the margin."*
