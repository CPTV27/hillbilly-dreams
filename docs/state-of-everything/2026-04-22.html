What happened

A pipe burst in the Bearsville house early in the day. Whether the place is livable is unclear. The morning's logistics turned into an afternoon of long voice memos from the road — thinking out loud about what kind of restructure a forced exit might enable.

Nothing was decided. A lot was sketched.

The threads that came up

  • A personal restructure. Bearsville lease exit (decided by end of day — summer in Woodstock continues via a rental and friends bridging the gap; Chase's recording-studio gear moves south). Finances centralized through MBT, debt on a paydown schedule, the truck transferring to Tracy as the last payment lands. Lease exit committed; the rest sketched, not papered.
  • Role reallocation. Chase moving toward creator, production manager, and artist-management work. Tracy on the Inn and the magazine. Amy on records, radio, and her touring schedule. Elijah running Tuthill day-to-day. Studio C as the production muscle. Sketched, not finalized.
  • Tuthill structure clarified. A 50/50 LLC with Elijah, with Studio C operating as a DBA underneath. An earlier voice memo about a 25% / franchise-fee model was earlier-stage thinking; the later doc supersedes it.
  • The magazine as Tracy's voice. Big Muddy Magazine is Tracy Alderson's editorial voice, not Chase's. Tracy decides when issues ship. Chase contributes to ecosystem content broadly but is not the magazine's primary author. Harper Lane stays as the reporter byline; the Editor's Note is Tracy's, not Chase's.
  • The Natchez tiny house community — working title "The". Paired with a small manufacturing operation. A roughly $415,000 Phase 1 envelope. Anchored on a barndominium with workshop bays, plus a first model unit. Tracy will finance Phase 1 from her bankroll. Recruitment strategy uses the magazine. Property search is open — looking at what's on the market in the Natchez area.
  • The Ritz Theater. Promoted from "parked" to active. A real business plan goes together first; once it makes sense on paper, Chase decides who to approach. The magazine article still serves as air cover.
  • Healthcare math. The shape of NY Essential Plan thresholds will affect what kind of MBT compensation structure makes sense. A real conversation for the CPA, not for tonight.

Where We Stand

The current position

The architecture (stable)

  • Three layers. HDI (the holding company), MBT (the technology platform), and Big Muddy (the consumer-facing implementations).
  • Three equal partners at MBT — Chase, Tracy, and Amy.
  • One codebase, one deployment. Next.js on Vercel, multi-tenant middleware, Sanity CMS, fourteen brand domains.
  • The shared platform. Modules built once and reused across every property — entity registry, photo archive, hospitality booking, podcast pipeline, payments, AI routing.

The brands (active)

  • Big Muddy Touring — the entertainment engine. Bands, transport, promotion.
  • Big Muddy Magazine — Chase's voice engine. Editorial publishing for the corridor.
  • Big Muddy Radio + Records — Amy's lanes. Streaming, label services, podcast.
  • The Big Muddy Inn — the Natchez anchor. Six rooms, a stage in the back parlor, a residential block off the bluff.
  • Studio C — production. Bearsville-anchored, with a Natchez branch on the horizon. Chase's personal recording-studio gear moves south as part of the lease exit; Studio C's Bearsville footprint stays put.
  • Tuthill Design — real estate photography, video, 3D, LiDAR with AI. Fifty-fifty with Elijah.
  • Chase Pierson Photography — editorial and documentary, the premium tier.

The phase (current)

Internal dogfood. Four internal clients are running on the platform — Big Muddy Touring, Big Muddy Magazine, Biscuits & Blues with Regina Charboneau, and the Big Muddy Inn. External sales are not yet a focus. The work right now is making the platform live up to itself before anyone else is invited to use it.
