## 1. What it is

A new service line inside Tuthill Design: **real estate photography**, marketed and sold under the Tuthill Design brand, with distribution in two regions — the Hudson Valley and the Deep South (Natchez corridor + Blues Highway).

It's operationally distinct from **Chase Pierson Photography (CPP)**, which keeps everything that isn't real estate — portraits, events, editorial, fine art, brand photography, Big Muddy Magazine work, Blues Room show documentation, travel/place photography.

The split in one line: **RE = Tuthill. Non-RE = CPP.**

## 2. The two-tier booking model

Every Tuthill Design Photography booking has two price tiers. The customer chooses:

| Tier | Rate | Who shoots |
|---|---|---|
| **Tuthill Design standard** | Market-competitive for the region | Any Tuthill Design photographer (may or may not be Chase). Tuthill assigns based on availability, geography, and fit. |
| **Chase Pierson premium** | Higher. Reflects Chase's track record + named-talent value. | Chase personally. Booked inside the Tuthill system as a named upgrade. |

Both tiers are bookable from the same Tuthill Design Photography surface. The client picks their tier at the point of booking. No dual funnel, no customer confusion.

**Why this works:**
- Tuthill Design gets a scalable service line that doesn't require Chase for every shoot.
- Chase gets a premium-tier channel where his name and eye command a real premium without having to market CPP separately for RE work.
- The client gets a real choice — "I want a professional Tuthill shoot" vs. "I want Chase specifically."
- Tuthill revenue compounds (more shoots possible because Chase isn't the bottleneck).
- Chase's time stays fractional on RE — the work routes to him only when the premium tier is selected.

## 4. Photography service-line revenue flow (framework — exact splits TBD with Miles)

### When the client books the Tuthill standard tier AND the assigned shooter is NOT Chase:
- Client pays Tuthill Design
- Tuthill Design pays the assigned photographer per the standard contractor rate
- Tuthill Design keeps the margin
- MBT receives the marketing-operator fee (TBD — flat or percentage)

### When the client books the Tuthill standard tier AND the assigned shooter IS Chase:
- Client pays Tuthill Design the standard rate
- Chase is paid at Tuthill's standard contractor rate (same as any other shooter)
- Chase's premium brand is not in play at this tier

### When the client books the Chase-premium tier:
- Client pays Tuthill Design the premium rate
- Chase is paid at his premium shoot rate (significantly higher than standard contractor rate)
- Tuthill keeps a coordination/delivery fee (smaller absolute margin; the premium belongs to Chase)
- MBT receives the marketing-operator fee

### Illustrative numbers (to be locked with Miles):

| Tier | Client pays | Tuthill keeps | Chase paid | MBT marketing fee |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tuthill standard (non-Chase shooter) | $800 | $300 | $400 (to assigned photographer) | $100 |
| Tuthill standard (Chase shoots) | $800 | $300 | $400 | $100 |
| Chase-premium tier | $1,500 | $300 | $1,100 | $100 |

These are placeholders. Real numbers depend on the Hudson Valley vs. Deep South market, deliverable package (photos + drone + twilight?), and whatever Miles and Chase agree is fair.

## 7. Operational workflow

**Intake** (any channel):
1. Client lands on Tuthill Design Photography booking page or contacts Tracy
2. Chooses property scope (square footage, rooms, drone yes/no, twilight yes/no, video yes/no)
3. Chooses tier: Tuthill standard vs. Chase-premium
4. Chooses date
5. Deposit collected via Stripe

**Assignment** (Tuthill operations):
- Tuthill-standard tier → Tuthill assigns from available pool based on date + geography
- Chase-premium tier → Chase personally, calendar-locked
- If Chase-premium is requested but Chase is unavailable on the requested date, client can either reschedule or drop to standard rate with different shooter (clearly communicated at booking)

**Delivery** (Tuthill standardized):
- Raw capture handled by assigned shooter
- Edits + color grading done by Tuthill's editing workflow (standardized look for the brand regardless of shooter)
- Delivery: Tuthill-branded gallery, 48–72 hour turnaround for standard, 72-hour for premium with Chase's personal selects/edits highlighted
- Client gets Tuthill Design Photography delivery, consistent with brand

**Studio C Video upsell:**
- At booking or at delivery, customer offered video walkthrough add-on
- Elijah / Studio C fulfills
- Creates a natural Tuthill + Studio C bundle under the partner-amplification thesis

## 10. Entity-structure decision for CPP (recommendation)

**Three options on the table:**

| Option | What it does | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Rename FarleyPierson LLC → Chase Pierson Photography LLC** | Keep the NY entity + EIN (81-4280721), file an amendment to change the name | Minimal disruption; EIN preserved; dedicated entity for CPP | NY filing fee + time; adds an ongoing LLC to maintain |
| **2. Close FarleyPierson LLC. Run CPP as a DBA ("doing business as") of MBT** | MBT = legal operating entity; "Chase Pierson Photography" is a DBA registered in whatever state MBT operates in | Simplest admin; MBT already exists; one entity, multiple brand surfaces (like Big Muddy Touring, Big Muddy Magazine, etc. could all be DBAs) | MBT's bookkeeping mixes platform + service revenue (not a real problem, just messier in reports) |
| **3. Close FarleyPierson LLC. Spin up new Chase Pierson Photography LLC** | New entity with new EIN, new bank account, new everything | Clean dedicated entity; clean liability boundary | Most admin overhead; new EIN means every client needs updated W-9s |

**Recommendation: Option 2 — close FarleyPierson LLC, run CPP as a DBA of MBT.**

Why:
- MBT is already the operating entity for the whole ecosystem. Adding CPP as a DBA is the same pattern Big Muddy Natchez LLC follows (a subsidiary of MBT for distinct liability profile). CPP's liability profile is low (service + IP, no guests, no road, no venue), so a DBA is sufficient without a full subsidiary LLC.
- Closes one legacy LLC (FarleyPierson) that was flagged for retirement back on 2026-04-19 anyway.
- Lets Chase bill clients under "Chase Pierson Photography" without maintaining a dedicated entity.
- Streamlines accounting — one operating entity for the ecosystem, multiple revenue streams by DBA + by department.
- Counsel still needs to rubber-stamp it, but it's the path with the fewest moving parts.

**Action:** before executing, confirm with counsel that (a) MBT can register "Chase Pierson Photography" as a DBA in its home state, (b) it's also registrable in NY (where Hudson Valley clients are), (c) closing FarleyPierson LLC doesn't have any outstanding tax or contract obligations.
