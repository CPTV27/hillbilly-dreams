## 1. Parties

- **Big Muddy Touring** ("BMT"), operating as a DBA under Measurably Better Things LLC (Mississippi) at signing. Principal: Chase Pierson, on behalf of the BMT operating group (Tracy Alderson-Allen and Amy Allen, equity partners in MBT).
- **Sean Davis** ("Collaborator"), individual, of Natchez, Mississippi, in his capacity as manager of Doug Duffy and Badd and as an independent collaborator.

## 2. Effective date and term

- **Effective date:** [TBD upon signature]
- **Initial term:** "Season One" — the period from the Effective Date through the end of the Big Muddy season as defined by the Big Muddy Inn programming calendar (anticipated ~6–8 months).
- **No automatic renewal.** At the end of Season One the parties will meet and decide jointly whether and how to continue. Continuation, modification, or termination is decided in writing.

## 3. Scope — three working tracks

This agreement governs three coordinated tracks of work:

### 3.1 Doug Duffy and Badd promotion
- **Collaborator's role:** Manager of record for Doug Duffy and Badd. Sole decision authority over the artist's bookings, calendar, public-facing decisions, and external partnerships.
- **BMT's role:** Subject to §5 below, provide non-exclusive promotional services including: rotation on Big Muddy Radio; consideration for Big Muddy Magazine editorial; consideration for Big Muddy Inn dates; consideration for Arcade Theater dates booked under §3.2.

### 3.2 Arcade Theater season
- **Collaborator's role:** Lead the relationship with the Friends of the Arcade (the venue's sponsoring organization) and the Delta Music Museum. Lead local logistics, sponsorship coordination, and venue-side relationships. Make warm introductions; **does not sign contracts on behalf of the Museum or the Friends-of organization in connection with this agreement.**
- **BMT's role:** Booking (artist selection, contracts with talent), ticketing platform selection and operation, talent flow, cross-promotion with the Big Muddy Inn, and contractual relationship with the Delta Music Museum for each show.
- **Conflict-of-interest provision:** Because Collaborator is the former director of the Arcade Theater, all transactions in which the Arcade Theater or Friends of the Arcade is a counterparty must be: (a) approved in writing by Chase Pierson on behalf of BMT, AND (b) documented in writing on both the BMT side and the Museum side. Collaborator does not unilaterally bind both sides.

### 3.3 Other artists
- **Collaborator's role:** Scout. Make first contact with artists who could be appropriate for BMT signings, Inn dates, Arcade dates, or other Big Muddy programs.
- **BMT's role:** Evaluate each artist against the BMT artist-first promo signing criteria. Decline or accept on a per-artist basis.
- Collaborator earns no fee for an introduction alone. Compensation for work done with each accepted artist is per §6 below.

## 4. Non-exclusivity

- Collaborator may manage other artists, work with other venues, and accept other engagements during the term of this agreement. No exclusivity on Collaborator's side.
- BMT may book other artists, work with other venues, sign other acts to Big Muddy Records, and pursue other programs during the term. No exclusivity on BMT's side.
- Neither party may, however, use confidential information of the other (per §10) in service of a competitor of the other.

## 5. Big Muddy Records promotional signing (separate sub-agreement)

- If Collaborator and BMT both wish to extend a non-exclusive promotional signing to Doug Duffy and Badd or any other artist Collaborator brings, that signing will be governed by a separate one-page **Non-Exclusive Promotional Signing Agreement** (TBD with counsel).
- **Core terms of any promo signing under this collaboration:**
  - Non-exclusive. Artists may sign with other labels concurrently.
  - Artists retain all masters.
  - Artists retain all rights to their original compositions.
  - Big Muddy Records receives a defined scope of promotional services to provide (rotation, editorial, social, distribution under the Big Muddy Records DistroKid Label as a promotional channel) — not ownership.
  - Term: 12 months, renewable by mutual agreement. Either party may exit on 30 days written notice.
  - No upfront cash exchanged. No advances.
  - No "label cut" of artist's revenue from any source outside the promotional channel.

## 6. Compensation

### 6.1 Arcade Theater shows
- Each Arcade show stands alone on a per-show P&L.
- After venue rent, talent, production, ticketing fees, and direct promo costs are deducted from gross ticket revenue, the net per show is allocated between Collaborator and BMT based on which side carried which costs and provided which services.
- **A per-show settlement memo will be exchanged within 14 days of each show**, signed by both parties, documenting: gross revenue, every cost line, the agreed allocation of net, and any disputed items.
- No fixed retainer, no minimum guarantee, and no per-show fee outside the allocated net.

### 6.2 Doug Duffy and Badd promotional work
- Promotional work delivered by BMT under §3.1 is in-kind. Neither party pays the other for the radio rotation, editorial, or social promotion of Doug Duffy and Badd.
- If Doug Duffy and Badd performs at the Arcade or the Inn under this agreement, those shows are governed by §6.1 (Arcade) or by the Inn's standard talent terms (Inn).

### 6.3 Scouting compensation
- An introduction alone is not compensated. If an introduced artist plays a BMT-booked show or signs a promo signing, the resulting show is compensated per §6.1; the resulting signing is governed by §5.
- No finder's fee structure exists outside per-show settlements.

## 7. Decision authority

- **Collaborator** has unilateral authority over: Doug Duffy and Badd's management decisions; Collaborator's own time and bookings; introductions and scouting.
- **BMT** has unilateral authority over: Big Muddy Records signing decisions; Big Muddy Radio programming; Big Muddy Magazine editorial; Big Muddy Inn calendar and programming; contract terms with venues and talent for any show in which BMT is the contracting party.
- **Joint decisions, requiring both written approval:** Arcade Theater dates that go on a published calendar; any external public-facing announcement that names both parties; any decision to extend this agreement past Season One.

## 8. Review checkpoint

- The parties commit to a **Season One Review** meeting within 30 days after the end of the Big Muddy season.
- At the Season One Review, the parties will jointly assess: shows completed, financial outcomes, working dynamics, what went well, what did not, what each party wants from the next period.
- Outcomes possible from the Season One Review include (illustrative, not exhaustive):
  - Continue under the same terms as Season Two.
  - Renegotiate the terms (compensation, scope, exclusivity, etc.) into a new agreement.
  - Move to a more formal operating role for Collaborator with defined compensation.
  - Discuss alternative structures including a future equity arrangement at the BMT entity level.
  - Conclude the collaboration cleanly with no further obligation on either side.
- **No structure beyond Season One is implied or committed to by this agreement.** The Season One Review produces the next decision.

## 9. Termination

- **For cause** (material breach by either party): either party may terminate with 7 days written notice if the other party materially breaches this agreement and fails to cure within that period.
- **For convenience:** either party may terminate this agreement with 30 days written notice at any time, for any reason, with no penalty.
- **At end of Season One:** the agreement expires automatically if not renewed in writing.
- **Effect of termination:** Per-show settlements for shows played before termination remain owed. Promotional work in flight at termination is wound down within 30 days. The Records promotional signing (if any) is governed by its own termination terms.

## 10. Confidentiality

- Each party will treat as confidential any non-public information of the other party received in the course of this collaboration, including but not limited to: financial figures, calendar information, artist negotiation details, vendor pricing, customer lists, and operational plans.
- Confidentiality survives termination of this agreement for 2 years.
- The Big Muddy Touring partnership framework memo and any operational artifacts shared with Collaborator are confidential to Collaborator and may not be shared with third parties without BMT's written consent.

## 11. Intellectual property

- Each party retains all pre-existing IP.
- Joint work product (the Arcade season programming, joint marketing assets, etc.) is owned jointly unless otherwise specified per-show.
- Original songs written for Big Muddy Records catalog use are governed by the separate Promotional Signing Agreement (§5).
- Big Muddy brand names, logos, and trademarks remain owned by Big Muddy Touring / Measurably Better Things LLC. Collaborator does not acquire any license to use them outside the scope of this agreement.

## 12. Independent contractor status

- Collaborator is an independent contractor, not an employee of BMT, MBT, the Big Muddy Inn, or any related entity.
- Collaborator is responsible for their own taxes, insurance, and any benefits.
- Nothing in this agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between Collaborator and BMT.

## 13. Public-facing references

- BMT may, with Collaborator's written consent, list Collaborator as a credited collaborator on Big Muddy Touring public properties (e.g., "Maintained by Big Muddy Touring with Sean Davis" on the venue directory page).
- Collaborator may, with BMT's written consent, list this collaboration in his own bio, EPK, or marketing materials.
- Neither party may issue press releases naming the other without prior written approval.

## 14. Dispute resolution

- The parties will attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith conversation, including a written summary of the dispute exchanged between principals within 14 days.
- If unresolved, the parties will engage a mutually agreed mediator before pursuing any formal legal action.

## 15. Governing law and venue

- This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Mississippi.
- Any legal action arising from this agreement will be brought in Adams County, Mississippi.

## 16. Entire agreement

- This document, together with any Promotional Signing Agreement executed under §5, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the Season One collaboration.
- Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
