## 1. The structural reframe

Scan2Plan is **a client of Tuthill Design, period.** Not three individual contractor relationships (Chase, Elijah, Miles), not a Chase-personal royalty stream, not a Studio C account. One entity contracts with one client; everyone who brought the account in gets paid out of the Tuthill pot.

**Why this matters:**

1. **Collective bargaining.** When there are three people billing a single client as individuals, each one negotiates small. When one entity bills on behalf of three people, the account has weight. Rate reviews, scope changes, and retention are stronger conversations from one integrated counterparty than three scattered ones.

2. **Operational clarity.** Scan2Plan invoices one vendor, pays one invoice, signs one contract, gets one deliverable workflow. No triangulation about whose hours are whose. Tuthill Design owns the relationship.

3. **Insurance alignment.** Tuthill Design holds the **production insurance policy that covers all of our production work across the ecosystem**. Anything Scan2Plan-related that happens on camera, on location, on a shoot, in production — Tuthill's insurance covers it. This only works if the billing entity is also the insured entity. Having it flow through Tuthill keeps that alignment clean.

4. **Chase's Scan2Plan royalty re-characterized.** The previous framing — "Chase's 2% royalty, ~$1,500/mo passive income" — is retired. Going forward, Scan2Plan royalty + fee income is **Tuthill Design revenue**, and Chase receives a distribution from Tuthill Design as one of the three principals who brought the account in. Same approximate dollar outcome to Chase (depending on Tuthill's distribution policy); cleaner entity structure.

## 4. Distribution policy among the three principals (TBD with Elijah)

Chase + Elijah + Miles all brought the Scan2Plan account into Tuthill. How Tuthill Design splits the Scan2Plan revenue between them needs a written policy. Three common models, in order of cleanest:

**Model A — Three-way equal split**
- Each of the three principals takes 1/3 of Scan2Plan revenue
- Tuthill keeps a small coordination/overhead fee (say 10–15%) for billing, insurance, and admin
- Simple, symmetrical, easy to defend

**Model B — Founder skew**
- Whoever originated the account gets a larger share (e.g., 50%)
- The two collaborators split the remaining 40% with Tuthill taking 10%
- More common in agency models; rewards origination

**Model C — Hours-worked**
- Tuthill tracks hours each principal contributes to Scan2Plan delivery and pays proportionally
- Most "fair" in a rigorous sense, but highest admin overhead
- Only makes sense if Scan2Plan requires ongoing hours (as opposed to being a passive royalty)

**Recommended:** Model A with a documented exception clause — if the nature of the Scan2Plan work shifts materially (e.g., one person starts doing 80% of the actual delivery), the split can be renegotiated. Simple baseline, clear trigger for change.

**Decision owner:** Chase + Elijah, with Miles in the loop. Needs to be documented in the Tuthill Design partnership / account register before the first post-restructure payment arrives.
