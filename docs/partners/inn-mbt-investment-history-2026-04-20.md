## Characterization — investment vs. fee-for-service

**Important for bookkeeping and entity-structure purposes:** Chase has been explicit that Tracy and Amy's contributions through this period should be treated as **investment in the MBT platform account**, not as arms-length fee-for-service revenue.

This matters for two reasons:

1. **Partner alignment.** Tracy and Amy are equity partners in the Big Muddy holding. Their early money wasn't buying software-as-a-service — it was co-financing the platform build that all three partners benefit from. Treating it as investment respects that structure.

2. **Capital structure question for counsel.** The historical $6k+ and the ongoing $1k/mo are candidates for being structured as:
   - **Capital contribution to MBT** — would grant Tracy and Amy some equity / distribution rights in MBT itself, not just in Big Muddy
   - **Intercompany loan from the Inn entity to MBT** — booked as receivable / payable, repaid out of Inn distributions over time
   - **Fee-for-service platform-subscription revenue to MBT** — simplest bookkeeping; no equity implications
   - **Hybrid:** the historical $6k as contribution / loan; the April-onward $1k/mo as subscription

Chase's stated intent is investment. The exact legal form is a counsel question that should be resolved during the same review that handles the FarleyPierson LLC closure and the CPP-as-DBA-of-MBT decision (see `docs/partners/tuthill-photography-scope-2026-04-20.md` §10).

## Chase's personal-account outlays since leaving Natchez

Chase has been spending from his personal account for ecosystem work since he was last physically in Natchez. These need to be recognized from an accounting standpoint as **ecosystem expenses with Chase as creditor**, with any revenue that came in through the same personal account treated as **ecosystem income** (not Chase's personal income).

**Framing per Chase 2026-04-20:** "Everything that I've done since I've been gone as an expense. This is the income that I generated from it."

Concretely what this means:
- Every dollar Chase paid out of his personal account for ecosystem purposes (software subscriptions, travel, shipping, equipment, contractor payments, hosting, domains, Sprinter-wrap deposits, anything) = an expense owed back to Chase by the ecosystem
- Every dollar that came into Chase's personal account from ecosystem work (photography invoices, consulting fees, platform services billed under Chase's name because the MBT account wasn't set up yet) = ecosystem revenue, not Chase personal income
- Net-out at the end of the period: if expenses > revenues, the ecosystem owes Chase. If revenues > expenses, Chase owes the ecosystem (or it's offset against his distributions).

This bookkeeping true-up is separate from the Tracy+Amy investment tracking above. The two ledgers don't touch each other — one is partners investing in the platform, the other is Chase's personal-account pass-through activity being properly reclassified.
