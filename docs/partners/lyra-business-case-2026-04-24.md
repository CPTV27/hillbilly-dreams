## Deal structure — three options

**Option A: Pure license.**
Lyra pays MBT an annual license fee for S2P access. MBT-as-licensor + MBT-as-equity-holder. Simple, but caps MBT's upside at the license fee.

**Option B: License + revenue share.**
Annual license fee + % of Lyra revenue tied to S2P-driven products. Aligns MBT's upside with Lyra's growth. More complex to administer.

**Option C: Strategic equity adjustment.**
In lieu of license fee, Lyra grants MBT additional equity above Chase's founder shares already contributed. Maximum upside, but ties MBT's revenue to Lyra's exit timeline (5–10 years).

**My recommendation: Option B.** The license fee floors MBT's revenue (predictable). The revenue share captures upside if Lyra's seed-stage projections come in. Avoids the equity-only path which leaves MBT with no operating cash from the Lyra relationship.
