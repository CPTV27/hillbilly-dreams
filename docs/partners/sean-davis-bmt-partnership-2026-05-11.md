The deal aggregates three things we don't currently have under one roof: a managed touring act, a 300-seat regional venue we can program a season into, and a year-round Natchez ops partner who lives walking distance from the Inn.

Doug Duffy and Badd joins an existing artist roster anchored on Amy Allen's three projects (Big Muddy Blues Band / The Amy Allen Experience / Arrie Aslin).

What Sean gets, if he wants it: the full Big Muddy production stack — Chase Pierson Photography, Studio C Video, Tuthill Design, the Sprinter van — plus the Glass Engine, the nine-module MBT platform that runs everything under the Big Muddy Touring umbrella (Radio, Records, Magazine, Touring, Commerce, Broadcasting, AI Content, Directory, Analytics — all served from `bigmuddytouring.com`). Recommended structure: non-exclusive, project-based partnership for the first season. Formalize an entity stake only after one full season of shows.

## Proposed structure

**Why start here:** Sean is one month into managing Doug Duffy. We've had one meeting. Both sides need a season of reps before deciding what we want this to be long-term. Doing the work first, defining the structure second, costs less than the other order — and keeps every option open at the end.

### Phase 2 — After Season One

Trigger: one full Arcade season completed and at least one Doug Duffy and Badd promo cycle through Big Muddy Radio + Big Muddy Records.

What the relationship looks like at that point is genuinely open. It could continue as project-based if that's working. It could move toward a more formal operating role on the touring side. It could grow further than that if both sides see something worth growing into. The honest answer is we don't know yet, and we shouldn't pretend to — the work we do between now and then will tell us.

Don't decide this in Season One. What we *should* do in Season One is write down what we'd want to see by the end of it that would point us toward one path or another.

## Business model options

Phase 1 is the structure we're proposing today: project-based, per-show, non-exclusive. Phase 2 — what comes after Season One — is genuinely open. The shapes below are **illustrative, not prescriptive.** They're a menu of what Phase 2 could look like. The right shape becomes obvious from doing the work, not from picking now.

### Option A — Extended project-based collaboration
Phase 1 keeps running indefinitely. Sean continues as a non-exclusive project-based collaborator on Doug Duffy and Badd promo + the Arcade season + acts he brings. Compensation per show. No fixed retainer, no formal title.

- **Strengths:** Easiest to start, easiest to walk away from. Tests fit with the lowest commitment. Both sides retain maximum optionality.
- **Limits:** Doesn't scale Sean's authority or align his incentives with BMT as a whole. If we're succeeding, this stops being the right shape eventually.

### Option B — Operating role inside BMT
Sean takes a defined seat in Big Muddy Touring with comp + scope of decision authority. Contractor or eventual W-2; part-time or full-time depending on load. Sean is paid for the work as a real role inside the operation.

- **Strengths:** Clean. Clear ownership and obligation on both sides. Scales with the work.
- **Limits:** Frames the relationship as employer-employee, which understates what's actually being built. Caps Sean's upside on what he helps produce.

### Option C — Co-operator with revenue share
Sean shares a defined percentage of BMT touring revenue — could be the Arcade season specifically, the broader touring P&L, or both. Share-of-outcome rather than fixed comp. Aligns Sean's incentives with the operation's success without requiring entity formation.

- **Strengths:** Real upside without ownership math. Strong alignment. Works under the existing DBA structure.
- **Limits:** Needs careful definition — which revenues are shared, calculated how, paid when. Doesn't give Sean the long-term stake that ownership would.

### Option D — Principal in Big Muddy Touring LLC
When Big Muddy Touring files as its own LLC (the planned next step in the entity structure), Sean is one of the principals. Mechanisms exist to phase a stake in over time so day-one commitment isn't required on either side. The most significant commitment in both directions; the largest aligned upside.

- **Strengths:** Strongest possible incentive alignment. Solves the trust question once instead of every season.
- **Limits:** Heaviest commitment. Hardest to untie if it doesn't work. Requires the kind of trust on both sides that a real shared track record produces — which is what Season One exists to build.

### How we'd decide between these

**Not in advance.** By the end of Season One, the right shape becomes apparent from what we've actually shipped together and what we've discovered we want next. The four shapes above are a menu we'll look at then, not a decision either of us has to make today.

The Season One work is the test. The Season One test produces the answer.
