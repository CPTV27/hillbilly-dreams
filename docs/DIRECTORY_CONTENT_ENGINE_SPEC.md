# DEEP SOUTH DIRECTORY — Content Engine
## How the directory and the media company work together
## March 26, 2026

---

## THE IDEA

The Deep South Directory isn't just a business network. It's a **distributed content studio** where the businesses themselves create the raw material, and Big Muddy's media company assembles it into professional content that markets the whole town.

No production crews. No agencies. No $50K tourism video budgets. Just business owners taking photos and short clips on their phones, following simple prompts, and contributing them to a shared pool. The platform and the editorial team do the rest.

---

## HOW IT WORKS

### Step 1: The Prompt
The platform sends a simple content prompt to participating businesses. Not a creative brief — just a question with a camera button.

Examples:
- "What's your best seller today? Snap a photo."
- "Show us your morning routine in 10 seconds of video."
- "What does your shop look like right now? One photo."
- "What's the one thing a first-time visitor should try? Tell us in 15 seconds."

The prompt goes out via the app, text, or email — whatever the business owner uses. One tap to open camera. One tap to submit. Done in under 30 seconds.

### Step 2: The Contribution
The business owner takes the photo or clip and submits it through the directory. The platform:
- Tags it with the business name, category, and location
- Adds it to the shared content pool for their town
- Runs it through the AI for caption, hashtags, and metadata
- Queues it for the business's own social channels (if they're on the $20 tier)

The business owner did one thing: took a photo. The platform did six things with it.

### Step 3: The Assembly
Big Muddy's editorial team (which is mostly AI + Chase's oversight) takes the pool of contributed content and assembles it into:

**Magazine content:**
- "A Saturday on Main Street in Natchez" — photo essay from 8 businesses' contributions
- "Where to Eat in Natchez" — city guide built from restaurant submissions
- "The Corridor This Week" — weekly roundup from businesses across multiple towns
- Business spotlights — full profiles generated from directory data + submitted photos

**Radio content:**
- "What's happening in Natchez this weekend" — scripted from events and submissions
- Business owner interviews (scheduled through the directory, recorded via phone)
- Background audio captured at businesses (the sizzle of a grill, the buzz of a barber shop)

**Video content:**
- Town sizzle reels assembled from 15-second clips
- Business highlight reels (one business, multiple submissions over time)
- Corridor overview videos for tourism boards
- Social clips cut from longer content

**Tourism board deliverables:**
- Monthly content packages for CVBs
- Seasonal campaign assets
- Event promotion materials
- Visitor guide content

### Step 4: The Distribution
Every piece of assembled content goes back out through multiple channels:
- Big Muddy Magazine (website, newsletter, social)
- Big Muddy Radio (on-air mentions, playlists, segments)
- Individual business social channels (auto-posted with approval)
- Deep South Directory listings (photo galleries, featured content)
- CVB/tourism board channels
- Google Business Profile updates

Every business that contributed gets credited and linked. Their directory listing gets richer. Their social presence grows. Their Google profile gets fresh photos. All from one photo they took in 30 seconds.

## THE CONTENT PROMPT SYSTEM

Prompts should be:
- **Simple:** One sentence. One action. Under 10 words.
- **Specific:** "Show us today's special" not "Create content for your business"
- **Fun:** "What's the weirdest thing in your shop?" not "Submit a product photo"
- **Seasonal:** Tied to what's happening (festivals, holidays, weather, events)
- **Collaborative:** "The Natchez Dinner Crawl needs your best dish photo" — framed as participation, not obligation

Prompt schedule:
- 2-3 prompts per week maximum
- Businesses can ignore any prompt (no penalty, no guilt)
- The AI learns which businesses respond to which types of prompts and adjusts
- High-response prompts get repeated. Low-response prompts get retired.

Example prompt calendar for one week:
- **Monday:** "What does your Monday morning look like? One photo."
- **Wednesday:** "What's your best seller this week? Show us."
- **Friday:** "What are you looking forward to this weekend? 15 seconds of video."

## THE ASSEMBLY PIPELINE

```
Business submits photo/clip
    ↓
AI processes:
  - Identify subject (food, interior, product, person, exterior)
  - Generate caption + hashtags
  - Tag with business, category, location, season
  - Quality check (too blurry? too dark? auto-enhance?)
    ↓
Content enters the pool:
  - Available for magazine articles
  - Available for radio show mentions
  - Available for video assembly
  - Available for social posts
    ↓
Editorial assembly (AI + human oversight):
  - Group by theme (food, Main Street, weekend, event)
  - Generate articles, city guides, roundups
  - Cut video sizzle reels from clips
  - Draft radio segments
    ↓
Distribution:
  - Publish to magazine
  - Schedule social posts
  - Feed to radio show
  - Update directory listings
  - Package for CVB
  - Auto-post to contributing businesses' channels
    ↓
Attribution:
  - Every contributing business credited
  - Links back to directory listing
  - Performance data fed back to business dashboard
```

## WHY THIS IS UNBEATABLE

1. **The content is authentic.** Real businesses, real products, real people. No stock photos. No staged shoots.

2. **The cost is near zero.** Businesses are creating content they'd create anyway (taking photos of their food, their shop, their work). The platform just gives it a destination.

3. **The volume is unlimited.** 20 businesses each submitting 2 photos a week = 160 fresh content pieces per month. No agency can match that volume at any price.

4. **The flywheel compounds.** More businesses on the directory = more content = better magazine/radio/video = more tourism = more customers for directory businesses = more businesses join the directory.

5. **Nobody else can do this.** You need the directory (the network), the media company (the editorial engine), AND the platform (the AI that processes and distributes). We have all three. Nobody else does.

6. **It's the Micromedia in a Bottle model.** Hotel + magazine + radio + directory on one engine. This is the licensable package — any town can run this system. We're proving it in Natchez first.
