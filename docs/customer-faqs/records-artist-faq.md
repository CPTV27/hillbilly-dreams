### Q: How fast do new releases go up on platforms?

1.  **Platform Processing:** DSPs need time to ingest, process, and properly catalogue your music.
2.  **Playlist Consideration:** Editorial playlist curators at Spotify, Apple Music, etc., typically require submissions at least 3-4 weeks in advance of your release date. This window is essential if you want your music to be considered for official playlists.
3.  **Promotional Runway:** A longer lead time gives you and Big Muddy Records sufficient time to plan and execute a meaningful promotional campaign – writing press releases, pitching to media, lining up social media content, and coordinating with any collaborators.

### Q: What about migrating my existing catalog from another distributor?

A: Migrating your catalog to Big Muddy Records is a common and straightforward process, and we're here to guide you through it. We'll help you ensure a seamless transition without any interruption to your music's availability or loss of streaming data.

Here's the general approach:
1.  **Don't Take Down First:** The most important rule is **do not take your music down from your current distributor before you've uploaded it to us.** This helps preserve your existing stream counts, playlist placements, and listener data.
2.  **Match Metadata:** We'll work with you to ensure all the metadata (track titles, artist names, album titles, ISRCs, UPCs) for your existing releases matches exactly what's currently live. The ISRC (International Standard Recording Code) for each track and the UPC (Universal Product Code) for each release are particularly important as they act as unique identifiers.
3.  **Upload to Big Muddy:** You'll upload your existing catalog to our system, providing the exact same audio files, artwork, and metadata.
4.  **Set Release Date:** We'll set the "release date" for these migrated tracks to their original release date, which helps DSPs recognize them as existing content.
5.  **Deliver to DSPs:** We'll deliver your catalog to all DSPs. Because the ISRCs and UPCs match, the DSPs will recognize these as the *same* tracks and albums, effectively "transferring" the ownership of the distribution to Big Muddy Records without creating duplicates or losing data.
6.  **Take Down from Old Distributor:** Once we confirm that your music is live on all platforms through Big Muddy Records, and you've verified everything looks correct, *then* you can request your previous distributor to take down your catalog.

We'll provide you with step-by-step instructions and support to make this process as smooth and worry-free as possible.
