# How to Use This Press Release Template

This template is designed to help you quickly and effectively communicate important announcements from your organization, MBT, to the media and public. It adheres to standard AP (Associated Press) style guidelines, ensuring clarity, conciseness, and professionalism.

**Key Principles for Effective Press Releases:**

1.  **Newsworthiness:** Before drafting, ask yourself: Is this announcement genuinely newsworthy? Does it impact a significant audience? Is it timely?
2.  **Clarity & Conciseness:** Get straight to the point. Use clear, simple language. Avoid jargon unless absolutely necessary and explained.
3.  **AP Style:** Follow AP style for grammar, punctuation, capitalization, and numbers. (e.g., spell out numbers one through nine, use figures for 10 and above; abbreviate states when used with a city; use "Mr.," "Mrs.," "Ms." sparingly, usually only on first reference for clarity).
4.  **Inverted Pyramid:** Present the most important information first (the 5 W's in the lede), followed by progressively less crucial details.
5.  **Objective Tone:** While promoting your news, maintain an objective, factual tone. Avoid overly promotional or hyperbolic language.
6.  **Quotes:** Use quotes to add a human element, convey emotion, and provide expert commentary from key individuals.
7.  **Call to Action (Implied):** While not explicitly a "call to action" like in marketing, a good press release should encourage journalists to learn more, interview spokespeople, or cover the story.

**Instructions for Filling Out the Template:**

*   **[BRACKETED] Placeholders:** All text enclosed in `[BRACKETS]` are placeholders that *must* be replaced with specific, accurate information relevant to your announcement. Do not leave any brackets in the final release.
*   **Headline:**
    *   Craft a compelling, active-voice headline that summarizes the core news in 8-12 words. It should grab attention and clearly state the most important aspect of the announcement.
    *   Capitalize the first letter of each major word (title case).
*   **Dateline:**
    *   `[CITY, STATE]` where the news originates or where MBT is headquartered. Use AP-style abbreviations for states (e.g., N.Y., Calif., Fla.). Do not abbreviate states that are five letters or fewer (e.g., Idaho, Ohio, Texas).
    *   `[DATE]` should be the date the press release is officially issued.
*   **Lede Paragraph (The 5 W's):**
    *   This is the most critical paragraph. It must answer the Who, What, When, Where, and Why/How of your announcement in one concise paragraph (typically 30-50 words).
    *   Think of it as the "elevator pitch" for your news.
*   **Body Paragraphs (3-4):**
    *   **Paragraph 1:** Expand on the "WHAT" and "WHY." Provide more context, details, and the significance of the announcement. Include a *quote* from a relevant spokesperson (e.g., MBT CEO, project lead) to add authority and a human voice.
    *   **Paragraph 2:** Detail the "HOW" and "WHEN." Explain specific features, benefits, or implications for your stakeholders (customers, partners, employees, industry). Provide data or examples if applicable.
    *   **Paragraph 3 (and 4, if needed):** Discuss future outlook, next steps, or broader impact. Reinforce MBT's mission or vision in relation to this announcement. Another quote can be used here if it provides a different perspective or emphasizes a future vision.
    *   **Length:** Aim for paragraphs of 3-5 sentences each. Break up long blocks of text.
*   **###:** This symbol indicates the end of the main body of the press release, as per AP style.
*   **About MBT (Boilerplate):**
    *   This is a standardized, evergreen paragraph about your organization. It should be consistent across all press releases.
    *   It briefly describes MBT's mission, what it does, its values, and its history or market position.
    *   *Customize this once and reuse it.*
*   **Media Contact:**
    *   Provide clear contact information for journalists who wish to follow up, ask questions, or request interviews.
    *   Ensure the person listed is prepared to handle media inquiries promptly.

**After Drafting:**

1.  **Proofread:** Carefully check for typos, grammatical errors, and factual inaccuracies. A fresh pair of eyes is always helpful.
2.  **Review AP Style:** Double-check adherence to AP style guidelines.
3.  **Read Aloud:** This helps catch awkward phrasing or run-on sentences.
4.  **Get Approvals:** Ensure all necessary internal stakeholders (legal, management, marketing) have reviewed and approved the release before distribution.
5.  **Distribution:** Send the press release to relevant media outlets, industry publications, and your own communication channels (website, social media). Consider using a wire service for broader distribution.
