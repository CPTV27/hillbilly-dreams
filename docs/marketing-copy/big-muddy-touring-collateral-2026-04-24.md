## The Route

**Eyebrow:** Memphis ⇄ New Orleans

**Headline:** The river is the route.

**Body:**
Most tours get stitched together city by city — a different promoter, a different room, a different van problem in every market. Big Muddy runs like a spine. Fewer dead miles. Cleaner room sequencing. Media output baked into every stop.

**Five anchor stops:**

| # | City | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | Memphis | Launch market. Press, talent inflow, regional credibility. |
| 02 | Clarksdale | Heritage stop. Intimate room, cultural weight, content gold. |
| 03 | Natchez | The owned anchor. Our venue, our team, our capture. |
| 04 | Baton Rouge | Scale-up market. Partner room, college reach. |
| 05 | New Orleans | Premium close. Media release, industry attention. |

**Typical run:** 5 stops · 6–8 days · zero dead legs.

---

## The Operating Promise

**Eyebrow:** Offer stack

**Headline:** Route control + room access + content capture.

**Body:**
That is the promise. It compounds three ways:

- **For artists** — room tiering matched to current draw, production coordination handled by one team, a content package leaves with you at the end of the run.
- **For managers** — predictable routing, fewer fragmented vendors, one number to call when something breaks.
- **For partners** — fill-rate optimization on under-booked rooms, recurring branded programming, year-round inventory instead of one-off festival math.

**We keep the media. You leave with assets. The next tour sells itself.**

---

## How To Engage

**Eyebrow:** Artist intake funnel

**Headline:** Submit the artist. Receive a route.

**Body:**
The funnel is built to feel like an operator, not a contact form. You give us enough signal — act, window, draw, economics — and we come back with a proposed corridor, room tier, and content package.

**Five steps:**

1. Submit artist + window
2. We score route fit
3. We match room tier
4. We assign a content pack
5. You receive a routed offer

**Form fields (intake):**
- Artist / act name
- Manager email
- Target route window (May / June / July / open)
- Expected draw (50–100 / 100–250 / 250–500 / 500+)
- Origin city, genre, merch strength, content needs, minimum economics

**CTA:** Submit routing request

## Live pitch opener (coaching note)

Don't lead with "vertical integration thesis" in conversation — it loses non-operator buyers. The doc opens with it because the doc is a controlled artifact; live, you open with the concrete and layer the thesis after.

**Live opener (verbatim):**
> "We run a fixed tour route from Memphis to New Orleans. Five rooms. Six to eight days. We book it, we route it, we capture it."

Then layer the thesis once they lean in.

## What this actually is (positioning anchor)

Not a touring brand. Not a booking agency. Not a content studio.

**A routing engine with a media flywheel.**

## Voice rules applied

- Direct second person where natural ("you give us enough signal…").
- Operator language preserved verbatim where Chase has locked it.
- No "we believe / we think / our mission is" — the buyer assumes our mission, they want to know what we deliver.
- No filler adjectives ("seamless," "best-in-class," "innovative") — buyers in this category register those as red flags.
- Short sentences, period at the end. Stack short sentences for cadence.
