## Why this slot matters

The stretch venue is the highest-leverage slot in the six-person send. Artists and managers are people-to-people. The venue slot is the only one that tests **infrastructure**: does a venue we don't control see the corridor as a real upgrade over their existing booking flow?

A "yes" from the stretch venue de-risks the entire corridor model. A polite-no still gives you a real signal on what to fix in the pitch.

Three candidates evaluated. One picked. Two held in reserve.

---

## Candidate 1 — The Hi-Tone, Memphis ✓ PICKED

**Corridor node:** #01 — Memphis (launch / press / talent inflow)
**Cap range:** mid-cap, multiple rooms (flexible tiering)
**Programming:** indie-forward, open to routing experiments
**Live Nation lock?** No.

**Why it fits the pitch:**
- Memphis is the critical entry node for the corridor — winning Hi-Tone validates the whole spine.
- Multiple rooms means we can slot different draw levels into the same venue across the season.
- Indie-forward booking culture aligns with "operator, not contact form" framing.

**Angle to lead with:**
> "This gives you recurring midweek programming tied to a corridor, not one-off bookings."

**Custom line for cover email:**
> "This would give you a recurring Memphis entry point tied to a 5-stop corridor — not one-off bookings."

**Risk:**
- Books a lot already. Differentiation has to be the routing-plus-content angle, not just "we have artists." Don't pitch like a booking agency.

**What it unlocks if they reply:**
- Validates Memphis node viability immediately.
- De-risks the entire corridor model.
- Strongest possible proof point for the next round of artist + manager outreach.

## Candidate 2 — Toulouse Theatre, New Orleans (HOLD)

**Corridor node:** #05 — New Orleans (premium close / media release)
**Cap range:** right-sized for a premium-close room
**Programming:** diverse, French Quarter cultural gravity
**Live Nation lock?** No.

**Why it fits:**
- Sits exactly at node #05. Built-in cultural weight. The "media release + industry attention" framing maps to it perfectly.
- Diverse programming = not locked into one pipeline = open to a routed corridor offer.

**Angle to lead with:**
> "We turn your room into the final stop — media release + industry attention."

**Risk:**
- Higher expectations on talent quality. The routing has to feel curated, not filler. Sending Toulouse a marginal act would burn the relationship before we even open one.

**Why held, not picked:**
- Hi-Tone is the entry node — winning it cascades through the corridor. Toulouse is the close, which only matters once the front of the spine is real. Save it for round two, after we have a proven booking on the spine to point at.

## Candidate 3 — Mid City Ballroom, Baton Rouge (HOLD)

**Corridor node:** #04 — Baton Rouge (scale-up market / partner room)
**Cap range:** college-driven, strong midweek potential
**Programming:** less saturated than NOLA, easier to establish recurring presence
**Live Nation lock?** No.

**Why it fits:**
- Maps cleanly to node #04. Less crowded market = easier to establish a recurring touch-point.
- College audience = midweek routing fits naturally.

**Angle to lead with:**
> "We bring you a consistent pipeline of touring acts that match your room size."

**Risk:**
- Lower national visibility. Has to be positioned as part of the system, not as standalone prestige.

**Why held, not picked:**
- Same logic as Toulouse — the middle of the spine isn't where you prove the model. The ends are. Mid City is a strong round-two add once we have Hi-Tone or Toulouse as a proof point.
