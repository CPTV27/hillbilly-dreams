## The goal in one sentence

**Structure Chase's MBT compensation so MAGI lands at $29,900–$31,000 for tax years 2026 and 2027, holding him inside NY Essential Plan 1 with a clear buffer below the 200% FPL cliff that hits July 1, 2026.**

Everything else in this memo is in service of that number.

---

## Why this number, in dollars

NY Essential Plan 1 (150–200% FPL, single individual, 2026):
- **$0 monthly premium**
- **$0 deductible**
- $15 doctor visit copay, $3 generic prescription copay
- **Dental + vision included**
- Real provider network (not the narrower Medicaid network)
- W-2 earnings count toward Social Security

The two cliffs on either side:

| Cliff | Threshold | What happens if we go under/over |
|---|---|---|
| **Lower (138% FPL)** | < $22,025/year ($424/wk) | Drops into Medicaid — narrower networks, asset tests in some categories, less portable, no Social Security earnings record |
| **Upper (200% FPL, post-July-1-2026)** | > $31,920/year ($614/wk) | Drops into Marketplace QHP — ~$500–700/month premium estimate for a single 40-something male in Hudson Valley, plus deductibles. Annual cost: ~$6,000–$10,000 in premiums + out-of-pocket. |

**Target:** $575–$600/week W-2 = $29,900–$31,200/year. Comfortably inside Essential Plan 1, ~$700–$2,000 of buffer below the 200% FPL cliff.

---

## The July 1, 2026 cliff (already on the books)

- Essential Plan eligibility drops from 250% FPL → **200% FPL** on 2026-07-01.
- Federal funding cut. ~450,000 New Yorkers losing coverage on that date.
- Anyone earning between 200% and 250% FPL ($31,920–$39,900) gets bumped to Marketplace.
- This is why we target the middle of the 150–200% range, not the top — a small income bump or a good Tuthill quarter shouldn't push Chase off the plan.

---

## The MAGI gotcha — the thing the CPA needs to know we already know

Essential Plan eligibility is calculated from **MAGI (Modified Adjusted Gross Income)**, not just W-2 wages. MAGI includes:

- W-2 wages ✓
- K-1 partnership income ✓ ← **this is the issue**
- Taxable interest, dividends, rental income ✓

This means **Chase's MBT partner distributions stack with his W-2 for FPL calculation purposes.** We can't just put him on a $30K W-2 and pay big distributions on top — the K-1 rolls back into MAGI and pushes him over the cliff.

This is the central design constraint for the comp structure.

## Two structural options to bring to the CPA

### Option A — Tight MAGI control
- **W-2:** ~$575–$600/week = $29,900–$31,200/year
- **Partner distributions:** capped low (say, $2–3K/year ceiling)
- **Cash flow to Chase beyond W-2:** routed through legitimate reimbursed business expenses (company card with monthly cap, properly documented)
- **MBT excess profits:** retained as retained earnings, not distributed
- **Pros:** Keeps MAGI inside Essential Plan range. $0 healthcare premium. Clean.
- **Cons:** Requires real MBT discipline on what gets distributed. Tracy + Amy need to be aligned because retained earnings affect all three partners' optionality. May not work if MBT scales fast and Chase needs liquidity.

### Option B — Accept the ceiling
- **W-2:** at "reasonable compensation" level (CPA's call on what defends to IRS)
- **K-1 distributions:** as needed
- **Healthcare:** Marketplace QHP, budget ~$500–700/month premium + deductibles as a business or personal expense
- **Pros:** No artificial constraint on MBT distributions. Simpler to administer.
- **Cons:** $6,000–$10,000/year out-of-pocket for healthcare that Essential Plan would have given for $0. Worse coverage.

**Chase's lean (per the analysis):** Option A, threaded carefully, with explicit acknowledgement that we may have to pivot to Option B if MBT scales faster than expected.

## Specific questions for the CPA

1. **Reasonable compensation floor.** What's the IRS-defensible W-2 floor for an LLC member who provides services to the LLC? $575/week works only if Chase's documented hours support that as the right number for the work he does (vs. shareholder-level work).

2. **Guaranteed payment vs. W-2.** Chase is open to either per the handoff. Does a guaranteed payment land in MAGI differently than W-2 wages? (Both flow through, but the mechanics differ.)

3. **NY vs. MS residency.** Chase is anchoring more activity in Mississippi (Big Muddy Inn, Natchez tiny house community) but keeping a NY footprint (Bearsville Studio C, summer ops). **Critical question:** Mississippi has not expanded Medicaid and the Essential Plan doesn't exist there. So even if the center of gravity moves south, **NY residency probably remains the right choice for healthcare**, as long as Chase has a defensible NY domicile (lease, address, voter reg, etc.). CPA confirms? Domicile vs. residency distinction matters here.

4. **Business card with monthly cap — clean or imputed income?** Can the "MBT debit card for personal expenses with monthly cap" model be papered cleanly, or does it create imputed income to Chase that goes into MAGI? If imputed, the cap has to be inside MAGI tolerance.

5. **K-1 timing.** Does it matter for FPL whether the K-1 is issued in 2026 vs. 2027? Can MBT defer distributions to manage MAGI year-by-year?

6. **MBT operating agreement.** The three equal partners (Chase / Tracy / Amy) — does that need to change for tax purposes? Is there an argument for unequal allocation of profit/loss vs. equal ownership of capital?

7. **Retirement plan as MAGI lever.** SEP-IRA or solo 401(k) contributions reduce MAGI. Could Chase do a $5–10K SEP contribution out of MBT to give himself a buffer at the 200% FPL ceiling, without losing all liquidity?

8. **HSA option.** If Chase moves to Marketplace QHP eventually, an HSA-eligible high-deductible plan could let him stash pre-tax money for medical. Worth understanding even if Plan A holds.

## What's outside our control (and the CPA's)

- NY benefit thresholds (state-set; federal funding cuts may continue under the current administration).
- The July 1, 2026 cliff (already on the books, won't move).
- MBT revenue volatility — we can't predict perfectly. Need a comp structure that survives a +/- 30% MBT revenue year without breaking.

---

## What needs to be true at MBT for Option A to work

These are operational, not CPA — flagging because they're load-bearing:

1. **MBT operating agreement** must specify a distribution policy. Without one, retained earnings are implicitly Chase's anyway, and the IRS will impute distribution income.
2. **Tracy and Amy need to sign off.** Three-way partnership. If we cap Chase's distributions to keep his MAGI low, that may affect the partnership's general distribution policy. They need to want to do this together, not feel constrained by it.
3. **Bookkeeping discipline.** Business expenses on the business card must be cleanly business expenses. Personal expenses with a monthly cap must be papered as compensation (imputed) or stay below imputed-income thresholds. Sloppiness here loses the strategy.
4. **Annual MAGI checkpoint.** Q3 of each tax year, project full-year MAGI and adjust distributions in Q4 if needed to stay under $31,920.
