## The framing (Chief of Staff summary)

Chase is **not** running a personal financial optimization. He's making a structural trade.

**What Chase contributes:**
- Full-time creative + strategic leadership across MBT, Tuthill, Studio C, Big Muddy properties
- The voice engine (Magazine, content, narrative — although per the 2026-04-22 magazine reframe, the magazine voice belongs to Tracy; Chase contributes other content)
- The S2P royalty stream (~$1.5K/mo passive)
- Technical infrastructure (codebase, MCP, AI routing, photo archive)
- 30+ years of relationships and taste

**What Chase needs in return:**
- A roof (compound apartment in the Hollow, when built)
- Health insurance (Essential Plan 1 via minimal W-2)
- A truck that runs (business-owned, business-insured)
- A phone that works
- Food
- Pocket money for the small stuff (guitar strings, gas)

**Who covers the rest:**
- Tracy + Amy (partners in MBT)
- The businesses themselves (operating expense lines, in-kind distributions)
- Kate (day-to-day hospitality, garden, kitchen)
- Chicken (if recruited — studio income, dog room)

This is, per the paste's framing, "how family farms, ranch operations, religious communities, creative compounds, and multi-generational businesses have worked for centuries. The business feeds the people; the people run the business."

## The three preconditions (the paste's framing, lightly compressed)

For household-as-enterprise to actually hold, three things have to be true:

### 1. The partnership holds

MBT has to survive 20+ years of three-way relationships through whatever life throws at it. That requires papered governance:

- Operating agreement with clear roles, voting rights, buyout mechanics
- "What happens if Chase can't work for 6 months" clause
- "What happens if Chase wants to leave" clause
- "What happens if Tracy or Amy wants to leave" clause
- Right of first refusal on equity transfers
- Key-person insurance policies on each partner

**Status:** none of this is currently papered. The MBT LLC formation plan at `docs/decision-briefs/measurably-better-things-llc-formation-plan-2026-04-21.md` is the first step, but operating-agreement detail of this depth is a counsel-drafted document, not a template.

### 2. The businesses actually generate enough

The "they take care of me" promise needs the businesses to throw off ~$50–75K/yr of discretionary capacity to cover Chase + business operations in-kind. **Right now MBT isn't revenue-positive. The Inn isn't yet either. Tuthill + Studio C have profit potential but aren't distributing consistently.**

This is a Y2-Y3 confidence interval, not a Y1 one. Y1 of the compound build, the math is tight.

### 3. Chase doesn't resent it later

The paste's words: "At 60, when Tracy is running the Inn and Amy's touring and you're still the content engine and the books guy and the tech guy, the mental math can shift. 'I gave them this whole thing. What did I get?'"

Mitigation: equal MBT ownership. Chase's 1/3 of MBT is the long-term answer — the compound is an asset, the magazine is a brand, the catalog is printing. That's retirement and "being taken care of" in the structural sense.

But: this only works if the operating agreement is bulletproof. Casual = handshake, and handshakes don't survive divorce or estate disputes.
