The Opportunity

Most people who spend a lifetime making things never own anything.

The working creative class — touring musicians, road crew, session players, photographers, writers, engineers, the people who actually make the things — spend entire careers renting. By 55+ they have a checking account, a pile of receipts, and no piece of ground anywhere with their name on it.

There is no existing answer at any scale. The retirement industry serves people with W-2 401(k)s. Co-living is for 20-somethings. RV parks aren't it. The "creative retirement community" doesn't exist.

If we build it once, in the right place, the same model runs in the Catskills, then Asheville, then anywhere with cheap land and a creative diaspora.

Tier 2
Associate Members
Own their unit. 60 days/yr personal use. We run the rental program for the other 305 days. 60/40 split owner/RRC. Most owners opt in.
Tier 3
Working Residents
Short-term artist residencies. Rent only — $900/mo. Use the units we retain for the program.
Tier 4
Founding Circle
Capped at 10. $35K cash or a 5-year vested role at the property. Equity stake in the land + buildings. The first ten people who built it.

Follow the Sun

Mississippi in winter. Catskills in summer.

Mississippi handles October through April — when the pine smells like cold smoke and the Inn does its best business. Catskills handles May through September — when MS gets brutal and the Hudson Valley creative network is at full volume.

One membership. One ledger. Two pieces of ground. Residents pack twice a year and live where the weather is good.

Important: Catskills is in the pitch we're using to talk to potential founders, but the financial model we're discussing tonight is Mississippi only. Catskills is Phase 3 — separate plan, separate capex, probably 2027–2028. Don't commit to it tonight.

Why Big Muddy Builds This

The infrastructure spine for everything else.

RRC = housing for the touring artists, studio talent, magazine writers, and Inn staff we're trying to keep close.

RRC = customer acquisition for MBT, the Inn, the studio, and Big Muddy Touring. Every founder + member is a node in the network.

Big Muddy Magazine = inbound funnel for the RRC. The Tracy-edited features write themselves into customer flow.

House band = both Big Muddy Touring revenue and RRC entertainment. Same artist roster, two business uses.

The four properties — RRC, Inn, Studio C, Tuthill — operate as one connected system. RRC is the piece that finally gives the system a place to live.

What Could Break This

Three risks matter most.

Risk 1: HOPA 55+ qualification doesn't hold. The whole model assumes Housing for Older Persons Act compliance. If Adams County zoning or the HOPA structure can't be made to work on agricultural-zoned land, we pivot to a mixed-age cooperative — different liability, different marketing, different exit. Needs legal sign-off before Phase 1 close.

Risk 2: Chicken says no. $180K of the funding stack is the Muscle Shoals house. If Chicken doesn't accept, the gap goes from $197K to $377K, and the Y1 sweat-founder slot is empty. Pitch artifacts deployed; conversation in Natchez in May.

Risk 3: Build cost overrun. 12% contingency. If we hit 30% (real risk in rural MS construction with material price volatility), Phase 1 goes from $627K to $700K+. Mitigation: regional sourcing, in-house labor, phasing the pool/pond to Y2.
