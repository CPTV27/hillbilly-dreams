# Perplexity Deep Research Prompt
## What features are people actually asking for in business tools?

Copy everything below the line into Perplexity Deep Research.

---

I'm building an AI-powered business platform for independent small businesses in small towns. The platform connects to their existing tools (QuickBooks, Square, Stripe, Google Business Profile, Booking.com, social media) and uses AI to give them actionable intelligence, automate marketing, monitor reviews, and run daily briefings — all without requiring them to learn new software.

I need to know what features real business owners are requesting, upvoting, and begging for across the tools they already use. I want to build the features that have the highest demand and the lowest satisfaction — the gaps where existing tools are failing people.

## SECTION 2: Cross-Platform Integration Gaps

What integrations do business owners most want between their tools that don't exist or work poorly?

Look for complaints like:
- "Why can't my POS talk to my accounting software automatically?"
- "I wish my booking system updated my Google hours"
- "Why can't I see my reviews and my revenue on the same screen?"
- "I enter the same data into three different systems"

I want the most painful data silos — places where business owners are manually copying information between systems.

## SECTION 3: AI Features People Actually Want (not what tech companies are shipping)

Search Reddit (r/smallbusiness, r/entrepreneur, r/ChatGPT, r/artificial), Twitter/X, and business forums for what small business owners actually want AI to do for them. Not what AI companies are building — what actual users are asking for.

Categories to explore:
- **Financial AI:** "I wish AI could look at my books and tell me..." What specific financial questions do they want answered?
- **Marketing AI:** "I wish AI could write my..." What marketing tasks do they want automated?
- **Customer AI:** "I wish AI could handle..." What customer interactions do they want help with?
- **Operations AI:** "I wish AI could track..." What operational tasks do they want monitored?
- **Decision AI:** "I wish AI could tell me whether I should..." What business decisions do they want data-driven support for?

Specifically look for:
- AI features people tried and loved
- AI features people tried and hated (and why)
- AI features people describe wanting that nobody has built yet
- The line between "I'd let AI do this" and "I'd never let AI do this"

## SECTION 4: Features That Drive Retention vs. Features That Drive Signup

Research churn data, retention studies, and user behavior analysis for small business SaaS tools:
- What features do people sign up for?
- What features keep them paying month after month?
- What's the difference? (often they're not the same thing)
- What features do people cite when they cancel? ("I'm leaving because you don't have...")
- What features do people cite when they recommend to others? ("I love this because it does...")

Look for this data in:
- SaaS churn analysis blogs and case studies
- Product-led growth research
- Small business software review patterns on G2 and Capterra
- NPS survey result summaries
- Cancellation reason databases

## SECTION 5: The "I Didn't Know I Needed This" Features

What features in business tools have surprised users — things they didn't ask for but now can't live without?

Examples of this pattern:
- Square's next-day deposits (nobody asked, everyone loves it)
- QuickBooks receipt scanning (solved a problem people didn't articulate)
- Google Business Profile's "popular times" (free insight nobody expected)

What are the equivalent opportunities in 2026? What data does a connected platform have access to that could generate insights nobody is currently asking for but would find incredibly valuable?

Think about:
- Cross-business intelligence (how your business compares to similar ones nearby)
- Predictive alerts (your margins are trending down before you notice)
- Automated compliance (tax deadlines, license renewals, health inspections)
- Weather-correlated planning (tourism/restaurant traffic vs. weather patterns)
- Local economic pulse (what's the overall business health of your town)

## SECTION 6: Feature Pricing Psychology

What are people willing to pay for specific features?

Research pricing pages, Reddit discussions about pricing, and review site comments about value to understand:
- What's a $0 feature? (must be free or nobody signs up)
- What's a $10-20/month feature? (nice to have, easy yes)
- What's a $50-100/month feature? (must demonstrably save time or money)
- What's a $200+/month feature? (must replace a person or another expensive tool)
- What features do people say "I'd pay double for this"?
- What features do people say "this should be free"?

## SECTION 8: The Dashboard Nobody Uses vs. The Alert Everyone Reads

Research patterns in how small business owners actually interact with business software:
- How often do they log into dashboards? (data from analytics/product studies)
- Do they prefer dashboards, email reports, text alerts, or app notifications?
- What's the ideal frequency for business updates? (daily? weekly? only when something's wrong?)
- What format do they prefer? (charts? plain text? bullet points? a single number?)
- Mobile vs. desktop usage patterns for business tools
- Time of day they check business metrics

I want to validate our hypothesis: that most small business owners don't want a dashboard. They want a daily text or email that says "here's your number, here's the one thing that needs attention." If the data supports this, show me. If it doesn't, show me what they actually prefer.

## FORMAT

For each section:
1. Top findings with specific quotes, upvote counts, or engagement data
2. Source (platform, forum, study)
3. Demand level (how many people are asking for this)
4. Build priority recommendation — should we build this for the $0 tier, $20 tier, $99 tier, or $499 tier?
5. Estimated impact on signup, retention, or upgrade conversion

Give me a final ranked list of the top 25 features we should build, ordered by demand × impact × feasibility, with tier placement recommendation for each.
