# Northeast Americana Venue Network

This document catalogues the regional touring infrastructure scaling outward from the Bearsville/Woodstock incubation hub, specifically targeting Americana, country, folk, and blues.

## Intimate Listening Rooms (20–50 Capacity)
These venues are designed primarily as pure listening environments, acoustic testing grounds, or premium ticketed house shows.
*   **Maverick Concert Hall (Woodstock):** A rustic, historic open-air wooden performance hall with pristine acoustic properties. Cultivates a high-end listener base.
*   **Levon Helm Studios (Woodstock):** The definitive focal point for regional Americana. "The Barn", built by The Band's Levon Helm, is an iconic, intimate, high-leverage booking target for premium shows.
*   **The Falcon (Marlboro):** Features a dedicated listening room environment, primarily operating on a sustainable donation-based model that heavily supports emerging roots and jazz circles.

## Bar/Club Circuit (100–300 Capacity)
These rooms support standard amplified acts, merch economies, and local bar traffic.
*   **The Colony (Woodstock):** A historic 1929 property completely overhauled into a cabaret-style room with a prominent outdoor beer garden that acts as a summer hub.
*   **Towne Crier Café (Beacon):** A historic, 50-year-old establishment directly modeled after Greenwich Village folk houses. A hard anchor for singer-songwriters navigating the Hudson Valley.
*   **Arrowood Farms (Accord):** A working brewery and outdoor farm stage highly integrated with alternative folk and Americana touring bills.

## Larger Network Hubs (500+ Capacity)
Mid-tier roadhouse rooms and premium seated theaters for established touring acts pushing out of the Hudson Valley footprint.
*   **Bearsville Theater (Bearsville/Woodstock):** Recently renovated and fundamentally tied to the Grossman management history. A high-production room serving as the logical local capstone.
*   **UPAC - Ulster Performing Arts Center (Kingston):** A 1,500-capacity historic theater serving broader Ulster County, suited for established theater runs that have outgrown the 300-cap clubs.
