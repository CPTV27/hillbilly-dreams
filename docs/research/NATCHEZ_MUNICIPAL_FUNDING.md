# Natchez Municipal Funding & DSD Partnership Matrix

This document maps the public sector capital flows available in Natchez/Adams County to model a B2B Municipal Licensing strategy for Deep South Directory (DSD).

## Local Funding Vectors
1.  **Visit Natchez (Tourism & CVB):**
    *   **Funding Source:** A dedicated 3% tax on hotel/motel rentals and a 1.5% tax on restaurants grossing over $100k.
    *   **Mandate:** "Heads in beds." Driving direct tourism to regional anchors.
2.  **Downtown Natchez Alliance (DNA):**
    *   **Program:** Operates as the official local arm of the Main Street America program.
    *   **Mandate:** Historic preservation, commercial occupancy, and downtown revitalization.

## Federal & State Grant Mechanisms
*   **EDA / ARPA Residuals:** Economic Development Administration grants and residual American Rescue Plan funds are often deployed to digitize local businesses and build "supply chain resilience."
*   **USDA Rural Development (RBDG):** Rural Business Development Grants are explicitly designed to fund technical assistance, training, and business incubators for enterprises in rural areas (Natchez qualifies).
*   **Mississippi Development Authority (MDA):** Provides matching funds for economic expansion and marketing at the county level.

## Positioning DSD as a Municipal Contract
Instead of asking local mom-and-pop shops to surrender $50-$99 a month from razor-thin margins, DSD pitches to the **City of Natchez / Visit Natchez / DNA**.

**The Pitch:** 
"You are currently spending grant money on fragmented billboards and disparate consulting workshops. Re-allocate a fraction of that budget to license the DSD 'Platform Hub.' By subscribing at the municipal level, you instantly grant 100 top Natchez small businesses an enterprise-grade digital footprint, unified SEO gravity, and a done-for-you website. In return, the City receives aggregated, real-time economic data and a unified tourism directory."

*Note: Specific top-line municipal budget figures (the exact annual operating budget of the City of Natchez) fluctuate annually and require direct stakeholder alignment when finalizing the proposal.*
