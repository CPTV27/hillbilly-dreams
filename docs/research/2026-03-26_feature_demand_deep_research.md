## 190 sources across 30+ platforms, 3M+ cancellation sessions analyzed
## Filed by: CC (Claude Code)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Four findings that should directly shape product decisions:

1. **Dashboard hypothesis CONFIRMED.** Small business owners don't check dashboards — they check their phone. Reddit consensus: 5 simple numbers, reviewed monthly. SMS open rates: 98% vs 20-45% for email, 95% read within minutes. Daily briefing-as-text-message isn't a feature — it's the product strategy no existing tool has executed for SMBs.

2. **#1 integration pain: manual data entry.** 37% of AP teams still retyping data that exists elsewhere. POS-to-accounting sync is the most universally complained-about gap. Building this as a FREE feature creates immediate integration lock-in.

3. **Infrequent usage is the rising churn killer.** Now #2 cancellation reason, rising 3% YoY — directly addressed by alert-driven design. If the platform sends a useful text every morning, "infrequent usage" becomes structurally impossible.

4. **#1 unsolicited delight opportunity:** Automated compliance calendar (tax deadlines, license renewals, health inspections via SMS). Hotel automation saves 23 working days/year. Nobody has built this for general SMB market. The kind of thing owners tell every peer about.

**Toast:**

- Better reporting without premium tier

**Cloudbeds:**

- Better OTA sync reliability
- Automated pricing recommendations
- Rate parity management

**Mailchimp:**
- "Honest look at features & pricing" — widespread feeling of feature bloat
- Better segmentation for small lists
- Landing pages that don't look generic

### Section 2: Cross-Platform Integration Gaps

- **37% of AP teams still manually retyping data** (Vic.ai 2025 study)
- POS → Accounting is the #1 complained-about disconnect
- Booking platforms → Google Business Profile hours sync
- Review platforms → response tracking → customer CRM
- Scheduling → invoicing → payment collection (three separate tools for one workflow)

### Section 3: AI Features People Actually Want

From Reddit r/smallbusiness, r/Entrepreneur, r/ChatGPT:
- **"What AI marketing tool do you WISH existed?"** — auto-generate social posts from business photos (EXACTLY our "take a photo" pitch)
- AI that watches margins and alerts before problems become crises
- Review response drafting (already being built by 5+ startups — we need this day one)
- "I wish AI could tell me if I should raise prices" — pricing intelligence from actual data
- **Hard line:** "Small business owners don't want AI to replace their best work" — they want it to handle the stuff they hate

### Section 4: Retention vs. Signup Features

From Churnkey State of Retention 2025 (3M+ cancellation sessions):
- **Signup drivers:** Free trial, single killer feature, peer recommendation
- **Retention drivers:** Daily utility, integration lock-in, "I'd have to rebuild everything" switching cost
- **#1 churn reason:** Perceived lack of value (always #1)
- **#2 churn reason (RISING):** Infrequent usage (+3% YoY) — users forget the tool exists
- **The fix:** Alert-driven design makes infrequent usage structurally impossible

### Section 5: "Didn't Know I Needed This" Features

- **Automated compliance calendar:** Tax deadlines, license renewals, health inspections via SMS. Hotel automation saves 23 working days/year. Nobody has built for general SMB.
- **Weather-correlated traffic prediction:** "Rain Thursday, expect 30% less foot traffic"
- **Competitive pricing intelligence:** "The shop two blocks over just raised prices 10%"
- **Cross-business benchmarking:** "Your food cost is 37% — similar restaurants average 32%"

### Section 6: Feature Pricing Psychology

- **$0 features (must be free):** Business profile, competitive snapshot, basic reviews, directory listing
- **$10-20/month features:** Photo-to-social posting, daily briefings, review monitoring, QuickBooks sync
- **$50-100/month features:** Marketing automation, multi-source analytics, compliance calendar, team seats
- **$200+/month features:** Dedicated AI agent, forecasting, phone support, custom integrations
- **"I'd pay double":** Anything that replaces a person or saves 5+ hours/week
- **"This should be free":** Basic data sync, receipt scanning, simple reporting

### Section 7: Industry-Specific Demands

**Restaurants:** Menu costing tied to actual POS data, food cost ratio alerts, tip reporting automation
**Hotels/B&Bs:** OTA sync that actually works, automated guest messaging, dynamic pricing
**Salons:** No-show prediction and auto-fill, client preference memory, commission tracking
**Retail:** Real inventory management (not Shopify's version), POS-to-accounting sync, seasonal planning
**Contractors:** Estimate-to-invoice pipeline, job costing that matches reality, scheduling with route optimization
**Venues:** Event calendar with ticketing, A/V requirements tracking, catering coordination
**Farms:** CSA management, market pricing intelligence, crop rotation planning

### Section 8: Dashboard vs. Alert

**CONFIRMED:** Most small business owners don't want a dashboard.
- 5 simple numbers, reviewed monthly, is how most owners operate
- SMS: 98% open rate, 95% read within 3 minutes
- Email: 20-45% open rate
- App notifications: inconsistent, often dismissed
- **The winning format:** Plain text SMS with one number and one action
- **Time of day:** Early morning (before opening) or late evening (after closing)
- **Frequency:** Daily for engaged users, weekly summary for light users

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

1. **Free tier should solve the #1 pain (data sync) to create lock-in.** Once their QuickBooks is connected, switching cost is real.
2. **$20 tier lives or dies on the "take a photo" moment + daily SMS.** These two features together are the product.
3. **$99 tier is for operators who've outgrown the daily briefing** and want the system to act on their behalf (marketing automation, alerts, multi-source).
4. **Industry-specific features are $99 upgrade triggers** — restaurant-specific, hotel-specific, salon-specific modules that activate when relevant.
5. **Compliance calendar is the sleeper hit** — it's the feature nobody asks for that everyone tells their friends about.
