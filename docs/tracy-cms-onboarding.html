Writing Your First Article

Nine steps from blank page to live article. Takes about twenty minutes the first time, five minutes once you know it.

  1. Open the studio and click Articles in the left sidebar.
    Screenshot: the left sidebar of Sanity Studio. "Articles" sits near the top with a little document icon.
  2. Click the + button at the top of the article list to create a new article.
    Screenshot: top-right corner of the articles list — the plus icon is the one you want.
  3. Type the title. The slug (that's the URL piece) auto-generates from the title — don't touch it unless you want to. Example: "Ground Zero at Midnight" becomes /articles/ground-zero-at-midnight
  4. Pick a category and city from the dropdowns. Categories: city-guide, feature, photo-essay, interview, food-drink, music. Cities: Memphis, Clarksdale, Natchez, New Orleans, Vicksburg, Greenville, Indianola, Oxford, Tupelo, Helena, Tunica, Jackson, Yazoo City.
  5. Write a one or two sentence excerpt. This is what shows on the magazine homepage — it has to pull people in. Think of it like the first line of a postcard.
  6. Pick a hero image from the library. 229 photos are already loaded — Natchez, the Delta, Memphis, all of it.
    Screenshot: the image picker opens a grid. Click one, click Insert, done.
  7. Write the body. Paragraphs, headings, pull quotes, photo galleries — all of it is in the toolbar at the top of the text box. Click and type. Highlight to format. Pull quotes are great for a line that deserves to stand alone. Photo galleries work for photo essays.
  8. Hit Publish (bottom right). You can also save a draft and come back to it later.
    Screenshot: the Publish button is green, bottom-right of the editor.
  9. Open https://bigmuddymagazine.com — your article is live. Takes about 60 seconds to show up.

The Style Guide

The voice of Big Muddy Magazine. Garden & Gun meets Kinfolk — editorial, not corporate.

Lead with a moment, not a thesis

The heat comes off the asphalt on Union Avenue like something you can hold in your hand.

Memphis is a vibrant cultural hub.

Short sentences. Real places. Real people.

Name the street. Name the cook. Name the song that was playing. Specifics build the world.

Keep paragraphs short

Three to five sentences. Then a break. Give the reader room to breathe.

End with a hook

Close on a city, a meal, a song, a face. Not a summary. Not a call-to-action. A door the reader can walk through.

Words we don't use

  • "ecosystem"
  • "platform"
  • "vertically integrated"
  • Anything that sounds like a deck from a consulting firm

If a sentence could show up in a McKinsey report, cut it.
