# The Artist Equity Model — Tiny Houses for Records

*Locked 2026-04-30 evening by Chase. The trade mechanic that fuses Big Muddy Acres + Big Muddy Records into one engine.*

## The thesis in one sentence

Instead of paying artists cash to make records on Big Muddy Records, we give them tiny houses (or shares of tiny houses) at Big Muddy Acres. Artist gets a piece of property. We get the record. The PR on the trade IS the marketing campaign. Demand for the rest of the property follows.

## The first deal — Robert "Chicken" Burke

Chase + Chicken meeting context: Chicken is producing for Big Muddy Records. Currently the deal is cash-based.

**The new offer:**
- We value Chicken's record at **$50,000**
- He receives either a full tiny house OR a meaningful equity share in one at Big Muddy Acres
- The record gets made, mixed, mastered, released through Big Muddy Records
- Chicken becomes the first resident-artist of Big Muddy Acres

**Why this works for him:**
- Tiny houses + land in rural Mississippi are a real, appreciating asset
- He owns a piece of a real place, not a cash check that disappears
- He becomes part of a cultural community of musicians
- Property income (when he's not there, the unit can be rented through the BMA system) flows back to him
- He's in on the ground floor of something he can talk about for years

**Why this works for us:**
- The record we'd otherwise pay $50K cash for, we pay in equity (real cost: lower than $50K once tiny home build cost is factored in)
- The PR around "musician traded record for tiny house" is genuinely interesting media — gets covered in music press AND real-estate press AND alt-economics press AND lifestyle press
- The story BECOMES the marketing for the rest of Big Muddy Acres — every other artist + retiree + creative who reads about Chicken's deal wants in
- Big Muddy Records has its first signing AND its first marketing engine in one move

## The scale question — how much land do we need?

Chase asked: "How much land can we get?"

If the model proves out, we want enough land for 20-50 artist tiny houses + 50-100 RV sites + the studio + common areas + future build.

**Acreage math:**
- Tiny house pads at decent spacing: ~1 acre per 4-6 units → 20 tiny houses = 4-5 acres dedicated to housing
- RV sites: ~1 acre per 8-10 sites → 50 sites = 5-6 acres
- Studio + common + parking: 2-3 acres
- Buffer + green + future build: 50%+ of total
- **Total minimum: 30-40 acres for full Phase 1+2 vision. Comfortable: 80-120 acres. Aspirational: 200+ acres.**

- Rural land: $1,500-$3,500/acre depending on access, water, road frontage

## What Big Muddy Acres IS — the entity description (2026-04-30)

**Name:** Big Muddy Acres

**Nature:** A standalone operating entity. Not a subsidiary. Not a DBA of another brand.

**Purpose:** Holds the land + buildings + infrastructure of the Mississippi creative community. Operates the RV park, the tiny house program, the artist equity agreements, the studio anchor, the guest hospitality side, and the broader BMA brand network if/when it expands beyond the first parcel.

**Revenue streams:**
- RV site rentals ($500/mo per site recurring)
- Tiny house rentals when residents are away (split with resident per agreement)
- Studio rental (when artist isn't using it for the in-house record)
- Event hosting (festivals, retreats, programmed weekends)
- Long-term ground leases (if applicable)
- Property appreciation (the underlying land + improvements)

**Equity agreements with artists / residents:**
- BMA grants tiny house equity in exchange for non-cash contributions (a record, a body of work, capital improvements, a multi-year residency commitment)
- Each agreement individually structured, signed by BMA + the artist
- BMA retains the property; artist holds an equity stake + use rights + a share of rental income when away

**Relationships to the rest of the ecosystem:**
- Receives recordings from Big Muddy Records as the asset side of artist equity trades
- Hosts touring artists routed by Big Muddy Touring (revenue share or trade)
- Provides editorial subject matter to Big Muddy Magazine + Outsider Economics
- Uses Studio C as its design + tech vendor (chat-based onboarding for new residents, MBT chat for guest questions, etc.)
- The MBT platform handles BMA's customer-facing chat (resident inquiries, booking, escalations to humans)

**What's deferred:**
- LLC vs C-Corp vs LLC + S-corp election — Tracy advises with counsel
- Cap table — open question, depends on capital structure
- Tax structure
- Insurance program (likely tied to the broader Insurance & Risk Management department captured 2026-04-10)

**Bottom line:** BMA is its own thing. It owns the dirt. It owns the buildings. It signs the artists. It collects the rent. The rest of the ecosystem (MBT, Studio C, Records, Touring, Magazine) interacts with it as a peer entity, not as a parent or subsidiary.

## What this isn't

- Not a charity model. Artists earn equity through real work — making real records that real people buy.
- Not a "starving artist relief" pitch. The framing is professional: this is how labels in 2026 should compensate the people who make the music.
- Not for every artist. Selective. Each deal vetted individually.
- Not a substitute for cash advances when cash is what an artist needs. The equity model is an OPTION, not the only path.
