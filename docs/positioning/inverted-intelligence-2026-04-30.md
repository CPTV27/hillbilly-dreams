# Inverted Intelligence

*Concept locked 2026-04-30 by Chase. The narrative through-line for everything MBT does — product, pricing, marketing, programming.*

## The thesis in one sentence

As AI gets better at the work that used to make humans valuable, the value of human work inverts: the things only humans can do — show up, take accountability, hold a room, make a moral call, be in a place — become the premium layer. We priced this correctly first.

## The longer version

For 200 years, "intelligence" meant the cognitive work humans did better than machines: analysis, writing, judgment, design. That was the high-margin work. Manual labor was cheap because machines (and later, software) commoditized it. The premium was on the mind.

Now the inversion: AI handles cognitive work for cents per call. The premium moves to the things AI can't do — being physically present, being accountable to a face, holding institutional knowledge inside a relationship, making a judgment call that someone has to live with. Human-in-the-loop becomes the luxury good. Pure software becomes the floor.

Every business model that priced "human time" at $50-200/hr against "software" at $20/mo seat licenses had it backward. The right pricing in the inverted regime: software cheap (commodity), humans expensive (premium), and the same interface for both.

## How MBT lives this thesis

Our token model IS the inverted intelligence model. The customer pays software-token rates for the AI work. They pay human-token rates — multiples higher — for the human work. Same chat, two meters. The customer sees the price ratio in real time and learns what they're paying for.

This is not theory. It's already built. The chat MVP is live. The Studio C operating company runs the human side. The tier structure (Free → Standard → $100 Service → Operator → Business → Enterprise) maps to how much human exposure the customer needs.

We didn't follow the trend. We saw it and priced for it.

## Where the radio show fits

Big Muddy Radio runs a weekly (or twice-weekly) program titled **Inverted Intelligence**. Format options:
- 30-min weekly essay — Chase + a guest, recorded conversation, lightly edited
- Long-form interview — pull operators, founders, researchers who are seeing the inversion in their own work
- Field reports — Tracy + Amy + others reporting from the inn / bar / road on what humans actually do that AI doesn't
- Listener questions — chat customers ask, the show answers

The show is the marketing — not the other way around. Listeners self-select into the MBT thesis, then convert.

## Where it fits in product marketing

Every customer-facing surface gets a one-line hook tied to the thesis:
- Pricing page tagline: "Software is cheap. Humans are the premium. We bill them differently because they are."
- Onboarding chat opener: "I can answer most things. The hard stuff goes to a human. Either way, you pay for the answer, not the path."
- Tier-upgrade prompt: "You've used the chat for X. The $100 tier connects you to a human when the chat hits a wall. That's where the real value lives."

## Why the timing matters

The trend is visible to anyone paying attention but few have priced for it. Frontier model labs are still selling per-seat or per-token-with-no-human-attached. Consulting firms still bill billable hours without an AI layer. Agencies are bolting AI onto their existing $20K retainers without restructuring the price.

We're early. Not because we're smart — because we built it from the customer side first (Big Muddy Inn needs to actually run on this stack), then noticed the model was generalizable. The marketing window is now: be the people who name the trend, not the people who explain it three years later.
