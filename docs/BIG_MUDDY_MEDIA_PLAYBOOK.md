# Big Muddy Media Playbook
**Version 1.0 — 2026-03-31**
*Operational document for Radio, Magazine, Directory, and Touring. Feeds directly into MBT pitch and entertainment programming.*

---

## The Core Promise

Every MBT customer at $49/mo or above gets placed in front of the Big Muddy audience.
Every show generates downstream Inn, Bar, and Directory revenue at a 2:1 multiplier.
Every piece of content we publish makes the next walk-in pitch easier.

**This is not a content calendar. It's an economic engine with a publishing cadence.**

---

## The Four Arms

### 1. Big Muddy Radio — The Ambient Anchor
**Domain:** bigmuddyradio.com
**Operator:** Chase (tech) + rotating DJ contributors
**Output:** 24/7 curated stream + 1 live show/week minimum

| Cadence | Output | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Daily | Automated playlist (12 genres, rotating) | Always-on presence |
| Weekly | 1 live or recorded show (30-60 min) | Audience hook, share-worthy |
| Monthly | 1 DSD Spotlight segment (5 min feature on a local business) | MBT upsell proof point |

**The MBT promise:** Engine tier ($99/mo) gets one DSD Spotlight per quarter. This is the tangible editorial deliverable that justifies the price.

**Content rules:**
- No corporate radio energy. DJ voice is fun outlaw, college radio meets juke joint.
- Playlists must include at least 1 track from Big Muddy Records artists per hour.
- DSD Spotlight is 5 minutes max, produced like a radio ad, not an interview.

---

### 2. Big Muddy Magazine — The Authority Signal
**Domain:** bigmuddymagazine.com
**Operator:** Chase + freelance contributors (pay scale: $50-200/piece)
**Output:** 1 flagship piece/week + 1 business feature/month

| Cadence | Output | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Weekly | 1 long-form piece (800-2,000 words) | SEO anchor + brand legitimacy |
| Monthly | 1 "Main Street Profile" (business feature) | MBT Engine tier deliverable |
| Quarterly | 1 photo essay (Tuthill Design produces) | Premium content, gallery crossover |

**The MBT promise:** Engine tier ($99/mo) gets one Main Street Profile per year. At 100 Engine customers, that's 100 features — this is the editorial volume constraint to monitor.

**Content rules:**
- Literary, observational. Garden & Gun meets Kinfolk — place as character.
- No listicles. No "Top 10 Things to Do in Natchez."
- Main Street Profiles read like journalism, not advertorials. Business is the subject, not the sponsor.
- Photo essays use Tuthill Design for production. Never stock photography.

**Editorial volume check:** At 100 Engine customers promising annual features, the team needs to produce ~8-9 profiles/month. This is not sustainable with one operator. Begin scouting a managing editor at 50 Engine customers.

---

### 3. Deep South Directory — The Revenue Engine
**Domain:** deepsouthdirectory.com
**Operator:** Chase (walk-in sales, 5 pitches/day)
**Output:** 5 pitches/day, 20% close rate target

| Tier | Price | Media Hook | Pitch line |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| The Listing | $20/mo | None | "Get found. Get alerts. Know your numbers." |
| The Works | $49/mo | Radio mention potential | "Your social media, handled. 4 posts/week." |
| The Engine | $99/mo | Radio Spotlight + Magazine Profile | "Get in front of the audience that stays in Natchez." |

**The walk-in math:**
- 5 pitches/day × 5 days/week = 25 pitches/week
- 20% close rate = 5 new customers/week
- 20 weeks to 100 customers (mid-August 2026 at April 1 launch)
- At 40/35/25 tier split: **$4,555/mo recurring by August**

**The media pitch (Engine tier close):**
> "At $99, you're not buying software. You're buying a seat in the Big Muddy ecosystem. Your business gets featured in the magazine, spotlighted on the radio, and connected to everyone who books the Inn and buys tickets to the shows. That's the audience we've built. You're just plugging into it."

**Honest competitive position:** Until Radio Spotlight and Magazine Profile features are fully operational, pitch The Works ($49) as the top tier. Do not promise Engine deliverables until the editorial pipeline can fulfill them. Claim ladder: $20 April 1 → $49 April 21 → $99 April 14 per existing schedule.

### 4. Big Muddy Touring — The Physical Anchor
**Domain:** bigmuddytouring.com
**Operator:** Tracy + Amy (Inn ops), Chase (digital)
**Output:** 4-6 anchor weekends/quarter + year-round lodging

| Cadence | Output | Revenue path |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Weekly | Inn availability + event calendar | Direct booking revenue |
| Per show | Event page (Entertainment → Touring crosslink) | Show night ecosystem multiplier |
| Monthly | "Corridor" travel feature (Magazine crosslink) | Editorial SEO + booking conversion |

**The ecosystem multiplier in practice:**
- Show announced on bigmuddyentertainment.com
- Radio promotes the show for 2 weeks prior
- Magazine publishes a "what to do in Natchez this weekend" anchor piece
- Touring page drives lodging bookings for that weekend
- Every $1 in show ticket revenue generates $0.50 in Inn + Directory revenue

**Tracking requirement:** Finance must tag all Inn bookings with show attribution. "Did you book because of the show?" is one field on the confirmation page. This data validates the 2:1 claim and strengthens the MBT pitch.

## What MBT Actually Sells

The software is the plumbing. The media is the product.

| Software feature | Why it matters |
|-----------------|---------------|

| Google review alerts | Gives Chase a reason to call — "saw you got a review, here's what to say" |
| Monthly report | Proof the $20 is working, upsell hook for $49 |
| Social posting (4/week) | The Works closes because nobody wants to do social |
| Magazine Profile | The Engine closes because no local media has done this before |
| Radio Spotlight | Turns a business owner into a local personality |

## Hard Rules for All Four Arms

1. **Never promise a media deliverable you can't produce this week.** If Radio isn't live, don't pitch the Radio Spotlight. If Magazine has no managing editor, cap Engine sales at 50 until you do.

2. **Main Street Profiles are journalism, not ads.** If a business owner tries to dictate coverage, decline the feature. The value is in editorial independence.

3. **Big Muddy Entertainment is the show arm.** It does not speak for Magazine, Radio, or Touring. Those brands have their own voices and their own operators.

4. **MBT has a hard firewall from Big Muddy.** Different fonts, different feel, different tone. The directory and the magazine share an ecosystem — they do not share a brand identity.

5. **Tracy and Amy are equity partners, not employees.** Inn ops decisions belong to them. Chase does not override hospitality decisions.
