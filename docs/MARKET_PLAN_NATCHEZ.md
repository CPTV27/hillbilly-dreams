# MBT Market Plan: Natchez, Mississippi

*Customer Zero. The proof market. Updated 2026-04-08.*

---

## Why Natchez

Natchez is a 14,000-person town with 700,000 annual visitors, 400+ new businesses since 2020, and $100M in building permits. Commercial air service starts July 2026 (SkyWest/United to Houston). The Eola Hotel ($31M Marriott Tribute) opens 2027. Trace Town Shopping Center ($30M, 150 jobs) opens spring 2026. Another $40M retail development from Horne Properties is closing nearby.

This is a town in a genuine boom cycle with dense institutional infrastructure for its size. MBT doesn't need to convince Natchez that activation matters — they're already investing. MBT gives them the coordination layer to make it measurable.

Big Muddy is already here. The Inn, the touring circuit, the magazine, the radio, the directory — all running. Natchez isn't a cold pitch. It's proving what we already built works for the whole town, not just our properties.

## The Pitch by Stakeholder

### To Mayor Gibson / City of Natchez
"You're telling the 'Natchez is Booming' story with press releases and ribbon cuttings. MBT gives you a live dashboard: how many businesses are active, how many are getting found by visitors, what the foot traffic looks like, where the gaps are. When you go to Jackson or Washington for funding, you hand them a report with real numbers — not anecdotes."

### To Chandler Russ / Natchez Inc.
"You just got a funding increase. Your job is business recruitment and retention. MBT gives you a directory of every active business in town, their visibility metrics, their participation in the local economy — and a system to onboard new businesses when they land. The Trace Town tenants, the Eola vendors, the Horne Properties retailers — they all need to be findable on day one."

### To Devin Heath / Visit Natchez
"700,000 visitors a year come to Natchez. Right now they Google 'things to do in Natchez' and get Tripadvisor and Yelp. MBT puts a real directory in their hands — powered by the same system that publishes the magazine, runs the radio, and promotes the shows. Every listing has real photos, real reviews, real editorial. You stop losing visitors to platforms that don't care about Natchez."

### To Leah Gibson Hunter / Downtown Natchez Alliance
"You're running a nationally accredited Main Street program. MBT is the operating system for that program. You onboard downtown businesses, they get listed, promoted, and measured. You get a monthly report showing participation rates, foot traffic, new businesses, gaps in the retail mix. Your board sees real data, not guesswork."

### To a Broker (Paul Green, Crye-Leike, etc.)
"Every business you sponsor into the program makes your listings more valuable. A vibrant Main Street with visible, promotable businesses sells properties. You sponsor 20 businesses at $99/mo each — that's $2K/mo for a monthly report showing your street is alive. Compare that to the ROI on one property sale."

## Rollout Plan

### Phase 1: Proof (Now — already running)
- Big Muddy Inn, Touring, Magazine, Radio, Records all operating
- Biscuits & Blues / Regina Charboneau as external test
- Directory with 50+ Natchez businesses seeded
- Shows generating content and audience

### Phase 2: First Institutional Conversation (April-May 2026)
- **Target:** Downtown Natchez Alliance (Leah Gibson Hunter) or Natchez Inc. (Chandler Russ)
- **Ask:** Pilot program — onboard 20-50 downtown businesses under the MBT umbrella
- **Offer:** Setup + 6-month pilot license at founding rate. Monthly outcome reports.
- **Proof:** Walk them through Big Muddy as the live case study

### Phase 3: Multi-Stakeholder Stack (June-August 2026)
- Once DNA or Natchez Inc. is on board, approach the others
- Visit Natchez for the tourism layer
- Chamber for member services
- One brokerage for the property value play
- Programs stack — each gets their own reporting, same underlying platform

### Phase 4: Regional (Q4 2026+)
- SWMPDD conversation — "Natchez works, let's do Adams County, then the 10-county region"
- Delta Regional Authority funding for regional deployment
- Each county gets its own Program under the same Organization
