## What Needs a Human vs What AI Handles

### Must Be Human
- Relationship building (venue visits, band meetings, Regina conversations)
- Final creative approval (Chase's photographer eye)
- Deal negotiation (band guarantees, venue splits, record deals)
- Gallery curation decisions (Tracy's taste)
- Live show operations (Amy's bar/sound/stage management)
- Sales conversations (walking into a business, shaking hands)

### AI Handles
- Content generation (social posts, article drafts, radio promos)
- Photo processing and tagging (Vision API, Bridge, Mac Mini)
- Campaign automation (release schedules, social posting via Postiz)
- Data enrichment (Google Places, embedding pipeline)
- Financial modeling (scenario analysis, budget projections)
- Scheduling and logistics (tour routing, show calendar)
- Voice AI (Southern Concierge for customer queries)
- Report generation (monthly report cards, analytics)

### Hybrid (Human Decision, AI Execution)
- Magazine articles (Tracy/Amy provide knowledge → AI structures → they approve)
- Band scouting (Amy identifies targets → R&D agent researches → Amy decides)
- Marketing campaigns (Chase sets strategy → AI generates content → Chase approves)
- Directory listings (business owner provides info → AI enriches → listing goes live)

## Agent Sales Rep Model

For scaling beyond Chase/Tracy/Amy:

### How It Works
1. Recruit local people as "Big Muddy Agents" (sales reps)
2. Each agent owns a territory or vertical
3. They earn commission on recurring revenue they bring in
4. The longer their clients stay, the more they earn (retention incentive)
5. They also earn from ecosystem participation (referrals across verticals)

### The Incentive Structure
- **Commission:** % of first-year revenue for each client signed
- **Residual:** Smaller % of ongoing revenue as long as client stays
- **Ecosystem bonus:** If a DSD client also books a show, the agent who brought them gets credit
- **Tiered advancement:** More clients = higher commission rate
- **Non-monetary:** Featured in Magazine, invited to shows, part of the inner circle

### Who We're Looking For
- People who already know the businesses in their town
- Musicians who know the venue owners
- Hospitality people who know the restaurant and hotel owners
- Not tech salespeople — relationship people who happen to sell a tech product
