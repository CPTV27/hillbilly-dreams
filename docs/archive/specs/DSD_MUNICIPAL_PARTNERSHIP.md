# DSD Municipal Partnership Structure

## The Opportunity
City governments and regional tourism bureaus possess marketing budgets designed to stimulate local economic development. Instead of selling ads to individual struggling business owners, DSD functions as an infrastructural asset that the municipality can license to support the *entire* commercial ecosystem.

## Viable Budget Constraints
Marketing funnels for DSD integration draw from three traditional municipal sub-budgets:
1.  **Tourism Promotion (Visit Natchez):** Funded by the 3% hospitality tax / 1.5% restaurant gross proceeds tax. Designed specifically to drive foot traffic to local anchors.
2.  **Economic Development (Natchez Inc. / Adams County):** Seeded by both local coffers and Mississippi Development Authority (MDA) grants focused on revitalizing commercial density.
3.  **Chamber of Commerce / Main Street Grants:** Matching funds dedicated to commercial digitalization and "Main Street" retention.

## The Proposal Template (B2B Licensing)

**Objective:** Equip every local small business with enterprise-grade marketing and networking infrastructure at zero direct cost to the merchant.

**Structure:**
*   **The Buy:** The municipality (e.g., Visit Natchez) subsidizes an enterprise multi-seat DSD license block.
*   **The Distribution:** The city grants $99/mo DSD "Marketing Tier" seats to participating local businesses (restaurants, boutiques, hotels) as an economic development stimulus.
*   **The ROI for the City:** The city gains access to the aggregated DSD data layer—giving them unprecedented, real-time heat maps of tourism traffic, local hiring velocity, and aggregate business health derived from our backend platform. 

## Case Study Target: Adams County
*   **Target Entity:** Visit Natchez & Natchez Inc.
*   **Mechanism:** Divert a fractional percentage of the existing 3% short-term rental/hotel tax to underwrite 100 deep-tier DSD licenses.
*   **Output:** 100 "shovel-ready" digital businesses, unified under one regional directory, generating cohesive inbound touring gravity. (*Note: Specific line-item appropriation budgets require verification during direct stakeholder pitching.*)
