## The Deal in One Paragraph

Big Muddy Entertainment invests ~$10K in production equipment. The equipment joins Studio C's existing inventory (~$30-50K). Studio C now has substantial gear in both Natchez and Bearsville. In exchange for the capital, Studio C provides production hours and creative leadership to build out the content platform. Both parties share in revenue from content produced. Same model Pete Caigan used at Utopia — invest in the equipment, become a revenue partner in everything it produces.

## The Exchange Model

### Option A: Straight CapEx + Revenue Share
- Big Muddy Entertainment invests $10K in equipment
- Equipment goes to Studio C inventory
- Studio C provides equivalent hours of production labor (~40-50 hours at $200-250/hr)
- Ongoing: Studio C earns revenue share on all content produced with the equipment
- Split: 60% Big Muddy / 40% Studio C on client revenue, or negotiate per project

### Option B: Credit Exchange (MBT Platform)
- Big Muddy invests $10K in equipment
- $10K in MBT credits issued to Big Muddy
- Studio C draws credits for production work (hourly rate in credits)
- Credits offset against future revenue share
- This uses the CreditLedger system already built in the platform

### Option C: Hybrid (Recommended)
- Big Muddy invests $10K in equipment → Studio C inventory
- Studio C provides 30 hours of production labor (immediate — building out content platform)
- Studio C provides ongoing production at preferred rate ($150/hr vs $250 market rate)
- Revenue share on DSD Engine clients: Studio C gets 30% of the photography/video component
- Revenue share on external Studio C clients: Big Muddy gets 20% (platform/marketing referral fee)
- Both markets (Natchez + Bearsville) covered under one agreement

## The Pete Caigan Precedent

This mirrors the Utopia Studios model:
- Pete invested in facility access and equipment at Utopia
- Chase owns all equipment outright
- Pete is a revenue share partner on venue work only
- Both benefit from the facility being active and producing

The difference: this deal is between Big Muddy Entertainment (Chase/Tracy/Amy entity) and Studio C (Chase 40% owner + Elijah/Miles). Chase is on both sides — which means the deal needs to be fair and documented, not just a handshake.
