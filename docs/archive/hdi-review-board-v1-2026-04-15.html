HILLBILLY DREAMS INC

Business Architecture Review

Purpose: Walk the full architecture. Defend the revenue. Find the weak points.

The Thesis

HDI is a regional music-hospitality-media operating company. Three verticals — Hospitality, Entertainment, Local Marketing — anchored in Natchez, Mississippi. Shows feed rooms. Rooms anchor weddings. Magazine + radio drive bookings. The whole thing runs as one ecosystem.

How We Run It

Three equity partners — Chase, Tracy, and Amy — plus a small bench of contributors and partners (Sean Davis on bookings, Studio C / Tuthill on production, Bearsville on the northeast). Each partner leads the work closest to their strengths. We move together. When the business grows enough to need more hands, we add them.

The 2:1 Multiplier

Every show feeds every revenue stream. A $1,000 show generates $500+ downstream — Inn bookings, bar sales, magazine features, radio rotation, record promotion. Entertainment is the engine. Hospitality is the flywheel.

Three Verticals, One Company

Hospitality (Tracy + Amy). Entertainment (Chase, with Sean on bookings). Local Marketing — Deep South Directory + publicity packages (Chase, then handoff). One brand portfolio, one team, one P&L.

What HDI Does

Three operating verticals. Clean ownership. No platform-licensing distractions on the Y1 critical path.

The Musician Catalog — A Place Where Musicians Should Be

Y1 target: 30 profiles by July, 100 by year-end. Not a thousand. Thirty real bands. Curated, not auto-imported.

Chase meets a band at a show. Hands them a card with a QR code. They scan, build their page in 5 minutes:

  • Pick 3 influences (forces taste)
  • Upload 3 best songs (forces curation)
  • 3 favorite photos (forces selection)
  • 1 video (forces choosing)
  • 2-sentence bio (forces brevity)
  • Opt-ins: radio rotation, corridor booking, studio recording

Built into DSD, not separate. Every band page feeds the radio submission queue, venue matching, magazine content. Free for every musician forever — revenue comes from publicity packages they choose to buy on top.

Decisions

What we just decided. The moves that define the next 30 days.

1. HDI is a 3-vertical operating company

Hospitality, Entertainment, Local Marketing. Three clean verticals with named owners. No platform-licensing distractions on the Y1 critical path.

2. Entertainment is the engine

Big Muddy Touring + Magazine + Radio + Records. Sean and the new Entertainment Director drive shows, which feed Inn bookings, bar revenue, magazine features, radio rotation. The 2:1 multiplier rides on this.

3. The Venue Directory is our operational moat

Lives as a feature of bigmuddytouring.com. Our database + our relationships. Bookers don't pay for databases — they pay for relationships. Chase and Sean hydrate it in person, corridor city by corridor city.

4. DSD is HDI walk-in, not licensed out

Chase walks Main Street, leads with the $25 tier, sells the directory + marketing service in person. Once the script and rep playbook are proven, hand off to a part-time rep or rep team. ~$120K Y1 target.

5. The Movement is the narrative arm

Outsider Economics + Born Free, broadcast from Natchez on Twitter + TikTok + YouTube. Not a revenue business — authority. The Deep South origin gives us a story nobody in SF or Brooklyn can tell. Every other bet gets more legible because of it.

6. Radio brand pivots to podcast + playlists + YouTube

Kill the 24/7 Icecast stream. Keep the Big Muddy Radio brand. Launch the podcast as the networking + guest tool. Spotify + Apple playlists for record label promo. YouTube for the leverage. If warranted later, we grow back into real radio broadcast.

7. Magazine = Inn marketing channel

Tracy edits in her voice. AI drafts, she approves. Every article drives Inn bookings. Sponsorships are upside on top.

8. Sean Davis joins the team now

Not an "open seat" or an outside hire. Sean's on the team, starting with the corridor runs — relationship hydration, field research, venue database. Grows into lead booker as show calendar fills.

9. Hire the Entertainment Director — URGENT

JP Houston vacated. The role is structured as a self-funding rev-share position ($50K+ commission upside) so it doesn't require runway. Without the ED, the calendar fills slower and the multiplier underperforms. Most important Q2 hire.

10. 50/50 with artists on NET

Door, merch, streaming, publishing — 50/50, after costs are recouped. We split the profit, not the gross. Costs come off the top. Simple enough to explain in one sentence.

11. Build the wedding pipeline

Single weddings = 12-17x a regular weekend. Tracy + Chase build a landing page, a referral kit, and pitch event planners across Natchez and the corridor. Target: 8 weddings Y1.

What We're Not Doing

Every "no" is a gift to the four things we're doing. Naming what's off the table = leadership.

Selling the platform to other towns this year

The technology underneath HDI is being pulled out as a separate venture. Licensing it to other regions, towns, and chambers is a Y2 question with its own timeline. See the venture brief. Y1 priority: HDI ships.

Enterprise / institutional sales pipeline

No 90-180 day enterprise cycles. No tourism office pilots. No chamber pitches. Those conversations belong to the separate technology venture, not HDI.

Artist Packages ($99/$250/$500)

3 people can't run a subscription service for dozens of artists. 50/50 on NET replaces it. Cleaner, simpler, scales with shows.

Bundle Packages

Those were leftover product bundles from a previous SaaS framing. Killed. DSD is the walk-in subscription product; nothing else needs a bundle.

Musician Platform

"MySpace for Deep South musicians" was a concept. Killed. If it ever ships, a partner builds it.

Born Free as a hardware business

Shipping pre-flashed Raspberry Pis at scale isn't the play. Born Free lives as open source on GitHub (see The Movement). Build-to-order kits are upside, not a business.

Land & Expand — even Marika pilot

$150K distraction right now. Talk to Marika, don't plan around it. Come back after the operating company stabilizes.

Bearsville activation beyond domain

Elijah + Miles keep doing what they're doing. We don't build or staff for it in Q2. Summer 2026 remains the earliest.

Where MBT Went

A quick footnote on a question you might have.

HDI runs on MBT software — that's the implementation detail customers don't need to know. But MBT-as-a-business — licensing the platform to other regions, towns, chambers — is its own question with its own timeline, its own sales motion, and its own cap table.

Y1 priority: HDI ships. The operating company stands on its own. The platform venture answers a different question on a different clock.

If the question comes up in this review: it's not a Y1 HDI conversation. Park it for the venture brief.
