Bookers don't pay for databases. They pay for relationships. That's what this is. Every corridor city we touch gets added. Every venue owner we meet gets a page. Over time, this becomes the most valuable asset in the business — more valuable than any individual show.

Decisions

What we just decided. The moves that define the next 30 days.

1. Touring is the business

We're running a music company. Big Muddy Touring is the engine — we book the corridor, run the promo, drive the van. Everything else (Inn, weddings, media, licensing) either feeds touring or rides on it.

2. The Venue Directory is our operational moat

Lives as a feature of bigmuddytouring.com. Our database + our relationships. Bookers don't pay for databases — they pay for relationships. Chase and Sean hydrate it in person, corridor city by corridor city.

3. DSD is licensed out via MBT Civic

We don't operate DSD. We facilitate one named deal — Paul Green Real Estate + City of Natchez as co-sponsors — then step back. Partners run it. Combined budget target: $5-10K/mo. Pitched as a regional tourism + marketing play, not SaaS.

4. The Movement is the narrative arm

Outsider Economics + Born Free, broadcast from Natchez on Twitter + TikTok + YouTube. Not a revenue business — authority. The Deep South origin gives us a story nobody in SF or Brooklyn can tell. Every other bet gets more legible because of it.

5. Radio brand pivots to podcast + playlists + YouTube

Kill the 24/7 Icecast stream. Keep the Big Muddy Radio brand. Launch the podcast as the networking + guest tool. Spotify + Apple playlists for record label promo. YouTube for the leverage. If warranted later, we grow back into real radio broadcast.

6. Magazine = Inn marketing channel

Tracy edits in her voice. AI drafts, she approves. Every article drives Inn bookings. Doesn't need its own revenue — it IS the marketing budget.

7. Sean Davis joins the team now

Not an "open seat" or an outside hire. Sean's on the team, starting with the corridor runs — relationship hydration, field research, venue database. Grows into dedicated booker as show calendar fills.

8. 50/50 with artists on everything

Door, merch, streaming, publishing — 50/50. No complicated contracts. Simple enough to explain in one sentence.

What We're Not Doing

Every "no" is a gift to the four things we're doing. Naming what's off the table = leadership.

DSD walk-in sales

Not walking Main Street pitching $99/mo subscriptions. DSD is a licensed product, not a sales motion Chase runs.

Artist Packages ($99/$250/$500)

3 people can't run a subscription service for dozens of artists. 50/50 on everything replaces it. Cleaner, simpler, scales with shows.

Bundle Packages

Those were DSD product bundles — they go with DSD when it's licensed out. Not Chase's product catalog.

Musician Platform

"MySpace for Deep South musicians" was a concept. Killed. If it ever ships, a partner builds it.

Born Free as a hardware business

Shipping pre-flashed Raspberry Pis to customers isn't the play. Born Free lives as open source on GitHub (see The Movement). If someone wants hardware, a partner ships it.

Land & Expand — even Marika pilot

$150K distraction right now. Talk to Marika, don't plan around it. Come back after touring revenue proves.

MBT enterprise sales outreach

90-day sales cycles to institutions steal from show bookings. Inbound only. Natchez pilot is the one exception because the budget already exists.

Bearsville activation beyond domain

Elijah + Miles keep doing what they're doing. We don't build or staff for it in Q2. Summer 2026 remains the earliest.
