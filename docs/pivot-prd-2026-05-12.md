## 1. Executive summary

Big Muddy Touring has pivoted from "multi-brand ecosystem with a productizable platform underneath" to **"two umbrella brands (Big Muddy Touring + Big Muddy Inn) anchored by destination events, funded by Inn + Chase Pierson Photography revenue, evolving toward a regional cultural institution."**

This PRD maps the work needed to make the pivot real. There are **10 workstreams** spanning brand, content, infrastructure, legal, partner communications, and event launch. Some are 30-minute edits; some are multi-week migrations. Several are blocked by open decisions Chase hasn't made yet (infrastructure A/B/C; Y1 event flagship; legal entity name; DSD fate).

**Top-line shape of the work:**
- ~30 doc and PDF updates (brand, framing, language)
- ~6 domain decisions (retire, redirect, park)
- 1 infrastructure migration (Option A or B) OR 1 cleanup pass (Option C)
- 1 partner comms cycle (Tracy + Amy alignment on the pivot)
- 1 legal entity decision + counsel touch-base
- 1 destination event launch (Y1 flagship)
- ~5 Asana task updates / new tasks

**Estimated effort:** 60–120 hours of Chase time over ~12 weeks, plus partner input on 4–6 specific decisions, plus counsel time on the legal track.

## 2. Strategic context — decisions already locked

These are NOT in scope for re-debate during execution. They are foundational.

1. **Drop MBT as a B2B-licensable platform (Job Two).** MBT is not sold to external markets.
2. **Drop MBT as a brand entirely.** No "Measurably Better Things" in any new content. No "Powered by Measurably Better Things" anywhere.
3. **Two-umbrella public brand picture:**
   - **Big Muddy Touring** — entertainment + media (Radio, Magazine, Records, Directory under it)
   - **Big Muddy Inn** — sister hospitality brand
   - Partner studios (CPP, Studio C, Tuthill) sit alongside as independent collaborators
4. **Three inputs, one bet** operating model: Inn = stability engine; CPP = founder subsidy / creative capital; BMT = growth bet funded by the other two.
5. **Cultural authority via destination events** is the strategic North Star for BMT — not platform productization, not maximum touring volume.
6. **No generic festival drift.** Doctrine: BMT competes on intimacy + regional specificity + documentation + aesthetic cohesion. 40–150 person events. Sponsorship as symbolic capital.
7. **Bearsville Creative defers to Y2+.** Chase's personal Hudson Valley residence is independent of brand activation.
8. **Photography is an explicitly protected revenue pillar**, not invisible support infrastructure.

Any decision in this PRD that conflicts with the above gets re-litigated against these, not against the PRD.
