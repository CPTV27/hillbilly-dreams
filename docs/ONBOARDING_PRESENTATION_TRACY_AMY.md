---

## Slide 2: The Big Change — What MBT Is Now

**MBT (Measurably Better Things)** is the technology that powers everything above. We built it for ourselves. Now we're licensing it to other communities.

**The old plan:** Sell $99/month subscriptions to individual businesses. One at a time. Door to door.

**The new plan:** Sell the whole program to a town, a chamber of commerce, a real estate brokerage, or a tourism bureau. They pay for the platform. Businesses join under their umbrella.

**Why this is better:**
- One institutional sale = 20-50 businesses onboarded at once
- Higher revenue per deal ($5,000-$50,000 vs $99/month)
- The institution does the selling for us — they want their businesses to participate
- Stickier relationships — a city doesn't cancel a platform the way an individual cancels a subscription

## Slide 5: The Multi-Stakeholder Model

In Natchez, multiple institutions can buy in at the same time:

1. **The City / Tourism Bureau** — pays for the directory, gets tourism metrics and grant reporting
2. **A Real Estate Broker** — sponsors 20 businesses on Main Street, gets monthly vitality reports (vibrant street = higher property values)
3. **Hospitality Cluster** — the Inn and nearby properties coordinate promotion, cross-sell

These programs stack. Each stakeholder pays separately. Each gets their own reporting. The businesses participate in one or more programs.

**The broker play:** A broker sponsors 20 businesses at $99/month each ($2,000/month). Those businesses get free listings, promotion, measurement. The broker gets a monthly report proving the street is alive. Their properties are worth more.
