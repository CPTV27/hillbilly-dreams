# `@mbt/modules/finance`

The MBT Finance Module (Phase C Block 3)

## 1. Purpose

This module is the core financial engine for the MBT platform, responsible for tracking, calculating, and reporting all key financial flows related to module usage and internal resource consumption. It owns the critical Tier 3 module engagement tracking, which underpins MBT's recurring revenue model, alongside Studio C bucket-hour consumption, granular per-entity Profit & Loss (P&L) statements, a comprehensive recurring revenue dashboard, and cross-entity invoicing. It is foundational for scaling MBT's platform monetization strategy and ensuring financial transparency across all associated entities.

## 4. Tier 3 Mechanism

The MBT platform operates with a tiered approach to how its modules are utilized and monetized, primarily distinguishing between internal use, one-off vendor services, and recurring module-powered services.

*   **Tier 1: Internal Use**
    *   **Description:** MBT itself, or its directly owned subsidiaries (e.g., Big Muddy Natchez LLC, Big Muddy Touring LLC), utilizes MBT modules for their own operational needs. This includes internal CRM, project management, content delivery, or website hosting for Big Muddy Natchez's venues.
    *   **Monetization:** No external monetization or platform fee is applied. Costs are internal resource allocations (e.g., Studio C hours) and are tracked purely for internal P&L.
    *   **Example:** MBT LLC uses the `ContentManager` module to manage blog posts on `mbt.com` and the `EventScheduler` module for internal team meetings.

*   **Tier 2: One-off Vendor Services**
    *   **Description:** An external vendor uses an MBT module to deliver a single, non-recurring service or product to their client. The engagement is transactional, not subscription-based from the vendor's side.
    *   **Monetization:** MBT typically charges a one-time fee to the vendor for module access or usage, but **does not** take a recurring platform fee percentage from the vendor's revenue, as the vendor's service is not recurring.
    *   **Example:** A local photography studio (vendor) uses MBT's `GalleryShowcase` module for a one-time online exhibition of their client's wedding photos. They pay MBT a flat fee for the module's use for that event.

*   **Tier 3: Recurring Module-Delivered Services**
    *   **Description:** This is the primary monetization strategy for the MBT platform. A vendor uses one or more MBT modules as a fundamental component to power a *recurring service* they offer to their own customers. The MBT module is integrated into the vendor's ongoing business model.
    *   **Monetization:** MBT takes a **platform fee percentage** (typically 15-30%) of the recurring revenue that the vendor generates from their customers through these module-powered services. This is tracked via the `ModuleEngagement` model.
    *   **Example:** A Tuthill realtor (vendor) offers a "Premium Listing Management" service to their clients, which includes automated listing updates, lead capture forms, and client communication, all powered by MBT's `ListingManager` and `CRMIntegrator` modules. If the realtor charges their client $100/month for this service, and MBT has a 20% platform fee, MBT invoices the realtor for $20/month. This forms a significant portion of MBT's Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR).

The Finance module's `ModuleEngagement` model and `calculatePlatformFee` function are specifically designed to manage and calculate fees for Tier 3 engagements, which are central to MBT's long-term revenue growth.

## 5. Bucket-Hour Math (Studio C)

Studio C, operated by Tuthill Design LLC, provides specialized development and design hours for the MBT platform and its associated entities.
